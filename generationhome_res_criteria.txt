# GENERATION HOME – RESIDENTIAL LENDING CRITERIA

---

**LENDER:** Generation Home
**PRODUCT:** Residential
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Lending Criteria
**LAST UPDATED:** 2025
**FOR INTERMEDIARY USE ONLY**

**Source:** [https://intermediaries.generationhome.com/criteria](https://intermediaries.generationhome.com/criteria)

This document contains comprehensive residential mortgage lending criteria covering all topics from the Generation Home intermediaries website.

---

## SECTION 1 – 90% New Build LTV Criteria

**Condition:** Applies to new build properties only.

**Requirement:** Clean credit history over the last 6 months.

**Definition:** Clean credit history means 0 missed payments over this period.

**Requirement:** At least 50% of the owners' total income must be derived from permanent employed income.

**Not acceptable:** Income received from a director's own limited company cannot count towards the 50% permanent employed income requirement.

**Not acceptable:** Income from fixed term contracts cannot count towards the 50% permanent employed income requirement.

**Not acceptable:** Income from zero hour contracts cannot count towards the 50% permanent employed income requirement.

**Not acceptable:** Applications where the owners have no employed income.

**Condition:** Maximum loan amounts apply.

---

## SECTION 2 – 95% LTV Criteria

**Not acceptable:** 95% lending on any new build properties.

**Requirement:** Clean credit history over the last 6 months.

**Definition:** Clean credit history means 0 missed payments over this period.

**Requirement:** At least 50% of the owners' total income must be derived from permanent employed income.

**Not acceptable:** Income received from a director's own limited company cannot count towards the 50% permanent employed income requirement.

**Not acceptable:** Income from fixed term contracts cannot count towards the 50% permanent employed income requirement.

**Not acceptable:** Income from zero hour contracts cannot count towards the 50% permanent employed income requirement.

**Not acceptable:** Applications where the owners have no employed income.

**Condition:** Maximum loan amounts apply.

---

## SECTION 3 – Absent Freeholder

**Allowed:** Properties with an absent or insolvent freeholder.

**Condition:** Only acceptable in blocks of up to 6 flats.

**Requirement:** An appropriate indemnity policy must be obtained on completion.

---

## SECTION 4 – Acreage

**Maximum:** Land up to 2 acres.

---

## SECTION 5 – Additional Borrowing (Existing Customers)

**Not available:** Additional borrowing through further advances to existing customers.

---

## SECTION 6 – Additional Duty / Responsibility Income

**Allowed:** Income from guaranteed additional duties or responsibilities.

**Weighting:** 100%.

---

## SECTION 7 – Address Verification

**Requirement:** Current address must be verified for all customers, including owners, income boosters and deposit boosters.

**Process:** Electoral roll verification attempted first.

### Documentary Proof of Address (Where Electoral Roll Fails)

**Requirement:** 1 documentary proof of address.

**Acceptable documents:**

* Bank statement dated within the last 3 months
* Credit card statement dated within the last 3 months
* Utility bill dated within the last 3 months
* Mortgage statement dated within the last 12 months
* Council tax demand dated within the last 12 months
* Current full UK/EU driving licence
* Current UK/EU photo provisional driving licence
* HMRC or government issued letter, statement or demand dated within the last 12 months

**Condition:** A driving licence used as proof of identity cannot also be used as proof of address.

**Not acceptable:** Optional services such as cable TV, internet or mobile phones as utility bills.

---

## SECTION 8 – Administration Order

**Not acceptable:** Applications from customers who, in the last 6 years, have entered or intend to enter into an administration order.

---

## SECTION 9 – Adoption Allowance

**Allowed:** Adoption allowance.

**Condition:** Must be guaranteed.

**Weighting:** 100%.

---

## SECTION 10 – Adverse Credit (General)

**Requirement:** All owners and income boosters are credit checked.

**Condition:** A fully submitted mortgage application will leave a hard credit footprint.

**Requirement:** Individuals associated with the owner(s) through continuing linked finances, marriage, or civil partnerships must be added to the application.

**Condition:** If not included, independent legal advice is required.

**Condition:** Proceeding without inclusion is subject to underwriter discretion.

**Condition:** Each customer is subject to the same checks whether contributing to the mortgage or not.

---

## SECTION 11 – Age

### Minimum Age

**Requirement:** At least 1 customer must be aged over 21.

**Requirement:** All remaining customers must be aged over 18.

### Maximum Age

**Maximum (sole customer):** 75 at end of term.

**Maximum (multiple customers):** Loan must be repaid by the eldest customer's 85th birthday.

**Condition:** Term may extend beyond an income booster's 85th birthday using ejector seat.

**Condition:** Ejector seat applied automatically when calculating maximum term.

---

## SECTION 12 – Agricultural Ties

**Not acceptable:** Working farms, smallholdings, and houses subject to an agricultural occupancy restriction.

**Calculation:** Average over last 2 years.

**Entry field:** Investment Income.

**Weighting:** 50%.

**Evidence Required:** Last 2 years' tax calculations and corresponding tax year overviews (TYOs).

## SECTION 19 – Bank Work

**Allowed (case-by-case):** Bank work income with 12 months' continuous employment within similar role.

**Allowed (case-by-case):** NHS bank work with 3 months' bank work history.

**Treatment:** Bank work treated as zero-hours contract.

**Weighting:** 100%.

## SECTION 20 – Bankruptcy / Sequestration Order

**Not acceptable:** Customers who, in the last 6 years, have entered or intend to enter bankruptcy or a sequestration order.

---

## SECTION 21 – Bedroom Count

**Allowed (case-by-case):** More than 6 bedrooms.

**Not acceptable:** More than 8 bedrooms.

---

## SECTION 22 – Benefit Income

**Requirement:** At least 50% of income required to support the loan must be from non-benefit income.

**Calculation:** If fluctuating, average last 3 months' payments.

**Weighting:** 100% of benefit income allowed except housing allowance.

**Not acceptable:** Housing allowance element of Universal Credit.

**Condition:** Avoid duplication across income fields.

**Evidence Required:**

* Latest DWP benefit award statement for current tax year
* Latest month's bank statement

---

## SECTION 23 – Bonus

**Requirement:** Must be earned within last 12 months.

**Entry:** Key full amount.

**Weighting:** 50%.

**Condition:** Only evidenced bonus used.

**Condition:** Annualised value must not be assumed.

**Evidence Required (one of):**

* 2 months' or last 8 weeks' payslips
* Latest P60
* Annual compensation statement

## SECTION 27 – Capital Gains

## SECTION 29 – Carer’s Allowance

## SECTION 32 – Cheque Payments

**Allowed (case-by-case):** Subject to underwriter assessment.

## SECTION 33 – Child Benefit

**Requirement:** At least 50% of income required to support loan must be from non-benefit income.

**Condition:** No household income over £60,000 per annum.

**Condition:** Must continue at least 5 years from application date.

**Age limit:** Children no older than 13 years at application.

**Evidence:** Latest bank statement.

**Requirement:** Valuer checks for cladding.

**Condition:** May proceed subject to valuer comments and valid EWS1 form.

**Not acceptable:** A3 or B2 rating.

## SECTION 35 – Close Relative

**Definition Includes:**

* Parents (including step-parents & in-laws)
* Children (including step-children & in-laws)
* Grandparents (including in-laws)
* Siblings (including half-siblings, step-siblings & in-laws)
* Uncles and aunts (siblings of parents or spouse's parents only)
* Nieces and nephews
* First cousins

**Requirement:** On loans over 80% LTV, income boosters must be close relatives.

**Requirement:** For family concessionary purchases, vendor must be close relative.

## SECTION 36 – Coach Houses

**Not acceptable:** Commercial buildings on same title (e.g. holiday let, rental cottage).

## SECTION 38 – Commission

**Maximum:** 50% of each customer's income.

**Calculation:** Average last 2 months.

**Entry:** Key full amount.

**Weighting:** 50%.

* 2 months' payslips or P60
* March payslip with YTD
* Relevant payslips
* Tax calculation

## SECTION 41 – Construction Industry Scheme (CIS)

**Allowed:** CIS income with 12 months' continuous employment.

**Calculation:** Average last 2 months annualised.

* 2 months' payslips or invoices

## SECTION 42 – Construction Types

**Allowed:** Standard construction (stone, concrete block, brick; slate, tile, thatch, felt roof).

**Allowed (valuer-led):** Non-conventional methods subject to suitability.

### Generally Acceptable Includes:

* Solid brick/stone
* Cavity brick/stone/block
* Timber frame built 1970 or after
* Craft techniques prior to 1900
* Laing Easiform post-1945
* Wimpey No-Fines
* PRC repaired under scheme
* LPS ≤2 storeys with engineer report
* ICF (with required certification)
* Steel framed after 2000
* Modern concrete frame
* Brick and Block Crosswall

### Not Acceptable Includes:

* Housing Defects Act (unless repaired under PRC scheme)
* Reinforced poured/shuttered concrete (excluding accepted types)
* Metal clad
* BISF
* Steel framed prior to 2000
* LPS >2 storeys
* Timber/metal frame with post-fill insulation
* Pre-1965 softwood (unless high standard location demand)
* HACC
* Mundic (1900–1960) unless Grade A
* Cranwell
* Scotswood Pine Style
* Reema (Conclad/Hollow Panel)
* Woolaway
* Concrete Crosswall

## SECTION 46 – Credit Commitments

**Requirement:** All commitments must be disclosed.

**Excluded:** Paid off before completion.

### Revolving Credit

**Excluded if:**

* Payment does not exceed net monthly income

**Condition:** Verified over last 6 months.

**Standing balances:** 3% of balance included as expense.

## SECTION – Customers

**Maximum owners:** Up to 4 owners (excluding income boosters).
**Maximum total customers:** Up to 6 customers in total (including income boosters).

**Associated individuals:** Any individuals associated with the owner(s) through continuing linked finances, marriage, or civil partnerships should be added to the application.
**If not included:** Independent legal advice (ILA) required.
**Condition:** Proceeding without including them is subject to underwriter discretion.

**Checks:** Each customer is subject to the same checks, whether contributing to the mortgage or not.

**Deposit boosters:** No limit to the number of deposit boosters on an application.

## SECTION – Defaults

**Definition:** A default is defined as an account in a defaulted status.

### Unsecured & Secured Accounts

* **Not acceptable:** Recorded defaults over £300
* **Considered:** Defaults up to and including £300 (subject to underwriter assessment and approval)
* **Considered:** Defaults satisfied more than 3 years ago

### Communication & Utility Accounts

* **Not acceptable:** Recorded defaults over £500
* **Considered:** Defaults up to and including £500 (subject to underwriter assessment and approval)
* **Considered:** Defaults satisfied more than 3 years ago

**Combined limit:** The above default limits are combined, allowing a maximum of **£800 of defaults within the last 3 years**.

**Definition:** A dependent is a person not named on the mortgage application but supported financially by a party to the mortgage.

**Requirement:** All child and adult dependants must be keyed for all customers (both owners and income boosters).

## SECTION – Deposit Boosters

**Overview:** Deposit boosters help with the deposit in return for a protected financial interest in the property.

**Structure options:**

* **Interest-free loan:** You get back what you put in.
* **Equity loan:** Booster’s stake increases or decreases in line with property value.

**Conversion:** Loans can be converted into a gift at any time.
**Ring-fencing:** Booster’s capital is ring fenced to preserve a first-time buyer’s stamp duty status.
**Legal agreement:** Deposit booster legal agreement provided free of charge; Gen H manages loan repayment.

**No maximum:** No maximum number of deposit boosters for a mortgage application.
**Residency:** Deposit boosters do not need to be UK residents.
**Funds conditions:** Funds must come from a UK-based bank account and be in the UK for 3 months prior to funds being released.
**ILA requirement:** Deposit boosters must take independent legal advice (ILA) at their own cost before signing the deposit booster agreement.
**Occupancy restriction:** Deposit boosters are not allowed to reside in the property.

### Requirements for a Deposit Booster

* Contact details (phone number, email, full name)
* National Insurance number
* Date of birth
* Latest 3 years of address history
* Details of deposit contribution (amount, source, terms: interest-free or equity stake)
* Proof of identification and address

### Checks Carried Out (KYC)

* Fraud
* PEP, sanction and media checks
* Address
* Identification
* Source of fund checks (where required)

---

## SECTION – Deposit Sources

**Acceptable deposit types:**

* Savings
* Sale of property or equity release
* Sale of shares or cryptocurrency
* Inheritance
* Concessionary purchase
* Gifted deposit from a close relative

**Gifter residing in property:** If a third party gifts any element of the deposit and plans to reside in the property, they must get independent legal advice.

**Not acceptable:** Unsecured loans as a deposit.

**Overseas funds:** Further due diligence checks applied where deposit funds come from overseas.

**Crypto deposit funds:** If deposit funds are from cryptocurrency trading, refer to acting conveyancer for further guidance on acceptability.

**Deposit booster option:** Friends/family can contribute via equity loan or interest-free loan (deposit booster proposition).

## SECTION – Direct Debit

**Payments method:** All payments collected by Direct Debit.

**Requirement:** Sufficient Direct Debits must be set up to cover the CMI before Gen H can approve the Certificate of Title.

**Payment management:** The buying group (owners and income boosters) decide how payments should be managed.

## SECTION – Disability Living Allowance (DLA)

**Acceptable:** Disability Living Allowance income.
**Weighting:** 100%.

**Requirement:** At least 50% of income required to support the loan must be from non-benefit income.

**Children:** DLA and Personal Independence Payments (PIP) for children allowed, but must be continuing for the next 5 years.

---

## SECTION – Discounted Market Sale

**Considered:** Applications where there is no sign of distress or vulnerability.

**Lending basis (general):** Lend against the lower of purchase price or market valuation.
**Deposit requirement:** At least a 5% deposit must be provided.
**Not acceptable as deposit:** The difference between purchase price and market value cannot be considered as the deposit source.

**Exception:** Family concessionary purchase.

---

## SECTION – EWS1

**Requirement:** Where properties require an EWS1 form:

* Must be signed and dated within 5 years of current date
* Appropriate rating must be clearly stated

**Approach:** Gen H is led by valuers on whether a property requires an EWS1.

---

## SECTION – Early Repayment Charges (ERCs)

**When payable:** If the customer repays the mortgage loan or switches to another product before the end of the initial interest rate.

**How calculated:** Based on a percentage of the amount repaid or switched.

**Overpayments:** ERCs also apply if the overpayment allowance is exceeded.

**Not payable:** ERCs do not apply after expiry of the initial fixed rate period.

**Reference:** See sourcing systems for current ERC details.

---

## SECTION – Ejector Seat

**Applicability:** Only applicable to cases with income boosters.

**Purpose:** Extend the term by removing income boosters part way through the term so they do not exceed maximum age criteria.

**Mechanism:** Condition added to the mortgage.
**Removal timing:** All boosters removed at the same time when the first booster exceeds age criteria.
**Early removal:** Owners can choose to remove income boosters earlier by remortgaging (subject to passing affordability at that time).

**Assessment:** Gen H calculates how much of the loan will have been paid off by the time boosters exceed age criteria, to assess whether owners can afford the remaining loan on their own.

**Note:** Ejector seat does not always allow extension to the full 40-year maximum; for accurate term range, obtain a DIP or contact Gen H.

---

## SECTION – Employed Income

**Acceptable:** 100% of employed basic income.
**Acceptable:** 100% of guaranteed contractual allowances (e.g., car allowance and London weighting).

### Packaging Guidance (Evidence)

* Most recent month’s or last 4 weeks’ payslips for basic pay only
* Most recent 2 months’ or last 8 weeks’ payslips where bonus/overtime/commission is evidenced
* Latest months’ bank statements showing income, expenditure, and any rent or mortgage payments

---

## SECTION – Employer Housing Allowance

**Acceptable:** Employer housing allowance.
**Weighting:** 100%.
**Condition:** Must be guaranteed.

---

## SECTION – Endowments (Interest Only)

**Acceptable as repayment vehicle:** Up to 100% of an endowment policy.

**Requirements:**

* Policy must mature prior to, or at, the mortgage loan’s maturity date
* Policy beneficiaries must solely be the applicants on the mortgage (deposit boosters not included)
* Where projected values are provided: use the middle outcome; if only two outcomes are given, use the lower outcome
* If projected value relies on continued contributions, those contributions must be included as a financial commitment

---

## SECTION – Estate Rent Charge

If freehold property is subject to estate rent charges:

**Acceptable (historic charge):** If not related to services and not collected for 12 years or more, obtain appropriate indemnity policy on completion to protect security.

**Acceptable (collected in last 12 years and/or service-related):** Acceptable if either:

* Rent charge owner is a management company owned by residents (shareholders) and owners become a shareholder on completion, **or**
* Section 121 Law of Property Act remedies are expressly excluded or do not apply, **or**
* Instrument contains notification to mortgagee of at least 2 months prior to enforcement action

## SECTION – Ex-Local Authority

**Acceptable where:**

* Security value exceeds £100k outside London & South East, and £150k inside London & South East
* Valuer deems property acceptable to most lenders and readily saleable on an owner occupier basis
* London & South East uses areas I&J (NUTS definition)

## SECTION – Family Business

**Acceptable:** Employment income at a family business where employed for a minimum of 12 months.

**Expectation:** Income remains largely consistent over the 12-month period.

**Self-employed treatment:** If more than 20% of shares in the business are owned, customer must be treated as self-employed.

### Packaging Guidance (Evidence)

* 12 months’ bank statements required as proof of duration of employment

## SECTION – Family Concessionary Purchase

**Allowed:** Where vendor is a close relative.

**Lending basis:** Lend against market value (valuation).
**LTV/product basis:** Based on market value, not reduced purchase price.

**Deposit:** Difference between market value and concession purchase price can be the sole deposit, but must be at least 5%.

### Restrictions

* Vendor must be a close relative of at least one owner
* Vendor must not reside in the property after completion
* Vendor cannot support the application as a deposit booster

### Additional Requirements

* Gen H solvency statutory declaration signed by seller (template on LMS portal)
* Compliance with UK Finance Handbook requirements for transactions at undervalue (Section 5.16.3 – 5.16.4)

---

## SECTION – Feudal Ownership

**Not acceptable:** Properties subject to feudal ownership.

## SECTION – First Time Buyers

**Acceptable:** First-time buyers and previous homeowners.

## SECTION – Fixed Term Contracts

**Requirement:** Minimum 3 months remaining on current contract at point of application, **or** a future contract in place.

## SECTION – Fixed Term Contracts (Packaging Guidance)

### Packaging Guidance (Proof of Income)

As proof of income on fixed term contracts, we require:

* Current contract with at least 3 months remaining at time of submission

Examples of acceptable documents to verify 12 months continued employment:

## SECTION – Foreign Nationals

**Definition:** Foreign nationals are individuals from countries outside the UK who hold a visa granting them the right to live and work in the UK.

**Note:** In certain situations, additional restrictions apply to foreign national customers.

### No Additional Restrictions Apply If ALL Are Met

No additional restrictions apply if all of the following criteria are met:

* The customer is a British or Irish citizen, **or**
* They have EU Settled or Pre-Settled Status, **or**
* They hold Indefinite Leave to Remain, **or**
* They have a Family Visa and are applying jointly with a customer who has a permanent right to reside.
* They must be applying with the person on whom they are dependent, where applicable.
* If a customer’s income is not taken into account, the additional restrictions do not apply, regardless of whether they meet the above criteria.

### Restrictions (Where Applicable)

* Maximum 90% LTV
* 2 years’ UK credit history (based on start date of oldest account as reported through Experian)
* No requirement for time remaining on the visa, but it is expected to be valid up until the point of offer (underwriter discretion on a case-by-case basis)

### Packaging Guidance (Visas Evidence)

* Leave to remain in the UK and all visas must be evidenced by the customer’s gov.uk sharecode (eVisa)

## SECTION – Ground Rent

**Maximum annual ground rent:** Must not exceed **£250** (**£1000 in Greater London**) **or** **0.2%** of the current property value (**new builds restricted to 0.1%**), whichever is lower.

**Review period:** Must be **≥ 10 years**.
**Doubling:** Should double **no sooner than 20 years**.

**If exceeds / could exceed limits:** Must be reduced within thresholds.

**Lease variation requirement:** Must include a clause stating that under no circumstances can rent be increased so that it comes within the applicable Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST) threshold in the Housing Act 1988 (as amended).

## SECTION – Income Boosters

**Use cases:** Income boosters can be used on both purchases and remortgages.

**How used:** Gen H factors income boosters’ disposable income into affordability to increase borrowing, with additional tests to ensure owners are not excessively reliant.

**Affordability:** Use affordability calculator for most accurate figure.

**Structure:** Income booster not on property deeds but on the mortgage (JBSP structure) to preserve first-time buyer stamp duty status.

**Eligibility/evidence:** Income boosters are subject to the same credit checks, eligibility criteria, and evidence requirements as owners.

**Ejector seat:** Can extend term by removing boosters part way through term so they do not exceed maximum age criteria.

**Who can be an income booster:**

* Up to and including 80% LTV: friends and family can act as income boosters
* Up to and including 95% LTV: only close relatives can act as income boosters

**If contributing to mortgage:** Treated as a gift.

**ILA:** Income boosters must take independent legal advice (ILA) at their own cost.

### Income Boosters Residing in the Security (May Reside)

May reside in the security, subject to underwriter discretion, and only where:

* Income booster is not currently on the title (cannot be vendor or be removed via transfer of equity)
* Income booster is not also a deposit booster
* Income booster is not providing a gift (including gifts towards deposit, legal fees and SDLT)
* Income booster receives mandatory ILA related to occupancy (in addition to standard income booster ILA; must be face-to-face)
* If income booster is spouse/civil partner: strong rationale required prior to submission; considered case-by-case; relationship status “other” must be selected

**Liability:** All customers, including income boosters, are liable for the full mortgage repayment.

## SECTION – Self-Invested Personal Pension (SIPP)

We accept Self-Invested Personal Pensions (SIPP) with 50% weighting.

### Keying Instructions

If keying under **Investment Income**:

* Take the pot value and divide by the mortgage term
* The investment income bucket will automatically weight at 50%

If keying under **Pension**:

* Take the pot value
* Divide by the mortgage term
* Then apply 50% weighting

## SECTION – Sole Traders / Partnerships

### Business Requirements

The business must:

* Be registered in the UK
* Have been trading for at least 1 year
* Provide appropriate evidence

Generally require 2 years trading.

1 year trading may be accepted where:

* Additional evidence demonstrates track record of similar income in related field.

### Income Assessment

* Use latest net profit for affordability where income deemed sustainable.
* Large increases/decreases require explanation.
* Additional documents may be requested.

### Packaging Guidance

**Where trading 2 years:**

* Last 2 years’ tax calculations + TYOs (most recent dated within last 18 months)
* Last 3 months’ bank statements (business or personal)
* If not used for trading, latest personal bank statement showing expenditure

**Where trading 1 year:**

* Latest year’s tax calculation + TYO (dated within 18 months)
* Evidence of previous track record (P60, contracts, period 12/52 payslip etc.)
* Last 3 months’ bank statements
* If not used for trading, latest personal bank statement showing expenditure

## SECTION – Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT)

### Married Applicants and SDLT

Married couples treated as a single unit for SDLT.

If non-applicant spouse owns property:

* Higher rate SDLT applies.

FTB status may be affected if spouse previously owned property.

### Lending Approach

* Sole applications allowed.
* Not a method to avoid SDLT.
* SDLT not considered in lending decisions (except affordability).

### Role of Conveyancer

* Responsible for ensuring correct SDLT paid.
* Any discrepancies addressed in conveyancing.

### Advising Customers

* Encourage professional tax advice.
* Customers must have sufficient funds for SDLT.

### Disclaimer

Information reflects understanding only and is not tax advice. Independent advice recommended.

## SECTION – Universal Credit

Accepted with restrictions:

* Must be for the customer
* Housing allowance must be deducted
* At least 50% of income must be non-benefit
* Child Tax Credit only if guaranteed for next 5 years (child ≤13 at application)

### Packaging Guidance

* Latest Universal Credit statement
* Latest bank statement

### Calculation Steps

1. Identify net monthly payment
2. Deduct housing benefit
3. Deduct child benefit if child >13
4. Adjust if income declared differs
5. Annualise

For variable income:

* Use one month total income
* Divide annual income by 12

Underwriter review required where circumstances changed.
