## SECTION 1 – LOAN PARAMETERS

- Minimum mortgage term: 5 years.
- Maximum mortgage term: 30 years (subject to applicant age).
- Maximum lending per obligor: £5,000,000.
- First £1,000,000: maximum 80% LTV (subject to product availability).
- Above £1,000,000: maximum 75% LTV.
- Offer validity: 90 days (up to 180 days for new build properties).
- Fleet Mortgages reserves the right to ask the borrower to select a new rate if the case has not progressed to offer within 90 days of application — new credit checks will also be carried out.

### Repayment Methods

- Capital and interest repayment.
- Part interest and part repayment.
- Interest only repayment.

### Application Types

- Purchase applications: accepted.
- Remortgage applications: accepted.
- Capital raising: considered for most legal purposes, except payment of tax or gambling debts.
- Payment of Inheritance Tax or Capital Gains Tax may be considered where the proposal does not suggest financial stress for the applicants.
- Properties owned for less than 6 months: considered on a case-by-case basis.

### Security

- First legal charge on security only.
- Second charges and further advances: not accepted.
- Leasehold with applicant interest in freehold: a charge over the freehold will also be required in addition to the first legal charge over the leasehold title. Consent of all other freehold owners required.

## SECTION 2 – LTV AND LOAN LIMITS BY PROPERTY TYPE

### Standard Properties (valued £75,000 or more)

- At 65% LTV: maximum loan £2,000,000.
- At 70% LTV: maximum loan £1,500,000.
- At 75% LTV: maximum loan £1,000,000.
- Minimum valuation: £75,000.

### Standard Properties (valued £74,999 or less)

- Maximum LTV: 70%.
- Maximum loan: £249,499.
- Minimum valuation: £50,000.

### HMO / Multi-Unit Block (MUB)

- At 70% LTV: maximum loan £1,500,000.
- At 75% LTV: maximum loan £1,000,000.
- Minimum valuation: £100,000 (outside London and South East); £150,000 (within London and South East).

### New Build

- Flats: maximum LTV 70%, maximum loan £750,000, minimum valuation £50,000.
- Houses: maximum LTV 75%, maximum loan £750,000, minimum valuation £50,000.

### Ex Local Authority / MOD Properties

- Flats: maximum LTV 70%, maximum loan £1,500,000, minimum valuation £75,000 (outside London and South East); £150,000 (within London and South East).
- Houses: maximum LTV 75%, maximum loan £1,500,000, minimum valuation £75,000 (outside London and South East); £150,000 (within London and South East).

### Conversions

- At 70% LTV: maximum loan £1,600,000 (£750,000 if converted within last 12 months).
- At 75% LTV: maximum loan £1,000,000 (£750,000 if converted within last 12 months).
- Minimum valuation: £100,000 freehold (outside London and South East); £150,000 freehold (within London and South East); £50,000 leasehold.

### Properties Above and Adjacent to Commercial

- At 70% LTV: maximum loan £1,500,000.
- At 75% LTV: maximum loan £1,000,000.
- Minimum valuation: £100,000.

## SECTION 3 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

### Residency

- All applicants must be resident in the UK.
- Must have resided in the UK for the last 3 years.
- Must be liable to UK tax.
- Must have permanent right to reside.

### Age

- Maximum of 4 applicants per application.
- Minimum age: 21 years old.
- First-time landlords: minimum age 25.
- Maximum age: 95 at end of mortgage term.

### Income and Ownership

- Primary applicant must currently own a property and have done so for the last 12 months.
- Primary applicant must be able to prove an income in excess of £15,000 per annum.
- First-time buyers: not accepted.

### Employment

- Primary applicant must have been in continuous employment for at least 12 months and have passed their probation period; or
- Have been self-employed, contracting or trading for at least 2 years in their current business.
- Retired applicants considered provided they can meet the minimum income requirement.

## SECTION 4 – LIMITED COMPANIES

- Must be a special purpose vehicle (SPV) registered in England and Wales.
- Acceptable SIC codes: 68100, 68209, 68320, 68201. Holding companies may also have SIC codes: 64209, 64203.
- Multi-layered company structures considered.
- Maximum of 4 applicants.
- All directors and any shareholders with a 25% or greater holding must be party to the mortgage.
- Where the cumulative shareholding of named applicants is below 75%, further shareholders must be added until a 75% shareholding is reached.
- Full personal guarantee required from all guarantors; independent legal advice required.
- Applications where individuals are transferring (selling) properties to an SPV limited company they own are classed as a purchase.

## SECTION 5 – PROPERTY CRITERIA

### Location and Tenure

- Lending in England and Wales only.
- Freehold or leasehold tenure accepted; commonhold not accepted.

### Leasehold Requirements

- Minimum 75 years unexpired term at completion.
- Minimum 50 years remaining at expiry of mortgage term.

### Blocks of Flats

- Up to 5 storeys (up to 10 storeys acceptable within the M25).
- Blocks exceeding 4 storeys (excluding basement, including ground floor) must have lift access to all floors.

### Accepted Property Types

- Standard residential houses and flats.
- HMOs up to 6 bedrooms.
- Multi-Unit Freehold Blocks (MUFBs) up to 10 self-contained units.
- Ex-local authority houses and flats, including flats with deck access.
- New builds.
- Converted properties.

### Tenancy Types

- All properties should be tenanted on an Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST) of up to 3 years.
- Also considered (with prior approval where noted):
  - Student lets.
  - Corporate lets (prior approval required).
  - Local Authority and Housing Association lets (prior approval required).
  - Standard format of Private Sector Lease as per the UK Finance Mortgage Lenders' Handbook.

## SECTION 6 – DEPOSIT CRITERIA

- Documentary evidence of the source of all deposit funds required (e.g. bank statements showing a build-up of savings, or completion statements showing the sale of a property).
- Gifts from immediate family members are allowed: spouse, mother, father, sibling or grandparent.

---

## SECTION 7 – PORTFOLIO LANDLORDS

**Definition:** An applicant is classed as a portfolio landlord where all applicants, individually or collectively, own four or more existing mortgaged BTL properties, or three properties where an application is being submitted to Fleet to purchase a fourth.

- Portfolio applications assessed by dedicated underwriters.
- The whole portfolio is stress tested at 125% at 5% with a maximum overall LTV of 75%.

### Required Documents

**Existing Property Portfolio Schedule:**
- Information gathered via CoreLogic (Buy to Let Hub), allowing instant submission directly to underwriters.
- Alternatively, client portfolio details can be entered into a downloadable spreadsheet and uploaded directly to the BTL Hub.

**Portfolio Landlords Questionnaire:**
- A combined business plan and cashflow forecast questionnaire.
- Required from existing landlords only.
- Must be uploaded via Fleet's Broker Portal before submitting the CoreLogic property schedule, to minimise underwriting time.
