## SECTION 1 – FINANCING PARAMETERS

- Minimum financing: £50,000.
- Maximum financing at up to 70% FTV: £1,500,000.
- Maximum financing at up to 80% FTV: £1,000,000.
- Maximum financing at up to 90% FTV: £750,000.
- Maximum FTV: 90% (see product guide for latest availability, which may be lower).
- New build houses: maximum 85% FTV.
- New build flats: maximum 80% FTV.
- Minimum term: 10 years.
- Maximum term: 40 years.
- Minimum property value: £85,000.
- Maximum property value: uncapped.
- Products are not portable.

## SECTION 2 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

### Age

- Minimum age: 21.
- Maximum age: 75 at end of term (based on the eldest applicant whose income is used for affordability).

### Number of Applicants

- Maximum 4 applicants per application.

### Residency

- All applicants must have been UK residents for the last 2 years.
- Joint applications where one applicant has not met the 2-year residency requirement may still be considered with additional criteria.
- Crown employees (including members of the diplomatic service working overseas): treated as UK residents.
- Members of the British Armed Forces stationed overseas: considered.

### Foreign Nationals

- Accepted if the applicant has permanent right to reside or is on a visa with at least 1 year remaining.
- Residency criteria applies.

### Marital Status

- A sole application can be considered even where the applicant is in a legally sanctioned relationship, for both purchases and refinances. Independent Legal Advice (ILA) may be required.

## SECTION 3 – INCOME AND AFFORDABILITY

- No minimum income restriction.
- Foreign currency income: not accepted.
- Expenditure evidence: latest 3 months' bank statements (further statements may be requested at underwriter discretion).

### Acceptable Income Sources (100% Considered)

- PAYE income.
- Car allowance.
- London/regional weighting.
- Special duties allowance.
- Travel allowance.
- Unsocial hours payment.
- Overtime.
- Second job income.
- Contract income.
- Pension income.
- Rental income.
- Maintenance payments.
- Child/working tax credits.
- Child benefit.
- Carer's allowance (including foster allowance).
- Widowed parents' attendance allowance.
- Employment and Support Allowance.
- Universal Credit.
- Incapacity allowance.
- Industrial injuries disablement benefit.
- Disability Living Allowance.
- Personal Independence Payment.
- Armed Forces Compensation Scheme.
- Armed Forces Independence Scheme.

### Income Sources (Partial Consideration)

- Bonus: 50%.
- Commission: 66%.

### Employed

- Minimum 3 months' employment history required (can include both current and previous roles where there are no gaps in employment).
- Applicants during probationary period considered (excluding zero hours contracts).

### Zero Hours Contracts

- Accepted where not the primary income source on the application.
- Applicant must have been employed on a zero hours contract within the same industry for at least 12 months.

### Second Job

- 100% of income considered.
- If PAYE: applicant must have been working both primary and second job for at least 6 months.
- Evidence: latest P60 and 3 most recent payslips.

### Contractors

- First-time contracting: if less than 6 months remaining on the contract, confirmation of 24 months' previous employment in the same line of work is required.
- Single contract: 3 months' contracting history required; minimum 12 months' history in the same industry (unless first-time contracting); minimum 3 months remaining on contract; confirmation of renewal from employer accepted if next contract is not yet available.
- Multiple contracts: treated as sole trader or limited company director; same evidence as above required.
- Umbrella company: treated as PAYE employed; evidence required: latest P60 and latest 3 payslips.
- Medical professionals: treated as a single contractor regardless of whether they work under one or multiple Trusts; day rates considered; SA302s not required.

### Self-Employed

- Most recent year's income accepted if trading for more than 2 years.
- Evidence: SA302 for last 2 years and Tax Year Overview (or 1 year if trading for less than 2 years), plus business bank statements.
- Where 2 years' SA302/Tax Year Overviews cannot be provided, other evidence of income stability may be considered at underwriter discretion.

### Financing into Retirement

- Applications where the term expires beyond age 70 up to a maximum age of 75 may be considered.
- Where retirement is less than 10 years away: affordability assessed on anticipated retirement income.

**Evidence required:**
- Less than 10 years from retirement: latest pension statement showing a forecast of the pension amount.
- More than 10 years from retirement: evidence of contributions into a pension fund (including sale of other property and/or buy to let rental income), reviewed on a proportionate basis.

## SECTION 4 – CREDIT HISTORY

### Secured Arrears or Missed Payments

- Maximum 1 missed payment in last 36 months.
- 0 in last 6 months.

### Unsecured Arrears or Missed Payments

- Worst status of 2 in last 24 months.
- Communications arrears up to £250 ignored.

### CCJs

- 0 in last 36 months; none outstanding.

### Defaults

- 0 in last 36 months; none unsatisfied.
- Communications defaults up to £250 ignored.

### Payday Loans

- Maximum 1 payday loan not exceeding £300 in last 36 months.
- 0 in last 12 months.

### Debt Management Plans

- Must have been satisfied at least 24 months before application.
- Must not relate to a credit event in the previous 60 months.

### Guarantors

- Not considered.

---

## SECTION 5 – PROPERTY CRITERIA

- England only.
- Property must be used for the customer's own occupation; no commercial use (work from a home office or study is not considered commercial use).
- Minimum unexpired lease: 85 years remaining at date of application.
- Right to Buy: not accepted.
- Retentions: not allowed, unless the property is a new build under construction (where the valuer will note a retention amount equal to the finished basis value).

### Accepted Property Types

- Standard residential properties.
- New build houses (maximum 85% FTV).
- New build flats (maximum 80% FTV).

---

## SECTION 6 – DEPOSIT AND EQUITY

- 100% gifted deposit acceptable from any number of sources (including outside close family members).
- Deposits from overseas accounts considered.
- Builder's deposit/incentives accepted up to 5%.

### Evidence of Deposit

- Savings from income: latest 6 months' bank statements.
- Sale of current home: Memorandum of Sale and redemption statement from current lender.
- Gift: gifted deposit form from each donor.

---

## SECTION 7 – APPLICATION TYPES

- First-time buyers: accepted.
- Remortgage/refinance: accepted where the property was last purchased or remortgaged at least 6 months ago (unless remortgaging out of bridge financing).
- Capital raising remortgage/refinance: accepted.
- Porting: not available — StrideUp products are not portable.

---

## SECTION 8 – DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

### Employed Income

- Latest P60.
- 3 most recent payslips.
- Latest bank statement showing salary credit.
- If recently started a new role with no payslips available: copy of contract confirming salary level, plus last 3 payslips from previous role.

### Self-Employed Income

- SA302 for last 2 years and Tax Year Overview (or 1 year if trading for less than 2 years) plus business bank statements.
- Where 2 years' SA302/Tax Year Overviews cannot be provided: alternative evidence of income stability considered at underwriter discretion.

### Other Income Sources

- Child benefit: most recent award letter and most recent bank statement showing the credit.
- Working or Child Tax Credits: most recent award letter and most recent bank statement showing the credit.
- Maintenance: copy of maintenance agreement received under a Court Order and latest bank statement showing the credit.
- Pension income: most recent pension statement (dated within last 12 months) and latest bank statement showing the pension credit.
- Universal Credit: most recent award letter and most recent bank statement showing the credit.
- State disability allowances: latest benefits statement and latest bank statement showing the credit.
- Carer's Allowance: latest award notice.
- Widowed Parents' Attendance: latest award notice.
- Employment and Support Allowance: latest award notice.
