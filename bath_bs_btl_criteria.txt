# SECTION 1 – BUY TO LET LENDING CRITERIA

## LOAN DETAILS

Minimum Loan Size:
- £50,000 for standard Buy to Let applications
- £75,000 for individual applications (Note: This may be a higher threshold for certain applications)
- £100,000 for Limited Company Buy to Let (Note: Society is not currently lending to Limited Companies)

Minimum Property Value:
- £100,000 for standard Buy to Let lending

Maximum Loan Size:
- £500,000 at 80% Loan to Value (LTV)
- £1,500,000 at 75% LTV
- £2,000,000 at 70% LTV

Maximum Lending Limit (Group/Lender):
- Maximum exposure limit: £1,500,000 per borrower with the Society or within the group

Loan Term:
- Minimum: 5 years
- Maximum: 40 years

Repayment Methods:
- Capital Repayment (Capital & Interest) - Acceptable
- Interest Only - Acceptable

## APPLICANT CRITERIA

Age Limits:
- Minimum Age: 21 years at application
- Maximum Age: No maximum age at the point of application
- Maximum Age at End of Term: No maximum (affordability must be proven into retirement)
- Maximum Age for Self Employed Borrowers: No Maximum (no maximum age for BTL borrowers)
- Note: Affordability must be proven into retirement for all applicants

Income Requirements:
- Minimum Household Income: £20,000 per annum (after all personal debts/commitments)
- Income must be at least £20,000 after commitments
- For Portfolio Landlords: Minimum income £20,000 after all personal debts

Residency:
- Applicants must be UK residents
- Minimum UK Address History: 3 years (36 months)
- Electoral Register: Not being on electoral register is acceptable subject to explanation and proof of address

First Time Buyers:
- Not Acceptable - Bath Building Society does not accept Buy to Let applications from 'First Time Buyers'

First Time Landlords:
- Acceptable - Bath Building Society can potentially accept Buy to Let applications from 'First Time Landlords'

Portfolio Landlords:
- Accepted - Portfolio Landlords are defined as having 4 or more completed qualifying rental properties (mortgaged or unencumbered)
- Qualifying properties include:
  * Privately owned Buy to Let rental properties
  * Company owned Buy to Let rental properties (where applicant has shareholding of more than 1%)
  * Consent to Let properties
  * Holiday Lets
  * Single title multi unit properties are counted as one property
- Portfolio Landlord Minimum Experience: No minimum experience requirement
- Maximum LTV Across Whole Portfolio: 75% (refer to lender - indicates maximum LTV of all properties owned by applicants across their entire portfolio, including mortgages held with lender/group and all other mortgages with other lenders)
- Maximum Aggregate Portfolio LTV (within group or lender): 75% (refer to lender - indicates maximum LTV of all properties owned by applicants that are mortgaged to them or their group)
- Lender or Group Maximum Number of Mortgages: No maximum (whole portfolio must be self financing)

Credit Reference Agencies:
- Uses Equifax (manual underwriting)
- Does not use Experian
- Does not use Transunion (formerly Callcredit)
- Credit Search: Full/hard footprint (not soft footprint)
- Number of credit reference agencies used: 1 (Equifax only)
- Credit Scoring: No - Manual underwriting applies (we credit search but not credit score)

Maximum Number of Applicants:
- Maximum: 4 applicants allowed on a Buy to Let mortgage application

Minimum Landlord Experience:
- No minimum experience requirement (Bath Building Society has no minimum experience requirement for applicants)

Minimum Length of Time Employed:
- No minimum (Bath Building Society has no minimum length of time applicants need to have been in continuous employment)
- Note: The applicant cannot be in a probation period

Returning to UK:
- Returning to UK - Employed: Not Acceptable (will not accept applications from applicants who are returning to the UK and going straight into employment)
- Returning to UK - Self-Employed: Not Acceptable (will not accept applications from applicants who are returning to the UK and going straight into self-employment)

Maximum Debt to Income Ratio:
- No Maximum (we don't have a maximum as long as it fits income multipliers and affordability)

## PROPERTY CRITERIA

Property Construction:
- Must be of standard construction
- Non-standard construction properties not accepted

Property Location:
- England and Wales: Acceptable
- Scotland: Acceptable (Mainland Scotland and specific islands)
    * The Shetlands
    * The Orkneys
    * Arran
    * Bute
    * Skye
    * Harris & Lewis
    * Mull
    * Islay
- Note: Valuations can take longer to book in the Scottish Isles

Property Types - Maximum LTV:
- Houses/Bungalows (Standard): 80% LTV
- Houses/Bungalows (New Build): 80% LTV
- Flats/Maisonettes (Standard): 80% LTV
- Flats/Maisonettes (New Build): 80% LTV

Property Type Restrictions:
- HMO (House of Multiple Occupation): Not Acceptable - Bath Building Society does not accept HMO applications
- HMO Rental Valuation Assessment: Not Applicable (we do not accept HMO applications - Bath Building Society does not accept HMO applications)
- Multi Unit Freehold (multiple properties on same title): Not Acceptable
  * Multi Unit Freehold Maximum Number of Flats: Up to 3 flats in a Multi Unit Freehold
- Ex Local Authority Houses/Bungalows: Acceptable (must be 75% privately owned, non-standard construction not accepted, flats not accepted)
  * Ex Local Authority Flats: Not Acceptable (we don't lend on ex-local authority flats)
  * Maximum Number of Storeys for Ex Local Authority Flats: Not applicable (we don't lend on ex-local authority flats)
  * Minimum Percentage of Properties under Private Ownership on Ex-Local Authority Blocks of Flats: 75% (Bath Building Society requires a minimum of 75% under private ownership in a block of ex-local authority flats)
- Flats Above Commercial Offices: Acceptable (refer to lender, case-by-case basis)
- Flats Above Commercial Food Outlet: Refer to lender
- Converted Office Premises: Not Acceptable (please note we do not lend on converted office premises)
- Converted Churches: Potentially acceptable (flats converted from commercial premises are not considered)
- Properties with Acreage: Acceptable (up to 6 acres max, as long as no commercial activity is taking place)
- Properties at Auction: Acceptable
- Grade 1 Listed Building: Acceptable (refer to lender, assessed on a case by case basis)
- Grade 2 Listed Building: Acceptable (refer to lender, assessed on a case by case basis)
- Basement Flat: Refer to lender
- Studio Flat: Acceptable (Bath Building Society can lend on studio flats)
  * Studio Flat Minimum Floor Area: 30 square meters (our minimum square metre requirement when lending on studio flats is 30 sqm)
- Freehold Flats and Maisonettes: Not Acceptable (does not lend on freehold flats and maisonettes in England & Wales)
- Coach House: Refer to lender
- Ex MOD Properties: Acceptable (please refer to lender)
- Maximum Number of Storeys for Flats: 6 storeys maximum (anything over 4 storeys needs to be lift served)

Property Construction - Specific Types:
- Flying Freehold: Acceptable (no more than 15% flying freehold, agreed on a case by case basis)
- Mundic Block: Acceptable (subject to valuer's comments and petrographic test)
- Cornish Construction: Not Acceptable
- Timber Framed: Acceptable (Modern Timber Framed post 1970 acceptable but exterior wall must be brick/block/stone)
  * Timber Framed (Post 1970): Acceptable (subject to a full structural warranty)
  * Timber Framed (1900 to 1970): Acceptable (subject to a full structural warranty)
  * Timber Framed (Pre 1900): Not Acceptable
- 100% Timber Construction: Not Acceptable
- Wholly Timbered: Not Acceptable
- Concrete Construction: Acceptable (subject to valuer's comments)
- Steel Framed: Acceptable (subject to valuer's comments, must be built post 1987)
- No-Fines: Acceptable (subject to valuer's comments)
- Easiform: Acceptable (subject to valuer's comments)
- Insulating Concrete Form (ICF): Acceptable
- Structural Insulated Panel Solutions (SIPs): Acceptable (subject to being BOPAS accredited)
- Modern Method of Construction (MMC): Acceptable (subject to valuer's comments and being an accredited scheme)
- Off Site Manufactured (MMC): Acceptable
- British Iron and Steel Federation (BISF): Acceptable (must be built post 1987)
- Repaired Prefabricated Reinforced Concrete: Acceptable (subject to valuer's comments)
- Non Repaired Prefabricated Reinforced Concrete: Not Acceptable
- Large Panel System (LPS): Not Acceptable
- Single Skin: Not Acceptable
- Thatched Roof: Acceptable (subject to valuer's comments)
- Woolaway: Acceptable (if repaired with the benefit of NHBC or Zurich warranty)
- Potton Homes: Acceptable
- Converted Church: Acceptable
- Dutch Barns: Not Acceptable
- BOPAS Accreditation: May be required for non-standard construction where the construction type is acceptable
- EWS1 Required: Yes - Required on properties with combustible cladding or materials on balconies (will not lend without EWS1 form)
- Tower Block with Cladding: Acceptable (will require an EWS1 form)
- Majority Flat Roof: Acceptable (reliant on valuer's comments, majority classified as anything over 50%)
- Properties with Asbestos: Acceptable (assessed on a case by case basis)
- Spray Foam Insulation: Acceptable (subject to valuer's comments)

Property Issues:
- Absentee Freeholder: Acceptable (providing solicitor confirms indemnity insurance would fully protect)
- Section 106 Restrictive Covenant: Acceptable (subject to mortgagee in possession clause, refer to lender)
- Properties Next to Commercial:
  * Class A1 (shops and retail): Acceptable
  * Class A2 (professional services): Acceptable
  * Class A3 (food and drink): Not Acceptable
  * Class A4 (drinking establishments): Not Acceptable
- Overhead Power Lines: Acceptable (if the lines do not run directly over the property we can potentially consider a case)
- Mine Shafts: Not Acceptable (will not lend on properties that are close to a mine shaft)
- Japanese Knotweed: Not Acceptable (if Japanese Knotweed or any other invasive species is identified within 7 metres of the property, the application may be declined)
- Property Needing Refurbishment Before Letting: Not Acceptable (will not consider a Buy to Let application where the property requires refurbishment before it can be Let)
- Underpinned Within Last 10 Years: Refer to lender
- Unadopted Roads: Acceptable
- Shared Access or Shared Services: Acceptable (Bath Building Society can potentially consider a property with shared access or shared services)
  * Note: CANNOT CONSIDER SHARED ACCESS WITH FAMILY MEMBERS
- Overage Clause: Not Acceptable (will not consider a property with an Overage Clause)
- Estate Rentcharges: Acceptable (can potentially consider lending on freehold properties where exists an estate charge for the general upkeep and maintenance of any shared areas)
- Escalating Ground Rents: Not Acceptable (will not lend on leasehold properties where an escalating ground rent clause exists)
- Maximum Ground Rent (as % of Property Value/Purchase Price): No Maximum (Bath Building Society has no maximum threshold percentage against the property value that the ground rent cannot exceed)
- Energy Performance Rating (EPC) Rating 'F' or 'G': Not Acceptable (we do not accept Buy to Let mortgages on properties that have an EPC rating of 'F' or 'G')
- Solar Panels: Acceptable (where the solar panels form part of the freehold of a building; where the solar panels are leased under an "airspace lease" the Solicitor will be required to ensure the lease does not negatively impact on our security)
- Ownership of Adjoining Property (Related Transaction): Acceptable (refer to lender)

Multi Unit Properties:
- Minimum Floor Area per Unit: 30 square meters

## TENANCY CRITERIA

Tenancy Type:
- Must be Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST)
- Minimum Length of AST: 6 months minimum
- Maximum Length of AST: No maximum (all property must be let under a standard AST)
- Maximum Number of Tenants on AST: 4 tenants (Bath Building Society can consider applications where there are no more than 4 tenants on the Assured Shorthold Tenancy)

DSS/Housing Benefit Tenants:
- Acceptable by exception
- Maximum LTV: 60%
- Stress Tested: 150%

Maximum Number of Students on Single AST:
- 2 students allowed on a single AST

Student Lets:
- Acceptable - Bath Building Society can potentially allow applicants to Let their Buy to Let property to Students
- Student Let Valuation: Not Acceptable (will not use the student Let rental valuation for affordability as opposed to the open market rental valuation)

Tenants in Situ (Purchase Applications):
- Acceptable - Bath Building Society can potentially accept situations on purchase applications where there is a tenant in situ
- The tenant must be occupying the property on an AST

Guaranteed Rental Schemes:
- Not Acceptable - Bath Building Society will not consider applications where a Guaranteed Rental Scheme will be set up

Other Tenant Types:
- Asylum Seekers: Not Acceptable (will not consider applications where the property will be let to an Asylum seeker)
- Corporate Lets/Company Lets: Not Acceptable (do not accept properties that are to be Let on a Corporate/Company Let basis)
- Housing Association Lets: Not Acceptable (does not accept Housing Association tenants)
- Protected Tenancies: Not Acceptable (will not consider applications where the tenant enjoys a 'Protected Tenancy')
- Serviced Properties: Not Acceptable (will not consider applications where the property will be Let as a 'Serviced Property')
- Third Party/Company (Sub-Letting): Not Acceptable (will not consider applications where the property will be Let to a third party who will in turn Sublet the property - sometimes referred to as 'Rent to Rent')
- Multiple Tenancies: Acceptable (can potentially consider a Buy to Let application with multiple tenancies; applicants would have to choose from one of our HMO properties)
- Letting Whole Property via AirBnB: Acceptable (allows borrowers to Let properties via AirBnB or similar on Holiday Let products only; would not accept this on BTL or MUFB plans)
- Premium Lease Agreements: Not Acceptable (does not accept 'Premium Lease Agreements' where a prospective tenant is paying their rent as an advanced payment)

## AFFORDABILITY AND RENTAL INCOME

Income Coverage Ratio (ICR) / Rental Coverage:

Standard Buy to Let:
- Basic Rate Taxpayer: Rent must cover a minimum of 125% of pay rate + 2% but must never be less than 5.5%
- Higher Rate Taxpayer: Rent must cover a minimum of 145% of pay rate + 2% but must never be less than 5.5%
- Note: If the interest rate is fixed for a period of 5 years or more, rent must cover 125%/145% of pay rate (without the +2%)

Top-Slicing:
- Not Permitted - Bath Building Society does not consider earned income in affordability assessment
- Can Consider Earned Income in Rental Assessment (Topslicing): No (does not consider earned income in affordability assessment)
- The use of any borrower's surplus income to subsidize a rental shortfall will NOT be considered

Rental Valuation:
- Cannot use valuer's rental estimate if higher than actual existing rent: Not acceptable
- Cannot use actual existing rent when higher than valuer's estimate: Not acceptable

## REPAYMENT AND TERM

Repayment Methods:
- Capital Repayment (Capital & Interest): Acceptable
- Interest Only: Acceptable

Term:
- Minimum Term: 5 years
- Maximum Term: 40 years
- Split Mortgage Terms: Acceptable (Bath Building Society can offer mortgages with split term durations)

Overpayments:
- Overpayment Facility: 20% of capital balance (as of 1st January) per calendar year without penalty
- Overpayments exceeding 20% limit: Early repayment charges apply

Early Repayment Charges:
- For 5-year fixed products:
  * Years 1-4: 3% of outstanding balance
  * Year 5: 2% of outstanding balance
  * Plus Closing Administration Fee: £100 (currently)

## LIMITED COMPANY APPLICATIONS

Limited Company Buy to Let:
- Not Currently Accepted - The Society is not currently lending to Limited Companies

Note: The following information is provided for reference only (as the Society is not currently lending to Limited Companies):
- Trading Limited Companies: Would require minimum 3 years trading if primary business is not property letting
- Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) Limited Companies: Would be acceptable from day one if formed solely for property letting
- Personal Guarantees: Would be required from all Company Directors
- Top-slicing: NOT permitted for Limited Company applications

## FEES AND CHARGES

Product Fees:
- Individual Applications: £999
- Limited Company Applications: £1,499 (Note: Society is not currently lending to Limited Companies)

Arrangement Fees:
- Cannot be added to mortgage advance if it would exceed product LTV limits: Not allowed
- Cannot be added to mortgage advance if it would exceed lending LTV limits: Not allowed

Closing Administration Fee:
- £100 (currently) - applies when fully repaying a 5-year fixed product

## REMORTGAGE CRITERIA

Remortgage Waiting Period:
- 6 months minimum ownership before remortgage application can be considered

Remortgage Purposes - Maximum LTV:
- Balance Swap (£4£ remortgage): 80% LTV
- Buy Out (partner/borrower): 80% LTV
- Gift Money: 80% maximum
- Repay Residential Mortgage: 80% LTV
- Home Improvements: 80% maximum
- Buy to Let Purchase: 75% LTV maximum
- Land Purchase: Refer to lender
- Purchase Share of Freehold: Acceptable
- Extending a Lease: Acceptable (Bath Building Society can potentially consider capital raising on a remortgage where the purpose of the funds is extending a lease)
- School/Education Fees: Acceptable
- Medical Expenses: Acceptable (Bath Building Society can potentially consider capital raising on a remortgage where the purpose of the funds is medical expenses)
- Lifestyle (holidays, cars, non-essential spending etc): Not Acceptable (will not consider capital raising on a remortgage where the purpose of the funds is lifestyle)
- Personal Investments (not property related): Not Acceptable (will not consider capital raising on a remortgage where the purpose of the funds is personal investments)
- Debt Consolidation: Not Acceptable
- Capital Raise for BTL (property not yet found): Not Acceptable
- HMO Conversion Back to Standard Residence: Not Acceptable (will not consider a remortgage application where an existing HMO property will be converted to a standard residence)

Back to Back Remortgages:
- Customer Funded: Acceptable (providing original transaction is fully registered at Land Registry)
- Inherited: Acceptable (providing original transaction is fully registered at Land Registry)
- Bridging: Acceptable (providing original transaction is fully registered at Land Registry)

Let to Buy:
- Acceptable - Bath Building Society can potentially accept Let to Buy applications where looking to convert existing residential mortgage to Buy to Let mortgage
- Must have an onward purchase

Let to Move in with Family:
- Not Acceptable (the applicant must be an owner-occupier; will not allow an applicant to remortgage their current residential property onto a Buy to Let mortgage and then move in with family as opposed to purchasing a new main residence)

Sale and Rent Back:
- Not Acceptable (does not accept 'Sale and Rent Back' transactions - where the vendor sells the property usually at a discount, remains in the property and pays a rent to the new owner)

## ADVERSE CREDIT CRITERIA

CCJs (County Court Judgments):
- Maximum Value (Satisfied): £500 (registered in last 3 years, satisfied at application)
- Maximum Number (Satisfied): No maximum (but total value must not exceed £500)
- Maximum Value (Unsatisfied): 0 (will not consider applicants with unsatisfied CCJs)
- Maximum Number (Unsatisfied): 0 (will not consider applicants with unsatisfied CCJs)
- CCJs registered over 36 months ago: Potentially ignored
- CCJs registered 24-36 months ago: Potentially acceptable
- CCJs registered 12-24 months ago: Potentially acceptable
- CCJs registered 3-12 months ago: Potentially acceptable
- CCJs registered in last 3 Months: Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that have had CCJs registered in the past 3 months)

Defaults:
- Maximum Value (Satisfied): £2,500 (satisfied over 2 years)
- Maximum Number (Satisfied): No maximum (but total value must not exceed £2,500)
- Maximum Value (Unsatisfied): 0 (will not consider applicants with unsatisfied Defaults)
- Maximum Number (Unsatisfied): 0 (will not consider applicants with unsatisfied Defaults)
- Defaults registered over 36 months ago: Potentially ignored
- Defaults registered 24-36 months ago: Potentially acceptable (max £2,500, satisfied over 2 years)
- Defaults registered 12-24 months ago: Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that have had Defaults registered more than 12 months ago and less than 24 months ago)
- Defaults registered 3-12 months ago: Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that have had Defaults registered more than 3 months ago and less than 12 months ago)
- Defaults registered in last 3 Months: Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that have had Defaults registered in the past 3 months)

IVAs (Individual Voluntary Arrangements):
- Acceptable if satisfied for over 3 years
- Must have been registered over 4 years ago
- Must have been satisfied for at least 3 years

Debt Relief Orders:
- Acceptable if applicant has been clear for at least 24 months

Debt Management Plans:
- Satisfied: Acceptable (must be satisfied for over 2 years)
- Current/Unsatisfied: Not Acceptable

Arrears:
- Unsecured Arrears: Acceptable (maximum status 1 in last 2 years, must be satisfied for at least 12 months; Utilities & mobiles ignored up to £250)
- Secured/Mortgage Arrears: Acceptable (maximum status 1 in last 2 years and must be satisfied for at least 12 months)
- Maximum Unsecured Arrears in last 24 Months: 1 (maximum status 1, must be satisfied for at least 12 months)
- Maximum Mortgage Arrears in last 24 Months: Not Acceptable (will not consider Buy to Let applicants with any mortgage arrears in the previous 24 months)
- Utilities & Mobiles: Ignored up to £250
- Mortgage Arrears: Not acceptable within last 36 months (3 years; will not consider applicants with any mortgage arrears on their credit file within the last 3 years)
- Months Passed Where Mortgage Arrears Can Be Ignored: 36 months

Late Payments (Not Missed):
- Acceptable (maximum status 1 in last 2 years)

Payday Loans:
- Previous: Acceptable (must be clear for at least 12 months)
- Current: Not Acceptable

Unauthorized Overdraft Charges:
- Not Acceptable - Bath Building Society does not accept applicants that have recently had unauthorized overdraft charges

Financial Associates with Adverse:
- Not Acceptable - Bath Building Society will not accept applicants that are financially associated with persons that have adverse credit

Other Adverse Credit:
- Discharged Bankrupt: Acceptable (can potentially accept a discharged bankrupt if the bankruptcy has been satisfied for over 3 years)
- Arrangement to Pay: Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that have an arrangement to pay registered on their credit file)
- Criminal Convictions: Not Acceptable (does not accept applicants that have criminal convictions)
- Previously Repossessed: Not Acceptable (does not accept applications from borrowers who have previously had a property repossessed)
- Gambling (Recurring Transactions): Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that have recurring gambling transactions)
- Unemployed Applicants: Not Acceptable (will not consider Buy to Let applications for unemployed applicants)
- Income Made Up Primarily of Benefits: Not Acceptable (will not accept applicants income that is made up primarily of benefits)
- Non Owner Occupier: Not Acceptable (does not accept Buy to Let applications from 'Non Owner Occupiers')
- Property Developers: Not Acceptable (does not accept Buy to Let applications from individuals they deem property developers)
- Politically Exposed Persons: Not Acceptable (will not consider an applicant that is classed as a politically exposed person)

## PROPERTY AND LEGAL CRITERIA

Deposit Sources:
- Gift from Immediate Family: Acceptable
- Gifted NOT from Immediate Family: Not Acceptable (does not accept gifts from friends, employer, cousin, etc.)
- Loan from Immediate Family: Not Acceptable (unable to accept deposits where the source is a loan from immediate family)
- Redundancy Payment: Acceptable (subject to this being from a previous employment, and current employment meeting our criteria)
- Builders Deposit: Acceptable (providing the applicant puts the same amount of contribution in)
- Concessionary: Acceptable (providing the correct amount of stamp duty is paid)
- Mortgage on Additional Property: Acceptable
- Originated Outside of EEA: Not Acceptable
- Unsecured Loan: Not Acceptable
- Limited Company Deposit (from applicant's other company): Not Acceptable (Society won't accept a deposit from another Ltd Company)

Inter-family Sales:
- No Discount: Acceptable (Bath Building Society can potentially consider an application from an inter-family sale with no discount being offered)
- Undervalue Transactions: Not Acceptable

Purchase Applications:
- Properties Purchased at Auction: Acceptable
- Existing Tenants Will Remain: Acceptable (tenant must be occupying property on an AST)
- Purchase from Limited Company to Individual Name (Related Transaction): Acceptable (purchase would have to be "at arms length")

Lending Against Uplifted Value Following Refurbishment:
- Acceptable - Bath Building Society will use the uplifted value in this situation (refer to lender)

Property Usage:
- Agricultural Restrictions: Not Acceptable (does not lend on properties with agricultural restrictions)

Legal Requirements:
- No Search Indemnity Insurance (Purchases): Not Acceptable
- No Search Indemnity Insurance (Remortgages): Not Acceptable
- Private Local Authority Search (Purchases): Not Acceptable
- Private Local Authority Search (Remortgages): Not Acceptable

Leasehold Properties:
- Minimum Remaining Lease at Application: 
  * Interest Only: Must have a minimum of 80 years at end of the mortgage term
  * Repayment: Must have a minimum of 50 years at end of the mortgage term
  * Note: Lease must have 50 years left from the natural term end of the mortgage
- Minimum Remaining Lease at End of Term:
  * Interest Only: Must have a minimum of 80 years at end of the mortgage term
  * Repayment: Must have a minimum of 50 years at end of the mortgage term
- Leasehold Property where No Separate Management Company Exists: Not Acceptable (Bath Building Society will not lend on leasehold properties where no separate management company exists)

## VISA AND RESIDENCY CRITERIA

Visa Holders:
- Tier 1 Working Visa (Investors, Entrepreneurs, Talent): Not Acceptable
- Tier 2 Working Visa (Long-term work visas): Not Acceptable
- Family Visa (Spousal etc): Not Acceptable
- Minimum Number of Months Remaining on Visa: Not applicable (will not consider an applicant on a visa)

EU Nationals:
- EU Nationals with 'Settled Status': Acceptable (does lend to EU Nationals with a 'settled status' under the EU Settlement Scheme)
- EU Nationals with 'Pre-Settled Status': Acceptable (does lend to EU Nationals with a 'pre-settled status' under the EU Settlement Scheme)

Foreign Nationals:
- Foreign Nationals (with indefinite leave to remain): Acceptable (can consider applications from foreign nationals WITH indefinite leave to remain)
- Foreign Nationals (WITHOUT indefinite leave to remain): Not Acceptable (does not accept applications from foreign nationals that do not have indefinite leave to remain)
- Foreign Nationals (Living Overseas): Not Acceptable (does not lend to Foreign Nationals living overseas)

Expats:
- Expat Buy to Let: Not Acceptable (does not accept Expat Buy to Let business)
- Expat Deposit Source Non-UK: Not Acceptable (will not consider expat applications where the source of deposit does not originate from the UK)
- Expat UK Credit Profile Required: Not applicable (does not accept Ex-pat applications)
- Expat Minimum Income: Not applicable (does not accept Ex-Pat applications)
- Expat Regulated Buy to Let (Let to Family Member): Not Acceptable (does not accept Buy to Let mortgages from Expat applicants where the property will be Let to Family Member(s))
- Expat: Holiday Lets: Not Acceptable (does not accept expat applications at this time)

## OTHER CRITERIA

Regulated Buy to Let (Let to Family Member):
- Acceptable - Bath Building Society can potentially accept 'Regulated Buy to Let' applications
- Any rental income will be ignored and the application must pass the Society's standard affordability test

Advisor Own Application:
- Acceptable - Bath Building Society will accept applications from advisors applying for their own mortgage

Sole Application Joint Proprietor:
- Not Acceptable - Bath Building Society will not consider applications on a sole application joint proprietor basis

Post Offer Changes:
- Not Allowed - Bath Building Society does not allow post offer changes

Mortgage Offer Validity:
- Standard Offers: 6 months (180 days)
- New Build Offers: 6 months (180 days)
- Offer Extension: No (offers valid for 6 months; applications falling outside this period will need to be reassessed on products available at the time - refer to lender)

Maximum Number of Mortgaged Buy to Let Properties:
- No Maximum - Bath Building Society has no maximum limit to the number of Buy to Let mortgages applicants hold with other lenders

Green Mortgage:
- Not Acceptable - Bath Building Society is unable to consider a Green Mortgage buy to let application

Sharia Compliant Mortgages:
- Not Available - Bath Building Society does not offer Sharia compliant Buy to Let mortgages

Holiday Lets:
- Acceptable - Bath Building Society will accept Holiday Lets properties on specific Holiday Let products
- Minimum Personal Income: £20,000.00 needed
- Holiday Lets in Scotland: Will need to have a valid licence or undertake to obtain one on any new purchase
- Holiday Let Sites: Not acceptable
- Permission to Self Occupy Holiday Let Property: Acceptable (will allow borrowers to 'self occupy' their Holiday Let property up to 60 days a year)

Converted Churches:
- Potentially acceptable (however, flats converted from commercial premises are not considered)

New Build Warranties (Acceptable):
- NHBC Guarantee: Acceptable
- Premier Guarantee Scheme: Acceptable
- ICW: Acceptable
- LABC: Acceptable
- Professional Consultants Certificate: Acceptable
- Checkmate (Castle 10): Acceptable
- Buildzone: Acceptable
- Zurich Municipal "New Build": Acceptable
- AEDIS: Not Acceptable (Bath Building Society does not accept AEDIS as an acceptable new build warranty)
- Building Life Plans: Refer to lender

New Build Warranties (Not Acceptable):
- CRL Management Ltd: Not Acceptable
- Global Home Warranties: Not Acceptable
- Protek: Not Acceptable
- Q Assure Build: Not Acceptable

Retrospective New Build Warranty: Refer to lender

Other Important Criteria:
- Consumer Buy to Let (CBTL): Acceptable (can potentially accept applicants classed as a 'Consumer Buy to Let (CBTL)' - often referred to as an accidental landlord)
- Full Time Landlord: Acceptable (can potentially accept applications from 'Full Time Landlords' - typically an applicant whose main income source is from their rental income from property)
- Portfolio Landlord Minimum Experience: No minimum experience requirement
- Portable Mortgages: Yes (all Bath Building Society mortgages are full portable)
- Offset Mortgages: No (does not offer offset mortgages)
- Transitional Arrangements Offered: Yes (can potentially offer Buy to Let mortgages on a transitional arrangement basis as defined by the FCA under MCOB 11.8)
- Contract Reassignment: Not Acceptable (does not accept applications where contract reassignment exists - where purchaser is looking to buy a new build property from a third party who has secured the property off plan and exchanged contracts but has not completed)
- Power of Attorneys: Acceptable (can potentially consider an applicant from someone acting as a power of attorney)
