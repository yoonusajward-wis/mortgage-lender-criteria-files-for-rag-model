## Submission Requirements

Although every application may have different submission requirements, the following documents may be requested. Documents not uploaded during the online application must be provided within **24 hours**.

Fully completed online application: Required for all applications

Property schedule:

* Required for landlords with four or more buy-to-let properties
* Required for limited companies with four or more buy-to-let properties
* Required for refurb-to-let applications

Employed applicants:

* Last 3 months’ payslips

Self-employed applicants:

* Last 2 years SA302 tax calculations or equivalent
* Tax Year Overview
* Tax return / SA100 (for sole traders or personal rental income)

Limited company accounts:

* Last 2 years accounts where available
* Must be prepared by an acceptable qualified accountant

Rental bank statements:

* Last 3 months required where landlord owns four or more buy-to-let properties

Refurb-to-let applications:

* Schedule of works
* Evidence of funds to complete works

## Loan Requirements

Minimum loan amount:

* Purchase or remortgage: £30,000
* Further advance: £5,000

Minimum property valuation: £75,000

### Loan to Value (LTV)

Individuals with three or fewer mortgaged buy-to-let properties:

* 70% LTV: Maximum loan £1,500,000
* 75% LTV: Maximum loan £1,000,000
* 80% LTV: Maximum loan £750,000

Individuals with four or more mortgaged buy-to-let properties, limited companies, HMOs or multi-unit blocks:

* 65% LTV: Maximum loan £4,000,000
* 70% LTV: Maximum loan £1,500,000
* 75% LTV: Maximum loan £1,000,000
* 80% LTV: Maximum loan £750,000

Fees added to the loan:

* Included in LTV and maximum loan calculations
* Total borrowing including fees must not exceed 80% LTV

### Maximum Aggregate Borrowing

Individuals with three or fewer mortgaged buy-to-let properties: £2,000,000

Individuals with four or more mortgaged buy-to-let properties, limited companies, HMOs or multi-unit blocks: £10,000,000

Portfolio exposure conditions:

* Up to £5,000,000 exposure: Maximum portfolio LTV 80%
* Above £5,000,000 exposure: Maximum portfolio LTV 75%
* New lending above £5,000,000 exposure: Maximum 70% LTV per property

Where aggregate lending with Paragon exceeds £2,000,000:

* Portfolio review meeting with senior underwriter
* Cash flow forecast required
* Business plan required
* Net worth statement required

### Loan Purpose

Accepted purposes:

* Purchase of buy-to-let property
* Remortgage of buy-to-let property
* Further advance

Capital raising: Only accepted for owning and renting residential property

Remortgage ownership requirement:

* Property must be owned by all applicants

### Purchase Applications

Loan calculation basis: Lower of purchase price or current valuation

Deposit funds:

* Must originate from the UK
* Source of funds must be evidenced

### Remortgage Applications

Ownership requirement: Property may have been owned for less than 6 months

Loan calculation basis: Lower of purchase price or current valuation

Current valuation may be used if:

* Significant improvement works completed
* Evidence of expenditure and schedule of works provided

Ownership registration: Property must be registered in applicant name at Land Registry or Registers of Scotland before completion

### Consumer Buy-to-Let

Consumer buy-to-let applications: Accepted subject to meeting relevant criteria

### Mortgage Term

Minimum term: 5 years
Maximum term: 35 years

Further advances: Must match the remaining term of the existing mortgage

### Repayment Methods

Allowed repayment methods:

* Interest only
* Capital and interest
* Part interest only and part capital repayment

### Property Investment Clubs and Syndicates

Applications connected to property investment clubs or syndicates: Not accepted

## Income / Affordability

Affordability assessment: Based primarily on expected rental income

Applicants remain responsible for covering:

* Mortgage payments
* Property letting costs
* Any rental shortfalls

### Income

Minimum income: £25,000 combined gross annual income

Acceptable income sources:

* Employment income
* Self-employed income
* Occupational pension income
* Private pension income
* State pension income

Applicants with four or more properties or limited companies:

* Applications may be considered without the £25,000 minimum if financial position is sufficient

Income restrictions:

Individuals with three or fewer buy-to-let properties:

* State benefits: Not considered
* Investment income: Not considered
* Land and property income: Not considered

Individuals with four or more buy-to-let properties, limited companies, HMOs or multi-unit blocks:

* State benefits: Not considered
* Investment income: Not considered

Land and property income for portfolio landlords:

* Calculated using taxable profit minus residential finance costs

### Affordability Calculations

Affordability validation based on:

* Verified income
* Rental income meeting Interest Cover Ratio (ICR)

Calculation rate:

* Product rate + 2% or reference rate (whichever is higher)
* For 5-year fixed rates: product rate or 5-year reference rate (whichever is higher)

Future affordability: Also assessed

Further advances:

* Assessment includes existing loans and proposed borrowing

### Interest Cover Ratio (ICR)

Single self-contained properties:

* Basic rate taxpayers (20%) and limited companies: 125%
* Higher/additional rate taxpayers: 140%

HMOs, multi-unit blocks and other property types:

* Basic rate taxpayers (20%) and limited companies: 130%
* Higher/additional rate taxpayers: 145%

Lenders may request:

* Evidence of contingency funds
* Additional affordability checks

## Personal Details

### Applicants

Minimum age: 21
Maximum age at end of term: 85

Applicants:

* Maximum 4 individuals

Residency requirement: Resident and liable to UK tax for minimum 2 years

Immigration status: Indefinite leave to remain required

Ownership requirement: At least one applicant must own a residential property

Further advance applicants: Must match existing mortgage parties

Identity verification:

* Required if identity cannot be verified through underwriting
* Certified documents accepted via solicitor or intermediary
* Post Office Identity Document Checking Service accepted

### Limited Companies / LLPs

Limited companies and LLPs:

* Must be registered and trading in England, Wales or Scotland
* Directors or members must have been resident and liable to UK tax for at least 2 years

Company purpose: Principal activity must be buying and holding residential investment property

Floating charge:

* May be required where companies have other material business activities

LLP requirements:

* All members must be designated members
* LLPs with corporate members are not accepted

Guarantees:

* Full personal guarantees required from all directors or members

Company ownership:

* Maximum 4 directors or members
* At least 80% of shares owned by directors/guarantors personally or through holding company with same ownership

Persons with significant control:

* Must be registered at Companies House
* Must be party to the mortgage application

## Credit History

Credit assessment: Comprehensive review including credit search and credit score

Applications will not be considered where an applicant:

* Has current or historic county court judgements
* Is a current or historic disqualified director
* Has poor credit history including defaults or arrears

Lender may conduct additional credit searches during application processing.

## Employment

Employment requirement: At least one applicant must be employed or self-employed.

### Employed Applicants

Employment type: Permanent employment

Probationary period: Must not be in probation

Contract workers: Considered on a case-by-case basis

### Self-Employed Applicants

Minimum trading history: 2 years

Documentation required:

* 2 years accounts or tax returns

Self-employed definition:

* Applicants owning 20% or more of a company
* Applicants employed by a family business

### Accepted Accountancy Institutes

Accounts must be prepared by members of:

* Institute of Chartered Accountants in England & Wales (ACA/FCA)
* Institute of Chartered Accountants of Scotland (CA)
* Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA/FCCA)
* Chartered Institute of Management Accountants (ACMA/FCMA)
* Association of International Accountants (AAIA/FAIA)
* Association of Authorised Public Accountants (AAPA/FAPA)
* Association of Accounting Technicians (MAAT/FMAAT – licensed practising member)

## Property

Minimum property valuation: £75,000

### Valuations

Security: First legal charge normally taken

Property types: Traditionally constructed residential property in England, Wales or Scotland

Further advances: Secured via second charge

Valuation report:

* Determines property value
* Confirms sustainable rental income
* Assesses suitability as security

Valuation copies:

* Provided for lending purposes only
* Not a full condition survey

Property condition: Must be lettable in current condition

Revaluation requirement:

* Required if original inspection is older than 4 months

Energy Performance Certificate (EPC):

* Minimum rating E unless exemption applies
* Compliance required throughout mortgage term

### Multi-Unit Property

Minimum landlord experience: 2 years

Maximum units per property: 20

Minimum property values:

* Up to 10 units: £100,000
* Up to 20 units: £150,000

Where units could be sold individually:

* Valuation discount applied reflecting single title purchase

Long lease restriction:

* Not accepted if more than 50% units held on long leases

Recent purchases:

* Within 12 months with uplift: Maximum 70% LTV
* Like-for-like refinancing may exceed 70% subject to product criteria

### House in Multiple Occupation (HMO)

Definition: Property occupied by at least three tenants forming more than one household sharing facilities

Minimum landlord experience: 2 years

Minimum property values:

* Up to 10 rooms: £100,000
* Up to 20 rooms: £150,000

Required documentation for conversions:

* HMO licence
* Planning permission or evidence of lawful use
* Building regulations approval
* Completion certificate

### Investment Valuations

Required for:

* Multi-unit properties
* HMOs with 5 or more tenants
* Properties no longer functioning as a single dwelling

### All Properties

Where property has been altered or converted:

* Building regulations approval required
* Completion certificate required

### Property Exclusions

Unacceptable property types:

* Ex-local authority flats or maisonettes
* Non-traditional construction (concrete, timber or structurally defective)
* Shared ownership properties
* Converted flats where applicant occupies another unit in same building
* Freehold flats or maisonettes in England or Wales
* Leasehold properties in Scotland
* Properties designated defective under housing legislation
* Agricultural or restrictive planning properties
* Properties affected by mining subsidence, landfill or contamination
* Right to Buy or Housing Association purchases
* New properties built within last 10 years without NHBC or acceptable warranty
* Properties with pre-emption clauses
* Properties within 10 metres of electrical substations
* Properties within 100 metres of high-voltage cables or communication masts
* Properties in Wales occupied under secure contracts
* Flats requiring external wall remediation schemes

Properties that may be considered on a case-by-case basis:

* Ex-local authority houses
* Flats in blocks above 10 storeys or with more than 100 units
* Studio flats (minimum 30 m²)
* New flats with potentially low rental demand
* Flats above commercial premises
* Properties with commercial elements up to 40% of floor space
* Flying freehold properties
* Self-build properties
* Properties with sale or occupancy restrictions (Section 106)
* Properties with chancel repair liability
* Properties affected by environmental search issues

### Tenure

Acceptable tenure in England, Wales or Scotland:

* Freehold/heritable

Acceptable tenure in England and Wales only:

* Leasehold, subject to a minimum of 85 years unexpired term at the commencement of the mortgage, and 65 years unexpired term at the end of the mortgage

Ground rent and other event fees must be reasonable at all times during the lease term.

It is acceptable for ground rent escalation to be linked to Retail Price Index (RPI) or a similar index where the review period is 10 years or greater. Reviews every five years are not acceptable. The passing ground rent must be in proportion to the capital value of the property and not materially affect its value.

Unreasonable multipliers of ground rent will not be permitted, for example, doubling every 10 or 15 years. If you are unsure as to whether the terms of a lease are unreasonable, please refer the details to us for consideration.

Please note, possessory title is not normally acceptable.

### Occupancy

Depending on the specific conditions of each loan, the property must be occupied by acceptable tenants (please see the let property section for details). The property may be vacant between lettings for up to 90 days, subject to the terms of the insurance cover being complied with.

### Legal Charges

First legal charge requirement: We require a first legal charge (or in Scotland, standard security) over the property.

Subsequent charges: We will not accept subsequent charges in favour of any other party, for any purpose, including borrowing from other lending institutions.

Further advances: We require a second legal charge over the property.

## Letting Requirements

Tenant legal status: The landlord is responsible for checking the legal status of a tenant to ensure they have a permanent right to reside in the UK.

### England Letting Requirements

Property may either be let:

* On an assured shorthold tenancy, subject to a minimum term of six months and a maximum of 36 months
* To a limited company registered in England, Wales or Scotland, or a suitable educational institute for up to three years
* To a local authority or housing association for up to five years

Where the property is occupied by private individuals:

* Their rights of occupation must not extend beyond the term granted to the acceptable tenant
* They must occupy the property only for the purposes of their main residence without the provision of any support services or assistance from any service provider

Where the property is let to an organisation:

* The limited company, suitable educational institute, local authority or housing association must not occupy or use the property for any business purposes
* They must not be an accommodation and support services provider that provides support, services or assistance to occupiers or engages third parties to provide such services

### Wales Letting Requirements

Occupation under a secure contract: Not permitted.

Property may only be let:

* Under a standard contract as defined by the Renting Homes (Wales) Act 2016 subject to a minimum term of six months and a maximum of 36 months
* To a limited company registered in England, Wales or Scotland, or a suitable educational institute for up to three years
* To a local authority or housing association for up to five years

Where the property is occupied by private individuals:

* Their rights of occupation must not extend beyond the term granted to the acceptable tenant
* They must occupy the property only for the purposes of their main residence without the provision of support services or assistance from any service provider

Where the property is let to organisations:

* The limited company, suitable educational institute, local authority or housing association must not occupy or use the property for any business purposes
* They must not be an accommodation and support services provider or engage third parties to provide such services

### Scotland Letting Requirements

Property may either be let:

* On a short assured tenancy with a minimum term of six months and a maximum term of 12 months
* On a private residential tenancy

Where the property is occupied by private individuals:

* Their rights of occupation must not extend beyond the term granted to the acceptable tenant
* They must occupy the property only for the purposes of their main residence without support services or assistance from any service provider

For full details please refer to the General and Special Conditions booklet at: [www.paragonbank.co.uk/btldocuments/intermediary](http://www.paragonbank.co.uk/btldocuments/intermediary)

### Tenancy Agreement Conditions

Rent payment intervals:

* Monthly
* Quarterly
* Termly (students)

Property must not be let to or occupied by:

* The applicant or any member of their family
* The guarantor or any member of their family
* The vendor or any member of their family
* Any previous owner or their family members
* An employee of the applicant
* An associated subsidiary or parent company of the applicant

Sale-and-rent-back arrangements: Not acceptable

Holiday lets: Not acceptable

Existing tenancy agreements before completion:

* Solicitor must ensure lender interests are protected
* Property must meet lender criteria

## Solicitors

Solicitors may act for both parties provided the following conditions are met:

Firm requirements:

* Minimum of four partners
* All partners must hold current practising certificates or licences
* Firm must operate from commercial premises
* Firm must have been in business for at least three years
* Firm must be registered with the Law Society or Council for Licensed Conveyancers

Solicitor requirements:

* Must hold a current practising certificate
* Must be registered with the Law Society or Council for Licensed Conveyancers

Professional Indemnity Insurance:

* Minimum £4 million cover where the loan amount is £2 million or more

Panel requirement:

* Firm must be registered on Lender Exchange
* Must agree to Paragon’s terms and conditions
* Must be accepted onto the Paragon conveyancing panel

If the applicant selects a firm not acceptable to Paragon:

* The firm will act for the applicant only
* The applicant must select another firm from the panel to act for the lender
* All associated legal costs will be payable by the applicant

Paragon reserves the right to instruct alternative solicitors to act solely for the lender, with all related costs payable by the applicant.

Panel verification: Solicitor status can be checked via the Lender Exchange website.

## Property Insurance

Buildings insurance:

* Must be index-linked
* Must cover the full rebuilding cost

Insurance arrangements:

* Arranged by the applicant
* Paragon may request a copy of the policy

Contingency insurance:

* Applicant must contribute if Paragon arranges contingency cover due to inadequate insurance

Insurance terms:

* Property must be acceptable to insurers on standard terms
* No exclusions or increased excesses permitted

Leasehold insurance:

* May already be arranged by the freeholder under lease terms

Solicitor confirmation requirements:

* Insurance policy meets mortgage contract requirements
* Policy is active at completion
* Lender interest noted where possible

Further advance requirement:

* Insurance must be increased if the reinstatement value rises

Paragon reserves the right to determine whether to make an offer and the terms of the offer.

* Telephone contact with applicants

Updated references required if completion does not occur within six months.

## Refurb-to-Let

Refurb-to-let product purpose:

* Refitting and modernisation works
* No material change to existing floor area
* Costs usually not exceeding 25% of property value

Excluded works:

* Alterations requiring planning permission
* Works requiring permitted development rights
* Works requiring building regulations approval
* Major structural works
* Change of use except C3 to C4 (up to six sharers)

Application documentation:

* Proposed schedule of works
* Detailed project plan where available
* Pricing, timescales and contractor details
* Evidence of funds for works

Surveyor process:

* Works information provided before valuation
* Valuation only carried out once details are received

Post-refurbishment:

* Loan replaced with a standard buy-to-let mortgage

Offers:

* Refurbishment loan and buy-to-let mortgage offered simultaneously

Fee reductions: Available as per the buy-to-let product guide

### Criteria Exceptions

Minimum loan amount: Purchase/Remortgage: £30,000

Maximum loan amount: Purchase/Remortgage: £1,000,000

Minimum property valuation: £50,000

**Loan to Value (LTV):**

* **Maximum LTV based on initial loan where interest is rolled up:** 70%
* **Maximum LTV based on end loan balance where interest is rolled up:** 75%
* **Maximum LTV based on initial loan where interest is serviced:** 75%
* **Maximum LTV where loan is greater than £750,000:** 70%

Fees added to loan: Excluded from LTV calculations

### Maximum Aggregate Borrowing

Maximum portfolio exposure: £5,000,000 per borrower

### Loan Purpose

Accepted purposes:

* Purchase of residential investment property
* Remortgage of residential investment property

### Consumer Buy-to-Let

Consumer buy-to-let applications: Not accepted for refurb-to-let products

### Mortgage Term

Minimum term: 1 month
Maximum term: 6 months

### Repayment Methods

Available repayment methods:

* Interest-only loans
* Serviced monthly payments
* Gross rolled-up interest

### Property Valuation

Minimum property valuation: £50,000

Valuation purpose:

* Assess current property value
* Determine sustainable rental income

Valuation documentation:

* Full schedule of works required prior to valuation
* Supporting documentation required

After works valuation:

* Capital value after refurbishment provided

Paragon reserves the right to accept or decline any property.

Post-completion revaluation: May be carried out to review progress of works.

### House in Multiple Occupation (HMO)

HMO licence: Current licence required where applicable

Conversion documentation required before funds release:

* Planning consent or lawful development certificate
* Building regulations approval
* Building regulations completion certificate

Indemnity insurance: Not accepted where the above documentation is missing

### All Properties

For altered, converted or change-of-use properties the following must be provided:

* HMO licence where applicable
* Planning permission or lawful development evidence
* Building regulations approval
* Building regulations completion certificate

### Occupancy

Property vacancy during refurbishment: Permitted subject to insurance conditions

### Offer of Loan

Refurbishment loan and buy-to-let mortgage: Offered simultaneously

Refurbishment offer validity: Three calendar months

Buy-to-let mortgage offer validity: Six calendar months

### Post-Completion Monitoring

Underwriting oversight: Dedicated team monitoring progress throughout the loan term.
