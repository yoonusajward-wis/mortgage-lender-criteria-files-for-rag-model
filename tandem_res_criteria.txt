## SECTION 1 – LOAN PARAMETERS

- Minimum term: 5 years.
- Maximum term: 35 years.
- Maximum LTV: 90%.
- Maximum LTI: 5x (inclusive of any fees added to the loan).
- Repayment type: capital and interest only.
- Minimum property value: £75,000.
- Lending in England and Wales only.

### Maximum Loan by LTV

- Up to 90% LTV: maximum loan £499,999.
- Up to 85% LTV: maximum loan £624,999.
- Up to 80% LTV: maximum loan £750,000.
- Maximum unsecured debt consolidation on remortgage: £60,000.

### New Build LTV Limits

- New build houses: maximum 85% LTV.
- New build flats: maximum 75% LTV.
- Maximum 10% exposure on one new build site.
- New build definition: construction complete and/or property first occupied within the previous 2 years.

## SECTION 2 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

- Minimum age: 21.
- Maximum age: 70 (loan must be repaid before the borrower's 70th birthday).
- Maximum 2 applicants.
- 3-year address history required.
- Nationality: UK resident; non-UK residents must have indefinite right to remain or permanent settlement.

### Employment

- Employed: minimum 1 month in current job with 12 months' continuous employment.
- Contracts: minimum 6 months remaining at time of offer; where less than 6 months remaining, evidence of renewal or extension taking it past 6 months must be provided.
- Agency workers: acceptable where with the same agency for 12 months or more.
- Self-employed: minimum 2 years' trading; latest year's figures used for affordability.
- CIS contractors: considered.

### Married or Co-habiting Applicants

- Loan must be in joint names unless there is a plausible reason to proceed in sole name (e.g. existing property currently owned in sole name).
- Where the property is or will be registered in one applicant's sole name: acceptable subject to both borrowers residing in the property and Independent Legal Advice (ILA) for the applicant who is not a registered owner (ILA cannot be obtained via remortgage free legals).

### First-Time Buyer Definition

- Someone who has never previously had a mortgage; or
- Someone with no mortgage in the last 36 months.
- 3 months' personal bank statements required for all tiers.

---

## SECTION 3 – INCOME AND AFFORDABILITY

- Maximum LTI: 5x (inclusive of fees added to the loan).
- Up to 100% of consistent monthly or quarterly bonus and commission considered.
- Up to 100% of consistent monthly or quarterly overtime, shift allowance and car allowance considered.
- Up to 100% of second income considered (minimum 6 months in the role).

### Unsecured Loans and Hire Purchase

- Unsecured loans or HP with 3 months or fewer remaining (6 months for purchases) can be excluded from the affordability assessment.
- Time remaining calculated as: outstanding balance ÷ monthly instalment.
- Care should be taken with car leases where renewal/continuation is expected.

### Debt Consolidation

- Debt consolidation available on remortgage applications only.
- On purchase applications: a solicitor's undertaking to evidence that an account will be settled on completion may be accepted, allowing it to be excluded from affordability.
- Evidence that the account was settled post-completion may be requested.
- Where a monthly payment is used for affordability, the account may be part-settled but the remaining balance is only excluded if it equates to fewer than 3 months' payments (6 months for purchases).

---

## SECTION 4 – CREDIT HISTORY

- Credit assessment based on the last 24 months.
- Credit conduct assessed per applicant, not per application.

### CCJs and Defaults

- Number of CCJs and defaults reviewed (not the value).
- CCJs and defaults do not need to be satisfied.
- CCJs and defaults owed to utility or communications providers with a value under £350 are not used to determine the product tier.
- CCJ or default balances over £2,000 must be referred and explained to an underwriter.

### Secured and Unsecured Arrears

- Mortgage and secured loan arrears considered.
- Secured arrears assessed on missed payments: 1 late payment on one account allowed before tier cascade.
- Unsecured arrears assessed on worst status basis: 1 late payment on one account allowed before tier cascade.
- Utility and communications providers not used to determine the product tier.

### Debt Management Plans, Bankruptcy and IVA

- Discharged bankruptcy: considered if discharged more than 36 months ago.
- Discharged IVA: considered if discharged more than 36 months ago.
- Debt management plans: must be satisfactorily completed more than 12 months ago.

## SECTION 5 – PRODUCT TIER CRITERIA

### Tier 0 – Maximum 90% LTV

- CCJs: 0 in last 24 months.
- Defaults: 0 in last 24 months.
- Unsecured account conduct: highest status 0–24 months = 0.
- Mortgage/secured loan conduct: 0 missed payments in last 24 months.
- IVA, bankruptcy, DRO: none ever.
- Debt management plans: none ever.
- Payday loans: none in last 12 months.
- Maximum loan: £499,999 at 90% LTV; £624,999 at 85% LTV.

### Tier 1 – Maximum 90% LTV

- CCJs: maximum 1 in last 24 months, with 0 in last 3 months.
- Defaults: maximum 1 in last 24 months, with 0 in last 3 months.
- Unsecured account conduct: highest status 0–6 months = 0; 7–24 months = 1.
- Mortgage/secured loan conduct: 0 missed payments in last 6 months; maximum 1 in last 7–24 months.
- IVA, bankruptcy, DRO: none in last 6 years.
- Debt management plans: none ever.
- Payday loans: maximum 3 in last 12 months; maximum 1 currently active.
- Maximum loan: £499,999 at 90% LTV; £624,999 at 85% LTV.

### Tier 2 – Maximum 85% LTV

- CCJs: maximum 2 in last 24 months, with 0 in last 3 months.
- Defaults: maximum 2 in last 24 months, with 0 in last 3 months.
- Unsecured account conduct: highest status 0–6 months = 1; 7–24 months = 2; current status = 1.
- Mortgage/secured loan conduct: 0 missed payments in last 6 months; maximum 2 in last 7–24 months; current status = 1.
- IVA, bankruptcy, DRO: discharged more than 36 months ago.
- Debt management plans: none ever.
- Payday loans: maximum 3 in last 12 months; maximum 1 currently active.
- Maximum loan: £500,000 at below 85% LTV.

### Tier 3 – Maximum 80% LTV

- CCJs: maximum 3 in last 24 months, with 0 in last 3 months.
- Defaults: maximum 3 in last 24 months, with 0 in last 3 months.
- Unsecured account conduct: highest status 0–6 months = 2; 7–24 months = 3; current status = 2.
- Mortgage/secured loan conduct: 0 missed payments in last 6 months; maximum 3 in last 7–24 months; current status = 1.
- IVA, bankruptcy, DRO: discharged more than 36 months ago.
- Debt management plans: completed satisfactorily more than 12 months ago.
- Payday loans: maximum 3 in last 12 months; maximum 1 currently active.
- Maximum loan: £500,000 at below 80% LTV.

**Notes applying to all tiers:**
- CCJs and defaults for utility and communications providers under £350 are not used to determine the product tier.
- CCJs and defaults do not need to be satisfied; balances over £2,000 must be referred to an underwriter.
- 1 late payment on one account is allowed before tier cascade (both secured and unsecured).

## SECTION 6 – DEPOSIT CRITERIA

- 6 months' deposit history required (except where funded by equity in an existing residential property, validated by Tandem's solicitors).

### Acceptable Deposit Sources

- Equity from sale of property.
- Savings, investments, shares or inheritance.
- Family gifted deposit.
- Family gifted equity.
- Assisted deposits (builder deposit or builder incentive).
- Personal loan (cannot be provided by any Tandem Group product).

### Gifted Deposits from Family Members

- A completed gifted deposit form is required.

### Purchases at Undervalue and Gifted Equity Transactions

- Permitted only where the donor is a family member.
- Required: gifted equity form; declaration of solvency from the vendor (to confirm the vendor is not bankrupt or approaching bankruptcy); evidence that vendors have owned the property for a minimum of 6 months.

### Deposit by Personal Loan

- Copy of the credit agreement confirming the amount borrowed and monthly repayments required.
- Monthly payments factored into the affordability assessment.

## SECTION 7 – PROPERTY CRITERIA

### Valuations

- Purchases: physical valuation required for all purchases.
- Remortgage AVM: acceptable up to 75% LTV subject to: no flats; minimum confidence level 5; property purchased or remortgaged in last 5 years.
- RICS full valuation: required up to maximum LTV in all other cases.

### Unacceptable Property Types and Construction

- Properties containing Mundic Block materials (unless classified as A1, A2 or A3 with a valid report in accordance with the 2nd edition or later of the RICS Guidance Note — The Mundic Problem).
- Steel-framed houses built prior to the year 2000.
- Timber-framed properties built prior to 1970 (1970 or later acceptable if they have a masonry outer skin).
- Properties with significant areas of single-skin construction (e.g. half-brick thick, less than 225mm, or other insubstantial materials adversely affecting saleability, thermal or structural performance).
- Large panel construction flats.
- Timber-framed properties with retrospective cavity fill.
- Any other non-standard construction: please refer prior to submission.

## SECTION 9 – FEES AND ADDITIONAL FEATURES

- No application fee.
- No valuation fee for remortgages up to £700,000.
- Free standard lender-only legals for remortgages.
- Broker fee of £2,495 or 8% plus Tandem's lender fee can be added to the loan with no interest charged.
- Fees can be added above maximum LTVs subject to affordability.
- Residential EPC discount: up to 0.30% for EPC A and B rated properties.
- Further advances considered after 6 months.
- Rates reserved for 7 days after accepted DIP and ESIS produced.

## SECTION 10 – VALIDITY PERIODS

- Offer validity (purchase): 6 months from date of offer.
- Offer validity (remortgage): 3 months from date of offer.
- On expiry: rescore and reunderwrite required.
- Decision in Principle: valid for 7 days.
- DIP to Offer: documents and credit search valid for 60 days; after 60 days a new credit search is required and new payslips and other documents may also be required.
