## Residential Criteria

### Applicants

Age:

* Minimum age: 18
* No maximum age for borrowers

Maximum number of applicants: 4

Nationality:

* UK citizens including those working overseas (see Expat criteria)
* Foreign nationals with Indefinite Leave to Remain
* EU nationals with pre-settled or settled status (verified via UK Gov website)

Address history: Minimum 3 years required

Proof of address accepted:

* Council Tax demand letter or statement (less than 12 months old)
* Recent bank statement issued by a regulated financial firm in the UK, EU or comparable jurisdiction (dated within last 3 months showing applicant address)
* Gas, electricity, water or telephone bill (not mobile) dated within last 3 months
* DWP entitlement letter or confirmation of pension or benefits (dated within last 12 months)
* Residential care home statement or invoice confirming residency

Lending into retirement age:

* Maximum permissible age for earned or self-employed income: 75 (subject to occupation)
* Beyond this age pension or other non-earned income will be assessed

Self-employed applicants accepted:

* Sole trader
* Limited company director
* Partnership or LLP
* Contractor including Construction Industry Scheme (CIS)

Armed Forces personnel:

* Accepted
* Armed Forces Help to Buy scheme not accepted

### Income

Minimum income:

* No minimum income requirement
* All applications assessed on affordability

Employment and income history:

* Previous 3 months payslips required
* Most recent P60 if available

Allowances accepted:

* 100% guaranteed contractual shift allowance
* 100% guaranteed contractual mortgage subsidy
* 100% Large Town Allowance
* 100% guaranteed car allowance (reduced by value of any car loan)
* 50% regular overtime, bonus or commission confirmed by payslips and P60

Probationary period:

* Must end before completion
* Employer confirmation required
* Cases may be considered where applicants have employment history in the same field

Self-employed income requirements:

* Accounts for last 2 years of trading
* Evidence of income and expenditure
* Future income projections may be requested if accounts older than 1 year or trading less than 2 years
* Accountant confirmation of profits may be required
* Last 2 years tax calculations
* SA302s where applicable

Pension income:

* Evidence required of anticipated pension income if mortgage term extends into retirement

Rental income:

* Income must be net of mortgage payments and adjusted for tax and repair costs
* Maximum 50% of anticipated rental income from lodgers or shared living arrangements accepted

Benefits accepted for affordability:

* Child Benefit
* Child Tax Credit element
* Working Tax Credit element
* Adoption Allowance
* Universal Credit (case-by-case)
* Pension Credit
* Disability Living Allowance
* Personal Independence Payment (PIP)

Benefit requirement:

* Minimum 5 years remaining

Benefits not accepted:

* Job Seekers Allowance
* Employment & Support Allowance
* Income Support
* Housing Benefit
* Carers Allowance
* Attendance Allowance

Maintenance income:

* 100% accepted if at least 5 years remaining
* Must be supported by court order or Child Maintenance Service
* Payments made into joint account with ex-partner disregarded

Foster care income:

* 100% of average earnings over 2 years accepted if applicant has been a foster carer for more than 2 years

Foreign currency income:

* Minimum income £30,000 sterling equivalent
* Affordability adjusted to account for currency fluctuation risk
* Wide range of currencies accepted
* Currency from FATF Black/Grey list countries not accepted

Cabin crew income: Accepted

### Sources of Deposit

Acceptable deposits:

* Gift from family
* Inheritance (within last 3 months requires executor confirmation, copy of will or solicitor letter)
* Stocks and shares
* Funds raised from another property
* Own savings

Unacceptable deposits:

* Cash
* Cash sale of assets (e.g. vehicle or jewellery)
* Unsecured borrowing

### Property

Location:

* England
* Wales
* Scotland (restrictions apply in Scottish Isles)

Construction:

* Wide range of construction types accepted subject to assessment
* Non-standard construction considered subject to insurance and valuation requirements

Flats:

* Maximum building height: 10 storeys
* Studio flats considered with minimum 30m² floor area (subject to referral)
* Tyneside flats accepted in Cumbria only
* Flats above commercial premises considered (subject to referral)

Leasehold:

* Minimum unexpired lease: 85 years at completion
* Minimum remaining lease: 60 years at mortgage expiry

Semi-commercial properties:

* Accepted where residential use is at least 40% of floor area

New build LTV limits:

* 90% for residential new build houses and flats (subject to MIG acceptance)
* 80% for Expat Residential
* 70% for Expat Buy to Let

Accepted new build warranties:

* NHBC
* Advantage HCI
* Aedis Warranties Ltd
* Build Assure
* New Homes/Build Assure 10
* Building Life Plans (BLP)
* Build-Zone New Home Warranty
* Checkmate Castle 10 Home Warranty
* Global Home Warranties Ltd
* International Construction Warranties Ltd (ICW)
* Local Authority Building Control (LABC) New Home Warranty
* Premier Guarantee
* Protek
* The Q Policy

Retrospective new build warranties: Not accepted

Builder's deposit: Maximum 5%

Self-build or renovation properties: Accepted on specific product range

Residential let: Accepted

Acreage limits:

* Up to 20 acres within Heartland Area
* Up to 10 acres nationally

Affordable housing:

* Must be discussed with the lender before submitting an application

Section 106:

* Accepted within Heartland Area

Ex-local authority properties:

* Accepted
* Properties in Scotland must be referred before application submission

### Credit

Adverse credit:
We will not lend to applicants who:

* Within the last 2 years have owed overdue payments equivalent to 3 months’ payments on a mortgage or other loan (secured or unsecured)
* Have had one or more County Court Judgments with a total value greater than £500 within the last 3 years
* Have been subject to an Individual Voluntary Arrangement or bankruptcy order at any time within the last 3 years

Bank statements:

* Bank statements for active accounts covering the last 3 months are required
* Statements will be reviewed for high-risk indicators

### Interest Only

Interest only LTV:

* Owner-occupier lending: Maximum 50% LTV with minimum £200,000 equity
* Self-build or renovation projects: Maximum 80% LTV during the build process

Acceptable repayment vehicles:

* Endowment
* Stocks and Shares ISA
* Pension plan (maximum 25% of lump sum allowed)
* Sale of second property
* Sale of main residence (subject to lender restrictions)

## Buy To Let Criteria

### Applicants

Minimum income: £25,000 (sole or joint)

Age:

* Minimum age: 18
* No maximum age

Maximum number of applicants: 4

First time landlords: Accepted

First time buyers and non-owner occupiers: Accepted

Expats: Accepted

Portfolio landlords:

* Not accepted if more than 3 mortgaged properties

### Property

Minimum loan value: £30,000

Minimum EPC rating: E

HMO / multi-occupancy / SPVs / Limited Companies: Not considered

Holiday lets: Accepted

Maximum LTV: 70%

### Income

Top-slicing: Allowed (including Expat BTL)

### Credit

Adverse credit:
We will not lend to applicants who:

* Within the last 2 years have owed overdue payments equivalent to 3 months’ payments on a mortgage or other loan (secured or unsecured)
* Have had one or more County Court Judgments with a total value greater than £500 within the last 3 years
* Have been subject to an Individual Voluntary Arrangement or bankruptcy order within the last 3 years

### Affordability

Rental cover rate:

* Basic rate taxpayers: 130%

## Expat Specific Criteria

All standard Residential and Buy-to-Let criteria apply unless specified otherwise.

### Applicants

Minimum income: £30,000 sterling equivalent (sole or joint) for both Residential and Buy to Let

Foreign currency income:

* Income from a wide range of currencies may be considered
* Income from countries listed on the FATF Non-Cooperative Countries or Territories list (Black/Grey lists) is not accepted

Age:

* Minimum age: 18
* No maximum age (non-earned income must support lending beyond age 75)

Nationality:

* UK citizens
* Foreign nationals with Indefinite Leave to Remain or EU pre-settled/settled status

Address history: Minimum 3 years required

Bank accounts:

* All expat applicants must hold a UK bank account
* Applicants residing in or funding deposits from FATF high-risk countries are not accepted
* Contact the lender for the latest country restrictions

## Circumstances Where We Will Not Lend

We do not lend in the following circumstances:

* Applicants with adverse credit where:

  * Overdue payments equivalent to 3 months’ payments occurred within the last 2 years
  * County Court Judgments exceeding £500 occurred within the last 3 years
  * An Individual Voluntary Arrangement or bankruptcy was in force within the last 3 years
* Foreign nationals residing outside the UK
* Expats residing in high-risk countries or earning income in unacceptable currencies
* Applicants relying on earned income beyond age 75
* Armed Forces Help to Buy cases
* Properties valued below £100,000
* Properties located in Northern Ireland
* Buy-to-let applications through SPVs or limited companies
* Portfolio landlords with more than 3 mortgaged BTL properties
* HMO properties
* Buy-to-let lending above 70% LTV
* UK BTL applicants with income below £25,000
* Expat BTL applicants with income below £30,000
* Expat applicants residing in Italy or the Netherlands

If a customer does not qualify under the standard criteria but exceptional circumstances apply, intermediaries should contact the Telephone Business Development Manager or Mortgage Team to discuss the case.
