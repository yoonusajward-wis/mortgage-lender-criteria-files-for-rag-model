# FAMILY BUILDING SOCIETY – RESIDENTIAL (OWNER OCCUPIER) LENDING CRITERIA

---

**LENDER:** Family Building Society
**PRODUCT:** Residential – Owner Occupier
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Owner Occupier Criteria A–Z
**SOURCE:** [https://intermediaries.familybuildingsociety.co.uk/criteria/owner-occupier/a-z](https://intermediaries.familybuildingsociety.co.uk/criteria/owner-occupier/a-z)
**LAST VERIFIED:** Based on website content as of file creation date

**IMPORTANT:**
This document covers Owner Occupier (Residential) criteria only.

---

## SECTION 1 – MINIMUM AGE AT APPLICATION (RESIDENTIAL)

**Requirement:**

* Minimum age at application: 18.

**Condition:**

* The minimum age of each applicant must be 18.

---

## SECTION 2 – RETIREMENT INTEREST ONLY (RIO): MAXIMUM AGE AT APPLICATION

**No Maximum**

* There is no maximum age at application for RIO mortgages.

---

## SECTION 3 – RETIREMENT INTEREST ONLY (RIO): MAXIMUM AGE AT TERM END

**No Maximum (RIO)**

* Our normal maximum age is 95 years; however on RIO mortgages there is no maximum age at end of term (repayment can be when the borrower dies or goes into care, as with Lifetime Mortgages).

---

## SECTION 4 – RETIREMENT INTEREST ONLY (RIO): MINIMUM AGE AT APPLICATION

**Minimum:**

* 55 (youngest borrower).

**Condition:**

* For the variable discounted rate for term products and the fixed rate for 5 years, the youngest borrower must be aged 55 or over at the time of application.

---

## AGE LIMITS (RESIDENTIAL – STANDARD)

### Maximum Age at Application

* 89 years. The maximum age of each applicant should be no greater than 89 years old; maximum term at this age would be 5 years.

### Maximum Age at End of Mortgage Term

* 95 years for capital and interest repayment. The maximum age based on an 89 year old applying for an interest only mortgage is 94. The actual term for other ages depends on age at application; use the Society’s Maximum Term Calculator.

### Interest Only – Maximum Age at Term End

* 89 years for the oldest applicant. Family Building Society allows interest-only mortgages up until a maximum age of 89 for the oldest applicant (refer to Maximum Mortgage Term Guidelines).

### Maximum Age at Term End for Non-Contributory Applicant

* 89 years. Normal mortgage term maximums apply whether an applicant contributes to the mortgage or not.

### Maximum Age at Term End Without Pension Proof (Max Retirement Age)

* 70 years. The Society will usually only allow a mortgage term to age 70 where no pension income is being assessed.

**Outside policy:**

* For non-manual roles the Society can consider earned income to age 75.

### Maximum Age for Self-Employed Borrowers

* 65 years generally (maximum age for a five year term). Self-employed income is generally taken to the applicant’s age 70 (normal retirement age); the Society can sometimes go beyond this.

**Acceptable**

---

## ADDRESS HISTORY (MIN UK ADDRESS HISTORY NEEDED)

**Requirement:**

* 3 years. The Society requires a three year UK address history.

## ADVISOR OWN APPLICATION

**Acceptable**

* Family Building Society can accept applications from advisors applying for their own mortgage.

---

## SECTION 6 – ARRANGEMENT FEES: EXCEEDING LENDING LTV LIMITS

**No**

* Family Building Society does not allow fees to be added where it would mean the lending loan to value limits will be exceeded.

---

## SECTION 7 – ARRANGEMENT FEES: EXCEEDING PRODUCT LTV LIMITS

**No**

* Family Building Society does not allow fees to be added where it would mean the product loan to value limits will be exceeded.

---

## SECTION 8 – ARRANGEMENT TO PAY (CREDIT FILE)

**Not Acceptable**

* Family Building Society will not consider applicants that have an arrangement to pay registered on their credit file.

**Outside policy:**

* Arrangements to pay that were cleared/satisfied over two years ago can be considered subject to referral to the Society with an up to date credit file.

---

## SECTION 9 – ARREARS (SECURED / MORTGAGE)

**Acceptable (subject to below)**

* Any existing mortgage or secured loan must be up to date at the time of application. A maximum of one month’s arrears within the last 12 months will be allowed, provided the account is currently up to date. An explanation of any arrears will be required.

---

## SECTION 10 – ARREARS (UNSECURED)

**Acceptable (subject to below)**

* Any existing mortgage, rent or loan must be up to date at the time of application. A maximum of one month’s arrears within the last 12 months will be allowed, provided the account is currently up to date. An explanation of any arrears will be required.

---

## SECTION 11 – ASSESSMENT OF ADDITIONAL RESIDENTIAL MORTGAGES

**Monthly Payment**

* Family Building Society will use the monthly payment of any additional residential mortgages when assessing affordability, as opposed to the outstanding balance.

---

## SECTION 12 – BACK TO BACK (REMORTGAGE – BRIDGING)

**Definition:**

* Customer funded the purchase from a bridging loan and now wishes to mortgage the property (ownership less than 6 months).

**Not Acceptable**

* The Society normally expects properties to have been owned at least 6 months.

---

## SECTION 14 – BACK TO BACK (REMORTGAGE – CUSTOMER FUNDED)

**Definition:**

* Customer funded purchase using own funds and now wishes to mortgage within 6 months.

**Requirement:**

* Property must be owned at least 6 months.

**Not Acceptable:**

* Ownership less than 6 months.

---

## SECTION 15 – BACK TO BACK (REMORTGAGE – INHERITED)

**Definition:**

* Inherited property owned for less than 6 months and applicant wishes to remortgage.

**Allowed:**

* Remortgage where inherited property owned less than 6 months.

---

## SECTION 16 – BACK TO BACK (REMORTGAGE – OTHER)

**Definition:**

* Ownership less than 6 months for reasons other than inheritance, porting, bridging repayment, or cash purchase.

**Not Acceptable:**

* Back to back remortgages.

---

## SECTION 17 – BACK TO BACK (REMORTGAGE – PORTED)

**Definition:**

* Customer recently moved and ported mortgage.
* Product expires shortly after move.
* Wishes to remortgage within 6 months of ownership.

**Not Acceptable:**

* Back to back remortgages.

---

## SECTION 18 – BACK TO BACK (SUB SALE PURCHASE – OTHER)

**Definition:**

* Vendor owned property less than 6 months and now selling (excluding part exchange or repossession).

**Requirement:**

* Property must be owned at least 6 months.

---

## SECTION 19 – BACK TO BACK (SUB SALE PURCHASE – PART EXCHANGE)

**Definition:**

* Property acquired via part exchange within last 6 months and now being sold.

**Not Acceptable:**

* Back to back remortgages or purchases in this situation.

---

## SECTION 20 – BACK TO BACK (SUB SALE PURCHASE – REPOSSESSED)

**Definition:**

* Property repossessed by lender within last 6 months and now being sold.

**Requirement:**

* Property expected to have been owned at least 6 months.

---

# SECTION 21 – BENEFIT INCOME (ALL TYPES)

The following benefit income sources are **Not Acceptable** for affordability purposes:

* Adoption Allowance
* Attendance Allowance
* Carers Allowance
* Child Benefit
* Child Tax Credits
* Constant Attendance Allowance
* Disability Living Allowance (DLA)
* Employment and Support Allowance
* Guardian Allowance
* Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit
* Personal Independence Payment (PIP)
* Universal Credit (Family Building Society does not accept universal credit as an acceptable income type.)
* Working Tax Credits

**Condition:**

* These benefit income types are not accepted as sources of income for affordability assessment.

## SECTION 22 – BUY FOR UNIVERSITY (STUDENT MORTGAGES)

**Not Offered**

* Family Building Society will not consider applications on a Buy for Uni Scheme (e.g. students borrowing to buy and rent rooms, with parents as joint borrowers).

---

## SECTION 23 – MORTGAGE OFFER EXTENSION

**Yes**

* The Society can extend a client's mortgage offer beyond the standard expiry.

**Condition:**

* Documentation and the valuation may need to be updated if these items exceed six months old.

# SECTION 24 – CCJs

## 24.1 – CCJs Registered in Last 3 Months

**Not Acceptable**

* Family Building Society will not consider applicants that have had CCJs registered in the past 3 months.

---

## 24.2 – CCJs Registered 3–12 Months

**Not Acceptable**

* Family Building Society will not consider applicants that have had CCJs registered more than 3 months ago and less than 12 months ago.

---

## 24.3 – CCJs Registered 12–24 Months

**Not Acceptable**

* Family Building Society will not consider applicants that have had CCJs registered more than 12 months ago and less than 24 months ago.

**Outside policy:**

* One satisfied County Court Judgement, total value up to £500, within the last three years may be considered, subject to a suitable explanation for the circumstances around the CCJ being registered.

---

## 24.4 – CCJs Registered 24–36 Months

**Not Acceptable**

* Family Building Society will not consider applicants that have had CCJs registered more than 24 months ago and less than 36 months ago.

**Outside policy:**

* One satisfied CCJ for up to £500 may be considered subject to a satisfactory explanation.

---

## 24.5 – CCJs Registered Over 36 Months

**Potentially Ignored**

* Family Building Society can potentially ignore any CCJs that were registered over 36 months ago (subject to overall credit profile).

---

# SECTION 25 – CONCESSIONARY PURCHASES

## 25.1 – Family

**Acceptable**

* The Society can accept the equity being 'gifted' as the deposit for a family concessionary purchase. The family member who is the vendor must vacate the property and not retain a financial interest.

---

## 25.2 – Landlord to Tenant

**Acceptable**

* The Society will lend up to 100% of the purchase price or 80% of the valuation, whichever is the lower.

**Requirement:**

* Insolvency indemnity to be arranged by the solicitors.

---

## 25.3 – Giftor Remaining in Property

**Not Acceptable**

* Family Building Society does not accept concessionary purchase where the giftor will remain an occupant of the property.

---

# SECTION 26 – CONSENT TO LET

## 26.1 – Let a Room (Airbnb or Similar)

**Not Acceptable**

* The Society is unable to accept applications where the borrower resides in the security property and lets a room (e.g. via AirBnB or similar).

---

## 26.2 – Tied Accommodation

**Allowed under Consent to Let**

* The Society may allow residential customers to rent out their property under a consent-to-let arrangement where: the customer is in or moving to tied accommodation linked to employment (e.g. boarding school teacher, vicar/minister, estate worker, army barracks); or the customer is a member of the Armed Forces serving elsewhere in the UK or overseas and the property has previously been used as or is intended to be their main residence in the future.

**Examples:**

* Boarding school teacher, vicar/minister, estate worker, Armed Forces

**Condition:**

* Property intended as future main residence; applicant in tied accommodation linked to employment.

---

# SECTION 27 – CONTRACT REASSIGNMENT

**Not Acceptable**

* The Society does not accept contract reassignment (e.g. purchasing a new build from a third party who has not yet completed).

---

# SECTION 28 – CONTRACTOR INCOME

## 28.1 – Agency Work

**Not Acceptable**

* The Society would not accept income from an agency.

**Exception:**

* If agency income has been in existence for 3 years or more, refer the application for further consideration.

---

## 28.2 – CIS Contractor

**Acceptable**

* The Society can accept income from a CIS contractor; they are regarded as self-employed.

**Condition:**

* Last 2 years SA302s or accounts required.

---

## 28.3 – Fixed Term Contract

**Acceptable**

* Generally acceptable but need to be assessed on a case-by-case basis; refer to the Society for individual assessment.

---

## 28.4 – IT Consultant

**Acceptable**

* Minimum annual income £30,000; two years consistent history of contracting (CV or LinkedIn acceptable).
* Income for 'employed' contractors: daily rate × 5 days × 48 weeks.

---

## 28.5 – Nursing Bank

**Acceptable**

* The Society would expect the applicant to have two years' experience as a bank nurse.

---

## Locum Doctor

**Acceptable**

* Family Building Society can potentially accept Locum Doctor as an income type; refer to your local BDM for further guidance.

---

## 28.6 – Piecework Contract

**Acceptable**

* The Society would accept this income if there is sufficient evidence justifying a consistent level of income over a reasonable period (normally two years).

---

## 28.7 – Probationary Contract

**Not Acceptable**

* Probationary contract is not the same as a probationary period; the latter has its own criteria.

---

## 28.8 – Rolling Contract

**Acceptable**

* Family Building Society can potentially accept income from a rolling contract.

---

## 28.9 – Seasonal Contract

**Not Acceptable**

* The Society does not accept this as a source of income.

---

## 28.10 – Short Term Renewable Contract

**Acceptable**

* If for self-employed contract workers these are acceptable.

---

## 28.11 – Sub Contractor

**Acceptable**

* Sub contractors normally refer to those under the CIS scheme; the Society regards them as self-employed.

---

## 28.12 – Temporary Contract

**Not Acceptable**

* The Society does not accept applications from those on temporary contracts as this income cannot be relied on.

---

## 28.13 – Umbrella Company

**Acceptable**

* The Society can potentially consider contractor income paid via an umbrella company. Income treated similarly to zero hours; look for history of employment in that industry or role and last two years' P60s or SA302s. Deductions for 'employer' National Contributions (where paid by the contractor) and umbrella company charges are taken into account in affordability.

---

## 28.14 – Zero Hours Contract

**Acceptable**

* A track record demonstrating income must be provided covering no less than a 2 year period.

# SECTION 31 – DAY 1 REMORTGAGE

## 31.1 – Inherited Property

**Allowed:**

* Day 1 remortgage permitted on inherited property.
* The Society would consider applications for a day one remortgage on an inherited property if it is to improve the property or buy out other beneficiaries.

**Condition:**

* Must be for property improvement or to buy out other beneficiaries.

---

## 31.2 – Not Inherited Property

**Not Acceptable:**

* Day 1 remortgage where property is not inherited.
* The Society would not accept a day 1 remortgage that is not an inherited property.

# SECTION 32 – DEBT MANAGEMENT PLANS

## 32.1 – Current / Unsatisfied

**Not Acceptable**

---

## 32.2 – Satisfied

**Not Acceptable**

* The Society does not accept applications from applicants who have had a satisfied debt management plan.

**Exception (Referral):**

* May be considered if satisfied at least 3 years ago; refer the application for further consideration.

---

# SECTION 33 – DEBT TREATMENT IN AFFORDABILITY

## 33.1 – Debt Being Repaid Before Completion

**Allowed:**

* If applicants indicate that a credit commitment is to be repaid on or prior to completion, it will be ignored from an affordability perspective.

**Condition:**

* It is standard policy to condition any mortgage offer to stipulate that the commitment be settled if affordability requires it.

---

## 33.2 – Expiring Debt

**Allowed:**

* The Society usually ignores loans which have less than 12 months to run.

**Requirement:**

* These still need to be declared on the application form.

---

## 33.3 – Payday Loans (Current)

**Not Acceptable**

* The Society does not accept applications where any applicant has a payday loan.

---

## 33.4 – Payday Loans (Previous)

**Not Acceptable**

* The Society does not accept applications from applicants who have taken a payday loan.

---

## 33.5 – Debt Relief Order (Satisfied)

**Allowed:**

* Family Building Society can potentially consider an applicant with a satisfied debt relief order as long as it has been satisfied for at least 3 years.

---

# SECTION 34 – DEFAULTS

## 34.1 – Registered in Last 3 Months

**Not Acceptable**

* Family Building Society will not consider applicants that have had defaults registered in the past 3 months.

---

## 34.2 – Registered 3–12 Months

**Not Acceptable**

* Family Building Society will not consider applicants that have had defaults registered more than 3 months ago and less than 12 months ago.

**Exception:**

* Defaults registered by mobile phone providers are ignored up to £100.

---

## 34.3 – Registered 12–24 Months

**Not Acceptable**

* Family Building Society will not consider applicants that have had defaults registered more than 12 months ago and less than 24 months ago.

---

## 34.4 – Registered 24–36 Months

**Potentially Acceptable**

* Family Building Society can potentially consider applicants that have had defaults registered more than 24 months ago and less than 36 months ago.

---

## 34.5 – Registered Over 36 Months

**Potentially Ignored**

* Family Building Society can potentially ignore any satisfied defaults that were registered over 36 months ago (and satisfied at least 24 months ago) subject to a satisfactory explanation.

**Condition:**

* Must be satisfied.
* Must have been satisfied at least 24 months ago.
* Satisfactory explanation required.

---

# SECTION 35 – BANKRUPTCY

## 35.1 – Discharged Bankrupt

**Minimum:**

* Discharged for at least 3 years.

**Conditions:**

* The Society can consider discharged bankrupts as long as they have been discharged at least three years, have no subsequent adverse registered credit and can provide a satisfactory explanation.
* Please refer these for consideration prior to an application being submitted.

# SECTION 38 – EMPLOYMENT INCOME TYPES

## Additional Duty Hours

* 50% accepted where this source of income is regular and can be evidenced in payslips.

---

## Additional Responsibility Allowance

* 100% of this source of income can be taken.

---

## Bonus (Annual / Half-Yearly / Monthly / Quarterly)

* 50% of regular bonus accepted (must be a regular feature of the employment).
* Flexibility to take more than 50% if the employment justifies it; refer cases where more than 50% is required.

---

## Car Allowance

* 100% of this source of income accepted.

---

## Commission (Annual / Half-Yearly)

* 50% of regular commission accepted (must be a regular feature of the employment).
* Flexibility to take more than 50% if the employment justifies it; refer cases where more than 50% is required.
* Where consistently high levels of commission are worked, more than 50% may be considered; refer prior to submission and provide last 2 years P60s in addition to last 3 months payslips.

---

## Commission (Monthly / Quarterly)

* Up to 50% accepted where this is a regular source of income.

---

## Employer Housing Allowance

* 100% accepted where this is a guaranteed source of income.

---

## Employer Mortgage Subsidy

* 100% of this source of income accepted.

---

## Employment Income from Family Business

* Acceptable.
* Employed income from a family business is acceptable subject to confirmation of the income from the external accountant.

---

## Flight Pay

* Acceptable.
* Family Building Society can potentially consider Flight Pay as an acceptable source of income for affordability purposes.

---

## Foreign Currency Income

* Acceptable.
* The Society accepts employed incomes paid in a foreign currency so long as the mortgage application is on an interest-only basis.

---

## Large Town Allowance

* 100% of guaranteed income can be taken.

---

## London Weighting

* 100% of this source of income accepted.
* Evidence must be evident in payslips or an employment reference.

---

## Mileage Allowance

* Not Acceptable.
* Family Building Society does not accept mileage allowance as an acceptable source of income for affordability purposes.

---

## Overtime (Less than Monthly / Weekly / Monthly)

* 50% of regular overtime accepted (must be a regular feature of the employment).
* Flexibility to take more than 50% if the employment justifies it; refer cases where more than 50% is required.
* Where consistently high levels of overtime are worked, more than 50% may be considered; refer prior to submission and provide last 2 years P60s in addition to last 3 months payslips.

---

## Parental / Maternity Leave

* Acceptable.
* Subject to check: applicant has statutory rights to return to work and has a stated intention to do so.
* Confirmation required of ability to service mortgage repayments during leave (e.g. income to be received or evidence of savings).
* Subject to the above proving satisfactory, income may be taken into consideration in the usual way.

---

## Salary (Cash in Hand)

* Acceptable.
* The Society accepts that some jobs may still be paid cash in hand; assessment is more cautious due to the reduced audit trail.

---

## Second Job

* 50% of income from second job accepted.
* Applicant must have been in the second job for at least six months and be permanently employed.

---

## Shift Allowance

* Up to 50% accepted where it is a regular source of income.

---

## Standby / Call Out

* Acceptable.
* Family Building Society can potentially consider Standby / Call Out as an acceptable source of income for affordability purposes.

---

## Supply Teacher

* Acceptable.
* Family Building Society can potentially accept supply teacher income for affordability purposes.

---

## Tronc Payments (Tips)

* Acceptable.
* Family Building Society can potentially accept applicants who receive tronc payments (tips etc) when assessing affordability.

# SECTION 39 – CONTRACTOR POLICY SUMMARY

## CIS Contractor Assessment

* Treated as self-employed.
* Family Building Society will treat as self-employed when assessing affordability of a CIS contractor.

---

## Contractor Minimum Experience

* 24 months.
* Family Building Society has a minimum experience requirement for contractors of 24 months.

---

## Contractor Minimum Income

* £30,000.
* Family Building Society has a minimum income requirement for contractors of £30,000.

---

## Contractor Minimum Time Remaining on Contract

* At least 3 months remaining on current contract required.
* Contact the Society to discuss your enquiry.

---

## Contractor Weekly Multiplier

* Weekly contract rate × 48 may be used (or income via accounts/SA302s).
* Contractor workers who are self-employed (possibly via their own limited company) can be considered using a multiple of weekly contract rate or income via accounts/SA302s.

# SECTION 40 – EMPLOYMENT STATUS

## Employed with Voluntary Breaks

* Not Acceptable if breaks exceed 2 weeks.
* The Society normally only accepts breaks that do not exceed two weeks.

---

## Minimum Length Employed

* No minimum period of employment.
* Three years' employment history requested; the Society would be concerned if employment has changed frequently within that period.
* Probationary period would normally be expected to have been completed before considering a mortgage application.

---

## Minimum Time in Current Job

* No minimum period in current job.
* The Society would normally expect that any probationary period has been completed before an application is submitted.

---

## Pending New Job Salary

* Not Acceptable.
* The Society would not normally consider income from a wholly new job prior to its start.

---

## Pending Pay Rise

* Acceptable.
* Pay rises can be considered providing they can be evidenced and are normally to occur within the next three months.

---

## Probationary Period

* Not Acceptable.
* The Society would normally expect an applicant's probationary period to have been completed prior to submitting an application.

# SECTION 50 – INTEREST ONLY POLICY

## Annual Lump Sum Repayment

* Not Acceptable.
