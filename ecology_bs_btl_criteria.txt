## SECTION 1 – REGULATORY STATUS

- Buy to let mortgages are not regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority.
- Borrowers must be aged 18 or over.
- Mortgage is only available to UK residents.
- Property may be repossessed if repayments are not maintained.

---

## SECTION 2 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

- Minimum age: 18.
- Must be a UK resident.
- Maximum of 3 buy to let mortgages in total, including the one being applied for with Ecology.
- The Society will not lend to an individual with a portfolio of more than 3 buy to let properties, including the one being applied for with Ecology.

---

## SECTION 3 – AFFORDABILITY AND RENTAL COVERAGE

- Rental income must be at least 35% more than the mortgage repayment due.
- Applicants must evidence sufficient personal income to maintain personal expenditure.
- 100% of net rental income is used for affordability.

---

## SECTION 4 – LOAN TO VALUE AND BORROWING

- Maximum LTV: 80% of the property's value or purchase price, whichever is lower.
- Mortgages available on a repayment or interest only basis, subject to eligibility.
- Maximum mortgage term: 25 years, subject to eligibility.

---

## SECTION 5 – INTEREST RATE AND EARLY REPAYMENT

- Current buy to let Variable Rate: 6.79%.
- Early repayment charge may be payable if all or part of the mortgage is repaid within the first 2 years.
- The buy to let Variable Rate applies during works on renovation or self-build projects.
- C-Change discounts are applied from the date evidence of completed works and the required Energy Standard rating is received.

### C-Change Discount Calculation

- Buy to let Variable Rate minus C-Change discount = Variable Mortgage Rate.
- Discount level is based on the Energy Standard rating achieved or qualifying energy measures installed.
- Discount is applied only after evidence of completed works and the required Energy Standard rating (if applicable) is received.

---

## SECTION 6 – FEES

- Non-refundable mortgage application fee: £350.
- Mortgage valuation fee payable in addition to the application fee.

---

## SECTION 7 – ENERGY AND ECOLOGICAL REQUIREMENTS

- Buy to lets requiring a renovation mortgage must improve the Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) by at least 2 grades.
- Property must comply with the Society's ecological and sustainability requirements.

### C-Change Discounts Available

- C-Change sustainable homes.
- C-Change retrofit.
- C-Change energy improvements.

### Evidence Required for C-Change Discounts

For sustainable homes or retrofit:
- Energy Standard certificate.
- Architect or Building Control certificate (if applicable).

For energy improvement discount:
- All invoices for qualifying works.

---

## SECTION 8 – PROPERTY TYPES AND GEOGRAPHIC AVAILABILITY

### Allowed Project Types

- Energy efficient self-build projects.
- Renovations to existing residential properties that improve energy efficiency.
- Barn conversion projects.
- Conversion projects.
- Extensive energy performance improvement projects.

### Stage Payments

- Stage payments released during the project as the property value increases during works.

### Geographic Availability

- England.
- Scotland.
- Wales.
- Northern Ireland.

---

## SECTION 9 – TENANCY REQUIREMENTS

- Letting must be conducted on a 6 or 12 month shorthold assured tenancy agreement.
- Holiday lets: not acceptable.
- Second homes: not acceptable.
- Property cannot be let to a family member.

---

## SECTION 10 – PROPERTY AND SECURITY CRITERIA

### Property Limits

- Property must not have more than 3 self-contained units.
- Flats: maximum 4 storeys for standard residential lending.
- Leasehold flats must have at least 35 years remaining at the end of the mortgage term.

### Acceptable Security Types (subject to ecological requirements)

- Houses.
- Bungalows.
- Flats.
- Shared ownership.

### Unacceptable Security Types

- Freehold flats.
- Holiday homes.
- Second homes.
- Mobile homes.
- Park homes.
- Bedsits.
- Houseboats.
- HMO properties.

### Renovations, Conversions, Self-Build and Shared Ownership

The following are required:
- Full plans.
- Build costs.
- Planning permission (where applicable).

---

## SECTION 11 – INCOME CRITERIA

### Employed Applicants

- Last 3 months' payslips.
- Last 6 months' payslips where variable income such as commission or overtime is being used.

### Self-Employed Applicants

- Last 3 years' tax calculations.
- Last 3 years' accounts.
- If the most recent year's figures are lower than the average, the average will be used.

### Unacceptable Income Types

- Crypto income.
- Foreign income.
- Trust income.
- Retained profits.
- Gifted income.
- Cash-paid income.

---

## SECTION 12 – CREDIT HISTORY

- For LTV below 80%: no more than 1 missed secured loan payment within the last 2 years.
- For LTV above 90%: stricter credit performance requirements apply.
- Not acceptable: applicant has previously had a property repossessed.

### Gambling

- Major gambling expenditure assessed on a case-by-case basis.

### Overdraft

- Overdraft misuse may lead to an assumed commitment of 3% of the highest overdraft level over the last 3 months.

---

## SECTION 13 – STANDARD OF SERVICE

- Where a mortgage is not regulated by the FCA, full information about available mortgage products will be provided so the borrower can make an informed decision.
