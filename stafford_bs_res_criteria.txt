## Overview

The Stafford Building Society offers specialist residential mortgage products designed for borrowers who may not fit standard lending criteria. The Society provides flexible lending solutions with an affordability-based approach, without income multiple constraints.

### Key Features

* **No income multiple cap** - Affordability-based underwriting without income multiple restrictions
* **Mixed use properties** - Up to 60% commercial usage accepted
* **No limit on maximum acreage**
* **4 applicants and 4 incomes** - Including Joint Borrower Sole Proprietor (JBSP)
* **Undrawn SIPP and Investment income** - Can be used towards affordability
* **Occupancy restrictions** - Agricultural Ties, section 106's and overage all considered
* **Properties in need of refurbishment** - Accepted
* **Annexes and outbuildings** - For own use or let out
* **Earned income to age 75**
* **Interest only** - With downsizing to 70% LTV
* **Manual DIPs** - Contact us with any enquiries

## Income & Affordability

### Affordability Assessment

* **No income multiple cap** - Affordability-based underwriting without income multiple restrictions
* **Affordability calculator** - Available on the Society's website for estimates
* **All loans subject to affordability assessments** - Including full credit reference check to verify declared commitments
* **Affordability model** - Without income multiple constraints
* **Committed expenditure** - All regular monthly costs, credit card balances, secured loans, hire purchase agreements, and other commitments are considered in affordability calculations

### Acceptable Income Sources

**Employed Income:**
* **100% of basic salary**
* **Up to 100% of additional pay** (providing a track record can be evidenced in P60 / payslips)
* An employment reference may be requested
* Earned income accepted up to age 75

**Second Job:**
* Subject to a minimum of 1 year's service
* Up to a maximum of 50 hours per week for both jobs

**NHS Bank Work:**
* Accepted subject to a minimum of 6 months' history
* Capped at 50 hours per week for all employment

**Probationary Period:**
* The probationary period does not need to have been completed if new employment is within the same industry or sector

**Pending Pay Rise:**
* Acceptable subject to written confirmation of the pay rise from the employer (refer if the increase is not due within three months)

**Maternity/Paternity Leave:**
* Require latest payslip prior to leave to confirm salary
* Reference from employer confirming return to work date and whether there is any change to working hours
* Confirmation of childcare costs
* Self-employed applicant(s) are by referral and an accountant's reference may be requested

**Mortgage Subsidy:**
* **100%** - Subject to written confirmation from the employer

**Agency Worker:**
* Specific criteria apply (refer to full criteria document for details)

**Self-Employed Income:**
* Self-employed income accepted
* Professionals such as doctors and dentists may be considered with one year of accounts
* Specific documentation requirements apply (typically 2-3 years of accounts, SA302s, tax year overviews)
* Business bank statements may be required

**Pension Income:**
* Pension income accepted
* Proof of pension income required post age 75 or declared retirement age, whichever is sooner

**Investment Income:**
* **Undrawn SIPP income** - Can be used towards affordability
* **Investment income** - Can be used towards affordability

**Other Income Sources:**
* Various income sources considered
* Specific documentation requirements apply based on income type
* Benefits, maintenance, rental income from other properties may be considered

## Credit History

### Acceptable Credit History

* **Missed payments:** No more than status 2 within the last 2 years (including mortgage payments)
* **Payment/Debt Management Plans:** Discharged and cleared over 12 months ago
* **Recent DMPs:** Cases within the last 12 months may be considered at underwriter discretion

### Not Acceptable

* **Unsatisfied CCJs or defaults:** Not acceptable, regardless of amount
* **Satisfied CCJs or defaults:** Not acceptable if within the last 3 years totaling more than £500
* **Individual Voluntary Arrangements (IVAs):** Not acceptable if active within the last 3 years
* **Bankruptcy orders:** Not acceptable if active within the last 3 years
* **Property repossession:** Evidence of previous or current property repossession not acceptable

## Deposit & Equity

### Acceptable Deposit Sources

* **Savings and investments** - Own resources
* **Gifted deposits** - From blood relatives, step-parents, children, spouse, guardian, or partner
  * Evidence required of deposit in donor's UK bank account
  * Gifted Deposit Form required
  * Not acceptable on Right to Buy applications
* **Builder's deposit** - New build only (subject to confirmation by valuer)
* **Builder's incentives** - New build only (if greater than 5% of purchase price/valuation, must be deducted when calculating maximum loan)
* **Inheritance** - That has already been received by the applicant or funds already held by the solicitor
* **Equity from sale of another property** - Acceptable
* **Deposit raised from another property owned** - Acceptable
* **Overseas deposits** - Can be considered on an individual basis (funds must be paid into applicant's UK bank and evidenced for at least 28 days)

### Minimum Deposit

* **Minimum deposit:** 5% from own resources (excluding Right to Buy and gifted deposit)
* **Subject to MIG approval** - Where personal contributions are less than 5%, LTV will be restricted to 80%

## Product Types

### Residential Mortgages

**2-Year Discounted Variable Rate Mortgages:**
* **90% LTV:** Initial variable rate of 5.34% for two years, followed by Standard Variable Rate (SVR) of 5.50%
  * Assessed based on borrower's ability to meet repayments without income multiple restrictions
* **80% LTV Options:**
  * 0.35% discount off SVR - Initial rate of 5.00% for two years
  * 0.15% discount off SVR - Initial rate of 5.20% for two years

### Joint Borrower Sole Proprietor (JBSP)

* **0.15% discount off SVR for the term**
* Current variable rate: 5.20%
* Allows multiple borrowers to support a single proprietor
* 4 applicants and 4 incomes can be considered

### Shared Ownership

* **0.55% discount off SVR for the term**
* Current variable rate: 4.95%
* Designed for individuals purchasing a share in a property, typically in partnership with a housing association

### Residential Mixed-Use Mortgage

* **Variable rate:** 5.59% for the term
* Suitable for properties that combine residential and commercial use
* Up to 60% of the available floor space can be used for commercial purposes
