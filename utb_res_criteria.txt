## SECTION 1 – RESIDENTIAL FIRST CHARGE – LOAN PARAMETERS

- Maximum LTV: 95%.
- Maximum loan: £2,000,000.
- Maximum age at end of term: 85 years.
- Maximum LTI: 6x up to 85% LTV.
- Maximum DTI: 50%.
- Loan term: up to 40 years (including part years).
- No ERC options available.
- New build and off-plan purchases accepted (up to 85% LTV).
- No debt consolidation LTV restrictions (excluding interest only).
- Debt consolidation: limited to £25,000 and only for home improvements completed in the last 6 months.
- Product transfer options available for all products; eligible existing customers offered within 90 days of expiry of current fixed rate.
- Lending available in England, Wales and Mainland Scotland.
- Minimum age: 18 years old.

---

## SECTION 2 – SECOND CHARGE – LOAN PARAMETERS

- Maximum LTV: 90%.
- Maximum loan: £1,000,000 up to 80% LTV.
- Maximum LTI: 6x up to 80% LTV.
- Maximum term: 30 years.
- No ERC options available.
- No lender fee on loans up to £40,000 net loan.
- Fixed rates and Bank of England lifetime trackers available.
- Overpayments: unlimited outside of fixed rate period; up to 10% per annum of existing mortgage balance within fixed rate or ERC product.
- Allows 1 missed mortgage payment within 12 months (applicable product range).
- Binding Offers can be issued with Special Conditions for non-affordability linked requirements (e.g. first mortgage consent to second charge).
- Lending available in England, Wales and Mainland Scotland (excludes BTL).
- Minimum age: 18 years old.

> All residential mortgage criteria apply to second charge products, with the exception of interest only, 50% DTI and £2,000,000 maximum loan.

## SECTION 3 – PROPERTY REQUIREMENTS

- Minimum mortgage history: 12 months.
- Properties must be located in England, Wales or Mainland Scotland.

### Minimum Property Values

- Properties valued under £90,000: not accepted at LTV below 85%.
- Properties under £125,000: not accepted at LTV above 85% (first charge).
- Properties under £150,000: not accepted at LTV above 85% (second charge).
- Minimum security property value (interest only): £100,000.

### Property Exclusions

- Commercial properties.
- Right to Buy within 4 years of purchase.
- Agricultural properties.

### Accepted Property Types

- Non-standard construction: timber frame, steel frame and concrete accepted with no LTV restrictions.
- High-rise properties and flats above retail: accepted with no LTV restrictions.

### Buildings Insurance

- No evidence required.

### Borrower Legal Representation

- Remortgage and unencumbered secured loans: solicitor representation not required.
- Purchases: minimum of two solicitors in practice required.
- Customer may choose own solicitor for purchase, but the firm must have a minimum of 2 partners.
- Indemnity insurance required for gifted equity.

## SECTION 4 – VALUATION REQUIREMENTS

### First Charge – Purchase

- Full valuation required.

### First Charge – Remortgage

- Hometrack AVM: up to 75% LTV, £250,000 gross loan, confidence score ≥5.0, maximum property value £1,000,000.
- Drive-by valuation: up to 75% LTV, £500,000 gross loan.
- Internal mortgage valuation required for all loans above 85% LTV.
- Specialist reports required if property has not been purchased or remortgaged in the last 5 years.

### First Charge – Unencumbered

- Hometrack AVM: up to 30% LTV, £50,000 gross loan, confidence score ≥5.0, maximum property value £1,000,000.
- Drive-by valuation: up to 75% LTV, £100,000 gross loan.
- Internal mortgage valuation: up to maximum LTV and gross loan.

### Second Charge – Hometrack AVM

Maximum £150,000 gross loan:
- Up to 85% LTV: minimum confidence score 6.5.
- Up to 80% LTV: minimum confidence score 6.0.
- Up to 75% LTV: minimum confidence score 5.0.
- Up to 60% LTV: minimum confidence score 4.0.
- Up to 40% LTV: minimum confidence score 3.0.

Maximum £250,000 gross loan:
- Up to 75% LTV: minimum confidence score 5.0.

Maximum property value: £1,000,000.

Properties above £1,000,000:
- Up to 75% LTV, maximum £150,000 loan, minimum confidence score 6.0.

### Second Charge – Drive-By Valuation

- Up to 75% LTV: maximum £50,000 gross loan.
- Up to 65% LTV: maximum £100,000 gross loan.

### Second Charge – Internal Valuation

- Allowed up to maximum LTV and gross loan.
- Specialist reports not required if property has been purchased or remortgaged in the last 5 years.

## SECTION 5 – ID AND RESIDENCY

- Documentary proof of ID required for all applications (usually via UTB Chat Hub).
- If digital ID cannot be provided, applicants must take Independent Legal Advice from a pre-approved firm with at least 2 partners.

---

## SECTION 6 – LOAN PURPOSE

- Available for purchase or remortgage only (not available to first-time buyers).
- Capital raise allowed for most legal purposes, including some business purposes, excluding bankruptcy avoidance.
- Debt consolidation: limited to £25,000 and only for home improvements completed in the last 6 months.
- On consolidation applications, all credit card, mail order and store card funds are sent to the client to pay rather than UTB sending cheques or electronic transfers.

---

## SECTION 7 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

- Minimum age: 18 (25 for interest only).
- Maximum age: 85 at end of term.
- Married and sole applications accepted.
- Mortgage prisoners: considered.

---

## SECTION 8 – INCOME AND AFFORDABILITY

### Minimum Income

- First charge at LTV ≤85%: £15,000.
- First charge at LTV >85%: £40,000.
- Second charge: £15,000 for all applications.

### Acceptable Income Sources

- Employed: 6 months in current role, or 3 months if 12 months' continuous employment and not in probation.
- 100% of guaranteed income (basic pay, car allowance, shift allowance).
- Regular income (bonus, overtime, commission): 75% with last 2 payslips and latest P60.
  - Bonus ≥£10,000: 75% of average over last 2 years.
  - Bonus <£10,000: 50% of average over last 2 years.
- Up to 75% of annual bonus, regular bonus, commission and overtime considered with 2 years' history.
- Self-employed (sole traders and partnerships): 100% of net profit.
- Limited companies: proportionate percentage of latest year's net profit (based on shareholding) plus salary. Retained profits and director loans not accepted. Projections considered on referral.
- Contractors: daily rate × 5 × 48 weeks minus all deductions at source.
- Zero hours contracts: considered with 12 months' history.
- Pension: 100% of regular income.
- Family and child benefits: 100%, provided total income ≥£25,000.
- Multiple income sources allowed: child benefits, foster income, maintenance, second jobs and pension.
- Unacceptable: any other benefits or income not in GBP.

### Affordability Assessment

- Must be assessed on the UTB portal.
- Assessment must be sustainable and evidenced for the entire loan term.
- Debt consolidation (status 1 and 2) must be ≤1.5x total gross income.

---

## SECTION 9 – PROOF OF INCOME

- Employed: last 2 payslips (computerised), or 2 manual payslips plus P60/bank statement.
- Self-employed (sole trader/partnership): last 2 years' Tax Calculations and corresponding Tax Year Overviews, plus latest business bank statement (TYO not required if 100% submitted).
- Limited company: accountant's certificate OR last 2 years' accounts, latest Tax Calculation and TYO, plus latest business bank statement.
- Pension: last pension statement plus latest bank statement.
- Maintenance, benefits, tax credits: Court order/CSA documentation or entitlement letter plus bank statement.
- Trust income: Trust Deed plus latest bank statement.

---

## SECTION 10 – INTEREST ONLY

- Affordability assessed on the interest-only monthly repayment.
- An Interest Only Declaration Form must be signed, confirming repayment vehicle(s).
- Minimum age: 25.
- Minimum income: £50,000.
- Minimum security property value: £100,000.

### LTV Limits

- Maximum 70% LTV for downsize repayment vehicle.
- Maximum 75% LTV for all other repayment vehicles.

### Repayment Vehicles

Multiple repayment vehicles are allowed:
- Downsize: minimum equity £250,000 (London) or £150,000 (nationally).
- Sale of investment property: 85% of net equity.
- Cash savings: 90% of current value.
- UK ISA: 90% of current value.
- Pension: 100% of tax-free PCLS amount.
- Endowment: 75% of projected lump sum.
- Unit Trusts, OEICs, or investment bonds: 75% of current portfolio value.

---

## SECTION 11 – DEPOSIT CRITERIA

- Equity from an existing property.
- Personal savings (must show build-up over time).
- ISA in customer's name.
- Gifted deposit from blood relatives or step-relatives.
- Gifted equity with Stamp Duty Land Tax paid at full open market value.

---

## SECTION 12 – CREDIT HISTORY

- Mail order and communications arrears and defaults: ignored.
- CCJs and defaults under £300: ignored.
- Missed mortgage payments made within 30 days of due date: ignored.
- IVA: 1 year discharged accepted.
- Product range available allowing 1 missed mortgage payment within 12 months.
- Prime Plus and Prime product ranges available where unsecured arrears have been brought up to date and evidenced.

### Status Definitions

**Prime Plus and Prime (LTV ≤85%):**
- Unsecured: must be currently up to date.
- Secured loans and mortgages: no arrears in last 3 months, none in last 12 months, no outstanding arrears.
- CCJs: none in last 24 months (under £300 may be ignored).
- Defaults: none in last 24 months (under £300 may be ignored).

**Prime Plus and Prime (LTV >85%):**
- Unsecured: must be currently up to date.
- Secured loans and mortgages: no arrears in last 3 months, none in last 12 months, no outstanding arrears.
- CCJs: none in last 36 months (under £300 may be ignored).
- Defaults: none in last 36 months (under £300 may be ignored).

**Near Prime (LTV ≤85%):**
- Unsecured: can be ignored if debts are being consolidated.
- Secured loans and mortgages: no arrears in last 3 months, maximum 1 arrear in last 12 months, no more than 1 outstanding arrear.
- CCJs: maximum 1 in last 24 months (under £300 may be ignored).
- Defaults: maximum 1 in last 24 months (under £300 may be ignored).

**Additional notes (all plans):**
- Mail order and communications debts are ignored.
- Previous conduct will still affect the applicant's credit score.
