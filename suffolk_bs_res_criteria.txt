# Suffolk Building Society – Residential Mortgage Criteria

---

**LENDER:** Suffolk Building Society
**PRODUCT:** Residential Mortgages
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Lending Criteria
**LAST UPDATED:** 2025
**FOR INTERMEDIARY USE ONLY**

**Source:** https://www.suffolkforintermediaries.co.uk/lending-criteria/?type=residential

---

## SECTION 1 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

- Minimum age: 18 at application.
- Maximum age: no maximum at application or at term end; applicants 75+ at application must obtain Independent Legal Advice (ILA).
- Standard application: up to 2 incomes accepted.
- Credit reference agency: TransUnion.
- No credit scoring used.
- Applicants not on voter's roll accepted; proof of address required.

### Joint Borrower Sole Proprietor (JBSP)

- Accepted for UK and Expat.
- UK: maximum 4 applicants / 4 incomes.
- Expat: maximum 2 applicants / 2 incomes.
- Residential LTV: up to 90%, with restrictions — 70% max if all applicants retired; 80% max if term takes youngest into retirement; 90% max if term does not take youngest into retirement. If >70% LTV, max age of any applicant at end of term is 90.
- Joint borrower(s) should be close family relative(s).
- Any applicant may provide the deposit (including non-proprietor). If non-proprietor gifts the deposit they cannot reside at the property.
- Fee-assisted legals not available for JBSP.

### Married Applicants in Sole Names

- Accepted where partner will also reside.
- Not available if partner omitted due to adverse credit.

### Non-Dependent Applicants

- Ignored for affordability if they have own income to self-support.
- Committed debts still included.
- Personal borrowing for self-employed business ignored if not in client's sole name.

### CIFAS and Criminal Convictions

- Applicants on the CIFAS register can be considered — refer cases.
- Criminal convictions: must be spent and not monetary-related (e.g. no fraud, money laundering); applicant must be able to obtain home insurance on standard terms.

## SECTION 2 – CREDIT HISTORY

- Arrears: historic arrears considered; no arrears in the last 3 years.
- Missed payments: none in the last 12 months.
- CCJs: ignored if satisfied for more than 12 months. Refer to Business Development Team if more than 1 CCJ, or CCJs over £1,000 up to 80% LTV / over £500 above 80% LTV.
- Defaults: ignored if satisfied more than 12 months ago. Refer to helpdesk if more than 1 default, or defaults over £1,000 up to 80% LTV / over £500 above 80% LTV.
- Arrangements (debt management / similar): maximum 75% LTV; must have been satisfied 3 years.
- Debt Management Plans: must have been satisfied 3 years; maximum 75% LTV.
- Debt Relief Order (DRO): up to 75% LTV; must have been satisfied 3 years.
- IVA: up to 75% LTV; must have been satisfied 3 years.
- Debts being repaid: ignored.
- Debts with less than 6 months remaining: ignored.
- Repossessions: acceptable up to 75% LTV if more than 3 years ago and any shortfall repaid in full by the applicant.
- Payday loans: refer to BDM with frequency, value and time period.

## SECTION 3 – INCOME AND AFFORDABILITY

### Age and Income Limits

- Earned income (manual employment): acceptable up to age 70.
- Earned income (non-manual employment): acceptable up to age 75.
- Self-employed income: acceptable up to age 75.
- Using pension assets: from age 55, pension assets can be used in the background.
- Minimum income: none on standard; £20,000 if already in retirement (single or joint).
- No formal DTI; recommend referral to BDM where debts outweigh income.

### Debt to Income and Deductions

- Background residential mortgages: include monthly payment and running costs in affordability.
- Ignored (not deducted): voluntary pension contributions, charitable donations, company pension payments, sharesave scheme.
- Deducted: childcare vouchers, maintenance payments, school fees, private healthcare, student loans.

### Income Multiples (LTI)

- Standard: 4.49× (single or joint).
- Enhanced to 5.49× (single or joint) if one applicant earns more than £75,000.
- Enhanced to 5.49× also where 12 months' rent can evidence payments at or above (or within 10% of) the new mortgage payment.

### Employed Income

- Manual roles: income taken to age 70 or retirement, whichever earlier.
- Non-manual roles: income taken to age 75 or retirement, whichever earlier.
- 50% accepted: overtime, commission, bonus; second job (with 6 months' track record).
- 100% accepted for allowances: employer's housing allowance, mortgage subsidy, large town/London weighting, car allowance. Shift allowance and bank income at 100% if 18-month track record. If guaranteed, 100% can be taken for the income types noted.
- Employed by family: acceptable; income must be verified by a qualified accountant.
- Maternity/Parental leave: acceptable if employer confirms return date and salary in writing.
- New employment: if less than 6 months in current job, must have 12 months' continuous employment history (else contact BDM). Can accept new salary if employer confirms and at least 1 payslip can be provided prior to offer.
- Probation period: can consider if probation ends before completion and prior job was in same line of work.

### Contractors and Fixed-Term Contracts

- Contractors: 12 months' track record in same line of work. If less than 3 months remaining on current contract, need 2 years' contract history. Income: day rate × 5 days × 46 weeks per annum. No minimum income for contractors. CIS contractors treated as self-employed (tax returns/accounts required). Umbrella companies calculated same as basic contractors.
- Fixed-term contracts: accepted; need 2 years' history in same line of contract work and at least 3 months remaining on current contract. If less than 2 years contracting history, current contract must have at least 12 months remaining.

### Self-Employed and Sub-Contractors

- Self-employed income taken to age 75 or retirement (whichever first).
- 25% or more shareholding: classed as self-employed.
- Ltd company owners: use higher of (net profit after tax + salary) or (salary + dividends).
- Evidence: Ltd Co directors — 2 years tax returns and accounts; sole trader — 2 years tax returns; LLP — 2 years tax returns and accounts (if available). Projections considered case-by-case — contact BDM.
- Year-on-year: increase less than 20% — use most recent year; increase more than 20% — use average of last 2 years; decline — use latest year (referral recommended).
- Sub-contractors: accepted at 100%; treated as self-employed.

### Zero-Hours and Other

- Zero-hours contracts: accepted if applicant has been in the role for more than 18 months.
- Sports professionals: assessed individually — contact BDM.
- Foster income: considered if 3 years' fostering and not sole income — contact BDM.
- Seafarers: considered — contact BDM.

## SECTION 4 – INCOME TYPES

- Benefits (100%): Child Benefit, Child Tax Credits, Working Tax Credits — must be payable for next 5 years minimum.
- Benefits (50%): Attendance Allowance, Constant Attendance Allowance, Disability Living Allowance (applicants only), Employment & Support Allowance, Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit, PIP, Universal Credit — must be payable for life.
- Maintenance: 100% accepted; must be court-ordered or show 12 months' receipt history.
- Pension income: 100% accepted — annuity, company/private, war disablement, war widower, private, state pension. Drawdown: 100% of drawdown (must last to end of term); uncrystallised funds: 80% of pension fund ÷ mortgage term. SIPPs: 100% of drawn amount (must last to end of term); uncrystallised same (80% ÷ term).
- Rental income: accepted; use tax calculation profit (average of latest 2 years); profit net of finance costs.
- Investment income: use 75%.
- Foreign currency income: accepted; convert to GBP and apply 20% haircut, then standard affordability. Permitted currencies (residential/regulated BTL): GBP, EUR, CHF, NOK, USD, CAD, SGD, HKD, AED, KWD, QAR, AUD, NZD, DKK, SEK, SAR. If Expat with foreign income, Expat product applies.

---

## SECTION 5 – NATIONALITY AND RESIDENCY

- Foreign nationals with ILR: accepted on standard criteria.
- EU/EEA/Swiss with pre-settled status (no ILR): maximum 80% LTV; evidence pre-settled status; 12 months UK residence; work permit/visa 12 months remaining; permitted sectors: Education, Healthcare, Finance & Banking, IT, Legal, Marketing Professionals, Science R&D, Energy Sector, Construction Industry Professionals (not trades) e.g. Architects.
- Foreign nationals without ILR: maximum 80% LTV; same pre-settled evidence and sector requirements.
- Expats: residential LTV up to 90%; residential and regulated BTL accepted. Currencies: GBP, EUR, CHF, NOK, USD, CAD, SGD, HKD, AED, KWD, QAR, AUD, NZD, DKK, SEK, SAR. Foreign currency income: convert to GBP, 20% haircut, then deduct tax/NI. Minimum income £40,000 (or equivalent). Non-UK national can be included if case fits policy and affordability using UK national's income and details. Not acceptable: sanctioned or war-torn countries; self-employed Expats. Returning Expats: can be accepted — refer to BDM.
- Visas: Family/Spousal — can be considered (referral recommended). Tier 1 working visas not accepted. Skilled Worker Visa (formerly Tier 2): accepted; must have 12 months remaining at application.
- Gifted deposits not acceptable on Expat or Foreign National applications.

---

## SECTION 6 – LOAN PARAMETERS

- Maximum loan: per individual product (see product sheets).
- Minimum loan: £75,000.
- Minimum term: 6 years.
- Maximum term: 40 years.
- Arrangement fees / CHAPS fees: can be added to loan or paid upfront; fees may be added above LTV limits.
- Overpayments: fee-free up to 50% of original loan (subject to product).
- Portability: portable as standard (subject to product).
- Offers: valid for 6 months; extensions possible; may require no-change confirmation or updated affordability — contact BDM.
- Length of time property owned: must have been owned for 6 months before remortgage/additional borrowing.
- Lending areas: Residential UK and Residential Expat accepted; Self Build accepted; Shared Ownership temporarily withdrawn.

---

## SECTION 7 – DEPOSITS

- Gifted deposit: accepted up to 100%; gift must be from close family (parent, grandparent, son/daughter, brother/sister). If only 5% is gifted, client must add 5% themselves or have 12 months' rental history. Not acceptable on Expat or Foreign National applications.
- Builder/vendor deposits: maximum 5%; applicant must also contribute 5% deposit.
- Concessionary purchase (transaction under value): treated as purchase under value; deposit required in line with product (e.g. 90% LTV scenario still needs the balance deposit).
- Sale of property or capital raise against applicant's limited company: accepted; Ltd Co must be SPV; all applicants must be named directors and shareholders; deposit must be from drawings, not director's loan.
- Forces Help to Buy: accepted.
- Loan repayable on sale of property (divorce cases): acceptable — contact BDM.
- Raising funds on another property: acceptable (included in affordability).

## SECTION 8 – PROPERTY CRITERIA

- Lending area: England and Wales.
- Age-restricted properties: not accepted.
- Ex-local authority houses: accepted.
- Ex-local authority flats: not accepted (unless Right to Buy).
- Section 106: can consider — dependent on wording and LTV; contact BDM.
- Annexes: accepted if no separate utilities (separate council tax allowed); up to 80% LTV; not acceptable on BTL. Annex used as short-term holiday let: acceptable (no formal AST).
- Leasehold houses: new build minimum 250 years; non-new build minimum 85 years.
- Wimpey No-Fines: by referral, considered up to 80% LTV case-by-case.
- Close to commercial: potentially acceptable — contact BDM with details.

### Flats (England & Wales)

- New build flats: up to 90% LTV.
- Standard flats: up to 95% LTV.
- No management company: acceptable if 4 or fewer units and all flats hold share of freehold.
- Lease: 85 years or more remaining at start; new build flats 125-year lease required.
- Ground rent: £250 or less (£1,000 or less if in London).
- Ex-local authority flats: Right to Buy only.
- Storeys: max 10; no lift — max 5 storeys.
- Absentee freeholder: not accepted.
- Basement flats: refer to BDM.
- Above commercial: accepted subject to type/location — referral recommended.

### New Build

- New build flats: up to 90% LTV (England & Wales).
- New build houses: up to 95% LTV.
- Acceptable warranties: NHBC, Premier, Building Life Plans, Build-Zone (incl. BuildCare), Checkmate (Castle 10), ICW, LABC, and Professional Consultants Certificate from listed professional bodies (RICS FRICS/MRICS, IStructE F/M, CIOB F/M, A/SI F/M, ABE F/M, CIAT, ARB-registered architect, ICE F/M). Conveyancer must obtain proof of PI insurance; cover 6 years or more.

### Other Security Points

- Splitting of title on completion: considered.
- EWS1: required where applicable; no combustible cladding/materials on balconies without EWS1. A1, A2, B1 acceptable subject to valuer. Max storeys: 10.
- Overage clause: can be accepted — contact BDM.
- Solar panels: accepted; if leased, must include lender repossession break clause.
- Unadopted roads: require full access and maintenance agreement.
- Flying freehold: max 25%, subject to standard-terms insurance — refer to BDM.
- Listed properties (Grade I / II / II*): acceptable.
- Maximum acreage: 10 acres.

## SECTION 9 – REPAYMENT METHODS

- Capital & Interest (Repayment): accepted.
- Part & Part: accepted.
- Interest-Only: accepted.
- Acceptable repayment vehicles: Endowment/ISA, pension lump sum, sale of security/downsizing (downsizing requires £250,000 or more equity on day 1 and max 70% LTV on IO element; can top up to 80% with repayment/part & part), sale of background property, other investments.
- Unacceptable vehicle: annual lump sum repayments.
- Interest-only LTV caps: 70% if standard/going into retirement (may be topped up with 10% repayment to 80%).
- Minimum income for IO: no minimum as standard.

## SECTION 10 – SPECIAL PRODUCTS AND REMORTGAGE PURPOSE

- Help to Buy: not accepted.
- Let-to-Buy: accepted.
- Lodgers: accepted (no AST; no independent utilities; max 1 lodger). Lodger income not used for affordability.
- Right to Buy: accepted. All applicants must be on RTB paperwork. Capital raising not accepted. Can lend up to 100% of discounted purchase price provided LTV on valuation 80% or less. Joint RTB where only one resides: accepted; max 80% LTV; subject to affordability (including running costs of RTB property and any other residence).
- Second homes / holiday homes: accepted up to 80% LTV; acceptable for dependant family members.
- Self Build: accepted up to 80% LTV (70% for larger loans). Acceptable construction: ICF, MMC, Modular/POD, No-Fines, off-site manufactured (MMC), Potton Homes, repaired PRC, steel frame, SIPs, thatched roof, modern timber framed (not with timber cladding), timber framed 1900–1970, post-1970 (with masonry skin), pre-1900. BOPAS accreditation required for non-standard construction. Not habitable properties can go through Self Build range. Renovations may be Self Build or standard — contact BDM. Shared access and multiplot acceptable (multiplot if builder owns less than 25% of site and 10 or fewer properties/sites). Fee-assisted legals not available for Self Build.
- Power of Attorney: acceptable on remortgages/like-for-like. Not acceptable: POA converting to BTL or purchasing into their own name a property they have powers over.

### Remortgage / Additional Borrowing – Purposes

- Home improvements: up to 90% LTV.
- Debt consolidation: up to 90% LTV.
- Gifting: accepted (£100,000 or over — refer).
- Second home (UK only): up to 80% LTV.
- Purchase of BTL or Holiday Let: up to 90% LTV (80% or less: no simultaneous completion; above 80%: simultaneous completion required).
- Purchase property in Ltd Co: must be SPV; all applicants named directors and shareholders.
- Repay Help to Buy: up to 95% LTV if sole reason for capital raise.
- Shared Ownership staircasing: accepted (subject to availability of SO products).
- Extend a lease: accepted.
- Medical expenses: referral recommended.
- Purchase of land: accepted.
- School fees: accepted.
