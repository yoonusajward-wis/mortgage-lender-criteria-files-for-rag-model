# Adverse Credit Criteria

## No Adverse

* CCJs: Must be satisfied at time of application, 0 in 72 months where >=£300
* Defaults: Must be settled at time of application, 0 in 72 months
* Secured missed payments: 0 in 36 months
* Unsecured worst status: 0 in 6 months

## Light Adverse

* CCJs: Must be satisfied at time of application, 0 in 24 months where >=£300
* Defaults: Must be settled at time of application, 0 in 12 months, 1 in 24 months
* Secured missed payments: 0 in 36 months
* Unsecured worst status: 1 in 6 months

## Light Adverse+

* CCJs: Must be satisfied at time of application, 0 in 12 months, 1 in 24 months (Max £3000) where >=£300
* Defaults: Must be settled at time of application, 0 in 24 months
* Secured missed payments: 0 in 12 months, 1 in 36 months
* Unsecured worst status: 2 in 6 months

# Concrete Construction

The following are unacceptable:

* Properties designated defective under Part XVI Housing Act 1985
* Pre-Cast Reinforced Concrete (PRC) where the property and the properties either side have not been repaired under a scheme licensed by PRC Homes Ltd
* Properties with mundic concrete designated as Class B or Class C

# Consumer Buy to Let (CBTL) Lending

* Applications classed as consumer buy to let (CBTL) cannot be considered

# Convictions

* An application cannot be considered until a criminal conviction is spent

Details of when convictions are classed as being spent can be found here:

* [https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rehabilitation-periods](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rehabilitation-periods)

# Energy Efficiency Regulations

In relation to the Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards as defined in The Energy Efficiency (Private Rented Property) (England and Wales) Regulations 2015 and all legislation and regulations in force and as amended from time to time in respect of the security property.

* It is the responsibility of the Solicitor to ensure the Borrower is aware there is a requirement, and will a condition of the mortgage, for any properties rented out in the private rented sector (including existing lettings, new lets and renewals) to normally achieve the minimum energy performance rating (an EPC) set out in the relevant applicable regulations in force

# Ex-Local Authority Flats

For ex-local authority flats, the following are unacceptable:

* Properties within Right to Buy pre-emption period
* Flats where there isn't an active and transactional market within the block/development

# Exposure Limits

* The limits below do not apply where lending on multiple units on a single freehold
* Where the number of units within a block / development is <= 8 no maximum unit percentage is applied
* Above 8 units the maximum exposure is set at 20%

# First-Time Landlords Including HMOs

* Lending is not acceptable if no applicant is an existing homeowner (residential owner occupied and/or buy to let)

# Flats

## Acceptable

* Self-contained flats/maisonettes/apartments with private facilities and direct access to the highway via common parts
* Blocks of flats where units are not self-contained or otherwise individually mortgageable on a block investment basis only

Where the security is situated above a commercial premises and/or it is a New Build it can only proceed subject to no adverse comments from the valuer/conveyancer.

## For Blocks of Flats

* Must be readily saleable and mortgageable
* Cannot be constructed of Large Panel Systems (LPS Blocks)

Individual Studio flats below 30 square meters gross internal area are not acceptable.

## For Ex-Local Authority Flats

* Properties within Right to Buy pre-emption period are unacceptable
* Flats where there isn't an active and transactional market within the block/development are unacceptable

# Flying Freehold

* Flying freeholds can be considered provided the percentage thereof does not exceed 20% of the total floor area

# HMOs (House in Multiple Occupation)

* House in Multiple Occupation (HMO) is permissible
* Generally, a HMO will be a property occupied by more than one household and more than two people, and may include bedsits, shared houses, non-self-contained flats and some self-contained flats
* Any property meeting the criteria above is deemed to be a HMO for lending purposes regardless of tenancy agreements in place or the type of tenant

## Planning Permission

HMO properties must have the appropriate planning permission in place as follows:

* Up to 6 bedrooms: C4 planning (where required due to removal of permitted development rights, such as the implementation of an Article 4 Directive)
* 7 or more bedrooms: Sui Generis planning

Where formal planning permission has not been granted as above, a Certificate of Lawful Use is also deemed as acceptable for both C4 and Sui Generis planning uses.

Where there is no formal planning permission and no Certificate of Lawful Use, confirmation of continued lawful use as a HMO should be obtained from the acting Solicitor.

## Licensing

### For remortgages

* The solicitor must confirm sight of a copy of the Licence prior to release of funds
* Or confirmation that a HMO licence application has been made

### For purchases of properties with ≤6 bedrooms

* Where the property is currently licenced:

  * The Group's Solicitor is to confirm sight of the licence
  * Undertaking from applicant to arrange for licence to be transferred into own name

* Where the property is not licenced but the applicant holds a licence on an alternative property:

  * The Group's Solicitor is to confirm sight of the licence
  * Undertaking from applicant to apply for a licence on the subject property within 60 days from completion

* Where the property is not licenced and the applicant does not hold a licence on an alternative property:

  * The applicant is to undertake to apply for a licence within 60 days from completion

### For purchases where the property has ≥7 bedrooms

* A copy of the licence on the security
* Or evidence that the applicant holds a licence on an alternative property

Must be received prior to release of funds.

# Intercompany Loans

Acceptable for limited company lending, subject to the following:

* The majority shareholder must be consistent across SPV and connected company
* If there is no singular majority shareholder, the ownership structure across the SPV and connected company must be consistent
* Must be UK to UK company (no offshore companies)
* Loan needs to be repayable, with a term agreed and documented loan agreement
* The loan term must not exceed the mortgage term
* The rate of interest applied should be HMRC’s ordinary rate of interest
* Any monthly payment must be included in the ICR calculation
* The connected company must not have any interest or charge over the Bank’s security

# Properties Above (or Attached to) Commercial Premises

Where the security is situated:

* Above a commercial premises, and/or
* Is a new build property

The case can proceed only if there are no adverse comments from the valuer or conveyancer.

# Remortgage

Remortgages cannot be accepted for the following purposes:

* To shore up a business
* To repay gambling debts

However:

* Capital raising is acceptable for any legal purpose
* The amount and purpose of capital being raised must be established

## Remortgage Within 6 Months

Remortgages may be considered where:

* The security property has been owned for less than 6 months
* The property is not a new build

### Where Improvement Works Have Been Undertaken

* LTV will be based on the post-works market value

### Where No Improvements Have Been Undertaken

* LTV will be based on the lower of:

  * Original purchase price
  * Current market value

## Inherited Properties

Where the property has recently been inherited:

* Probate or letters of administration must be granted prior to application
* This must be verified prior to offer

# Rental Income Requirements

Rental income requirements depend on the property type and complexity.

## Standard Buy to Let

Definition:

* A single dwelling
* HMO or student let with ≤6 letting rooms
* ≤6 flats in one block under one title

Rental coverage requirements:

* Higher/additional rate taxpayer: 140%
* Basic rate taxpayer / Ltd Company / LLP: 125%

## Complex Buy to Let

Definition:

* HMO or student let with >6 letting rooms
* >6 flats in one block under one loan
* Multiple houses under one title

Rental coverage requirements:

* Higher/additional rate taxpayer: 175%
* Basic rate taxpayer / Ltd Company / LLP: 145%

# Source of Deposit

The deposit should normally come from the applicant’s own resources without recourse to additional borrowing.

However, the following alternative sources may be acceptable.

## Capital Raising Remortgage / Secured Loan

* Acceptable where the applicant has sufficient equity in an existing property
* Funds may be raised via remortgage or second charge

## Gifted Family Deposit

Subject to the following:

* The amount must be a non-refundable gift
* Funds must not come from a child’s bank or savings account
* The individual making the gift must have no interest in the property
* Gifted deposit indemnity insurance must be arranged by the acting solicitor

## Director Loan

Subject to the following:

* The individual is transferring or purchasing a property into a company structure
* The purchase must be at full market value
* The person providing the loan must be a shareholder in the business
* The investment is treated as a director’s loan within the company accounts
* The difference between the mortgage and purchase price is covered by the director loan
* The loan must be non-interest bearing

## Inter-Company Loan

Subject to the following:

* The majority shareholder must be consistent across SPV and connected company
* If there is no singular majority shareholder, the ownership structure must be consistent
* Must be UK to UK company
* The loan must be repayable with a documented loan agreement
* The loan term cannot exceed the mortgage term
* Interest rate must be HMRC ordinary rate
* Monthly payments must be included in the ICR calculation
* The connected company must not hold any charge over the lender’s security

## Vendor Equity / Deposit and Builder Deposit / Incentive

* The value of any incentive must be deducted from the purchase price or value (whichever is lower)
* Lending will be based on the lower figure

# Tenancy

The property must be in a suitable condition to be let.

## England

The property may be let under:

* A single Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST), or
* A contractual/common law tenancy (e.g. company lets or annual rent above £100,000)

## Wales

The property may be let under an Occupational Contract in accordance with Welsh Government tenancy rules.

## Multiple Tenancies

* Multiple tenancies are acceptable for HMOs and MUFBs

## Tenancy Agreement Requirements

There must be a written tenancy agreement that restricts the tenant from:

* Sharing, assigning, sub-letting, multi-letting, charging or parting with possession of the property
* Using the property for any purpose other than a private dwelling
* Making alterations or allowing the property to fall into disrepair

Additional requirements:

* Maximum fixed term: 36 months
* Where the tenancy exceeds 12 months, the AST must include rent review provisions at least every 12 months, unless the mortgage rate is fixed for the same duration

## Unacceptable Tenancy Arrangements

Applications will not be considered where:

* The tenants are family members of the borrower
* The tenant has or may acquire an overriding interest in the property

# Tenure

## Freehold

* Applicable to houses and MUFBs only

## Leasehold

* The lease must have at least 50 years remaining at the end of the mortgage term
* Subject to satisfactory valuation

Where the remaining lease term is less than 85 years:

* Maximum LTV will be restricted to 75%.
