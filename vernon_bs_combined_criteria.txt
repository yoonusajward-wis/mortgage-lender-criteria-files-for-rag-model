# CREDIT AND ELIGIBILITY

## IMPAIRED CREDIT HISTORY

The Vernon Building Society does not offer sub-prime lending and therefore applicants with any of the following impaired credit history cannot be accepted:

* Secured loan account with a payment status of 3 or worse in the last 24 months
* Unsecured loan account with a payment status of 3 or worse in the last 24 months
* 1 or more County Court Judgments (CCJs) with a total value greater than £500 within the last 36 months
* Subject to an Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) or Administration Order within the last 36 months
* Bankruptcy order or Scottish Sequestration in the last 36 months
* Repossessions or voluntary repossessions within the last 5 years
* Debt Relief Orders are equivalent to an IVA

The society can consider applications where the impaired credit history (including defaults) is outside the above criteria.

In such cases, the underwriters will assess the case using all relevant information including a full explanation for any adverse credit.

In all cases, the enquiry should initially be referred to the Intermediary Sales Team at [intermediaries@thevernon.co.uk](mailto:intermediaries@thevernon.co.uk).

A Decision in Principle can only be obtained following a satisfactory credit report by the Society.

# INTEREST-ONLY

## INTEREST-ONLY – MAXIMUM LTV

Residential = 60% LTV
Buy to Let = 75% LTV
Buy for Uni = 100% LTV
Retirement Interest-Only (RIO) = 50% LTV

Part Capital & Interest and Part Interest-Only = 80% LTV.

At least 50% of total loan must be on Capital & Interest.

If LTV is 60% or below, there is no minimum percentage requirement for Capital & Interest.

---

## INTEREST-ONLY – REPAYMENT VEHICLES

* Regular savings plan (current value must be at least 50% of loan amount)
* Sale of assets (current value must be at least equal to loan amount)
* Periodic capital repayments (minimum 2 years history of bonus/dividend payments)
* Sale of mortgaged property (property valued at minimum £450k and plausible downsize plan required)

The Society will review any proposed repayment plan for plausibility.

---

# RETIREMENT – ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

## >10 YEARS TO RETIREMENT

Period: >10 years to retirement and extends <5 years beyond retirement.

Proof required:

* Evidence of pension contributions
* Completed Lending into Retirement declaration

Underwriting approach:

Affordability based on earned income.

---

## <10 YEARS TO RETIREMENT

Period: <10 years to retirement and/or loan extends >5 years beyond retirement.

Proof required:

* Evidence of pension income

Underwriting approach:

Affordability based on lower of earned or pension income.

---

## ALREADY IN RETIREMENT

Proof required:

* Evidence of pension income

Underwriting approach:

Based on pension income.

Consideration given to whether index linked depending on circumstance.

* Mobile homes
* Farms and small holdings
