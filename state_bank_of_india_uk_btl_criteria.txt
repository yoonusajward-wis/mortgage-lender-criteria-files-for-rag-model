# Adverse Credit

SBI UK will not accept an application if there is history of any of the following:

Bankruptcy
Administration Orders
Debt Relief Orders
Repossessions
Payment Arrangements
Debt Management Programmes
Individual Voluntary Arrangements (IVA)
Company Voluntary Arrangements (CVA)

**CCJ/Default**

SBI UK will accept applications with CCJ's/Defaults under the following scenario:

Where cases of one satisfied CCJ’s/one satisfied defaults up to GBP 2500 and outstanding defaults of up to £250 exist, subject to receipt of a detailed, “satisfactory” explanation outlining the circumstances of the CCJ/default.

**Payment Arrears (Not including mortgage arrears)**

Any late or missed payments can be considered in the context of the case. Refer to BDM.

**Mortgage Arrears**

If the credit search or mortgage statement provided reveals no more than one missed or late mortgage payment this can be considered in the context of the application but must be supported by:

An explanation from the customer
Evidence that the late/missed payment did not occur as a result of an inability to pay

Evidence is expected in the form of bank statements showing sufficient funds were available at the time.

**Payment Holidays**

SBI UK will consider applications where payment holiday took place on account of COVID19.

Underwriters will ensure applicants are not facing financial difficulty.

Where the property is subject to a payment holiday:

Release of funds will be subject to evidence rental income has restarted.

Portfolio stress calculations:

Mortgage payment will be considered
Rent will only be considered if actually received

**Members Voluntary Liquidation (MVL)**

SBI UK will accept application from customers who opted into MVL subject to satisfactory reason.

# Age Requirements

Minimum Age: 21 years at application

Maximum Age: Applicants age must not exceed 80 years at the end of the mortgage term.

**Joint Applications**

All applicants must satisfy minimum and maximum age criteria.

**SPV Applications**

Accept SPV applications where the loan term exceeds one or more applicants’ 80th birthday provided:

One or more remaining applicants is under age 80 at end of term
Remaining applicant meets income requirements
Remaining applicant holds at least 25% stake in SPV

# Aggregate Borrowing

Maximum total borrowing: £5,000,000 per client (personal name and/or SPV, LTD, LLP)

Existing customers:

Maximum exposure £7 million subject to 12 months satisfactory repayment history.

Borrowing above £5 million:

Restricted to 60% LTV.

Maximum loan: £3,000,000

# Arms-Length Transactions

Arms-Length Transactions are preferred.

Non-Arm’s length/Private Sales permitted subject to:

Maximum 5% variation of Open Market Value.

**Individual to SPV Transfers**

Permitted subject to:

Minimum 6 months ownership
Solicitor confirmation of purchase structure
Stamp duty confirmation
Insolvency indemnity policy
Purchase price aligned with valuation
Existing owners must be shareholders/directors
Directors loan requires Subordination Deed

# Back To Back Transactions

- SBI UK will only consider a remortgage application once:

- A clear Land Registry search has been conducted by SBI UK evidencing the applicant's ownership of the property; or
- Sight of TR1 to confirm registration has been submitted.

- SBI UK will accept remortgage applications where ownership is less than 6 months. In these cases:

- The purchase price and cost of improvement will be considered for LTV purposes; or
- The value given by the valuer will be used;
- Whichever is lower will apply.

- A special condition must be added to the mortgage offer requiring the acting solicitor to:

- Confirm property ownership; and
- Ensure the applicant’s name is updated upon completion with a sole charge registered in favour of SBI UK.

- For new purchase applications:

- The existing vendor must have owned the property for a minimum of 6 months.
- Exception: New build properties which have not yet been registered at the Land Registry.

# Builders Deposits & Sales Incentives

- The total amount of any builder or developer sales incentives must be:

- Discounted from the purchase price; or
- Discounted from the valuation (unless already discounted by the valuer);
- When calculating the maximum loan available.

# CCJs/Defaults

- SBI UK will accept applications with CCJs/Defaults under the following scenario:

- One satisfied CCJ/default up to £2,500; AND
- Outstanding defaults up to £250.

- In all cases:

- SBI UK must be made aware of the exact reasons why the CCJ/default has been registered.
- Six months bank statements must be provided as evidence that there are no issues with the applicant’s cash flow.

# Company Applications (SPV/LLP)

- Applications will be accepted from:

- UK incorporated Limited Companies; or
- Limited Liability Partnerships.

- The sole activity must be:

- Purchase and/or remortgage of residential property for letting.

- All shareholding directors (Companies), partners (Partnerships), or members (LLP or others):

- Must be named on the application.

- A minimum of 75% of shareholders:

- Must be applicants for the Buy to Let mortgage.

- SBI UK requires:

- All applicants to have the correct board authorisations to conduct the transaction.

# Company Criteria (SPV/LLP)

- Lending can only be considered to:

- Ltd Companies; or
- LLP Companies.

- SBI UK will not lend to:

- Trusts;
- Offshore Companies;
- Any similar structures.

- Maximum of four applicants permitted.

- Business type must be:

- Purchase/remortgage and letting of properties in the UK.
- Evidenced by trading accounts and SIC code recorded at Companies House.

- The business must:

- Have been profitable for the last 2 years; and
- Have a clear credit history.
- Exception: Newly formed companies.

- SPV Group Structures:

- Parent Company Application:

  - If the parent company meets the SIC code criteria, subsidiaries may have different SIC codes.
  - This is acceptable provided the subsidiary does not control the parent company operations and funds.

- Subsidiary Company Application:

  - All companies within the Group must be in the same line of business.
  - Directors and shareholders must be named applicants on the loan proposal.
  - Shareholding percentage need not be identical across group companies, provided shareholders are the same.

# Consultants / Contractors

- Contract workers are accepted under the following criteria:

### 12 Months or More

- Current contract must have at least 3 months remaining at the time of full mortgage application; or
- Evidence of a new or renewed contract for 12 months.
- Previous contract(s) must be provided demonstrating continuous employment for 12 months.

### 6 to 12 Months

- Current contract must have at least 3 months remaining; or
- Evidence of a new or renewed contract.
- Previous contract(s) must be provided demonstrating continuous employment for 12 months.
- Income must be a minimum of £50,000 gross (annualised).

### Less than 6 Months

- Not acceptable.

- SBI UK will not accept applicants under the following contract types:

- Seasonal Contract
- Zero Hours Contract (Piecework)
- Agency Staff
- Part Time Contracts
- Fixed Term Contracts below 6 months

- Self-Employed Contractors:

- Where income is derived from own business and turnover is solely from third party contracts:

  - Applicant will be assessed under contractor rules above;
  - Not treated as self-employed.
- Example: IT Industry Professionals.

# Consumer Buy To Let

- SBI UK does not accept any mortgage application that could be classified as a Consumer BTL.

# Criminal Convictions

- SBI UK will not accept any application where:

- The applicant (Director, Shareholder, or sole applicant); or
- A financial associate;
- Has a criminal conviction or prosecution pending.

- Exception:

- The conviction or pending prosecution is for a minor traffic offence; or
- The conviction is considered “spent” under the Rehabilitation of Offenders Act 1974.

# Deposit

- Where a property is being purchased, the applicant/s are required to provide a deposit of at least **25% of the purchase amount**.

- The amount and source of deposit must be declared on the application and evidence provided to ensure full understanding of the nature of the transaction.

- The deposit is expected to be provided from one, or a combination, of the following sources, and evidenced as outlined below:

### Applicants Own Savings

- Latest month bank or savings statement showing the required balance, taking into consideration any other anticipated costs (i.e., Stamp Duty, Solicitors fees).
- If the funds have not been in the account for 1 month, the source of these monies must be confirmed.

### Sale of Existing Property (one of the following required)

- Copy of bank statement showing sale proceeds; or
- Copy of the completion statement from the solicitor with full details of the property; or
- Letter from the acting solicitor confirming that the sale is currently going through and a Land Registry search to evidence ownership.

### Equity Release from an Existing Property (one of the following required)

- Copy of bank statement showing equity release proceeds; or
- Copy of existing mortgage statement and new mortgage offer evidencing equity being drawn on; or
- Letter from the acting solicitor confirming that the equity release mortgage is currently going through, confirmation of the funds being raised and a Land Registry search to evidence ownership.

### Gifted Deposits

- A declaration from the donor (see “Gifted Deposits”).
- Evidence of the donor monies in the format outlined in this section.
- Only gifts from immediate relatives are acceptable:

- Spouse
- Parents
- Grandparents
- Children
- Siblings

### UK Limited Company

- A letter from the company accountant confirming how the monies are being taken from the Ltd Company.
- Confirmation that the shareholders/directors will not have an interest in the property.
- The most recent set of accounts.
- Last 3 months business bank statements evidencing sufficient funds to be drawn upon.
- If monies are being taken as a loan, this is not acceptable (see SPV Directors Loan section below which confirms that a loan is acceptable when the borrower is an SPV).

### SPV Directors Loan

- Where lending is to an SPV and the deposit is being loaned to the SPV from the director/s of the organisation, this is acceptable to SBI UK provided that:

- A Subordination Deed is signed by the director/s prior to release of funds.
- The Subordination Deed ensures that the repayment of SBI UK's debt takes priority over any loan provided to fund the initial purchase.

# Direct Debit

- The Direct Debit must be taken from a bank account in the name of the applicant(s).
- For Company applications (LTD / LLP / SPV):

- The Direct Debit must be set up from a business bank account.

# Exposure

- Maximum individual loan: £3,000,000.

# First Legal Charge

- All mortgage lending must be secured by a legal charge with first priority.

- Property must be located in:

- England (including the Isle of Wight); or
- Wales.

- SBI UK does not offer secured lending against properties located in:

- Scotland
- Northern Ireland
- Isle of Man
- Isles of Scilly
- The Channel Islands

- The legal charge must be:

- Fully enforceable.
- Entered into by all legal owners of the property.
- Have full effect against all other charges and interests.

- SBI UK will not lend if another party’s charge has priority.

- If a second charge is present at application:

- It must be discharged prior to completion.
- A letter from the lender confirming repayment must be provided.
- A Land Registry search must confirm removal before completion.

# First Time Buyers

- An applicant who does not own their current main residence is classified as a first-time buyer.

- In most cases, first-time buyer applications are not acceptable.

- Exceptions:

- Applicant is already an existing BTL investor and owns at least one additional BTL property other than the one being mortgaged to SBI UK (applies to all applicants in joint applications); OR
- Joint application from Husband & Wife (or equivalent relationship) where only one applicant owns their current main residence.

# First Time Landlords

- Applications from first-time landlords are acceptable provided they own a residential property.

# Foreign Embassy Staff & Diplomats

- Applications from non-UK applicants employed in a Foreign Embassy are unacceptable and must be declined.

- Reason:

- Embassy staff may claim diplomatic immunity from legal action in the UK.
- They may be required to leave the country at short notice.

- UK Nationals employed by an Embassy in the UK:

- Cannot claim diplomatic immunity.
- Are exempt from this restriction.

- 130% of monthly interest at product rate + 2.00% OR 5.50% (whichever higher).

# Identity / KYC Requirements

- All applicants must provide:

- One document from List A and one from List B; OR
- Copy of passport plus any one document from either list evidencing address.

- If applicant:

- Is recorded on the Voters Roll at current address; AND
- Holds a credit match at same address;
- Then only a document from List A is required.

- Documents under List A must be valid for a minimum of 6 months at time of application.

### List A

- Current full signed UK passport.
- Full UK driving licence (new style photo card) – address must be current.
- Provisional UK photo card driving licence (new style) – address must be current.
- Full UK driving licence (old style paper) – address must be current.
- Foreign Driving Licence – photocard only.
- Current full signed foreign passport.
- National Identity Card – incorporating signature and photograph.

### List B

- Bank / Building Society / Credit Card Statement – less than 3 months old (internet statements not acceptable).
- Utility Bill – less than 3 months old (mobile phone and internet utility bills not acceptable).
- Latest Council Tax Demand – less than 1 year old.
- Latest UK Mortgage Statement for main residence – must show credit transactions and be less than 1 year old.

# Portfolio Landlord (Definition)

- An applicant is considered a “Portfolio Landlord” where:

- They own four or more mortgaged BTL properties; OR
- They currently own fewer than four mortgaged BTL properties but, as a result of the application(s) to SBI UK, will exceed four properties.

- This definition applies to:

- Individual applicants;
- Directors / Shareholders / Members in Ltd / LLP applications.

### Additional Documentation Required

In addition to ordinary case requirements, the following must be provided:

- Full schedule of BTL properties in SBI UK approved format.
- All background properties stressed at 6.00% interest rate.

- If present interest rate exceeds 6.00%, actual rate will apply.
- Exception: Properties being remortgaged to SBI UK must meet Rental Cover Assessment rules.

### Rental Coverage Requirements

- Individually owned properties:

- Basic Rate Taxpayers – 125% rent cover.
- Higher / Additional Rate Taxpayers – 145% rent cover.

- Company owned properties:

- 125% rent cover.

- Full Assets & Liabilities Statement (excluding properties listed in BTL schedule).

- Business Plan including:

- Short-term objectives.
- Long-term objectives.
- Planned expenditure.
- Further expansion plans.
- Strategy to cover long-term voids.
- Understanding of current BTL market.

# Portfolio Landlord (Requirements)

- BTL Property Schedule

- Requests to change details from an Attorney appointed after the application date.

- After completion:

- Some instructions from an Attorney may be accepted.

- No other parties will claim rights over security post-completion.
- Information provided is accurate and not misleading.
- All parties will receive independent legal advice.
- Property purchased as Vacant Possession or tenanted to third party.
- Seller will not occupy property after completion.

### Valuation & LTV Rules

- Standard valuation will provide OMV.

- LTV restricted to 75% of:

- Purchase Price; OR
- OMV;
- Whichever is lower.

- Applicant must evidence additional deposit funds where applicable.

- If purchased at OMV:

- SBI UK may lend 75% of OMV.
- Remaining balance may be covered by Gifted Deposit from seller, confirming:

  - Equity gifted without repayment or charge.
  - Seller waives all future rights to property.
