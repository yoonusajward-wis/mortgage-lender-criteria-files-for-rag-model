# Beverley Building Society – Residential Criteria

---

**LENDER:** Beverley Building Society
**PRODUCT:** Residential
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Lending Criteria
**LAST UPDATED:** Updated daily
**FOR INTERMEDIARY USE ONLY**

**Source:** https://beverleybs.co.uk/lending-criteria/

---

## SECTION 1 – LOAN PARAMETERS

- Minimum loan size: £45,000.
- Minimum property value: £100,000.
- Minimum term: 2 years (all mortgages except Retirement Interest Only — RIO has no maximum age at which the term must end).
- Maximum term: 40 years (25 years for commonhold properties).
- Longer terms may be considered in exceptional cases depending on the applicant's age, financial and other relevant circumstances.
- Repayment types: capital and interest or interest only.

### Maximum Loan by LTV

- Up to 65% LTV: £850,000.
- Up to 80% LTV: £600,000.
- Up to 90% LTV: £300,000.
- Up to 95% LTV: £250,000.

### LTV Limits by Lending Type

- Standard residential properties: up to 95% of purchase price or value, whichever is lower.
- Remortgages: up to 90%.
- Flats and apartments: maximum 80% LTV.
- Interest-only loans: lower LTV limits apply (see Section 5).

---

## SECTION 2 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

### Age and Lending into Retirement

- Maximum age for mortgage lending: 90 years (RIO has no maximum age).
- Mortgage terms beyond state retirement age can be agreed, including for applicants already retired, provided they can demonstrate sufficient income and affordability to maintain payments.

**Conditions for lending into retirement:**
- Employed or self-employed income considered only where there is a likelihood of it continuing to a maximum age of 75; otherwise only pension income or other non-salaried income is considered.
- Must be satisfied that payments would remain sustainable in the event of the death of a joint borrower — evidenced via life insurance, savings or pension income. Where this is not the case, downsizing may be considered as a repayment vehicle.

### Geographic Reach

- England: acceptable.
- Wales: acceptable.
- Scotland: not acceptable.

### Non-UK Nationals

- EU nationals who were resident in the UK as of 31 December 2020 and have been granted Settled or Pre-Settled status: acceptable. Proof of UK residency for the last 3 years and of residence in the UK on 31 December 2020 required.
- EU or non-EU nationals with permanent right to reside (indefinite leave to remain) who have lived in the UK for the last 3 years: acceptable, subject to underwriting.
- Foreign nationals on specified visas, subject to a minimum of 3 years in the UK, a UK bank account and a credit footprint:
  - Skilled Worker Visa.
  - Health and Care Worker Visa.
  - British National Overseas Visa.
  - UK Ancestry Visa.
  - Maximum LTV: 80% (residential owner-occupied, excluding self-build).
  - Deposit must come from a UK bank account.
  - Minimum 1 year remaining on visa.
  - Subject to underwriting.

### Identity Verification

- Proof of identity required for all applicants.
- Acceptable: photo ID (passport or driving licence); proof of address from an official source dated within the last 3 months; AML report, credit reference and electoral roll confirmation.

### Intermediary Requirements

- Applications on behalf of members of the public accepted only from FCA-registered intermediaries.
- Thorough due diligence and cross-referencing carried out on all applications.

## SECTION 3 – AFFORDABILITY ASSESSMENT

### Stress Rate

- Affordability calculated at the Society's standard variable rate (SVR) plus 1%.

### Loan-to-Income (LTI)

- Standard LTI ratio: 4.49x or less.
- Higher LTIs may be considered for trapped borrowers or in other exceptional cases, at the discretion of the Head of Lending.
- Maximum income multiple when lending into retirement: 4x.

### Acceptable Income Sources

- Employment income.
- Self-employment income.
- Pension income.
- Investment income (with 10% reduction applied to non-guaranteed income to allow for market downturns).
- Rental income.
- Foster income.
- A combination of the above.

### Income Verification

- Income must be verified in all cases.
- Self-certified applications: not accepted.
- Applicants in a probationary period: considered on a case-by-case basis, taking into account affordability and previous employment continuity.

### Income Assessment — In and Into Retirement

- Employed income: considered only up to age 75.
- Other non-salaried income (rental, investments, pensions): considered where expected to continue. Professional landlords or company directors deriving income from investments considered at the Head of Lending's discretion.
- Pension income: certainty of ongoing income assessed, including potential exhaustion of drawdown pension pots. Pension valuation statements dated within 1 month of application required. 10% haircut applied to non-guaranteed income.
- Joint mortgages: affordability assessed to ensure both borrowers could maintain payments should the other die. A declaration to sell and downsize is acceptable for affordability purposes.

---

## SECTION 4 – CREDIT HISTORY

- Discharged bankrupt: acceptable after 6 years.
- All CCJs: must be settled for over 3 years.
- One satisfied CCJ under £500 in the last 3 years: acceptable if not credit-related and satisfied for over 3 months.
- All defaults: must be settled for over 3 years.
- Maximum of 2 defaults under £1,000 and not credit-related: acceptable if settled less than 3 years ago.
- Discharged IVA or DMP: acceptable after 3 years.
- Active DMP: acceptable after 2 years.
- A common sense view will be taken where a credit record shows a minor blip (e.g. one late or missed payment over a substantial period, swiftly settled, resulting from genuine oversight).
- COVID-19 payment breaks taken in line with UK government measures: considered.

---

## SECTION 5 – INTEREST ONLY

- Interest-only borrowers must declare in writing how they intend to repay the mortgage at the end of the term; all parties must sign this document (in introduced cases, signatures must be witnessed by the broker).
- Maximum LTV: 70%.

### Repayment Vehicles

**Downsizing:**
- Minimum equity required: £150,000 (or more, depending on local house prices).

**Savings and Investments (held for at least 2 years):**
- Must be in the names of all borrowers, held in GBP, and be at least equal to the loan advance (or projected to be so on lowest projections evidenced by statements within the last 12 months).
- High-risk investments (single stock, exotic or speculative) not accepted.
- Acceptable investment types and evidence:
  - Managed investment plans, unit trusts, OEICs, investment bonds, stocks and shares ISAs: statements less than 12 months old.
  - Managed shareholdings: share certificates, nominated account statements or confirmation from an authorised stockbroker.
  - Endowment policies: latest projected value statement dated within 1 month of application.
- 10% haircut applied to non-guaranteed investment values.

**Sale of Another Property:**
- Must be in England and Wales.
- Evidence of equity required; valuation may be carried out by the Society; most recent mortgage statements required if mortgaged.

**Sale of the Mortgaged Property (Downsizing):**
- Minimum equity: £150,000; maximum LTV: 70%.
- Borrower must commit to repaying the existing mortgage on sale and demonstrate sufficient remaining funds to purchase a new home.

## SECTION 6 – RETIREMENT INTEREST ONLY (RIO)

- Interest-only loans repaid when the borrower dies, moves into long-term care, or sells the property.
- All general lending-into-retirement criteria apply, plus:
  - Minimum eligible age: 55.
  - Maximum of 2 borrowers; no other occupiers (a family carer may reside with the borrower subject to a signed Occupiers' Consent form confirming no interest in the property).
  - Maximum LTV: 60%; 30% where the property is in a retirement complex.
  - Minimum property valuation: £125,000.
  - Minimum loan size: £25,000 (purchase); £40,000 (remortgage).
  - If the property is a flat, it must have lift access if not on the ground floor.

### Acceptable Income for RIO

- Net income from buy-to-let property.
- Pension income.
- Other non-salaried income, investments (e.g. stocks and shares ISAs, defined contribution pensions) — 10% haircut applied.

---

## SECTION 7 – INCOME TYPES

### Self-Employed

- Business accounts and income confirmation and/or SA302s required for periods appropriate to the LTV.
- Rising income: most recent income level applied unless the latest year is more than 20% higher than previous years, in which case a risk director review may be required.
- Income calculation: applicant's share of net profit, salary and dividends used for multiplier and affordability purposes.
- Falling income: rarely accepted.
- Unusual circumstances: personalised cases considered on an individual basis.

### Contractors

- Available across the current product range up to a maximum LTV of 80%.
- Day 1 contracting: available for NHS and medical professionals with a 12-month track record of earnings in a related field.
- Zero-hours contracts: available for professionals with 12 months' history and no gaps; and non-professionals with a minimum of 2 years' track record and no gaps in the last 12 months.
- All contractor types considered on a case-by-case basis: CIS members, agency, nursing bank, fixed, temporary and short-term contracts, IT consultants and self-employed contractors — from 1 year's contract with evidence of a track record in a related field.
- Umbrella companies: accepted with evidence of 1 year's track record in a related field.
- No credit scoring — all decisions made on a case-by-case basis with individual underwriting.

---

## SECTION 8 – CAPITAL RAISING AND REMORTGAGE

### Capital Raising

- Lending considered for almost any legal purpose, including purchasing another property or a high-value item, on a case-by-case basis.

### Debt Consolidation

- Acceptable on purchases and remortgages for personal debts (business debts considered at the Head of Lending's discretion).
- Maximum amount: £35,000 (above this requires referral).
- Maximum LTV: 75%.

### Remortgage — Additional Borrowing LTV Limits

- Home improvements: 80% LTV.
- Debt consolidation: 75% LTV.
- Lifestyle, major purchase or project: 75% LTV.
- Purchase of equity: 90% LTV.
- Freehold reversion: 90% LTV.

### Trapped Borrowers

Affordability criteria for LTI ratios may be flexed for remortgage purposes, subject to the following conditions:
- The customer's mortgage existed before 26 April 2014.
- The contract was in keeping with affordability requirements at that time.
- No additional borrowing is involved and no additional borrowing has been taken since the original mortgage.
- The existing mortgage is fully up to date at application and has been so for the previous 12 months.
- The monthly payment at the Society's SVR would not exceed the amount currently paid to the existing lender.
- Affordability can be validated based on the borrower's current income.

## SECTION 9 – PROPERTY CRITERIA

### Location

- England: acceptable.
- Wales: acceptable.
- Scotland: not acceptable.

### Accepted Property Types

- Freehold properties.
- Leasehold flats and apartments (minimum 20% deposit; owner-occupied only).
- Commonhold properties (maximum 25-year term).
- Agricultural or forestry restricted properties: acceptable within Yorkshire and the Humber only.
- Second homes: acceptable where borrowers can demonstrate genuine residential (non-letting) use.
- Properties with an annexe or outbuilding: acceptable; annexe/outbuilding used as a holiday let or AirBnB also acceptable.
- Unusual or non-standard construction: considered on a case-by-case basis, including:
  - Timber-framed and 100% timber properties.
  - Thatch-roofed properties.
  - System-built or modular buildings (subject to a licensed repair scheme).
  - Grade II listed buildings.
  - BOPAS-accredited schemes.
- Properties with agricultural restrictions anywhere in England and Wales: considered.
- Overages: considered where the overage clause is reasonable and not passively triggered, the valuer is made aware, and maximum LTV does not exceed 75%.
- Properties with Japanese knotweed or other invasive species: considered subject to the valuer's advice and an insurance-backed eradication scheme with a specialist company.
- Properties with wholly-owned solar panels: acceptable (the Society does not advise on servicing obligations — independent advice recommended).

### Not Acceptable Properties

- Freehold flats.
- Local authority flats.
- Grade I listed properties.
- Properties classed as defective under Part XVI of the Housing Act 1985.
- Properties with leased solar panels.
- Commercial properties.

### Flats and Apartments

- Leasehold flats and apartments only; minimum 20% deposit; owner-occupied only.
- Maximum 4 residential storeys (standard).
- Flats in larger buildings considered at lower LTVs — an appropriate external fire wall review by a suitably qualified professional is required.

### Leasehold Requirements

- Minimum remaining lease: must exceed the mortgage term by at least 50 years.
- Lease terms must be acceptable to the Society's solicitors.

### Commonhold Properties

- Acceptable subject to the borrower supplying details of the relevant commonhold association.
- Maximum mortgage term: 25 years.

### New Build and Conversions

- Acceptable with appropriate certification and insurance from reputable providers.
- Must have NHBC or equivalent certificate issued by a registered builder; or
- Construction must be designed and supervised by a suitably qualified chartered architect or chartered surveyor carrying professional indemnity insurance of at least the finished property value, producing the RIBA/RICS standard New Build Professional Consultants Certificate on completion.
- Minimum insurance/certification period: 10 years.
- Professional Consultants Certificates (PCCs) in standard CML format may be accepted for properties built or converted within the last 10 years.

### Self-Build

- Self-build mortgages available subject to satisfactory certification and insurance (same requirements as new build above).
- Staged payment advances available, subject to valuation at each stage.
- Maximum LTV at any stage: 80%.
- Monies released only once written confirmation of activated planning permission is received from the local authority.
- Standard payment stages: 25% at roof plate level; 25% at roof on; 25% plastered out; 25% on completion. Different combinations considered at the Head of Lending's discretion.

### Property Insurance

- Buildings insurance must be in place and evidenced at completion.
- All borrowers must sign a declaration confirming insurance is in place.

### Valuations

- RICS-qualified valuers only.
- Accepted valuation types (RICS Guidance Notes): mortgage valuation, Homebuyers report and valuation, building survey with the Society's standard mortgage valuation report.
- Hometrack automated valuation (AVM): acceptable for remortgages and further advances at LTV below 75% and total loan below £500,000.

### Roads and Sewers

- All access roads and sewers must be adopted by the local authority or subject to a road agreement backed by a bond under Section 38 of the Highways Act 1980 (roads) and Section 104 of the Water Resources Act 1991 as amended by the Water Act 2003 (sewers).
- Where roads or sewers are not adopted, there must be adequate margin between the advance and the security; funds will be retained to cover adoption costs and a maintenance charge will apply to the borrower until adoption.

### Right to Buy

- Acceptable in East Yorkshire only.
- Maximum LTV: 100% of the discounted purchase price.

### Second-Charge Lending

- Not available; the Society must always hold the first charge.

---

## SECTION 10 – GUARANTORS AND FAMILY ASSIST

- Family members accepted as guarantors on a case-by-case basis, assessed for affordability in the same way as the borrowers.
- The borrower must be working and not wholly reliant on the guarantor's support.

**Options available:**
- Straight guarantor (not party to the mortgage): guarantor legally agrees to assume liability for mortgage payments.
- Joint Borrower, Sole Proprietor: guarantor is party to the mortgage without incurring stamp duty or ongoing mortgage payment obligations.

---

## SECTION 11 – SUPPORTING DOCUMENTATION

- Fully completed application form from all potential borrowers.
- Employed income: most recent P60(s), three most recent payslips, and 3 months' bank statements for all accounts.
- Current mortgages: most recent mortgage statements.
- Self-employed: business accounts and/or SA302s for periods appropriate to the LTV; bank statements also required.
- Investments and assets: evidence of deposits in savings, shares and other asset forms where applicable.
- Additional documentation may be required depending on the case, as directed by the Society or the introducing broker.
- RIO mortgages: additional specific documentation required (see Section 6).
