# Principality Building Society – Buy to Let & Holiday Let Criteria

---

**LENDER:** Principality Building Society
**PRODUCT:** Buy to Let & Holiday Let
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Lending Criteria
**LAST UPDATED:** 2025
**FOR INTERMEDIARY USE ONLY**

**Source:** https://www.principality.co.uk/intermediaries/our-mortgage-lending-criteria/buy-to-let-and-holiday-let

---

## SECTION 1 – MORTGAGE TERM

- Minimum repayment term: 5 years.
- Maximum total mortgage term: 40 years.
- Maximum term where any element is interest only: 25 years.
- For buy to let or holiday let applications, the mortgage must be repaid before the eldest applicant's 85th birthday.
- Leasehold properties: minimum unexpired lease term at start of mortgage is 85 years.

---

## SECTION 2 – OFFER PERIOD

- Residential and buy to let purchase and remortgage offers: maximum validity 180 days (6 months).
- New build purchase offers: maximum validity 240 days (8 months).

---

## SECTION 3 – CAPITAL RAISING

Further lending, Lifestyle Loan, or remortgage for existing borrowers is allowed.

**Allowed purposes:**
- House purchase.
- Buy to let.
- Further advances for home improvements.
- Purchase of freehold.
- Transfer of equity.

- Debt consolidation on buy to let mortgages: not acceptable.

---

## SECTION 4 – MINIMUM LOAN AMOUNT

- Minimum loan amount (new build, buy to let, holiday let): £25,000.

---

## SECTION 5 – HMO AND STUDENT LETS

Not acceptable:
- Houses with multiple occupancy (HMOs).
- Student lets.

---

## SECTION 6 – AGE LIMITS (BUY TO LET AND HOLIDAY LET)

- Minimum applicant age: 21.
- Maximum age at end of term: mortgage must be repaid before the eldest applicant's 85th birthday.

### Retired Applicants

Where lending extends past age 70 or the intended retirement age (whichever comes first):

If retirement is more than 10 years away AND the term does not extend beyond age 75:
- Affordability assessed using current income.
- Proof of payment into a private pension required (payslip, bank statement, or pension statement).

In all other circumstances:
- Affordability assessed based on projected pension income.

### Joint Borrowers

- Where the term extends beyond the oldest borrower's 76th birthday, the underwriter must be satisfied the loan remains affordable following the death of either borrower.

---

## SECTION 7 – OVERPAYMENTS

- Overpayments are allowed on all non-flexible residential, buy to let and holiday let mortgages.
- Maximum annual overpayment allowance: 10% of outstanding balance as at 1 January each calendar year.
- Regular or lump sum overpayments are allowed.
- Overpayments above the annual allowance may incur an early repayment charge.

---

## SECTION 8 – HOLIDAY LET ELIGIBILITY

- Mortgage type: non-regulated holiday let (advised basis).
- Applicants who do not currently own and live in their own home are allowed.
- Minimum applicant age: 21.
- Maximum number of mortgaged holiday lets: 2 (including current application, in sole or joint names).
- No minimum income requirement.
- Maximum LTV: 75%.
- Minimum property value: £50,000.
- Minimum purchase price: £50,000.
- Minimum loan size: £25,000.

### Loan Limits by LTV

At 60% LTV:
- Minimum loan: £25,000.
- Maximum loan: £1,000,000.

At 75% LTV:
- Minimum loan: £25,000.
- Maximum loan: £750,000.

---

## SECTION 9 – HOLIDAY LET RENTAL COVERAGE

- Remortgage where property was purchased before January 2017 on a holiday let basis: minimum 125% at 7.80%.
- Remortgage where property was purchased after January 2017 on a holiday let basis: minimum 145% at 7.80%.

---

## SECTION 10 – HOLIDAY LET RENTAL INCOME CALCULATION

- Rental income is calculated as the average of projected low, mid and high season weekly rental yields.

**Calculation method:**
- Average weekly rent × 30 weeks.
- Divide by 12 months.
- Result applied to the rental coverage calculation.

- Assumed occupancy level: 30 weeks.
- Maximum personal use by applicant: 2 months per annum.

---

## SECTION 11 – CONSUMER BUY TO LET (CBTL)

**Allowed when:**
- The applicant does not own other rental properties; and
- The applicant or a related person previously lived in the property; or
- The applicant inherited the property; or
- The property was received as a gift.

- New regulated buy to let applications: not acceptable.
- Existing regulated buy to let accounts: serviced.

---

## SECTION 12 – BUY TO LET ELIGIBILITY

- Mortgage type: non-regulated buy to let (advised basis).
- Minimum energy performance certificate (EPC) rating: E.
- Applicants who do not currently own and live in their own home are allowed.
- Minimum applicant age: 21.
- Applicants may collectively own no more than 3 mortgaged buy to lets.
- Non-EEA applicants considered.
- No minimum income requirement.
- Maximum total borrowing including Principality lending: £2,000,000 (includes any mortgages the applicant is party to jointly; may exclude residential and/or holiday let mortgages).
- Maximum LTV: 75%.
- Minimum property value: £50,000.
- Minimum purchase price: £50,000.
- Minimum loan size: £25,000.
- Interest Only: allowed up to 75% LTV.

### Loan Limits by LTV

At 60% LTV:
- Minimum loan: £25,000.
- Maximum loan: £1,000,000.

At 75% LTV:
- Minimum loan: £25,000.
- Maximum loan: £750,000.

---

## SECTION 13 – BUY TO LET RENTAL COVERAGE

- Standard new purchases: minimum 145% at 5.50%.
- Remortgage where property was purchased before January 2017 on a BTL basis: minimum 125% at 5.50%.
- Remortgage where property was purchased after January 2017 on a BTL basis: minimum 145% at 5.50%.
- Remortgage with additional borrowing on a BTL property: minimum 145% at 5.50%.

---

## SECTION 14 – BUY TO LET LETTINGS REQUIREMENTS

Lettings must be conducted under:
- Assured Shorthold Tenancy Agreements (ASTA) — England.
- Written Statement of Standard Occupation Contract (WSSOC) — Wales.

**Conditions:**
- No more than 12 months remaining on the tenancy at application.
- Rolling contract is acceptable where the tenant/contract holder continues beyond the initial ASTA/WSSOC.
- Verified by solicitor in line with mortgage conditions.
- All borrowers must be named on the tenancy agreement or Written Statement of Standard Occupation Contract.

**Not acceptable:**
- Third party lettings.
- Sub-lettings.
- Company lets.
- Local authority lets.

---

## SECTION 15 – GIFTED EQUITY

- For purchases using gifted equity, contact the local BDM before submitting the case.
