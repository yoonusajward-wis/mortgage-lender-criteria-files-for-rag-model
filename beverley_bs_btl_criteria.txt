LENDER NAME: Beverley Building Society
PRODUCT: Buy to Let

BEVERLEY BUILDING SOCIETY - BUY TO LET LENDING CRITERIA
====================================================

Source: https://beverleybs.co.uk/lending-criteria-buy-to-let/
Last Updated: Information updated daily

================================================================================
LOAN DETAILS
================================================================================

Minimum Loan Size:
- £45,000

Maximum Loan Size:
- £850,000 up to 65% LTV
- £500,000 up to 70% LTV

Maximum LTV:
- Maximum: 70% LTV

Minimum Property Value:
- £100,000

Loan Term:
- Minimum Term: 2 years
- Maximum Term: 40 years

Repayment Methods:
- Capital Repayment (Capital & Interest): Acceptable
- Interest Only: Acceptable

================================================================================
APPLICANT CRITERIA
================================================================================

Age Limits:
- Minimum Age at Application: 21 years (both applicants)
- Maximum Age at End of Term: 90 years
- Maximum Age for Mortgage Lending: 90 years

Availability:
- Available to both purchase and remortgage customers including further advances
- Maximum Number of Applicants: 4
- Applicants must have permanent rights to reside in the UK
- First Time Buyers: Not Acceptable (not available to first time buyers - any applicant)
- First Time Landlords: Acceptable (first time landlords allowed)

Exposure:
- Maximum Properties: An applicant cannot have more than 1 main residence and 3 BTL properties

================================================================================
AFFORDABILITY AND RENTAL INCOME
================================================================================

Income Coverage Ratio (ICR) / Rental Coverage:
- Rate Used to Stress: The higher of 2% above payrate or 5.5%
- Higher Rate Taxpayers: Minimum ICR is 130%
- Basic Rate Taxpayers: Minimum ICR is 130%

Top-Slicing:
- Not Permitted - Top slicing cannot be used in affordability assessment

Minimum Income Requirements:
- Minimum Income: £20,000 per annum (Buy-to-Let applicants must be in receipt of a minimum income of £20k)
- Joint Applicants: For joint applicants this is the joint income minimum

Income Types (Acceptable):
- Employed: Basic PAYE income only
- Pension Income: Acceptable
- Self Employed: Latest year's income

Income Types (Not Acceptable for Minimum Income):
- Overtime: Not acceptable for minimum income purposes
- Bonus: Not acceptable for minimum income purposes
- Commission: Not acceptable for minimum income purposes
- Additional Benefit Incomes: Not acceptable for minimum income purposes

Employment Requirements:
- Minimum Term of Employment: No minimum term of employment (outside probation)
- Self-Employed Minimum Business Ownership: 1 year (self-employed applicants must have owned the business for a minimum of 1 year)

================================================================================
ADVERSE CREDIT CRITERIA
================================================================================

CCJs (County Court Judgments):
- All CCJs: Must be settled over three years
- One Satisfied CCJ: Under £500 in the last three years and not credit related, must be satisfied over three months

Defaults:
- All Defaults: Must be settled over three years
- Maximum of Two Defaults: Under £1,000 and not credit related, can be accepted if settled less than three years

IVAs (Individual Voluntary Arrangements):
- Discharged IVA: After three years

Debt Management Plans:
- Discharged DMP: After three years
- Debt Management Plans: After two years

Discharged Bankrupt:
- Acceptable: After six years

================================================================================
PROPERTY CRITERIA
================================================================================

Property Location:
- England: Acceptable
- Wales: Acceptable
- Scotland: Not Acceptable (will only lend on buy to let properties within England and Wales only)

Property Types:
- Flats: Acceptable (leasehold flats and apartments only, where the borrower has a deposit of at least 30%)
- Maximum Number of Storeys for Flats: 4 residential storeys (as a rule, will only consider flats in complexes with no more than four residential stories)
- Flats in Larger Buildings: Acceptable at lower LTVs (will consider flats in larger buildings at lower LTVs. In such cases, requires a copy of an appropriate external fire wall review, conducted by a suitably-qualified and competent professional)
- Freehold Flats: Not Acceptable (does not offer mortgages for freehold flats)

Unacceptable Properties:
- Bed-sits: Not Acceptable
- Flats over 4 storeys: Not Acceptable
- Property Split into Multiple Units: Not Acceptable (e.g. house converted into two flats without separate leases)
- Freehold Flats: Not Acceptable
- Ex Local Authority Flats: Not Acceptable
- Flats Over Commercial: Not Acceptable
- Farms or Small Holdings: Not Acceptable
- Grade 1 Listed Properties: Not Acceptable
- Grade 2 Listed Properties: Not Acceptable
- Non-traditional PRC Houses (Not Repaired): Not Acceptable (including any adjoining properties)
- Properties with More Than 10 Acres of Land: Not Acceptable
- Properties with Restricted Occupancy/Covenants: Not Acceptable, including:
  * Age restriction (e.g. over 55s)
  * Time spent at property (e.g. 11/12 months)
  * Agricultural use

Leasehold Properties:
- Minimum Remaining Lease: Must exceed the mortgage term by at least 50 years
- Lease Terms: Must be acceptable to solicitors

Energy Performance Certificate (EPC):
- Minimum EPC Rating: E or above (all properties at application must have an EPC rating of E or above)

New Build Properties:
- Acceptable: Will lend on new properties or modern conversions, such as barn conversions, which is backed by appropriate certification and insurance from reputable providers
- NHBC or Equivalent Certificate: All newbuild property must be subject to a NHBC or equivalent certificate issued by a registered builder
- Alternative: Construction must be designed and supervised by a suitably-qualified chartered architect or chartered surveyor who can provide written confirmation that they carry professional indemnity insurance of at least the value of the finished property, and will produce the Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA)/Royal Institute of Chartered Surveyors (RICS) standard New Built Professional Consultants Certificate on completion of construction
- Minimum Insurance/Certification Period: 10 years (all new builds are subject to an appropriate insurance/certification scheme covering a minimum of 10 years, and this will be brought to the attention of the valuer, for them to consider as part of the valuation)

================================================================================
TENANCY CRITERIA
================================================================================

Tenancy Type:
- Must be Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST)
- Minimum Length of AST: 6 months
- Maximum Length of AST: 12 months
- Purchase Applications with Tenants in Situ: Acceptable (purchase applications where tenants will remain in the property)

Unacceptable Tenancy Types:
- Multiple Tenancies: Not Acceptable (where each tenant signs a separate agreement and/or has separate facilities)
- Rent Act Protected Tenants: Not Acceptable (or an agent of these)
- Housing Association or Local Authority Lets: Not Acceptable (intended letting to housing association or local authority)

Unacceptable Lets:
- Companies or Trusts: Not Acceptable
- Houses of Multiple Occupancy (HMOs): Not Acceptable
- Holiday Lets: Not Acceptable
- Sale and Leaseback: Not Acceptable (where the purchaser intends letting the property to the seller on a 'sale and leaseback' arrangement)
- Sub-letting: Not Acceptable

Family Occupancy:
- Letting to Immediate Family Member: Acceptable (allows letting to an immediate family member)

================================================================================
DEPOSITS
================================================================================

Source of Deposit (Acceptable):
- Savings/Investments: Acceptable
- Family Gift: Acceptable
- Concessionary: Acceptable (minimum 20% concession)
- Raised from Another Property: Acceptable

================================================================================
REPAYMENT AND TERM
================================================================================

Repayment Methods:
- Capital Repayment (Capital & Interest): Acceptable
- Interest Only: Acceptable

Term:
- Minimum Term: 2 years
- Maximum Term: 40 years

================================================================================
CAPITAL RAISING / REMORTGAGE PURPOSES
================================================================================

Capital Raising - Acceptable Reasons:
- Debt Consolidation: Acceptable (maximum debt consolidation amount £35,000)
- Home Improvements: Acceptable
- Repaying Family Gift: Acceptable
- Giving Family Gift: Acceptable
- Deposit for Another Property Purchase: Acceptable

Capital Raising - Unacceptable Reasons:
- For Business Purposes: Not Acceptable
- Pay Tax Bill: Not Acceptable

Debt Consolidation:
- Maximum Amount: £35,000
- Maximum Number of Debts: 5 debts (maximum debt consolidation amount is £35,000 across a maximum of 5 debts)

================================================================================
CONSUMER BUY TO LET
================================================================================

Consumer Buy to Let:
- Acceptable - Can lend on properties that have either been inherited; where the applicant wishes to rent out a property they have previously lived in or intends to let to a family member

================================================================================
EX-PAT APPLICATIONS
================================================================================

Ex-Pat Buy to Let:
- Acceptable - Available to ex-pats

Ex-Pat Requirements:
- Applicants: Must be UK citizens or have permanent rights to reside who are currently resident abroad
- Maximum LTV: 70%
- Employment: Available to employed applicants only
- Minimum Income: £40,000 (GBP equivalent) following a currency exchange using XE.com
- Contract of Employment: Must provide contract of employment
- UK Bank Statement: Must provide UK bank statement
- Rent Payment: Rent must be received in GBP into UK bank account
- Mortgage Payment: Mortgage to be paid from UK bank account in GBP
- Deposit Source: Deposits can come from non UK source (e.g. country they are currently residing in)

Ex-Pat Eligible Countries:
- Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Canada, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hong Kong, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Japan, Kingdom of the Netherlands (The Netherlands, the Netherlands Antilles and Aruba), Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, New Zealand, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Singapore, Slovak Republic, Slovenia, South Korea, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, United Kingdom*, and UAE
- Note: *Does not include UK Crown dependencies; the Isle of Man and the Bailiwicks of Jersey and Guernsey

Ex-Pat Service of Notices:
- A UK named individual (a family member or solicitor) and their address for service of notices will be required
