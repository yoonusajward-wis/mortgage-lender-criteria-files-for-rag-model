# Beverley Building Society – Buy to Let Criteria

---

**LENDER:** Beverley Building Society
**PRODUCT:** Buy to Let
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Lending Criteria
**LAST UPDATED:** Updated daily
**FOR INTERMEDIARY USE ONLY**

**Source:** https://beverleybs.co.uk/lending-criteria-buy-to-let/

---

## SECTION 1 – LOAN PARAMETERS

- Minimum loan size: £45,000.
- Maximum loan at up to 65% LTV: £850,000.
- Maximum loan at up to 70% LTV: £500,000.
- Maximum LTV: 70%.
- Minimum property value: £100,000.
- Minimum term: 2 years.
- Maximum term: 40 years.
- Repayment methods: capital and interest or interest only are acceptable.

---

## SECTION 2 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

- Minimum age at application: 21 years (all applicants).
- Maximum age at end of term: 90 years.
- Maximum number of applicants: 4.
- Applicants must have permanent right to reside in the UK.
- Available for purchase and remortgage, including further advances.
- First-time buyers: not acceptable (applies to any applicant on the application).
- First-time landlords: acceptable.
- Maximum exposure: an applicant cannot hold more than 1 main residence and 3 BTL properties.

---

## SECTION 3 – AFFORDABILITY AND RENTAL INCOME

### Interest Cover Ratio (ICR)

- Stress rate: the higher of pay rate + 2% or 5.5%.
- Minimum ICR for higher rate taxpayers: 130%.
- Minimum ICR for basic rate taxpayers: 130%.
- Top-slicing: not permitted.

### Minimum Income

- Minimum income: £20,000 per annum.
- For joint applicants: £20,000 is the combined joint income minimum.

### Acceptable Income Types

- Employed: basic PAYE income only.
- Pension income: acceptable.
- Self-employed: latest year's income acceptable.

### Unacceptable Income Types (for Minimum Income Purposes)

- Overtime.
- Bonus.
- Commission.
- Additional benefit income.

### Employment Requirements

- Minimum term of employment: no minimum (outside probation).
- Self-employed: must have owned the business for a minimum of 1 year.

---

## SECTION 4 – CREDIT HISTORY

### CCJs

- All CCJs: must be settled for over 3 years.
- One satisfied CCJ under £500 in the last 3 years: acceptable if not credit-related and satisfied for over 3 months.

### Defaults

- All defaults: must be settled for over 3 years.
- Maximum of 2 defaults under £1,000 and not credit-related: acceptable if settled less than 3 years ago.

### IVAs

- Discharged IVA: acceptable after 3 years.

### Debt Management Plans

- Discharged DMP: acceptable after 3 years.
- Active DMP: acceptable after 2 years.

### Bankruptcy

- Discharged bankrupt: acceptable after 6 years.

---

## SECTION 5 – PROPERTY CRITERIA

### Location

- England: acceptable.
- Wales: acceptable.
- Scotland: not acceptable.

### Accepted Property Types

- Leasehold flats and apartments: acceptable where the borrower has a deposit of at least 30%.
- Flats in complexes of up to 4 residential storeys: acceptable.
- Flats in larger buildings: acceptable at lower LTVs — a copy of an appropriate external fire wall review conducted by a suitably qualified and competent professional is required.
- New build and modern conversions (e.g. barn conversions): acceptable, subject to appropriate certification and insurance from reputable providers.

### Not Acceptable Properties

- Bed-sits.
- Freehold flats.
- Flats over 4 storeys (standard).
- Ex-local authority flats.
- Flats over commercial premises.
- Properties split into multiple units without separate leases.
- Houses of Multiple Occupation (HMOs).
- Farms or small holdings.
- Grade 1 listed properties.
- Grade 2 listed properties.
- Non-traditional PRC houses (not repaired), including any adjoining properties.
- Properties with more than 10 acres of land.
- Properties with restricted occupancy or covenants (e.g. age restrictions, time limits at property, agricultural use).

### Leasehold Requirements

- Minimum remaining lease: must exceed the mortgage term by at least 50 years.
- Lease terms must be acceptable to solicitors.

### Energy Performance Certificate (EPC)

- Minimum EPC rating: E or above.

### New Build Requirements

- Must be subject to an NHBC or equivalent certificate issued by a registered builder; or
- Construction must be designed and supervised by a suitably qualified chartered architect or chartered surveyor who carries professional indemnity insurance of at least the value of the finished property, and who will produce the RIBA/RICS standard New Build Professional Consultants Certificate on completion.
- All new builds must be covered by an appropriate insurance/certification scheme for a minimum of 10 years.

---

## SECTION 6 – TENANCY CRITERIA

### Acceptable Tenancy

- Must be Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST).
- Minimum AST length: 6 months.
- Maximum AST length: 12 months.
- Purchase applications with tenants in situ: acceptable.
- Letting to an immediate family member: acceptable.

### Unacceptable Tenancy Types

- Multiple tenancies where each tenant signs a separate agreement and/or has separate facilities.
- Rent Act protected tenants (or an agent of these).
- Housing association or local authority lets.
- Company or trust lets.
- Holiday lets.
- Sale and leaseback arrangements.
- Sub-letting.

---

## SECTION 7 – DEPOSIT CRITERIA

- Savings and investments: acceptable.
- Family gift: acceptable.
- Concessionary purchase: acceptable (minimum 20% concession).
- Raised from another property: acceptable.

---

## SECTION 8 – CAPITAL RAISING AND REMORTGAGE

### Acceptable Purposes

- Debt consolidation: acceptable (maximum £35,000 across a maximum of 5 debts).
- Home improvements: acceptable.
- Repaying a family gift: acceptable.
- Giving a family gift: acceptable.
- Deposit for another property purchase: acceptable.

### Not Acceptable Purposes

- Business purposes.
- Paying a tax bill.

---

## SECTION 9 – CONSUMER BUY TO LET

- Acceptable where the property has been inherited; where the applicant wishes to rent out a property they have previously lived in; or where the applicant intends to let to a family member.

---

## SECTION 10 – EX-PAT BUY TO LET

- Available to ex-pat applicants who are UK citizens or have permanent right to reside and are currently resident abroad.
- Maximum LTV: 70%.
- Available to employed applicants only.
- Minimum income: £40,000 (GBP equivalent, converted using XE.com).
- Must provide a contract of employment.
- Must provide a UK bank statement.
- Rent must be received in GBP into a UK bank account.
- Mortgage payments must be made from a UK bank account in GBP.
- Deposits may come from a non-UK source (e.g. the country of current residence).
- A UK-named individual (family member or solicitor) and their address must be provided for service of notices.

### Eligible Countries

Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Canada, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hong Kong, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Japan, Kingdom of the Netherlands (including the Netherlands Antilles and Aruba), Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, New Zealand, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Singapore, Slovak Republic, Slovenia, South Korea, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, UAE, and United Kingdom (excludes UK Crown dependencies: Isle of Man, Bailiwick of Jersey and Bailiwick of Guernsey).
