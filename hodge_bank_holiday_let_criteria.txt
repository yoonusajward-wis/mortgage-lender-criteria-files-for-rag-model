## SECTION 1 – LOAN PARAMETERS

- Minimum loan: £50,000.
- Maximum loan: £1,500,000.
- Minimum property value: £100,000.
- Maximum property value: £10,000,000.
- Minimum term: 5 years.
- Maximum term: 40 years.
- Maximum LTV (capital and interest): 75%.
- Maximum LTV (interest only): 75%.
- Debt consolidation: allowed up to 75% LTV.
- Repayment types: capital and interest, or interest only.

---

## SECTION 2 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

- Minimum age at application: 21.
- Maximum age at application: 88.
- Maximum 2 applicants.
- Must be UK resident for at least 3 years.
- Must have indefinite leave to remain.
- Limited companies: not accepted — personal applications only.
- No minimum personal income requirement; assessment based solely on rental performance.
- Maximum 4 mortgaged properties in total at completion (including main residence).

---

## SECTION 3 – AFFORDABILITY AND RENTAL ASSESSMENT

- Rental income must cover 140% of stressed mortgage payments.
- Stress calculated on interest only payments at the higher of: product rate + 2% or 5.5%.
- Personal occupancy: up to 90 days per year allowed.

### Rental Income Calculation

- Based on 30 weeks' estimated letting per year.
- Rental income calculated as the average of high-season, mid-season and low-season weekly rent.

---

## SECTION 4 – LOAN PURPOSE

- Purchase of a holiday let.
- Remortgage of an existing holiday let.
- Remortgage of an existing residential property to convert to a holiday let.

---

## SECTION 5 – PROPERTY LOCATION

### Acceptable Locations

- England (including Isle of Wight).
- Wales.
- Scotland, including islands connected by road bridge.

### Unacceptable Locations

- Northern Ireland.
- Isle of Man.
- Channel Islands.
- Any island not accessible by road bridge (Isle of Wight excepted).
- Holiday parks and holiday complexes.
- London: not accepted due to the 90-day letting cap.

---

## SECTION 6 – PROPERTY CRITERIA

### Condition and General Requirements

- Must be in readily lettable condition.
- Allowance made for furnishing, decorating and minor updating.
- Maximum 6 bedrooms.
- Up to 2 kitchens acceptable.
- One fully self-contained annexe acceptable if let on a short-term basis only.

### Minimum Property Values

- Standard: £100,000.
- Above or adjacent to commercial premises: £150,000 (outside London); £250,000 (Greater London).
- Ex-public-sector houses: £150,000.
- Ex-public-sector flats and maisonettes: £250,000.

### Unacceptable Property Types

- Retirement or age-restricted properties.
- Sheltered accommodation.
- Park homes.
- Mobile homes.
- Caravans.
- Houseboats.

### Restrictive Covenants

- Properties with restrictive covenants affecting usage are likely to be unacceptable. Refer if unsure.

---

## SECTION 7 – FLATS AND MAISONETTES

- Maximum 6 storeys as standard.
- Private flats over 6 storeys may be accepted if the developer has signed the Developer Remediation Contract (England) or the Welsh Government equivalent (Wales).
- Flats above the 3rd floor must have a lift (except Scottish tenements).
- Non-standard construction flats: limited to 4 storeys.
- Freehold flats and maisonettes: not acceptable.
- Minimum lease remaining at completion: 85 years.

### Service Charge and Ground Rent

- Service charge: must not exceed 1% of property value.
- Ground rent: must not exceed 0.1% of property value.
- Ground rent escalation must not double more frequently than every 20 years.
- Ground rent must not be linked to property value.

---

## SECTION 8 – NEW BUILD CRITERIA

- New build houses: acceptable.
- New build flats: acceptable once fully completed with all communal areas finished.
- Conversions completed within the last 12 months: acceptable.
- Developer must be registered with the New Homes Ombudsman Service (NHOS).
- If part of a development with more than 3 units, the development must be registered with the New Homes Quality Board (NHQB).
- Must have a suitable structural warranty or Professional Consultant's Certificate (PCC).
- Appropriate warranty/certification required for all new build flats.

### New Build Incentives

- Maximum 5% incentives allowed, including: cashback, stamp duty paid, deposit contributions and legal fee incentives.
- Incentives do not need to be deducted from the valuation or purchase price.
- Incentive value must be matched by the applicant's own funds.

---

## SECTION 9 – EX-PUBLIC-SECTOR PROPERTIES

- Houses: acceptable with minimum value £150,000.
- Flats and maisonettes: acceptable with minimum value £250,000, subject to:
  - No more than 4 storeys.
  - No communal balconies.
  - No open-access walkways.
- Ex-public-sector properties bought under Right to Buy still within the pre-emption period: not acceptable.

---

## SECTION 10 – NON-STANDARD CONSTRUCTION

### Acceptable (With Conditions)

- Single skin or half-brick walls: allowed only in single-storey non-habitable rooms.
- Modern timber-framed properties built after 1950 (Scotland) or 1970 (England and Wales).
- Modern methods of construction (MMC): considered on a case-by-case basis.

### Not Acceptable

- Timber-frame properties where the cavity was retrospectively filled with insulation.

---

## SECTION 11 – ABOVE OR ADJACENT TO COMMERCIAL PREMISES

Considered on a case-by-case basis for:
- Restaurants.
- Takeaways.
- Cafes.
- Pubs and bars.

Minimum property values apply:
- Outside London: £150,000.
- Greater London: £250,000.

---

## SECTION 12 – SCOTTISH TENEMENT FLATS

Acceptable if all of the following apply:
- Value of £100,000 or above (£120,000 or above if above commercial premises).
- Built before 1915.
- Masonry construction with pitched roof.
- Maximum 5 storeys.
- No communal balconies or open-access walkways.
- A Holiday Let licence may be required — must be referred for review.
