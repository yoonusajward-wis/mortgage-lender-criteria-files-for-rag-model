## SECTION 1 – FINANCING PARAMETERS

- Minimum financing: £50,000.
- Maximum financing per BTLPP: £1,000,000.
- Maximum financing on portfolio with StrideUp: £2,000,000.
- Maximum FTV at up to £750,000: 80%.
- Maximum FTV at up to £1,000,000: 70%.
- HMO/MUB: FTV capped at 75%.
- Minimum term: 5 years.
- Maximum term: 30 years.
- Minimum property value: £85,000.
- Maximum property value: uncapped.
- Products are not portable.

## SECTION 2 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

### Age

- Minimum age: 21.
- Maximum age: no maximum unless top-slicing applies.
- If top-slicing applies: applicant must not exceed age 75 at end of term (based on the eldest applicant whose income is used for affordability).

### Number of Applicants

- Maximum 4 applicants per application.

### Residency – Permanent Right to Reside

An applicant has a permanent right to reside if they are:
- A British citizen.
- A British expat residing abroad.
- A citizen of Ireland.
- Holder of pre-settled or settled status through the EU Settlement Scheme.
- Holder of Indefinite Leave to Enter (ILE) or Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) in the UK.
- Holder of a UK spouse or equivalent (long-term) visa where the partner falls into any of the above categories and they are legally married or in a civil partnership.

### Residency – Non-Permanent Right to Reside

Where an applicant does not have permanent rights to reside, all of the following must be met:
- Resident in the UK for the last 24 months (12 months for applicants on the Government's Skilled Workers and Visa list).
- Visa/residency permit with at least 12 months remaining (for skilled worker/visa list applicants).
- Valid passport.

### Crown Employees and Armed Forces

- Crown employees (including members of the diplomatic service working overseas): treated as UK residents.
- Members of the British Armed Forces stationed overseas: considered.

### Expats

- Accepted up to 80% FTV.
- Maximum 2 expatriate applicants (plus up to 4 UK-based applicants in total).
- Applicant must have a UK bank account.
- Proof of UK footprint must be provided.
- Applicant must confirm that if they return to the UK, they do not intend to reside in the security property (unless the property is transferred to a Residential Home Purchase Plan).
- StrideUp requires 3 years' address history regardless of where the applicant(s) have lived.
- Proof of ID and certification accepted from: FCA-authorised UK intermediary, international law or accounting firm, UK Embassy official, bank staff, or a public body/notary.
- If the only available overseas address is a PO Box: supported by employer's letter on company headed paper confirming address, plus last 3 payslips (employed); or proof of residency from accountant (self-employed); or satisfactory evidence via pension statements (retired). Residency cards also acceptable.
- Top slicing is not available for expats.

### Marital Status

- A sole application can be considered even where the applicant is in a legally sanctioned relationship, for both purchases and refinances. Independent Legal Advice (ILA) may be required.

### Guarantors

- Not considered.

## SECTION 3 – RENTAL INCOME AND AFFORDABILITY

- The property must not be used by any applicant or a member of their immediate family.
- Standard BTL: no minimum income requirement (unless top-slicing applies).

### Top Slicing / Rental Top-Up

- Top slicing is available where rental income is insufficient to reach the Rental Coverage Ratio (RCR).
- A minimum RCR of 110% must be met before top slicing is considered.
- Top slicing is not available for portfolio landlords or expats.
- All income requirements for top slicing mirror the HPP Residential Criteria.
- StrideUp will only use ONS data to support expenditure assessment.

**Minimum income for top slicing (first-time buyers only):**
- At least one applicant must have a minimum income of £25,000 (£30,000 for properties in London and the South East).

**Age limit when top-slicing applies:**
- Applicant must not exceed age 75 at end of term.

**Financing into Retirement (top slicing cases):**
- Less than 10 years from retirement: latest pension statement showing forecast pension amount required.
- More than 10 years from retirement: evidence of contributions into a pension fund (including sale of other property and/or buy to let rental income), reviewed on a proportionate basis.

## SECTION 4 – CREDIT HISTORY

### Secured Arrears or Missed Payments

- Maximum 1 missed payment in last 36 months.
- 0 in last 6 months.

### Unsecured Arrears or Missed Payments

- Worst status of 2 in last 24 months.
- Communications arrears up to £250 ignored.

### CCJs

- 0 in last 36 months; none outstanding.

### Defaults

- 0 in last 36 months; none unsatisfied.
- Communications defaults up to £250 ignored.
- 0 secured mortgage defaults in last 5 years.

### Payday Loans

- Maximum 1 payday loan not exceeding £300 in last 36 months.
- 0 in last 12 months.

### Debt Management Plans

- Must have been satisfied at least 24 months before application.
- Must not relate to a credit event in the previous 60 months.

## SECTION 5 – PROPERTY CRITERIA

- England only.
- Minimum unexpired lease: 85 years remaining at date of application.
- Builder's deposit/incentives accepted up to 5%.

### Standard Buy to Let

- Standard residential properties accepted for rental.
- Subject to valuation and standard property criteria.

### Houses in Multiple Occupation (HMOs)

- Accepted up to a maximum of 6 bedrooms.
- Applicant must hold a minimum of 2 years' buy to let experience, or 1 year's experience as a HMO/MUB landlord.
- FTV capped at 75%.
- Top slicing not available for HMOs.

### Multiple Unit Buildings (MUBs)

- Accepted up to a maximum of 5 units on a single freehold title.
- FTV capped at 75%.
- Top slicing not available for MUBs.

## SECTION 6 – DEPOSIT AND EQUITY

- Proof of deposit required in all cases.
- 100% gifted deposit acceptable from any number of sources including friends and family.
- Deposits from overseas accounts considered.

### Acceptable Deposit Sources

- Customer's own funds.
- Remortgage of a property already owned by the customer.
- Gifted deposit.
- Inheritance.
- Directors' funds (when purchasing via an SPV).

### Evidence of Deposit

- Savings from income: latest 6 months' bank statements.
- Sale of current home: Memorandum of Sale and redemption statement from current lender.
- Gift: gifted deposit form from each donor.

## SECTION 7 – APPLICATION TYPES

- First-time buyers: accepted.
  - UK resident first-time buyers: FTV up to 80%, minimum income of £25,000 (£30,000 in London and the South East).
  - Expat first-time buyers: FTV capped at 75%.
- Refinance: accepted.
- Capital raising remortgage/refinance: accepted.

## SECTION 8 – LIMITED COMPANY / SPV APPLICATIONS

- StrideUp accepts BTL applications from private limited companies set up as Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) for property investment purposes only.
- Trading companies will not be accepted.
- No minimum trading period required — newly incorporated SPVs are acceptable.
- SPV applications are subject to the same BTL criteria as individual applications.

### Acceptable SIC Codes

Only SPVs with the following SIC codes will be accepted:
- 68100 – Buying and selling of own real estate.
- 68209 – Other letting and operating of own or leased real estate.
- 68320 – Management of real estate on a fee or contract basis.
- 68201 – Renting and operating of Housing Association real estate.
- No other SIC codes are acceptable, either in addition to the above or on their own. Mixed SIC codes including non-property-related codes are not acceptable.

### Financing (SPV)

- Same FTV limits apply as individual applications:
  - 80% FTV up to £750,000.
  - 70% FTV up to £1,000,000.
  - HMO/MUB: 75% FTV capped.
- Maximum financing per BTLPP: £1,000,000.
- Maximum financing on portfolio with StrideUp: £2,000,000.
- Top slicing available for SPV applications (not available for portfolio landlords or expats).
- All standard BTL property types accepted (HMO and MUB subject to standard criteria).

### Deposit (SPV)

- Directors' funds, gifted deposits from directors, inheritance, remortgage of company-owned property, and company own funds all accepted as deposit sources.
- Proof of deposit required in all cases; directors' funds must be evidenced appropriately.

### Directors and Guarantors

- Directors of the SPV are subject to standard applicant requirements including credit history and residency.
- Guarantors not considered.
- For expat SPV applications: UK bank account required.

### Documentation (SPV)

- Company registration documents.
- Articles of Association.
- Confirmation of SIC codes.
- Company bank statements (if applicable).
- Directors' identification and proof of address.
- Standard BTL documentation (property valuation, rental assessment, application forms).
- Top slicing documentation (if applicable).

## SECTION 9 – DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

*Documentation for income only applies to cases using top-slicing/rental top-up. Standard BTL applications do not require income documentation unless top-slicing is used.*

### Employed Income

- Latest P60.
- 3 most recent payslips.
- Latest bank statement showing salary credit.
- If recently started a new role with no payslips available: copy of contract confirming salary, plus last 3 payslips from previous role.

### Self-Employed Income

- SA302 for last 2 years and Tax Year Overview (or 1 year if trading for less than 2 years), plus 3 months' business bank statements.
- Where 2 years' SA302/Tax Year Overviews cannot be provided: alternative evidence of income stability considered at underwriter discretion.

### Contractor Income

- First-time contracting: confirmation of 24 months' previous employment in the same line of work required where less than 6 months remain on the contract.
- Single contract: 3 months' contracting history; minimum 12 months' history in the same industry; minimum 3 months remaining; confirmation of renewal accepted if next contract not yet available.
- Multiple contracts: treated as sole trader or limited company director; same evidence as above.
- Umbrella company: treated as PAYE; latest P60 and latest 3 payslips required.
- Medical professionals: treated as a single contractor regardless of the number of Trusts; day rates considered; SA302s not required.

### Second Job Income

- 100% of income considered.
- If PAYE: applicant must have been working both primary and second job for at least 6 months.
- Evidence: latest P60 and 3 most recent payslips.

### Other Income Sources

- Child benefit: most recent award letter and most recent bank statement showing the credit.
- Working or Child Tax Credits: most recent award letter and most recent bank statement showing the credit.
- Maintenance: copy of maintenance agreement received under a Court Order and latest bank statement showing the credit.
- Pension income: most recent pension statement (dated within last 12 months) and latest bank statement showing the pension credit.
- Universal Credit: most recent award letter and most recent bank statement showing the credit.
- State disability allowances: latest benefits statement and latest bank statement showing the credit.
- Carer's Allowance: latest award notice.
- Widowed Parents' Attendance: latest award notice.
- Employment and Support Allowance: latest award notice.

### Expenditure

- Latest 3 months' bank statements.
