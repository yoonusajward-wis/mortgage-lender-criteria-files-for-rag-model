## SECTION 1 – LOAN PARAMETERS

- Maximum 2 applicants.
- Minimum loan (Hodge Residential): £50,000.
- Minimum loan (Resi-Retire 50+ and RIO): £20,000.
- Maximum loan: £2,000,000.
- Minimum term: 5 years.
- Maximum term: 40 years.
- RIO: lifetime mortgage with no end date.
- Repayment types: capital and interest, or interest only.

### Maximum Loan by LTV

- Up to £600,000: maximum LTV 95%.
- Up to £850,000: maximum LTV 90%.
- Up to £1,000,000: maximum LTV 85%.
- Up to £2,000,000: maximum LTV 75%.

### LTV Limits by Repayment Type

- 95% LTV: repayment purchase (Resi and Resi-Retire).
- 90% LTV: repayment remortgage (Resi and Resi-Retire).
- 75% LTV: interest only (all products).
- Debt consolidation: allowed; maximum LTV 85% (repayment only).

## SECTION 2 – LOAN PURPOSES

### Permitted

- Purchase of main residence or second home.
- Remortgage with capital raising.
- Debt consolidation.
- Transfer of equity.
- Business injection.
- Payment of taxes.

### Not Permitted

- Clearing gambling debts.
- Purchase of timeshare.
- Right to Buy.
- Shared Equity.
- Shared Ownership.

---

## SECTION 3 – INCOME MULTIPLES

### Hodge Residential

- At 90% LTV: 5x income.
- At 80–90% LTV: 5.5x income.
- At 80% LTV or below: 6x income.
- Interest only: 5x income.

### Hodge Resi-Retire (50+) and RIO

- At 90% LTV: 5x income.
- At 90% LTV or below: 5.5x income.
- Interest only: 5x income.

### Special Rule

- Like-for-like remortgages (£ for £): 6x income regardless of LTV.

## SECTION 4 – INCOME AND AFFORDABILITY

### Employed Income

- 100% of PAYE income used up to the expected retirement date.
- Can be used up to age 80.
- If used past age 70, plausibility of continued employment is assessed.

### Overtime and Commission

- Average of last 12 months used.
- If stable: up to 100%.
- If not stable: 50% or 2-year average.

### Bonus

- Annual bonus: up to 75% of the average of the last 2 payments.
- Monthly bonus: treated as overtime/commission.

### Self-Employed – Sole Traders and Partners

- Minimum 12 months' trading.
- Latest year's figures used if sustainable; projections may be requested.

**Evidence requirements:**
- Trading 2 years or more: 2 years' SA302s and tax year overviews.
- Trading less than 2 years: minimum 1 year's accounts; accountant's projection plus latest 3 months' business bank statements.

**New partners:**
- Replacing an existing partner: 2 years' partnership accounts plus actual drawings.
- Newly created partner: expected percentage of drawings.
- If insufficient evidence: accountant's certificate acceptable.

### Limited Company Directors

- Minimum 12 months' trading.
- Affordability based on salary plus dividends; retained profit considered in certain cases.

**Retained profit allowed if:**
- Borrower(s) own 100% of shares.
- Business has been trading for 2 years or more.
- Retained profit is stable or increasing.
- Accountant confirms funds can be drawn safely.
- Evidence requirements mirror sole trader requirements.

### Contractors

**Day Rate Contractors:**
- Minimum 12 months' contracting.
- Minimum 3 months remaining on contract or evidence of renewal.
- Affordability: 48 weeks × day rate.
- Maximum 3-month gap between contracts in the last year.
- Day-1 contractors: considered with relevant experience and minimum income of £50,000.
- Multiple concurrent contracts may be accepted on a case-by-case basis.

**Fixed-Term Contractors:**
- Employer must pay Tax and National Insurance.
- Minimum 12 months' experience in a similar role.
- Minimum 3 months remaining on contract or evidence of renewal.
- No minimum income.

## SECTION 5 – RETIREMENT INCOME

### State Pension

- Amounts in payment or expected entitlement accepted.

### Pensions in Payment

- 100% accepted (annuities, personal and company pensions).

### UFPLS, Drawdown and Collective Investments

- Income calculated based on projected fund value × drawdown rate.

**Drawdown rate by age:**
- Age 50–59: 3.3–3.9%.
- Age 60–69: 4.2–5.6%.
- Age 70–76: 5.7–7.6%.
- Age 77 and above: 7%.

**Additional rules:**
- Use 100% of projected pot less declared tax-free withdrawal.
- Investments: use 75% of projected pot.

### Surviving Partner Stress Test

- If term extends beyond the 87th birthday of either applicant: affordability tested for each borrower individually.
- Life insurance can be used if it repays the loan.

### Guarantors

- Not permitted.

## SECTION 6 – CREDIT CRITERIA

### Unsecured Arrears

- Maximum 2 months' arrears in last 2 years.
- Must be up to date at application.
- Communications and utilities arrears ignored.

### Secured and Mortgage Arrears

- No arrears in last 6 months.
- Maximum 1 month's arrears in last 12 months.
- Maximum 2 months' arrears in last 2 years.

### Defaults

- Satisfied: up to £500 in last 3 years.
- Unsatisfied: allowed if over 3 years old and total under £250.
- Communications and utilities ignored.

### CCJs

- Satisfied: maximum £500 in last 3 years, or over 3 years old regardless of amount.
- Unsatisfied: under £250 and over 3 years old.
- Communications and utilities: allowed at underwriter discretion.

### Debt Management Plan and IVA

- Acceptable if discharged 3 or more years ago.

### Repossession

- Acceptable if over 6 years ago.

### Bankruptcy and Sequestration

- Acceptable if discharged 6 or more years ago.

---

## SECTION 7 – RESIDENCY CRITERIA

### Foreign Nationals

- With Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR): treated as UK nationals.
- Without ILR: acceptable on the following visas:
  - Global Talent Visa (minimum 18 months' evidence).
  - Skilled Worker Visa (permanent employment).
  - Health and Care Visa.
  - Spousal Visa (joint applications only).
  - Maximum LTV: 90%.
  - Minimum 2 years' UK residency.
  - Minimum 2 years' UK credit history.

### EU Settlement Scheme and Irish Nationals

- Minimum 2 years' UK residency.
- Must be a UK taxpayer.
- Must have 2 years' UK credit history.

### Returning Expats

Accepted from day 1 if:
- Returning to permanent employment or retired.
- Residing in the UK at time of application.
- UK credit conduct identifiable.
- Maximum time abroad: 2 years.

If abroad more than 2 years, or self-employed, or no UK credit file:
- Minimum 6 months' UK residency required before application.

## SECTION 8 – INTEREST ONLY

- Maximum LTV: 75% (including debt consolidation).
- No minimum income requirement for interest only.

### Acceptable Repayment Strategies

- Sale of mortgaged property (subject to regional equity minimums below).
- Sale of another property (equity must fully cover the loan).
- Realisation of investments.
- Existing endowment (mid-growth projection).
- Pension lump sum: 25% of midpoint projected maturity value.

### Minimum Equity for Sale of Mortgaged Property

- Scotland (excluding Edinburgh and Dundee) and North East: £100,000.
- North West, Yorkshire and Humber, and Wales: £120,000.
- Rest of UK: £150,000.
- London: £250,000.

---

## SECTION 9 – RETIREMENT INTEREST ONLY (RIO)

- Minimum age: 50.
- Maximum age: 88.
- Repayment type: interest only.
- No repayment vehicle required.
- Loan repaid on death or move into long-term care.
- Lifetime mortgage — no end date.
- Maximum LTV: 75%.
- Minimum property value: £100,000 (no specific minimum equity requirement).
- Borrower must demonstrate ability to maintain payments for life.
- Joint cases: affordability assessed on each applicant individually in the event of one borrower's death.
- Life insurance proceeds may be considered.

---

## SECTION 10 – PROPERTY CRITERIA

### Property Values

- Minimum property value: £100,000.
- Maximum property value: £10,000,000.
- At 95% LTV: minimum property value £300,000.

### Acceptable Locations

- England (including Isle of Wight).
- Wales.
- Scotland (including road-bridge connected islands).

### Unacceptable Locations

- Northern Ireland.
- Isle of Man.
- Channel Islands.
- Any island without a road bridge (Isle of Wight excepted).
- Holiday parks and complexes.

### Unacceptable Property Types

- Retirement, sheltered or age-restricted properties.
- Park homes.
- Mobile homes.
- Caravans.
- Houseboats.

---

## SECTION 11 – PROPERTY FEATURES

### Bedrooms

- Up to 6 bedrooms; more than 6 by referral.

### Kitchens

- Up to 2 kitchens acceptable.

### Annexes

- One self-contained annexe acceptable if let on a short-term basis only.

### Non-Standard Construction

- Single-skin walls: accepted only in single-storey non-habitable rooms.
- Modern timber-frame: accepted (post-1950 in Scotland; post-1970 in England and Wales).
- Timber-frame with retrospectively filled cavities: not accepted.
- Modern methods of construction (MMC): considered on a case-by-case basis.

### Above or Adjacent to Commercial Premises

- Restaurants, takeaways, cafes and pubs may be considered.
- Minimum property value: £150,000 (outside London); £250,000 (London).

---

## SECTION 12 – FLATS AND MAISONETTES

- Maximum 6 storeys as standard.
- Over 6 storeys: accepted only if developer has signed the Developer Remediation Contract (England or Welsh Government equivalent).
- Flats above the 3rd floor: building must have a lift (except Scottish tenements).
- Non-standard construction flats: maximum 4 storeys.
- Freehold flats and maisonettes: not accepted.
- Minimum lease remaining at completion: 85 years.
- New build flats: acceptable when fully complete with appropriate warranty; not allowed at 95% LTV.

### Service Charge and Ground Rent

- Service charge: must not exceed 1% of property value.
- Ground rent: must not exceed 0.1% of property value.
- Ground rent must not double more frequently than every 20 years.
- Ground rent must not be linked to property value.

### Scottish Tenement Flats

Acceptable if all of the following apply:
- Minimum value £100,000 (£120,000 if above commercial premises).
- Built before 1915.
- Masonry construction with pitched roof.
- Maximum 5 storeys.
- No communal balconies or open-access walkways.

---

## SECTION 13 – NEW BUILD

- Houses and flats built or converted within the last 12 months: acceptable.
- Developer must be registered with the New Homes Ombudsman Service (NHOS).
- Must have a structural warranty or Professional Consultant's Certificate (PCC).
- Developments of more than 3 units: must be registered with the New Homes Quality Board (NHQB).
- Not available at 95% LTV.

### New Build Incentives

- Maximum 5% incentives allowed, including: cashback, Stamp Duty Land Tax contribution, deposit gift and legal fees.
- Incentives do not reduce the valuation or purchase price.
- Incentive value must be matched by the borrower's own funds.

---

## SECTION 14 – EX-PUBLIC-SECTOR PROPERTIES

- Houses: acceptable with minimum value £150,000.
- Flats and maisonettes: acceptable with minimum value £250,000, subject to:
  - Maximum 4 storeys.
  - No communal balconies.
  - No open-access walkways.
- Not allowed at 95% LTV.
- Properties still within the Right to Buy pre-emption period: not acceptable.
