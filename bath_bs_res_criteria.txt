BATH BUILDING SOCIETY - RESIDENTIAL LENDING CRITERIA
====================================================

Source: https://www.bathbuildingsociety.co.uk/intermediaries/browse-general-information/residential-lending-criteria/
Last Updated: Information updated daily via Criteria Hub

================================================================================
LOAN DETAILS
================================================================================

Minimum Loan Size:
- £50,000 for standard residential applications
- Minimum Loan Size Retirement Interest Only (RIO): £50,000

Maximum Loan Size:
- £1,000,000 for standard residential applications
- £300,000 for first time buyers (subject to product availability and LTV)
- Maximum Loan Size Retirement Interest Only (RIO): £500,000

Minimum Property Value:
- £100,000 for standard residential applications
- Minimum Property Value for Retirement Interest Only (RIO): £100,000

Maximum LTV:
- Houses/Bungalows (Standard): 95% LTV
- Houses/Bungalows (New Build): 95% LTV
- Flats/Maisonettes (Standard): 95% LTV
- Flats/Maisonettes (New Build): 95% LTV
- Retirement Interest Only (RIO) Balance Swap: 50% LTV
- Retirement Interest Only (RIO) Capital Raising: 50% LTV
- Holiday/Second Home: 80% maximum LTV (we will consider it on a case-by-case basis as a 2nd property for the customer's own use up to a maximum of 80% LTV)
- Home for Dependant: 80% maximum LTV (Bath Building Society does lend on properties where the property usage is home for a dependant. Up to 80% LTV)
- Right to Buy: 100% of discounted purchase price (see Right to Buy section below for full details)

Loan Term:
- Minimum Term: 5 years
- Maximum Term: 40 years (for standard residential mortgages)
- Maximum Term Retirement Interest Only (RIO): No Maximum (mortgage repayment date can be when the borrower dies or goes into care)
- Self Build Maximum Term: 40 years

Repayment Methods:
- Capital Repayment (Capital & Interest): Acceptable
- Interest Only: Acceptable
- Retirement Interest Only (RIO): Available

================================================================================
APPLICANT CRITERIA
================================================================================

Age Limits:
- Minimum Age at Application: 18 years
- Maximum Age at Point of Application: 79 years (unless on the RIO product)
- Maximum Age at End of Mortgage Term: 85 years
- Maximum Age at End of Term for Non Contributory Applicant: 85 years
- Maximum Age at Term End Without Pension Proof: 70 years (proof of pension income will be required after age 70 or the applicant's stated retirement age, whichever is lower)
- Maximum Age for Self Employed Borrowers: 85 years (maximum retirement age is earlier of 70 or stated retirement age, beyond this retirement income will be assessed for affordability)
- Interest Only Maximum Age at End of Term: 85 years (for the oldest applicant)

Retirement Interest Only (RIO):
- Minimum Age at Application: 55 years
- Maximum Age at Application: No Maximum
- Maximum Age at Term End: No Maximum (mortgage repayment date can be when the borrower dies or goes into care)

Residency:
- Minimum UK Address History: 36 months (Bath Building Society requires applicants to have been residents in the UK for at least 36 months)
- Applicant Not on Electoral Register: Acceptable (subject to an explanation for not being on the electoral register and acceptable proof of address)

EU Nationals:
- EU Nationals with Pre-Settled Status: Acceptable (can potentially lend to EU Nationals with a 'pre-settled status' under the EU Settlement Scheme)
- EU Nationals with Settled Status: Acceptable (can potentially lend to EU Nationals with a 'settled status' under the EU Settlement Scheme)

Foreign Nationals:
- Foreign Nationals (Living Overseas): Not Acceptable (does not lend to Foreign Nationals living overseas)
- Foreign Nationals (WITHOUT indefinite leave to remain): Not Acceptable (does not accept applications from foreign nationals that DO NOT HAVE indefinite leave to remain)
- Foreign Nationals (with indefinite leave to remain): Acceptable (can consider applications from foreign nationals WITH indefinite leave to remain)

Expat Residential Mortgage:
- Not Acceptable (does not accept residential applications from expat applicants)

Visas:
- Family Visa (Spousal etc): Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that hold a family visa)
- Minimum Number Months Remaining on Visa: Not Applicable (we do not lend to foreign nationals on a visa - Bath Building Society will not consider an applicant on a visa)
- Working Visa (Tier 1 - Investors, entrepreneurs and talent visas): Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that hold a 'Tier 1' working visa)
- Working Visa (Tier 2 - Long-term work visas): Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that hold a 'Tier 2' working visa)

Advisor Own Application:
- Acceptable - Bath Building Society will accept applications from advisors applying for their own mortgage

Maximum Number of Applicants:
- Maximum: 4 applicants allowed

Maximum Number of Applicants Income Used for Affordability:
- Maximum: 2 applicants (further income can be used at underwriter discretion)

Credit Reference Agencies:
- Number of Credit Reference Agencies Used: 1 (we underwrite all cases manually)
- Credit Scoring: Manual underwriting (all cases are manually underwritten)

Joint Borrower Sole Proprietor (JBSP):
- Acceptable (now treated as JBSP - guarantors are now treated as JBSP)
- Joint Application Sole Occupier: Acceptable (can potentially accept applications on a joint application sole occupier basis - where one applicant will NOT reside in the property)
- Joint Application Sole Proprietor: Acceptable (can accept applications on a joint application sole proprietor basis - where only ONE applicant will OWN the property)
- Sole Application Joint Proprietor: Not Acceptable (will not consider applications on a sole application joint proprietor basis - where a property will be jointly owned but only one person will be named on the mortgage)

Guarantors:
- Acceptable (now treated as JBSP)
- Reverse Guarantor (younger applicant guarantor for older applicant): Acceptable (can potentially consider a reverse guarantor application)
- Where Applicant Significant Income Growth is Not Expected: Acceptable (can potentially consider a guarantor application where significant income growth is not expected)

First Time Buyers:
- Maximum Loan Size: £300,000 (subject to product availability and LTV)
- [Additional first time buyer specific criteria - see standard criteria which applies]

AFFORDABILITY ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

Items That Can Be Ignored:
- Additional Voluntary Pension Contributions: Can Be Ignored
- Charitable Donations: Can Be Ignored
- Company Pension Contributions: Can Be Ignored
- Private Healthcare Payments: Can Be Ignored
- Sharesave Schemes: Can Be Ignored
- Non Dependant Household Occupants: Can Be Ignored

Items That Are NOT Ignored (Included in Affordability):
- Child Care Vouchers: Not Ignored (will be included when assessing borrowers affordability)
- Maintenance Payments: Not Ignored (will be included when assessing borrowers affordability)
- School Fees: Not Ignored (will be included when assessing borrowers affordability)
- Season Ticket Loans: Not Ignored (will be included when assessing borrowers affordability)
- Student Loans: Not Ignored (will be included when assessing borrowers affordability)
- Personal Borrowing for Self Employed Business Purposes: Not Ignored (will be included when assessing borrowers affordability)

Assessment of Additional Residential Mortgages:
- Monthly Payment or Outstanding Balance: Monthly Payment (Bath Building Society will use the monthly payment of any additional residential mortgages when assessing affordability as opposed to the outstanding balance. We will stress this monthly payment at 1.2% above their current rate)

INCOME TYPES
================================================================================

Employment Income:
- Additional Duty Hours: Acceptable (can potentially consider Additional Duty Hours as an acceptable source of income. We will use 100% if guaranteed and 50% if regular but non guaranteed)
- Additional Responsibility Allowance: Acceptable (can potentially accept additional responsibility allowance as an income type. Can accept if the employer confirms this is guaranteed and permanent)
- Bonus (Annual): Acceptable (can potentially accept annual bonus as an income type. We will use 100% of guaranteed bonus/commission and 50% of regular but non guaranteed bonus/commission)
- Bonus (Half Yearly): Acceptable (can potentially accept half yearly bonus as an income type. We will use 100% of guaranteed bonus/commission and 50% of regular but non guaranteed bonus/commission)
- Bonus (Monthly): Acceptable (can potentially accept monthly bonus as an income type. We will use 100% of guaranteed bonus/commission and 50% of regular but not guaranteed bonus/commission)
- Bonus (Quarterly): Acceptable (can potentially accept quarterly bonus as an income type. We will accept 100% of guaranteed bonus/commission or 50% of regular but not guaranteed bonus/commission)
- Car Allowance: Acceptable (acceptable to Bath Building Society. We use 100% of car allowance)
- Commission (Annual): Acceptable (can accept commission as an income type. We will accept 100% of guaranteed bonus/commission. 100% of average 2 years commission can be used where >2yr track record (3 required for credit repair) 50% if <2 yr track record, of annual figure, or latest 3 months)
- Commission (Half Yearly): Acceptable (can accept commission as an income type. We will accept 100% of guaranteed bonus/commission. 100% of average 2 years commission can be used where >2yr track record (3 required for credit repair) 50% if <2 yr track record, of annual figure, or latest 3 months)
- Commission (Monthly): Acceptable (can potentially accept monthly commission as an income type. We will accept 100% of guaranteed bonus/commission. 100% of average 2 years commission can be used where >2yr track record (3 required for credit repair) 50% if <2 yr track record, of annual figure, or latest 3 months)
- Commission (Quarterly): Acceptable (can accept commission as an income type. We will accept 100% of guaranteed bonus/commission. 100% of average 2 years commission can be used where >2yr track record (3 required for credit repair) 50% if <2 yr track record, of annual figure, or latest 3 months)
- Employers Housing Allowance: Not Acceptable (does not accept employers housing allowance as an income type)
- Employers Mortgage Subsidy: Acceptable (does accept employers mortgage subsidy as an income type)
- Employment Income from Family Business: Acceptable (can potentially accept employment income from a Family Business as an income type. Acceptable subject to company accountant confirming the income. Pay slips would need to match entries into their bank account. The society may also request full accounts)
- Flight Pay: Acceptable (can potentially consider Flight Pay as an acceptable source of income - please refer to lender)
- Foreign Currency: Not Acceptable (does not accept foreign currency as an income type)
- Large Town Allowance: Acceptable (acceptable to Bath Building Society)
- London Weighting: Acceptable (can potentially accept London weighting as an income type)
- Mileage Allowance: Not Acceptable (does not accept mileage allowance as an acceptable source of income for affordability purposes)
- Overtime (less than monthly): Acceptable (can potentially accept overtime (less than monthly) as an income type. We will accept 100% of guaranteed overtime and 50% of regular but non guaranteed overtime)
- Overtime (weekly or monthly): Acceptable (can potentially accept weekly or monthly overtime as an income type. We will accept 100% of guaranteed overtime and 50% of regular but non guaranteed overtime)
- Parental/Maternity leave pay (back to work salary): Acceptable (can potentially accept parental/maternity leave pay (back to work salary) as an income type. We can accept 100% of normal pay, subject to confirmation from employer regarding return to work salary. Will also need to see a level of savings in the background to support any shortfall of income throughout maternity)
- Salary (paid cash in hand): Not Acceptable (does not accept salary paid in cash as an income type)
- Second Job: Acceptable (up to 100% of income from a second job can be considered subject to sustainability by underwriting)
- Shift Allowance: Acceptable (can potentially accept shift allowance as an income type. We will use 100% if guaranteed, or 50% if regular but non guaranteed)
- Standby / Call Out: Acceptable (can potentially consider Standby / Call Out as an acceptable source of income - refer to lender)
- Supply Teacher: Acceptable (can potentially accept Supply Teacher income - refer to lender. No set criteria on this)
- Tronc Payments (Tips etc): Not Acceptable (does not consider tronc payments (tips etc) when assessing affordability)

Employment Requirements:
- Minimum Length of Time Employed: 12 months (12 months continuous employment required)
- Minimum Length of Time in Current Job: 1 month (at least the first month's pay must be evidenced in the bank and first month's payslip. We do not accept income from applicants currently in probation, and the applicant must have 12 months continuous employment history)
- Probationary Period: Acceptable (subject to condition within offer that probation has completed prior to drawdown)
- Pending New Job Salary Accepted: Acceptable (subject to confirmation from employer and payslip prior to completion)
- Pending Pay Rise Accepted: Acceptable (subject to confirmation from employer and payslip prior to completion)
- Employed with Voluntary Breaks: Not Acceptable (will not consider income from an applicant who takes unpaid prolonged breaks from employment with their existing employer)
- Zero Hours Contract Minimum Time in Position: 36 months (we will accept zero hours contracts providing they have a 3 year track record and they are still employed at the time of application)

Self-Employed Income:
- Net Profit (for sole traders and partnerships): Acceptable (can potentially accept net profits for sole traders and partnerships when assessing income. Must evidence 3 years accounts)
- Limited Company Directors (Salary and Dividends): Acceptable (can potentially accept salary and dividends as the primary income used when assessing income from directors of a limited company. We require a minimum of 3 years accounts completed)
- Limited Company Directors (Salary and Net Profits): Acceptable (can accept salary and net profits as the primary income used when assessing income from directors of a limited company. Must demonstrate at least 3 years accounts)
- Limited Company Directors (Salary and Share of Net Profits AFTER CORPORATION TAX): Acceptable (can potentially accept salary and share of net profits AFTER CORPORATION TAX as the primary income used when assessing income from directors of a limited company)
- Limited Company Directors (Salary and Share of Net Profits BEFORE CORPORATION TAX): Not Acceptable (does not use salary and share of net profits BEFORE CORPORATION TAX as the primary income used when assessing income from directors of a limited company)
- Limited Company Retained Profits: Acceptable (can potentially accept retained profits as an acceptable source of income when assessing affordability)
- Directors Loan Payback: Not Acceptable (does not accept payback of a directors loan as an income that can be used in our affordability assessment)

Self-Employed Assessment Requirements:
- Amount of Shareholding to be Considered Self Employed: 20% (20% shareholding required to be considered self-employed)
- Accounts or Accountant Certificates where Accountant is not Chartered or Certified: Not Acceptable (does not accept accounts or accountants certificates from accountants that are not either 'Chartered' or 'Certified')
- Can Use Projection as Latest Year: No (does not accept a projection for the latest years self employed figures for affordability purposes)
- Declining Profits: Acceptable (can potentially accept income from self employed applicants where profits have declined year on year. The last 3 years profit should be used to justify income. Where profits are declining, this can be acceptable subject to Underwriter's discretion and a fully documented justification)
- Latest Years Self Employed Figures for Affordability: Acceptable (can use the latest years self employed figures where justified by a trend. We will consider 2 years figures to justify lending on the latest year only. Contact your BDM)

Contractor Income:
- CIS Contractor: Acceptable (can potentially accept CIS contractors as an income type. Would be considered a sole trader and subject to standard policy)
- Fixed Term Contract: Acceptable (we will consider fix term contracts where there is 24 months to run or a track record of 24 months and have at least 6 months to run)
- IT Consultant: Acceptable (can potentially accept IT Consultant as an income type. Would need evidence of 3 years earnings as an IT Consultant)
- Locum Doctor: Acceptable (can potentially accept Locum Doctor as an income type. We would treat as self employed, using an average of the last 2 years figures)
- Sub Contractor: Acceptable (can potentially accept sub contractors as an income type. Would need evidence of 3 years of earnings)
- Zero Hours Contract: Acceptable (can potentially accept zero hours contracts as an income type. Must have evidence of 3 years earnings)

Contractor Income (Not Acceptable):
- Agency Work: Not Acceptable (does not accept agency work as an income type)
- Nursing Bank: Not Acceptable (does not accept nursing bank as an income type)
- Piecework Contract: Not Acceptable (does not accept piecework contracts as an income type)
- Probationary Contract: Not Acceptable (does not accept probationary contracts as an income type)
- Rolling Contract: Not Acceptable (does not accept income from a rolling contract)
- Seasonal Contract: Not Acceptable (does not accept seasonal contracts as an income type)
- Short Term Renewable Contract: Not Acceptable (does not accept short term renewable contracts as an income type)
- Temporary Contract: Not Acceptable (does not accept temporary contracts as an income type)
- Umbrella Company: Not Acceptable (will not consider contractor income that is paid via an umbrella company)

Contractor Assessment Requirements:
- CIS Contractor Assessment: Treated as Self Employed (can potentially use income from CIS contractors but would treat the applicants as self-employed so would take an average on the last 2 years income)
- Contractors Minimum Experience Requirement: 24 months (fixed term contracts required a minimum 24 months track record with a minimum of 6 months remaining)
- Contractors Weekly Multiplier for Income Assessment: Treated as Self Employed (we would use an average of the last 2 years)
- Contractors Minimum Income Requirement: £20,000 (Bath Building Society allows contractors, subject to 24 month history and minimum 6 months left to run)
- Contractors Minimum Time Remaining: 6 months (Bath Building Society will consider contractors with 6 months to run. A 24 month contract history must be provided)

Other Income:
- Lodger Income: Acceptable (can accept lodger income as an income type providing it is shown on tax returns)
- Maintenance (through the courts): Acceptable (100% used, subject to evidence it will continue for at least 5 years)
- Maintenance (not through the courts): Acceptable (can consider maintenance not through the courts provided it is guaranteed for a minimum of 5 years and a CMS letter is evidenced. If there is a long standing informal arrangement and the child's age means it is viable to continue at least 5 years then we may consider the income as an exception but please refer on this basis at DIP)
- Pension Income (Company): Acceptable (can potentially consider Company Pension as an acceptable source of income)
- Pension Income (War Disablement): Acceptable (can potentially accept pension (war disablement) as an income type)
- Pension Income (Private Pension): Acceptable (can potentially accept private pension as an income type)
- Pension Income (Self Administered Drawdown Pension): Acceptable (can potentially consider self administered drawdown pension income. We will use 4% of the pension pot)
- Pension Income (Self-invested Personal Pension - SIPPs): Acceptable (can potentially accept income from a Self-invested Personal Pension (SIPPs))
- Pension Income (State Pension): Acceptable (can potentially accept state pension as an income type)
- Pension Credit: Not Acceptable (does not accept pension credits as an income type)
- Seafarer Income: Not Acceptable (will not consider Seafarer income as an allowable income type)
- Stipend: Not Acceptable (does not accept stipends as an income type)
- Income Protection/Permanent Health Insurance: Not Acceptable (will not accept Income protection/Permanent Health Insurance as an income type)

ADVERSE CREDIT CRITERIA
================================================================================

CCJs (County Court Judgments):
- CCJs registered in last 3 Months: Not Acceptable
- CCJs registered between 3 to 12 Months: Not Acceptable (Bath Building Society will not consider applicants that have had CCJs registered more than 3 months ago and less than 12 months ago)
- CCJs registered between 12 to 24 Months: Potentially Acceptable
  * 95% LTV: Not acceptable
  * 80% LTV (Standard residential): Maximum £500 registered in last 3 years, satisfied at application
  * 70% LTV (Credit repair): Maximum £5,000 registered over 2 years
- CCJs registered between 24 to 36 Months: Potentially Acceptable (Available up to 70% LTV - Maximum £5,000 registered over 2 years)
- CCJs registered over 36 Months ago: Potentially Ignored (Available up to 70% LTV - Maximum £5,000 registered over 2 years)

Arrears:
- Secured/Mortgage Arrears: Acceptable
  * 80% to 95% LTV: None accepted in the last 3 years
  * 70% to 80% LTV: Maximum status 1 in the last 2 years and must be satisfied for at least 6 months
  * Up to 70% LTV (Credit repair): Maximum status 2 in the last 2 years, and must be satisfied for at least 3 months
- Unsecured Arrears: Acceptable
  * 80% to 95% LTV: None accepted in the last 3 years
  * 70% to 80% LTV: Maximum status 1 in the last 2 years (Utilities & mobiles ignored up to £250)
  * Up to 70% LTV (Credit repair): Maximum status 2 in the last 2 years (Utilities & mobiles ignored up to £500)
  * Note: Must be currently up to date on the credit search

Arrangement to Pay:
- Acceptable (Bath Building Society can potentially consider applicants that have an arrangement to pay registered on their credit file. Applicants would have to be clear for the last 12 months)

Defaults:
- Defaults registered in last 3 Months: Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that have had Defaults registered in the past 3 months)
- Defaults registered between 3 to 12 Months: Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that have had Defaults registered more than 3 months ago and less than 12 months ago)
- Defaults registered between 12 to 24 Months: Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that have had Defaults registered more than 12 months ago and less than 24 months ago)
- Defaults registered between 24 to 36 Months: Potentially Acceptable (Available up to 70% LTV - Maximum £5,000 registered over 2 years)
- Defaults registered over 36 Months ago: Not Ignored (Available up to 80% LTV when satisfied 2 years. Available up to 70% LTV - Maximum £5,000 registered over 2 years)
- Maximum Number of Defaults (Satisfied): 6 (80% to 95% LTV none accepted in the last 6 years. 70% to 80% LTV, Satisfied over 2 years. Up to 70% LTV, Maximum £5,000 registered over 2 years ago and still not satisfied. Subject to underwriter review. For any cases with multiple instances of adverse or other policy exceptions, please discuss with your BDM before placing the case with us)
- Maximum Number of Defaults (Unsatisfied): 6 (Available up to 70% LTV - Maximum £5,000 registered over 2 years)
- Maximum Value of Defaults (Satisfied): No Maximum (80% to 95% LTV none accepted in the last 6 years. 70% to 80% LTV, Satisfied over 2 years, up to 70% LTV, Maximum £5,000 registered over 2 years ago and still not satisfied)
- Maximum Value of Defaults (Unsatisfied): £5,000 (Available up to 70% LTV - Maximum £5,000 registered over 2 years)

IVAs (Individual Voluntary Arrangements):
- Acceptable (80% to 95% LTV satisfied for 6 years. 70% to 80% LTV satisfied over 3 years, up to 70% LTV (Credit repair) satisfied for 2 years)
- Years Satisfied: 3 years minimum (80% to 95% LTV satisfied for 6 years. 70% to 80% LTV satisfied over 3 years, up to 70% LTV (Credit repair) satisfied for 2 years)
- Years Since Registered: 3 years minimum (80% to 95% LTV satisfied for 6 years. 70% to 80% LTV satisfied over 3 years, up to 70% LTV (Credit repair) satisfied for 2 years)
- Note: An IVA usually stays on the credit file for 6 years from the date it was REGISTERED not from the date it was satisfied

Debt Management Plans:
- Satisfied: Acceptable (80% to 95% LTV none accepted in the last 6 years. 70% to 80% LTV, must be satisfied for over 2 years, up to 70% LTV (Credit repair), must be satisfied for over 1 year)
- Current/Unsatisfied: Not Acceptable (does not accept applicants who have a current/unsatisfied debt management plan)

Debt Relief Orders:
- Satisfied: Acceptable (80% to 95% LTV none accepted in the last 6 years. 70% to 80% LTV, must be satisfied for over 2 years, up to 70% LTV, must be satisfied for over 1 year)
- Note: None within the last 12 months

Discharged Bankrupt:
- Acceptable (80% to 95% LTV satisfied for 6 years. 70% to 80% LTV satisfied over 3 years, up to 70% LTV (Credit repair) satisfied for 2 years)

Previously Repossessed:
- Not Acceptable (does not accept applicants that have been previously repossessed)

Late Payments (Not Missed):
- Acceptable (can potentially accept applicants with previous 'Late Payments'. Maximum of 2 within the last 24 months)

Payday Loans:
- Previous: Acceptable (can accept applicants with previous payday loans as long as all payday loans have been satisfied for 12 months)
- Current: Not Acceptable (does not accept applicants with a current payday loan)

Debt Related:
- Debt Being Repaid Fully or Partially Ignored: Yes (can ignore debts being repaid, subject to proof of funds)
- Expiring Debt Can Be Partially or Fully Ignored: Yes (anything ending within 6 months or under can be ignored)

Financial Associates with Adverse:
- Not Acceptable (will not accept applicants that are financially associated with persons that have adverse credit)

Maximum Debt to Income Ratio:
- No Maximum (we don't have a maximum as long as it fits income multipliers and affordability)

Gambling (Recurring Transactions):
- Not Acceptable (will not consider applicants that have recurring gambling transactions)

CIFAS:
- Historic CIFAS Record: Not Acceptable (does not accept applicants that are named on the CIFAS register)

Criminal Convictions:
- Not Acceptable (does not accept applicants that have criminal convictions)

Power of Attorney:
- Acceptable (can potentially consider an applicant from someone acting as a power of attorney)

Politically Exposed Persons:
- Not Acceptable (will not consider an applicant that is classed as a politically exposed person)

BACK TO BACK TRANSACTIONS
================================================================================

Back to Back Remortgages:
- Customer Funded: Acceptable (subject to policy - Remortgages within 6 months of acquisition will be acceptable subject to: LTV based on original purchase price unless evidence of refurbishment or conversion is seen; The title must be fully registered with no pending applications; There must be a plausible explanation for the transaction)
- Inherited: Acceptable (subject to same policy as above)
- Bridging: Acceptable (subject to same policy as above)
- Ported: Acceptable (subject to same policy as above)
- Other: Acceptable (subject to same policy as above)

Back to Back Purchases:
- Part Exchange: Acceptable (Bath Building Society will accept back to back transactions on this basis)
- Repossessed: Acceptable (Bath Building Society can potentially accept back to back purchases of this nature)
- Other: Not Acceptable (Bath Building Society does not accept back to back purchases of this nature)

Day 1 Remortgages:
- Day 1 Remortgage (NOT inherited property): Acceptable (remortgages within 6 months of acquisition will be acceptable subject to: LTV based on original purchase price unless evidence of refurbishment or conversion is seen; The title must be fully registered with no pending applications before an application is made to us; There must be a plausible explanation for the transaction)
- Day 1 Remortgage (if inherited property): Acceptable (subject to same policy as above)

Non Simultaneous Sale and Purchase:
- Acceptable (can potentially accept non simultaneous sale and purchase. Subject to circumstance, please refer)

CONCESSIONARY PURCHASES
================================================================================

- Concessionary Purchase (Family): Acceptable (providing the correct amount of stamp duty is paid)
- Concessionary Purchase (Landlord): Acceptable (Bath Building Society can potentially accept this type of concessionary purchase - please refer the case with full details, agreed on a case by case basis)
- Concessionary Purchase Where Giftor Will Remain: Not Acceptable (Bath Building Society does not accept the situation where it is a concessionary purchase where the giftor will remain an occupant of the property)

- Enhanced Terms Where Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) Exists: Yes (can potentially offer better lending terms where a 'Lasting Power of Attorney' exists. We offer a better interest rate when lasting power of attorney is in place)

- Investment Income: Not Acceptable (won't consider investment income as part of its ongoing affordability assessment)
- Life Cover: Not Acceptable (won't consider projected proceeds of a life policy as part of its ongoing affordability assessment)
