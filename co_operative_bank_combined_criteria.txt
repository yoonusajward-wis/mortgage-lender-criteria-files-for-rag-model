# CO-OPERATIVE BANK – LENDING CRITERIA (RESIDENTIAL & BUY TO LET)

---

**LENDER:** The Co-operative Bank
**PRODUCT:** Combined (Residential & Buy to Let)
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Lending Criteria
**LAST UPDATED:** June 2025
**FOR INTERMEDIARY USE ONLY**

**Source:** [Lending criteria](https://www.intermediary.co-operativebank.co.uk/lending-criteria/)

This document contains comprehensive lending criteria for The Co-operative Bank. All criteria apply to mortgage applications unless otherwise stated. For professional Mortgage Advisors only.

---

## SECTION 1 – ACCEPTABLE EMPLOYED INCOME CRITERIA

In calculating employed income, we will use:

* 100% of basic income
* 100% of any contractual allowance such as London Weighting or shift allowance
* 50% of regular additional payments (overtime, bonus, commission allowances)
* 50% of income from interest or UK listed company dividends
* We do not use expenses/reimbursements in the income calculation

## SECTION 2 – ACCEPTABLE INCOME

* Income from employment/self-employment
* Income from trust funds
* Pension income
* Second and other jobs (see Additional Income section)
* Investment or dividend income
* Rental Income
* Maintenance
* Allowances confirmed by the DWP
* Tax Credits
* Stipend income
* We do not accept income derived from foster care

## SECTION 3 – ACCESS

Access provides secured mortgages to applicants with a history of credit issues, based on demonstrated evidence of recent strong credit performance.

The following criteria applies:

* Maximum loan size is £1,000,000
* Maximum LTV is 85% for purchase and remortgage only (replacing the existing mortgage £ for £). For all other loan purposes the maximum LTV is 80%
* Interest only maximum LTV is 65%LTV. This can be increased to 75% LTV where the extra borrowing over 65% LTV is on a repayment basis
* CCJs are acceptable when over 36 months old. If less than or equal to 36 months old, the total value of CCJs must be <= £300. There is no distinction between satisfied and unsatisfied CCJs
* Defaults are acceptable when over 36 months old. If less than or equal to 36 months old the total value of defaults must be <=£1,000. There is no distinction between satisfied and unsatisfied defaults
* Maximum number of defaults is 5 (satisfied or unsatisfied)
* No missed payments in the last 6 months
* Bankruptcy or Sequestration discharged more than 3 years ago will be considered
* Individual voluntary arrangement (IVA) or a Trust Deed (TD) which ended more than 3 years ago will be considered
* Applicants deemed to have high indebtedness will be declined
* Access offering is only applicable to residential mortgages

Standard criteria applies to all other elements of the Access product. The time since CCJ/Default/Bankruptcy/IVA occurred is based on when the credit search is completed.

## SECTION 4 – ACREAGE

Normal lending terms apply to properties up to ten acres, providing the applicant does not intend to carry out a business from the property.

Properties with agricultural restrictions, smallholdings/crofts, farms, commercial elements or significant non-residential use are not acceptable.

If neighbouring land is in the same ownership of the applicant or close family member and there are contiguous land considerations this is not considered acceptable.

## SECTION 5 – ADDITIONAL INCOME

The following additional income may be used:

* 100% Maintenance income can be taken into consideration, providing this is evidenced by the latest 3 months bank statements showing maintenance credits which must show payments have been received in each of the last three months or a court order or CSA/Child Maintenance Service(CMS) documentation
* 50% of income from trust funds providing they are evidenced via an accountant or latest years SA302's and Tax year overview (most recent proof of income can be no more than 18 months old)
* 50% of investment or dividend income providing this is evidenced via an accountant or latest years SA302's and Tax year overview (most recent proof of income can be no more than 18 months old)
* 100% of net profit from property rental with a minimum of 2 years figure where this can be evidenced by an accountant's certificate or latest SA302 and tax year overview, the most recent years figure will be utilised. A deduction will be made for outgoing buy to let mortgage payments, where applicable, and may require review by an underwriter. This could result in a change in the lending decision
* 100% of Stipend income can be taken into consideration, stipend income is acceptable as a supplementary income. The applicant must have a minimum contract of 12 months with 6 months unexpired and stipend cannot be the only form of income on an application. Stipend can be evidenced via a letter (to the customer) from the stipend supplier which details the length of the stipend and the income being received and supported with a bank statement evidencing the most recent stipend income credit
* 100% of Disability Living Allowance, Personal Independence Payment or Employment Support Allowance (ESA) confirmed as permanent by the Department for Work and Pensions(DWP)
* 50% of Tax credits providing these are confirmed via HM Revenues and Custom (HMRC) documentation
* 100% of pension income where the applicants are already retired. The following income evidence is acceptable: latest payslip, latest P60, latest annual pension notification or latest bank statement showing pension payment

## SECTION 6 – ADVERSE CREDIT – MAINSTREAM PRODUCTS

We will not consider any applicant who:

* Is bankrupt, has bankruptcy proceedings pending, or who is registered on the Scottish Debt Arrangement Scheme
* Has been discharged from bankruptcy within the last 6 years
* Has an individual voluntary arrangement (IVA) or a Trust Deed (TD) or Administration Order, recorded during the last 6 years
* Has an unsatisfied CCJ
* Has a satisfied CCJs recorded during the last 6 years where the total value of the judgment is more than £100. The Scottish equivalent is a Decree (DEC) and the same rules apply
* Has any Defaults(s) during the last 6 years totalling more than £500
* Has any type of repossession

Please refer to the Access product section for criteria details.

## SECTION 7 – AFFORDABILITY (RESIDENTIAL ONLY)

All applications are subject to affordability assessment. The model uses the combination of total gross annual income with tax and national insurance applied, credit commitments, other significant expenditure, council tax, basic expenditure, stressed mortgage payment and Help to Buy loan (where applicable) to calculate affordability. The result is then used to ascertain whether or not the loan is affordable.

## SECTION 8 – AGRICULTURAL RESTRICTIONS

We will not lend on any property with an agricultural restriction.

## SECTION 9 – ANNEXES

Property with annexes will be considered on residential loans as long as the immediate family of the borrower will occupy, and it is suitable for the type of security.

## SECTION 10 – APPLICANT

The maximum number of applicants is two who, for Residential mortgages, should both be occupying the property on completion.

## SECTION 11 – NUMBER OF APPLICANTS

The maximum number of applicants is two who, for Residential mortgages, should both be occupying the property on completion.

## SECTION 12 – BACK TO BACK TRANSACTIONS

We will not lend where the property is subject to a back to back transaction. Our definition of back to back is a property purchased by the vendor within the last 6 months.

## SECTION 13 – BUILDERS INCENTIVES

For new build properties, builder incentives may be acceptable subject to valuation and standard new build criteria. Cash incentives up to 5% of purchase price may be acceptable. Non-cash incentives (fixed/fitted items including white goods) are accepted without affecting the loan amount. See New Build section for full details.

## SECTION 14 – BANK STATEMENTS

Bank statements evidence may be required to support an application. Internet bank statements are acceptable (for non-ID purposes). Where statements are also required for ID purposes, these should comply with the Bank's standard ID requirements guidelines.

## SECTION 15 – BENEFIT INCOME

The following additional income may be used:

* 100% of Disability Living Allowance, Personal Independence Payment or Employment Support Allowance (ESA) confirmed as permanent by the Department for Work and Pensions(DWP)

## SECTION 16 – BUY TO LET – AFFORDABILITY

A residential affordability assessment will be completed on a BTL application factoring in the applicant's credit commitments and expenditure.

Key criteria:
* Rental income must cover the stressed mortgage payment at a minimum of 120% ICR (Interest Coverage Ratio)
* Personal income may be utilised to cover shortfall in rental income (subject to criteria)
* See Income Coverage Ratio (ICR) – Buy to Let section for detailed ICR requirements

## SECTION 17 – BUY TO LET – INCOME

For Buy to Let applications, the minimum household income is £25,000.

Rental income assessment:
* Rental income must cover the stressed mortgage payment at the required ICR
* Personal income may be used to cover shortfall in rental income (top slicing)
* See Buy to Let - Affordability section for full details

## SECTION 18 – BUY TO LET – KEY CRITERIA

Key criteria for Buy to Let applications:

* Minimum age: 21 at application
* Maximum age: 75 at expiry of mortgage term
* Minimum time in current employment: 6 months
* Minimum trading period for self-employment: 2 years
* Maximum 3 BTL properties (including application security)
* Must be currently owner occupier for 6 months
* Minimum household income: £25,000
* See Buy to Let section in detailed criteria for complete requirements

## SECTION 19 – BUY TO LET – LOAN PURPOSE

We allow BTL loans for:
* Purchase of a BTL property where the borrower, or a close relation has solely a commercial interest
* Remortgage of a BTL property where the borrower has not lived in the property in the last 2 years
* Remortgage of a residential property onto a buy to let (with evidence of new residential mortgage offer)

## SECTION 20 – BUY TO LET – LOAN SIZE AND PURPOSE

Loan Size:
* Minimum loan: £25,001
* Maximum loan: £750,000
* Maximum portfolio exposure: 3 Buy to Let properties totalling £1,500,000
* If applicant also has main residential property with the bank, total exposure must not exceed £3,750,000

Loan Purpose:
See Buy to Let - Loan Purpose section for acceptable purposes.

## SECTION 21 – BUY TO LET – LTV BY LOAN SIZE

* Up to £350,000: 75%
* £350,001 to £750,000: 65%

By purpose:
* Purchase: 75%
* Remortgage only (replacing existing mortgage £ for £): 75%
* Home improvements (to subject property): 75%
* Remortgage capital raising debt consolidation: 70%
* Remortgage capital raising (excluding business/speculative purposes): 70%

## SECTION 22 – BUY TO LET – PORTFOLIO LANDLORDS

We can only lend to portfolio landlords if they are requesting a remortgage with no additional borrowing.

Portfolio landlord definition: Borrowers with four or more mortgaged BTL properties (including any BTLs held in a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV), and for joint applications, all mortgages are considered whether held together or separately).

Limits:
* Maximum 3 BTL properties with Clydesdale Bank
* Maximum total borrowing: £1,000,000 (£1,500,000 if properties in London/South East)
* Maximum 8 properties allowed in total portfolio

## SECTION 23 – BUY TO LET – PROPERTY

Property Requirements:
* Property should be let on a single (AST) Assured Shorthold Tenancy agreement or Private Residential Tenancy (PRT) in Scotland
* We do not lend on HMO's (3 or more tenants sharing facilities)
* Must ensure buildings insurance is suitable for Buy to Let property
* Multi let properties are not acceptable
* We do not offer BTL loans on property in Northern Ireland
* Maximum LTV on new build: 75%
* Tenant must not be family member or previous owner
* Maximum 25% exposure in any block of flats
* Property must be suitable for immediate letting
* EPC rating must be E or above (MEES compliance required)

## SECTION 24 – BONUSES

We will use 50% of regular bonus payments as allowable income. See Acceptable Employed Income Criteria for details on how bonuses are calculated.

## SECTION 25 – CHILD MAINTENANCE

100% Maintenance income can be taken into consideration, providing this is evidenced by the latest 3 months bank statements showing maintenance credits which must show payments have been received in each of the last three months or a court order or CSA/Child Maintenance Service(CMS) documentation.

## SECTION 26 – CHILD TAX CREDITS

We will accept 50% of Tax credits providing these are confirmed via HMRC documentation.

## SECTION 27 – COMMISSION

We will use 50% of regular commission payments as allowable income.

Where customer's income is made up of more than 50% commission, we can use 100% of the commission income (plus basic salary). To use 100% of the commission income we will require the last 2 years commission and salary income figures. The overall commission and salary needs to be static or increasing year on year. If the overall income is declining we may not use it. We will use an average of the last 2 years income figures or the latest income figure if that is lower.

Evidence will need to be provided with P60's and last 3 months payslips. All applications using commission at 100% will be referred to the underwriters and further information or evidence could be requested.

## SECTION 28 – CONSTRUCTION TYPES

We will only lend on property that is built of standard construction as advised by the valuer. We will lend on:

* Fully repaired PRC properties with brick external skin and suitable guarantees from a licensed scheme
* Laing Easy form
* Concrete No Fines
* Cross wall construction
* Timber frame with brick exterior
* Standard roof construction (this includes thatch)
* Standard flat roof construction

Modern Methods of Construction (MMC) will be considered with the minimum following requirements:

* Accredited certification with a minimum 60-years design life assessment through Builtoffsite Property Assurance Scheme (BOPAS), British Board of Agrément (BBA), Building Research Establishment (BRE) or NHBC Accepts
* Acceptable new build warranty (in line with the Banks approved list)/ if built in the last 10 years, Professional consultant certificate is not suitable for MMC
* Meets the Bank's overall guidance and RICS requirements
* Proposed security has the necessary Building and Planning regulation in place
* Proposed security is suitable for mainstream lending
* Standard building insurance available
* MMC is subject to the valuers assessment of the saleability and demand

## SECTION 29 – COUNTY COURT JUDGEMENTS (CCJ)

We will not consider any applicant who:
* Has an unsatisfied CCJ
* Has a satisfied CCJs recorded during the last 6 years where the total value of the judgment is more than £100. The Scottish equivalent is a Decree (DEC) and the same rules apply

Please refer to the Access product section for criteria details on acceptable CCJs.

## SECTION 30 – CREDIT HISTORY

A credit search against all UK addresses occupied by each of the applicants over a period of 3 years prior to the date of the application will be carried out by us. In addition a credit search will be carried out on any undisclosed addresses.

We will not consider any applicant who:
* Is bankrupt, has bankruptcy proceedings pending, or who is registered on the Scottish Debt Arrangement Scheme
* Has been discharged from bankruptcy within the last 6 years
* Has an individual voluntary arrangement (IVA) or a Trust Deed (TD) or Administration Order, recorded during the last 6 years
* Has an unsatisfied CCJ
* Has a satisfied CCJs recorded during the last 6 years where the total value of the judgment is more than £100
* Has any Default(s) registered during the last 6 years totalling more than £500
* Has any type of repossession

Please refer to the Access product section for criteria details.

## SECTION 31 – CREDIT SCORING

All applications must pass the Bank's credit score.

## SECTION 32 – CRIMINAL CONVICTIONS

Applications from applicants with criminal convictions will be assessed on a case-by-case basis. Serious criminal convictions may result in application decline. Please contact us to discuss individual cases before applying.

## SECTION 33 – DEBT CONSOLIDATION

Debt consolidation is acceptable subject to standard LTV limits.

Residential:
* Maximum LTV: 85% for capital raising debt consolidation
* Maximum LTV: 80% for debt consolidation on remortgage
* For such applications there is no requirement to input the monthly payments as expenditure items for the financial commitments being consolidated

Buy to Let:
* Remortgage capital raising debt consolidation: 70% LTV
* See LTV by Loan Purpose section for full details

## SECTION 34 – DIRECT DEBIT MANDATES

Payment by Direct Debit mandate is compulsory for all mortgages. Payments can be taken from an account on any day of the month between the 1st and the 28th. We cannot issue a mortgage offer until a fully completed Direct Debit mandate has been submitted. If no date is stated your payment will be taken on the 1st of the month.

If the client will have more than one mortgage product i.e. part and part, then we will require a Direct Debit mandate for each mortgage product.

## SECTION 35 – DIRECTORS/LIMITED COMPANIES

For directors of limited companies, income assessment is as follows:

Shareholding:
* If the combined shareholding of all applicants is under 25% of a limited company → treat as employed
* If 25% or more → treat as self-employed

Income used:
* For directors of limited companies: the applicant's salary and dividends
* Use 2-year average of (share of net profit after tax + director's salary)
* If the most recent year is lower, use that lower figure

Eligibility:
* Business must have traded for 2 years
* Most recent accounts' year-end must be within 21 months and not overdue
* Usually must show profit in each of the last 2 years and no negative net current assets or shareholders' funds in the latest year

See Self-Employed section for full details and documentation requirements.

## SECTION 36 – DEPENDANTS

For the purpose of affordability, a dependent is defined as someone not named on the mortgage but who is financially supported by a party to the mortgage.

## SECTION 37 – DEPOSIT

See Source of Deposit section.

## SECTION 38 – DEPOSIT – GIFT

Gifted deposits from family members (spouse, partner, parent, sibling, child, grandparent, aunt/uncle, nephew/niece) are acceptable.

For all house purchase applications where the deposit is being gifted, a letter or the Third Party Use of Personal Information / Gifted Deposit form must be obtained from the person, or persons, gifting the deposit to confirm that the deposit monies are a gift, and the person does not expect to be repaid these monies either whilst the borrower(s) owns the property or upon its sale.

Where the deposit is being loaned by a family member, consideration must be given to the repayment schedule, and this must be included in the expenditure in the application.

## SECTION 39 – DEPOSIT – PROOF

For all house purchase applications, the source of the deposit must be captured and evidenced. The deposit should be provided from the applicant's own funds or from acceptable sources (see Deposit - Source section).

Evidence required:
* Bank statements showing deposit funds
* Gift letter if deposit is gifted (see Deposit - Gift section)
* Evidence of deposit source for all applications

## SECTION 40 – DEPOSIT – SOURCE

For all house purchase applications, the source of the deposit must be captured. The deposit should be provided from the applicant's own funds.

Acceptable sources:
* Applicant's own funds
* Gifted deposits from family members (spouse, partner, parent, sibling, child, grandparent, aunt/uncle, nephew/niece)
* Forces Help to Buy Scheme (for Armed Forces members)

Not acceptable:
* Funds withdrawn from a business
* Loans, credit cards and other forms of unsecured credit
* Vendor gifted deposits
* Rotating Savings and Credit Associations (ROSCA)

## SECTION 41 – EMPLOYMENT

The applicant's position must be permanent. Fixed term contracts are acceptable where the applicant has a minimum contract of 12 months with 6 months unexpired. We will decline any application where someone is employed on a casual or seasonal basis. The applicants must not be under notice of termination or redundancy. We do not accept applicants who are paid in cash.

## SECTION 42 – MINIMUM TIME IN EMPLOYMENT

For employed applicant(s) there is no minimum time in employment.

## SECTION 43 – EX-PATS

We do not offer mortgages for expatriates. If the applicant(s) are not subject to UK or Guernsey tax, then they are not acceptable.

## SECTION 44 – FAMILY PURCHASE

Applications where the applicant is purchasing a property from a family member are acceptable subject to the following:

* There must be an existing associated relationship with the vendor
* For LTV, use the lower of the purchase price and the valuation (the discounted price). Both figures must be included in the application
* We can consider up to 100% of the purchase price, subject to standard credit requirements
* The solicitor/conveyancer must:
  - Confirm the gifting family member is not insolvent, and
  - Arrange indemnity insurance for the full property value if necessary
* Related-party purchase: separate legal representation is required — the same solicitor cannot act for both buyer and seller

See Undervalue Transactions section for full details.

## SECTION 45 – FIRST TIME BUYER

Our definition of a first time buyer is an applicant who has never acquired an interest in property anywhere in the world.

## SECTION 46 – FIRST TIME BUYERS – DEFINITION

Our definition of a first time buyer is an applicant who has never acquired an interest in property anywhere in the world.

First time buyers are accepted for both Residential and Buy to Let mortgages. There is no requirement to be an owner occupier for Buy to Let applications.

## SECTION 47 – FIXED TERM CONTRACTORS

Fixed term contracts are acceptable where the applicant has a minimum contract of 12 months with 6 months unexpired.

For contractors:
* Fixed term, renewable, and seasonal employment contracts are acceptable if they meet the minimum contract requirements
* Fixed-term: 2 years continuous employment and ≥ 12 months left on contract
* Renewable: Term ≥ 6 months and renewed at least once
* Seasonal/temporary short-term: Not acceptable

See Contractors section for full details on contractor income assessment.

## SECTION 48 – FLATS

LTV Guidelines:
* ≤ 4 storeys: up to 95% LTV (Residential)
* ≥ 5 storeys: up to 85% LTV (Residential)
* Ex-LA/HA/MOD: up to 85% LTV (Residential)
* New-build flats: up to 80% LTV (Residential), 75% LTV (Buy to Let)

Types:
* Converted flats treated as new-build until 2 years after conversion or first occupation (later of)
* Studios acceptable if ≥ 30 m²
* Maisonettes treated as flats

Multi-Storey Requirements:
* Flats on 5th storey+ must have lift access
* Storey count includes all floors
* Ex-LA/HA/MOD: not in buildings over 7 storeys
* Deck/balcony access subject to valuer

Commercial Proximity:
* May be acceptable if commercial use does not affect demand or enjoyment
* Entrance must not be via commercial premises
* Flats above/adjacent to food/drink or dry-cleaning/launderette premises usually unacceptable

Tenure:
* Flats must be leasehold (or heritable/leasehold in Scotland)
* Share of freehold acceptable if underlying lease is acceptable

## SECTION 49 – FORCES HELP TO BUY

Forces Help to Buy Scheme (for Armed Forces members) is acceptable as a source of deposit.

The scheme allows Armed Forces members to borrow up to 50% of their annual salary (up to a maximum of £25,000) interest-free to use as a deposit.

Treated as savings for deposit purposes. The loan and insurance must be included in expenditure (loan under liabilities, insurance under expenditure).

## SECTION 50 – FOREIGN CURRENCY

All income must be UK sterling denominated for new customers. Foreign currency income is permitted for existing customers in line with prevailing financial crime policy.

We are unable to consider using any non-sterling income (whether this is regular or additional / bonus income). We are unable to consider any non-sterling repayment vehicle for interest only mortgages. We are unable to lend to any individual who is not resident in the United Kingdom.

## SECTION 51 – FOREIGN CURRENCY INCOME

All income must be UK sterling denominated for new customers. Foreign currency income is permitted for existing customers in line with prevailing financial crime policy.

## SECTION 52 – FOSTER CARE

We do not accept income derived from foster care.

## SECTION 53 – NON UK NATIONALS

All borrowers must have right to live and work in the UK.

The mortgage applicant(s) must usually be resident in the UK at the time of application and at least one of the applicants must be permanently residing or planning to reside in the property when the mortgage completes. We do not offer mortgages for ex patriots.

If the applicant(s) are not subject to UK tax, then they are not acceptable.

Where the applicant is not a UK national (excluding Republic of Ireland) then the application may be acceptable where all of the following apply:

* Applicant(s) is subject to UK tax laws
* Applicant(s) is not a Foreign Diplomat or Embassy Staff
* Applicant(s) has lived in the UK for a minimum of 3 years
* Applicant(s) has proof of indefinite leave to remain in the UK

For clarity indefinite leave to remain can be evidenced by the following means:
* Indefinite leave stamp in passport
* An unexpired Biometric Residence Permit showing 'indefinite' rights to remain (this includes 'indefinite leave to remain', 'indefinite leave to enter' or 'no time limit', this is also classed as "settlement")
* Letter from the Home Office confirming indefinite right to remain in the UK
* Immigration status share code so we can confirm the settled or pre-settled status.

## SECTION 57 – GROUND RENT

## SECTION 58 – HELP TO BUY

The Bank participates in the UK Government Help to Buy Shared Equity Schemes in London, England and Wales for residential purchase applications.

How it works:
* An equity loan allows a customer to buy a new build home with just a 5% deposit
* Government lends up to 40% of property value in London and 20% in England and Wales
* The mortgage makes up the remaining funds
* The equity loan will be registered as a 2nd Charge

Eligibility Criteria:
* Applicants cannot own or have an interest in any other property
* The new property is for owner occupation only (cannot sublet)
* Property must be New Build and covered by Bank's approved New Build warranty providers
* Builder/Developer must be participating in the Help to Buy Scheme
* Minimum applicant deposit: 5% of purchase price (excluding builder incentives)
* Maximum loan the Bank can consider: 75%LTV
* Bank to have first charge on the whole property
* Minimum lease term: 250 years (houses), 125 years (flats)
* Maximum Standard Mortgage term on capital and interest basis only

Affordability Assessment:
* Takes into account the interest only payments to the equity loan (payable after year 5) at flat interest rate of 3%
* Maximum LTI multiplier: 4.49

Equity Loan Repayment:
* Can be repaid at any time
* Minimum payment: 10% of property's market value at the time
* Further Advance only considered to purchase increased share in property (staircasing)
* Subject to HCA approval and satisfactory Bank valuation
* Minimum Further Advance: 10% of property's market value at application, or £5,000 if lower

See Staircasing - Help to Buy section for details on further advances.

## SECTION 59 – HMO (HOUSES IN MULTIPLE OCCUPATION)

We do not lend on HMO's (3 or more tenants sharing facilities) for Buy to Let applications.

An HMO is a property with 3 or more tenants forming more than 1 household who share a toilet, bathroom or kitchen. A household is either a single person or a family (including unmarried partners).

## SECTION 60 – ID REQUIREMENTS

Bank statements evidence may be required to support an application. Internet bank statements are acceptable (for non-ID purposes). Where statements are also required for ID purposes, these should comply with the Bank's standard ID requirements guidelines.

For identification purposes, applicants must provide acceptable proof of identity and address in accordance with the Bank's standard requirements.

INCOME COVERAGE RATIO (ICR) – BUY TO LET
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Rental income must cover the stressed mortgage payment at a minimum of 120% ICR (Interest Coverage Ratio).

Interest Coverage Ratios (ICR):
* Standard rate tax payer (20%): 128% ICR
* Higher/Additional rate tax payer (40% & 45%): 145% ICR
* Exception: Higher/Additional rate tax payer BTL remortgage replacing existing mortgage £ for £: 128% ICR

Personal income may be utilised to cover shortfall in rental income (subject to criteria). See Buy to Let - Affordability section for full details.

## SECTION 61 – INCOME MULTIPLE

For applications with a LTV less than or equal to 85% (incorporating any fees added) a maximum multiplier of 5.5 can be considered.

Enhanced multipliers:
* 5.5 x income multiplier:
  - LTV ≤85%
  - Gross annual income: ≥£75,000 combined
* 5 x income multiplier:
  - LTV ≤85%
  - Gross annual income: ≥£45,000 combined

Applications that do not meet the above criteria would be considered for the maximum 4.49 multiplier.

Professional mortgages: Up to 6 x income multiplier available for eligible professionals.

## SECTION 62 – INCOME PROOF – EMPLOYED

We require full details (including start and end dates) of all employment held within the last 6 months.

Conditions:
* The applicant's position must be permanent
* Fixed term contracts are acceptable where the applicant has a minimum contract of 12 months with 6 months unexpired
* We will decline any application where someone is employed on a casual or seasonal basis
* The applicants must not be under notice of termination or redundancy
* We do not accept applicants who are paid in cash

Required documentation:
* The latest 3 month's computerised pay slips (hand written documents will not be accepted)
* OR an employer's reference or employment contract (dated within the last 12 months) PLUS the applicant's personal bank statement for the latest full month

## SECTION 63 – INCOME PROOF – SELF-EMPLOYED

Minimum trading period: 2 years

Required documentation:
* Last 2 years tax assessments (SA 302 AND Tax Year Overview)
* OR Certificate from a suitably qualified accountant with 2 years figures PLUS the customer's personal bank statement

The proof of income figures provided must include the last financial year-end and this must be no more than 18 months old.

See Self-Employed - Accountants Qualifications section for acceptable accountant qualifications.

## SECTION 64 – INTEREST-ONLY

Interest only is available on new borrowing:
* Maximum LTV: 85% where any part of the loan is interest only
* Interest only maximum LTV: 75%LTV (can be increased to 85% LTV with extra borrowing on repayment basis)
* Minimum income requirements for residential interest only:
  - Single: £60,000
  - Joint: £80,000

All repayment strategies must be in sterling. Provide full details with the application. See Repayment Strategies section for acceptable types and evidence requirements.

## SECTION 65 – IVA

We will not consider any applicant who has an individual voluntary arrangement (IVA) or a Trust Deed (TD) or Administration Order, recorded during the last 6 years.

Please refer to the Access product section for criteria details on acceptable IVAs (which ended more than 3 years ago).

## SECTION 66 – JAPANESE KNOTWEED

Properties affected by Japanese Knotweed will be assessed on a case-by-case basis. Specialist reports may be required.

Japanese Knotweed is categorised as:
* Category A: Causing visible damage to the property
* Category B: Impacting the amenity space of the property
* Category C: Within the boundaries but not impacting the amenity space of the property
* Category D: On a neighbouring property or land

For categories A & B, the surveyor will usually request a report by a PCA or ISSE registered firm. All recommended remedial works must be undertaken and covered by an insurance-backed 10-year guarantee. Funding for the works must be ring-fenced.

For categories C & D, we are usually able to lend with no further requirements.

2. THE APPLICANT

### 2.1 CREDIT SCORING

All applications are subject to affordability assessment. The model uses the combination of:
* Total gross annual income with tax and national insurance applied
* Credit commitments
* Other significant expenditure
* Council tax
* Basic expenditure
* Stressed mortgage payment
* Help to Buy loan (where applicable)

The result is then used to ascertain whether or not the loan is affordable. The maximum loan available will be calculated based on both the affordability results and the product/LTV criteria available.

For the purpose of affordability, a dependent is defined as someone not named on the mortgage but who is financially supported by a party to the mortgage.

We do not offer mortgages for expatriates. If the applicant(s) are not subject to UK or Guernsey tax, then they are not acceptable.

Where the applicant is not a UK national (excluding Republic of Ireland) then the application may be acceptable where all of the following apply:
* Applicant(s) is subject to UK or Guernsey tax laws
* Applicant(s) is not a Foreign Diplomat or Embassy Staff
* Applicant(s) has lived in the UK for a minimum of 3 years

Our definition of a first time buyer is an applicant who has never acquired an interest in property anywhere in the world.

A credit search against all UK addresses occupied by each of the applicants over a period of 3 years prior to the date of the application will be carried out by us. In addition a credit search will be carried out on any undisclosed addresses.

* Has any Default(s) registered during the last 6 years totalling more than £500
* Has any type of repossession

7. HELP TO BUY – SHARED EQUITY LOAN SCHEME
==========================================


### 7.1 SCHEME OVERVIEW

The Bank participates in the UK Government Help to Buy Shared Equity Schemes in London, England and Wales for residential purchase applications.

How it works:
* An equity loan allows a customer to buy a new build home with just a 5% deposit
* Government lends up to 40% of property value in London and 20% in England and Wales
* The mortgage makes up the remaining funds
* The equity loan will be registered as a 2nd Charge


### 7.2 ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

* Applicants cannot own or have an interest in any other property
* The new property is for owner occupation only (cannot sublet)
* Property must be New Build and covered by Bank's approved New Build warranty providers
* Builder/Developer must be participating in the Help to Buy Scheme
* Minimum applicant deposit: 5% of purchase price (excluding builder incentives)
* Maximum loan the Bank can consider: 75%LTV
* Bank to have first charge on the whole property
* Minimum lease term: 250 years (houses), 125 years (flats)
* Maximum Standard Mortgage term on capital and interest basis only


### 7.3 AFFORDABILITY ASSESSMENT

* Takes into account the interest only payments to the equity loan (payable after year 5) at flat interest rate of 3%
* Maximum LTI multiplier: 4.49


### 7.4 EQUITY LOAN REPAYMENT

* Can be repaid at any time
* Minimum payment: 10% of property's market value at the time
* Further Advance only considered to purchase increased share in property (staircasing)
* Subject to HCA approval and satisfactory Bank valuation
* Minimum Further Advance: 10% of property's market value at application, or £5,000 if lower
