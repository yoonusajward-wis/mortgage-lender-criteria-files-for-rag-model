## 1. APPLICATION

### APPLICANTS
- Maximum Number of Applicants: 4 (per application)
- Changing Applicant Order: Go to applicant summary table at bottom of personal details screen, use ↓ and ↑ arrows to amend order, select 'Submit' button when done
- Removing Applicant: Go to applicant summary table, select 'Delete' button on right-hand side of applicant to remove, select 'Submit' button when done
- Adding Applicant: Select 'Add another applicant' button on personal details screen, complete new applicant details, select 'Save applicant' button, select 'Submit' button when done
- Important: Must not change any information in name, date of birth, or other personal details sections from one applicant to another
- Questions: Speak to Business Development Manager before making any changes to an application

### DECISION IN PRINCIPLE
- Provides credit decision using minimal information
- States how much can be lent
- Valid for: 3 months (subject to valuation and information supplied being correct)

### DIRECT DEBIT DETAILS
- Required: Details of personal current account where mortgage will be paid from (at application stage)
- Business current accounts: Cannot be used
- Direct Debit mandate form: Not required to complete, but account number and sort code needed to issue mortgage offer

### DOCUMENTATION NEEDED
- Must obtain proof of income, deposit, gross monthly rent, and identification from everyone named on mortgage before keying application
- Must retain on file the following documentation for all BTL and LTB applications and submit upon request:

Employed:
- Latest Payslip
- If commission/overtime/bonus received: Latest 3 monthly or 6 weekly payslips averaged and annualised

Self-Employed:
- Latest 2 years SA302 tax calculation and tax year overviews
- Full accounts
- Latest year must not be older than 18 months from date of application

Retired:
- Latest bank statement showing gross pension credited
- Latest annual statement
- Latest P60

Profit from UK Land and Property (for Employed, Self-Employed and Retired):
- Up to latest 2 years SA302 tax calculations and tax year overviews if available
- Required for all customers who have existing BTL properties and rental income declared on application
- Latest year must not be older than 18 months old

Gross Monthly Rent (the amount paid by the tenant):
- Required for subject property, for all cases: Either of the following must be kept on file:
  * Tenancy Agreement – current, in date and signed by tenants, landlord or letting agent
  * Letter from Letting Agent – confirming named parties, current tenancy term and monthly rent
- May also request for some cases:
  * SA105 Rental Property Form
  * Latest 3 months bank statements
  * Evidence of gross rent for other properties owned by the client (if needed, will ask before case completes)
- All evidenced income sources must be recorded on the application
- Applicants may have different sources of income from employed PAYE, Self-Employment, Profit from UK Land and Property, Pension, and Gross Monthly Rental Income - all need to be recorded separately and accurately on application form, with evidence held on file, and submitted upon request

### FINDERS' FEES
- Not Acceptable: Any application involving a fee paid to a third party (such as an investment club) for finding or introducing a buyer
- Acceptable: Normal fee or commission payable to an estate agent handling the sale

### FIRST MONTHLY PAYMENT
- Collection: Always collected in the month following completion of mortgage (e.g., if completes in June, first payment collected in July)
- Payment Date: Normally collected on customer's chosen monthly payment date
- Exception: If mortgage completes in last few days of month and there are fewer than four working days until customer's chosen payment date, first payment will be collected on 10th of that month, then revert to customer's chosen payment date on following month
- Initial Interest: First monthly payment includes amount of 'initial interest' from day of completion to end of that month, plus first monthly mortgage payment (e.g., if completion is on 15th, interest calculated from 15th to end of month and added to first payment)
- Notification: Will write to all customers to tell them when monthly payments will be collected and that first payment might be different to normal monthly payments

### FREE CONVEYANCING
- Available: Free conveyancing for remortgaging to BM Solutions available on most remortgage products
- Service: Where customer uses remortgage service, BM Solutions will pay conveyancer's legal fees and any registration fees
- Customer Pays: Any fees existing lender might charge for repaying current mortgage, including existing lender's conveyancer's fees if existing lender doesn't allow BM Solutions conveyancer to act for them too
- Own Conveyancer: If customer chooses product without free conveyancing or uses own conveyancer, conveyancer must be on BM Solutions panel, customer must pay any legal charges, search fees, and registration fees or other costs even if they don't go ahead with remortgage, insurance search won't be available
- Additional Costs: May include:
  * Transfer of equity
  * Purchasing an additional tranche
  * Additional TTs where a cashback is involved
  * Second charges
  * Possessory title
  * Unregistered land
  * Leasehold properties
- Mortgage-Free Properties: Remortgage service also available for customers raising capital on mortgage-free property

### NORTHERN IRISH CUSTOMERS
- Northern Irish law: Solicitors appointed by lender can't provide any additional services to customer
- Additional Services: If customer needs any additional services, they'll need to instruct another solicitor

### SCOTTISH PROPERTIES - CLOSURE OF SASINE REGISTER
- Closure Date: General Register of Sasine closed to standard securities on 01 April 2016
- Affects: Remortgaging of Scottish properties on old Sasine register
- Service: Will cover standard costs for transferring from Sasine register to Land Registry during conveyancing part of remortgage service, wherever possible
- Complex Transactions: Won't cover all transactions if considered too complex - conveyancer should discuss additional fees with customer
- Complex Transaction Examples (not limited to):
  * Plans report shows either overlap or shortfall between legal and occupational extent
  * Title is complex with split-off writs, exceptions, and additions that need full examination to draft security and obtain relevant deed plan
  * Property is flat or part of tenement block with common parts where obtaining neighbouring titles would be needed
  * Rural properties where copies of neighbouring titles are needed to check servitudes/access rights etc.
- Costs: Specific costs associated with complex transactions will vary, panel firms will apply hourly rate and agree everything with customers before starting any complex transaction

### PROOF OF RESIDENTIAL PURCHASE - LET TO BUY
- Requirement: All let to buy applications must involve a related residential purchase
- Acceptable: Applications where customers are intending to rent out their current residential home, so they can buy another residential home
- New Residential Mortgage: If customer is taking new residential mortgage when purchasing new home, need to see copy of new residential offer before can issue LTB offer
- No Mortgage/Unable to Issue Offer: If customers are buying new property without mortgage or new residential lender is unable to issue offer, need letter addressed to BM Solutions from conveyancer dated within three months of application to confirm:
  * Future residential address
  * Purchase price
  * If property will or won't have a mortgage
- Offer Issue: Can't issue let to buy offer until received everything needed

## 2. CUSTOMER

### ID & ADDRESS VERIFICATION
- Requirement: All customers must be fully and properly identified as part of mortgage application process
- Recording: Accurate information must be recorded on application system, including confirmation of whether this is face-to-face or non-face-to-face application
- Electronic Verification: If customer doesn't pass electronic verification checks, need to certify and retain evidence of customer's identification documents
- Document Acceptance: Documents you can accept depend on whether you've met with customer in person, or remotely by telephone or video

### SINGLE ID & V
- Use: For face-to-face verification, where customer is physically present and documentation is taken and verified, one document from single identification list can be used to verify customer
- Cannot Use: Single ID can't be used if customer isn't present when ID&V is collected (includes video interviews) - in this case, use standard ID&V process
- Single ID & V Documents:
  * UK photo card driving licence, showing customer's current address
  * UK passport
  * Some EU/EEA identity cards
  * Some non-UK passports
- Former Names: These documents can be accepted in former name with evidence of name change (marriage certificate, Deed Poll or Decree Absolute)

### STANDARD ID & V
- Use: Must be followed when customer isn't present when ID&V is obtained, or customer doesn't have single ID document
- Requirement: Customer will need to produce two separate documents - must be originals and can only be used once in process for either address verification or identification
- FCA Requirement: FCA registered firms must make sure any documentation relied upon to verify customer's identify is copied, certified, and retained in accordance with money laundering regulations. BM Solutions reserves right to contact you and request copies of identity documents at any time during life of mortgage and for up to five years after cessation

Standard ID&V Documents:
- UK passport: Identification: Yes | Address: No
- UK photocard driving licence displaying customer's current address: Identification: Yes | Address: Yes
- UK paper full driving licence (issued up to 30th June 1998): Identification: Yes | Address: Yes
- Non-UK passports: Identification: Yes | Address: No
- EU/EEA identity cards: Identification: Yes | Address: No
- Biometric residence permit: Identification: Yes | Address: No
- EU/EEA photo card driving licence: Identification: Yes | Address: Yes
- UK Benefits/State Pension notification letter - dated within last 12 months: Identification: Yes | Address: Yes
- HMRC correspondence (excluding P45/P60) - must show Tax reference or NI number dated within last 12 months: Identification: Yes | Address: Yes
- Bank, building society or credit union statement dated within last 6 months and issued by regulated financial sector firm in UK: Identification: No | Address: Yes
- Mortgage statement from bank or building society dated within last 12 months and issued by regulated financial sector firm in UK: Identification: No | Address: Yes
- Council Tax correspondence (excluding arrears and reminders) - dated within last 12 months: Identification: No | Address: Yes
- Utility bills dated within last 6 months: Identification: No | Address: Yes
- Solicitor's correspondence relating to house purchase and dated within last 3 months: Identification: No | Address: Yes

### EXPATRIATES
- Not Acceptable: Do not accept applications from Expatriates

### FOREIGN NATIONALS
- Requirement: All applications from non-UK, non-Irish, nationals, EEA and non-EEA customers - proof of permanent right to reside will be required for all applicants on applications which do not meet the following criteria:
  * Customer (or at least one customer on joint or multiple application) has lived continuously in UK for more than 5 years
  * To determine if customer has lived in UK for more than five years, will use data provided by credit reference agencies
- Acceptable Documents: Will accept indefinite leave to remain/Right of Abode, UK Residency stamps in non-UK passports, biometric residence permits, and Home Office documentation confirming indefinite leave to remain in UK
- EEA/Swiss Nationals: For EEA/Swiss nationals with Settled or Pre settled status, 'Share code' can be used as residence

### HOW TO CERTIFY A DOCUMENT
- Requirement: Only identification and address verification and right to reside documents need to be certified
- Process: Must certify documents when they're collected and keep copies on file - don't need to upload copies to application unless asked
- Certifying Documents: Documents must be certified by FCA-registered mortgage intermediary
- Certification Must Include:
  * Certifier's signature, name, and job title
  * Date of certification, which must be within three months of application date
- Written Confirmation: Certifier must also add written confirmation on document - suggested wording: 'I certify this is a true and correct copy of the original document as sighted by me' (can use own variation if prefer)
- Separate Certificates: Separate 'certificates' are acceptable (sometimes called ID verification header sheets, broker/adviser verification checklists or similar)
  * Details on certificate must match those of ID&V documents provided
  * Wording to confirm true and correct copies of documents should be included
  * Intermediary name and signature must be included on certificate/document and date must be within three months of application date

### REFUGEES
- Requirement: Refugees and asylum seekers must have indefinite leave to remain
- Document: Usually given in letter confirming they've been granted 'Indefinite Leave to Remain' from Immigration and Nationality Directorate
- Not Acceptable for Mortgage Purposes:
  * Exceptional leave to remain in UK
  * Humanitarian protection
  * Discretionary leave

## 3. INCOME

### AFFORDABILITY AND SUSTAINABILITY
- Assessment: Affordability is assessed using anticipated rental income from property
- Personal Income: Will also assess personal income when making lending decision, so important customer's income is keyed accurately as it might affect decision
- Personal Commitments: Expect to see customer can afford their personal commitments, so can be confident lending decision is sustainable if tenant stops paying or property needs maintenance or repairs
- Decline: Application might be declined if assessment shows there's not enough disposable income to support borrowing and customer's personal commitments
- Rental Cover: In rental cover calculation, want customer to have more rent coming in than normal mortgage payment, so situations mentioned earlier can be covered
- Tax Liability: Will also take additional cost of increased tax liability for some customers into account
- Understanding: Understand personal income might not fully reflect overall financial picture for applicant - they could have other assets and savings, for example. Also know buy to let might be in single name, but personal financial commitments could be held jointly with someone else
- Manual Underwriting: Some information might need to manually underwrite application, includes:
  * How customer manages their commitments
  * How they've built up any reserves, including deposit
  * Whether they have previous experience of buying to let
  * Rationale for sole applications, where finances are joint
  * Business details for self-employed customers

### APPLICANTS NOT PAID IN STERLING
- Definition: Foreign currency income is any income other than sterling received by customer
- Note: Foreign currency income paid into UK bank account and transferred to sterling is still considered to be foreign currency
- Not Acceptable: Foreign currency income won't be accepted for new mortgage or remortgage applications for new and existing customers, including porting, transfers of mortgaged property (TOMP), and further advances
- Exceptions: Only exceptions are further advance applications for essential repairs to mortgaged property and product transfers

### CONTRACTORS
- Employed: If customer is classed as employed, need to have been in current role for at least three months
- Self-Employed: If customer is classed as self-employed, need at least two years' worth of trading figures

Sub-contractors, fixed/short term contracts, and agency workers:
- Consider: Will consider applications from these customers if they have continuous employment of 12 months or more with six months of contract remaining, or customer has two years' continuous service in same type of employment
- Evidence: Evidence of their employment record might be needed

IT and other contractors:
- IT Contractors: For IT contractors on any income and other contractors whose income is more than £500 per day or £75,000 per annum, whether employed or self-employed, accept gross value of contract as evidence of income
- Requirement: All applicants must have current continuous employment of 12 months or more with six months of contract remaining or two years continuous service in same type of employment
- Multiple Contracts: Customer should be treated as self-employed if they have more than one contract, or where they've set up limited company and employ other contractors

Construction Industry Scheme Contractors (CIS):
- Requirement: Need to provide average of their latest 3 months income
- Self-Employed: Should be treated as self-employed if they pay their own tax or sub-contract to more than one company

Zero-hours contracts:
- Consider: Applications from customers employed on zero-hours contract will be considered
- Income: Will only take income from total of last 12 calendar months into account
- Verification: Applicants will have to verify their income

Professional sports people:
- Requirement: Must confirm they've either been in continuous employment for 12 months or more with six months of contract remaining, or two years' continuous service as at date of application in same type of employment
- Verification: Essential to verify these customers will be able to meet their monthly repayments as they near end of their career or if their career's ended early because of injury

### INCOME TYPES
- Requirement: For all applications, at least one of customers' needs to have an earned income and/or pension income
- Recording: All income sources must be recorded accurately - includes all full years of self-employed income, PAYE, pension income, and all gross monthly rental
- Location: All income sources need to be within UK
- Minimum Income: There's no minimum income level
- UK Tax: Customers must be registered for UK tax
- Residential Mortgages: Any residential mortgages must be covered by customers' income
- Payslips: Must show customer and employer name, pay date, basic income, gross and net pay, and any additional payments being used in affordability assessments
- Employer's Reference: Will request employer's reference where customer can't supply preferred documentation - doesn't apply where customer's employed in family business
- Hand-written Payslips: Only acceptable alongside bank statements to confirm income paid by employer
- Bank Statements: Must show customer's full name or initial, surname, and account number
- Internet Bank Statements: Must show bank heading/name and http address

### LENGTH OF EMPLOYMENT/SELF-EMPLOYMENT
- Employed: Employed customers will be expected to have been employed for minimum of three months in their current job

### RENTAL INCOME
- Requirement: For employed, self-employed, and retired customers, all rental income and finance costs captured in 'Land and property income' section of application should match customers' tax calculations from HMRC
- Keying Customer Whose Only Source of Income is from Land and Property:

### SELF-EMPLOYED APPLICANTS

### SELF-EMPLOYED/CONTRACTORS
The following table shows how different contractor and self-employed types should be treated:

- Employed in Family Business Equal to or greater than 25% Invested interest and/or receives dividend or net profit as part of reward package (Treat as Self Employed) - Acceptable: Yes - Main or Other: Self Employed - Treat as: See Self Employed Applicants - Evidence: Last 2 years Tax Calculations and corresponding Tax Year Overviews

- Ltd Company - Director/Shareholder - Wanting to use - Basic PAYE salary ONLY less than 25% shareholding (Treat as employed) - Acceptable: Yes - Main or Other: Main - Treat as: Basic Annual Income - Evidence: Latest months payslip(s)

- Ltd Company - Director/Shareholder - Wanting to use - Basic PAYE salary ONLY Equal to or greater than 25% shareholding (Treat as self employed) - Acceptable: Yes - Main or Other: Self Employed - Treat as: See Self Employed Applicants - Evidence: Last 2 years Tax Calculations and corresponding Tax Year Overviews

- Ltd Company - Director/Shareholder - Wanting to use - Basic PAYE salary + dividends ONLY Irrespective of share holding (Treat as Self Employed) - Acceptable: Yes - Main or Other: Self Employed - Treat as: See Self Employed Applicants - Evidence: Last 2 years Tax Calculations and corresponding Tax Year Overviews

- Ltd Company - Director/Shareholder - Wanting to use - Basic PAYE salary + Net Profits ONLY irrespective of share holding (Treat as Self Employed) Note: Net Profits cannot be used without referral to Underwriters - Acceptable: Yes - Main or Other: Self Employed - Treat as: See Self Employed Applicants - Evidence: Last 2 years Tax Calculations and corresponding Tax Year Overviews

- Self employed - Sole Trader - Acceptable: Yes - Main or Other: Self Employed - Treat as: See Self Employed Applicants - Evidence: Last 2 years Tax Calculations and corresponding Tax Year Overviews

- Self employed / Share holding of Non Ltd Company (ie Partnership) Taxable Income - Acceptable: Yes - Main or Other: Self Employed - Treat as: See Self Employed Applicants - Evidence: Last 2 years Tax Calculations and corresponding Tax Year Overviews

- Partner of LLP receiving profit share as part of salary package Irrespective of share of equity (Treat as Self employed) - Acceptable: Yes - Main or Other: Self Employed - Treat as: See Self Employed Applicants - Evidence: Last 2 years Tax Calculations and corresponding Tax Year Overviews

- Employee of LLP - not equity shareholding partner (Treat as employed) - Acceptable: Yes - Main or Other: Main - Treat as: Basic Annual Income - Evidence: Latest months payslip(s)

- Piecework Contract - Acceptable: No

### BUSINESS/CONSUMER BUY TO LET
- Offer: Offer business buy to lets (BBTL) and consumer buy to lets (CBTL)
- BBTL Definition: Mortgage used to buy or remortgage property to let to non-family member or where majority of borrowing is for BTL purposes
- CBTL Definition: Where property wasn't originally purchased for letting, and customer doesn't let out any other properties - customer or family member might have previously lived in property, for example, and/or majority of borrowing is for non-business purposes
- Regulation: BBTL mortgages are not regulated and CBTL mortgage is regulated by HM Treasury - need Consumer FCA permissions before submitting any CBTL applications
- Classification: Customer answers will establish whether loan is classified as BBTL or CBTL
- Loan Purpose: Vital in deciding whether lending will be unregulated BBTL or regulated CBTL

BBTL Loan Purpose (when applying for remortgage with additional borrowing or further advance):
- Buy BTL property freehold
- Buy land adjacent to BTL property
- Buy extension of lease to BTL
- Buy final share being mortgaged
- BTL repairs
- BTL loan/BTL deposit
- Repay existing BTL loan

Consumer Goods/Funds:
- Non BTL debt consolidation
- Non BTL home improvements
- Non BTL (residential/second home) loan
- Repay subsequent charge - clearing bank
- Repay subsequent charge - non-clearing bank

Note: This list might not be available for every application - availability of each loan purpose will depend on circumstances of individual application

### CONCESSIONARY (DISCOUNTED) PURCHASE
- Not Acceptable: Don't accept concessionary purchase applications where property is being purchased below full market value

- Family Member Purchase: Acceptable to purchase BTL property from family member, as long as deposit is sourced from applicant's own resources and property is purchased at full market value

### CREDIT SCORE DECLINE APPEALS
- Process: All applications will be credit scored

### PORTFOLIO LANDLORDS

- 4-10, including new application, will need proof of income and supporting documents to be uploaded

- Full information about portfolio must be keyed

Information Needed Includes:
- Addresses
- Year built and year of purchase
- Original purchase price
- Gross monthly rent
- Estimated property valuation
- Whether property is leasehold, managed, and mortgaged

- Progress: Application will not progress until all of above has been fully completed - including completion of portfolio details screen in full application

Portfolio Landlords Criteria:

- Minimum Income: Minimum income of £30,000 each application from employment, self-employment, property portfolio profit, or pension/investment income
- Maximum Aggregate Portfolio: 75% LTV (based on entire portfolio, including acquiring property and any unencumbered let property)
- Minimum Aggregate Portfolio Rental Cover Ratio: 145% stressed at 5.5%
- Property Criteria: Property must meet standard criteria
- Calculator: Please check maximum amount customer can borrow using rental income calculator

Unencumbered Let Properties:
- Not Included: These aren't included in total mortgaged BTL properties
- Declaration: But rent and property value must be declared in property schedule and portfolio details sections of application

Finance Costs:

- Tax Calculation: Accordingly, profit from land and property as it appears on tax calculation no longer has finance costs deducted

- Studio Flats: Studio flats are acceptable subject to valuer's comments - there is no minimum size requirement

Not Acceptable:
- Houseboats and Mobile Homes: Houseboats and mobile homes/park homes aren't acceptable
- Right to Buy/Shared Ownership: Right to Buy/Shared ownership applications aren't acceptable
- Large Conversions: If flat is part of conversion, where total number of flats in scheme is above eight, valuer must confirm whole conversion project has been or will be completed to high standard
- Flats Above Own Business: Flats above/next to business owned by same person aren't acceptable

### DISTRESSED SALE AND LEASEBACK
- Not Acceptable: Applications involving distressed sale, or sale and leaseback aren't acceptable

### ENERGY PERFORMANCE CERTIFICATE
- Definition: Energy performance certificate (EPC) gives domestic properties energy-efficiency rating from A to G
- Validity: EPC is valid for 10 years - if property is sold in that time, EPC can be used by new owner
- Minimum Rating: Properties in England, Northern Ireland, Scotland, and Wales must have valid EPC rating of E or above
- F or G Ratings: Purchase, remortgage, and Let to Buy applications on properties with F or G EPC ratings are only allowed where registered exemption is in place
- Further Advances: Do not accept further advance applications where EPC rating is F, G or exemption is in place, except when purpose of loan is for BTL repairs
- Product Transfers: Product transfers are exempt
- New Build: For new build properties, use Predicted Energy Assessment (PEA) or Standard Assessment Procedure (SAP) rating if no EPC is available
- Exemptions: If property is exempt, evidence isn't needed unless requested
- Evidence: Evidence of EPC, PEA, SAP, or exemption evidence should be kept on file

### EXPOSURE LIMITS
- Assessment: When site is assessed as suitable for lending, will no longer apply initial cap on lending exposure
- Monitoring: Will continue to monitor applications (exposure) as development progresses and might restrict further lending if concerns arise
- Site Restrictions: There might be small number of sites where decide not to lend on because of specific market or site conditions

### HOUSES IN MULTIPLE OCCUPATION
- Acceptable: Properties subject to Local Authority Homes in Multiple Occupation (HMO) licensing might be acceptable, subject to valuer's assessment of suitability based on requirements:
- Maximum Tenants: Maximum number of unrelated tenants is five - they must all be named on agreement
- Responsibility: Landlords' responsibility to make sure all legislation is complied with, including (but not restricted to) legislation relating to Tenancy Deposit schemes and HMOs
- Property Suitability: Property must be suitable for standard residential occupation
- Not Acceptable: Institutional type properties, for example hostel accommodation/bedsits/shared accommodation comparable to halls of residence, aren't acceptable
- Larger Properties: For larger properties, where there is potential of more than five lettable rooms, valuer must be satisfied property and local market reflects single family letting arrangements
- Tenancy Types: Property can be let on single assured shorthold tenancy in England and Wales, private residential tenancy in Scotland, and private tenancy in Northern Ireland - tenancy in Northern Ireland must comply with provisions of Private Tenancies (Northern Ireland) Order 2006

### INHERITED PROPERTIES

### SUB-SALES AND BACK-TO-BACK TRANSACTIONS
- Not Acceptable: Sub-sales and back-to-back transactions aren't acceptable
- Ownership Requirement: Current vendor must have owned property for at least six months before can accept application to purchase property
- Exception: Exception to this is if property is inherited and customer hasn't owned it for six months
- Ownership Calculation: Use date of registration at Land Registry to work out length of ownership
- Definition: Back-to-back transaction is type of sub-sale where intervening seller buys from original seller and sells on to borrower on same day or within few days - also class cases where seller acquires freehold - or superior leasehold - title to property, and immediately sells on to borrower by grant of lease or sub-lease as sub-sales

Acceptable Sales Within Six Months:
- Personal representative of registered proprietor
- Institutional mortgagee exercising its power of sale
- Receiver, trustee-in-bankruptcy or liquidator
- Developer or builder selling property acquired under part-exchange scheme
- Registered housing provider or housing association exercising power of sale

- Conveyancer Responsibility: Conveyancer will be responsible for making sure application meets acceptable criteria
- Assignable Contracts: Applications involving assignable contracts or irrevocable powers of attorney in favour of intervening sellers aren't acceptable - should tell BM Solutions about any other transaction structure with similar effect

### TENURE
- Acceptable: Freehold and leasehold houses are acceptable
- Minimum Lease: Leasehold properties must have minimum of 70 years left on lease at application
- Conversions: If property's been converted into no more than four units or is within purpose-built block, any leases should already be in place and not created at same time as mortgage application

Leasehold - Applicant Does Not Own Freehold or Share of Freehold:
- Conveyancer: Conveyancer will make sure lease meets standard requirements

Leasehold - Applicant Owns/Will Own Freehold of Whole Building on Completion:
- Charge Registration: Charge must be registered on freehold of whole building and leasehold interest of unit
- Conveyancer: Conveyancer will make sure lease meets standard requirements
- Name Requirement: Mortgage must be in same name as freehold and leasehold titles
- Not Acceptable: Not acceptable to hold freehold in Ltd company with customer as sole director, majority shareholder, or with any family member
- Ownership Limit: Only acceptable for customer to own one unit in whole building, including any new application

Leasehold - Applicant Owns/Will Own Share of Freehold of Whole Building on Completion:
- Charge Registration: Charge must be registered on leasehold interest only
- Conveyancer: Conveyancer will make sure lease meets standard requirements
- Ownership Limit: Only acceptable for customer to own one unit in whole building, including any new application

Freehold:
- Security: Security will be registered on freehold of whole building and processed as freehold house
- Ownership: Applicant must own freehold in their own name
- Name Requirement: Mortgage and freehold title must be in same name
- Not Acceptable: Not acceptable to hold freehold in Ltd Company with customer as sole director, majority shareholder, or with any family member
- Other Units: All units other than freehold unit on which lending must have minimum lease of 70 years
- Ownership Limit: Only acceptable for customer to own one unit in whole building, including any new applications

Developments of More Than Four Units:
- Tenure: Tenure must be leasehold
- Management Company: Management company must be in place
- Share in Management Company: Customer can hold share in management company
- Conveyancer: Conveyancer will make sure arrangements in connection with management company are acceptable
- Charge Registration: Mortgage must be registered on leasehold title

- Large panel system type lats or maisonette blocks without acceptable structural appraisal
- Load bearing panels made of asbestos or gypsum plaster

## 6. EXISTING CUSTOMER

### ARREARS
- Further Advances: Can't accept further advance applications if mortgage has been at least one month in arrears within last three months
- Advice: Need to advise customer to bring account up-to-date with no arrears for at least three months before re-applying
- Product Transfers: Standalone product transfers can be processed as soon as account is up to date without need to wait three months

### EARLY REPAYMENT CHARGES
- Application: Some products have early repayment charges (ERCs) - will apply if customer repays some or all of mortgage in ERC period
- Overpayment Allowance: For mortgage products with ERCs, up to 10% of amount outstanding can be repaid without having to pay ERC - will use balance as of 01 January of current calendar year to work this out - can change or withdraw this at any time
- Porting Application: On porting application, any ERC which might apply will be waived or refunded if ERC period ends in next three months
- Product Transfer: On product transfer application, any ERC which might apply will be waived or refunded if ERC period ends in next four months
- Timing: Can't start product transfer application more than four months before ERC period ends
- Multiple Sub-Accounts: If account has multiple sub-accounts with different ERC periods, can only complete product transfer application on sub-account with ERC expiring soonest
- Subsequent Transfers: Once application has been submitted and new product has started, will be able to apply for product transfer on other sub-accounts - alternatively, can wait until ERC on all sub-accounts have three months left until expiry and apply for product transfer application on all eligible sub-accounts
- Overpayments: For product transfers and further advances, ERCs will be charged on any overpayments made over 10%, even if customer is within four months of ERC expiry

### FURTHER ADVANCES
- Minimum Amount: Minimum further advance amount is £5,000
- Product Requirement: When applying for further advance, product chosen must be from same scheme as main borrowing - BTL or LTB
- EPC Rating: Do not accept further advance applications where EPC rating is F, G or exemption is in place, except when purpose of loan is for BTL repairs
- Timing: Further advances can't be taken within 6 months of completion of original mortgage
- Subsequent Advances: Further advance must have completed at least 12 months ago before new further advance application can be taken

Arrears:
- Requirement: If customer hasn't made normal monthly payment and is in arrears, can't apply for further advance until arrears have been cleared
- Payment History: Payments must also be made in full, on time for three consecutive months
- Product Transfer: Further advance and product transfer can't be applied for at same time - if product transfer application has been applied for, need to wait until new product has started before further advance application can be submitted
- Cancelled Transfer: If product transfer application is submitted and subsequently cancelled, need to wait until 1st of following month before applying for further advance application

Acceptable Reasons for Further Advance:
- See Remortgage & Capital Raising section

Maximum LTV:
- Total lending on individual property including new further advance:
  * 65% LTV between £1,000,000 and £2,000,000
  * 75% LTV up to £1,000,000
  * 80% LTV available for lending up to £350,000, including all fees, on EPC rated properties A-C
- Revaluation: Where LTV is over 80% at application based on current index valuation, revaluation and revaluation fee will be needed - lending will be subject to value and rental figure following revaluation

### OVERPAYMENTS
- Availability: One-off or regular overpayments can be made at any time
- ERCs: If early repayment charges (ERCs) apply to product, please refer to ERC section for more details

### PAYMENT HOLIDAYS
- Not Allowed: Payment holidays aren't allowed

### PORTABILITY
- ERC Waiver: Any early repayment charges (ERCs) won't have to be paid on existing BTL mortgage products ported to new application
- Portability: If product is portable, it will be outlined in original mortgage offer and supporting documentation
- Remortgage Limitation: Can't port product to remortgage application if property is currently mortgaged
- Unencumbered Remortgage: Can port to remortgage of unencumbered (mortgage free) property customer already owns or new purchase
- Own Conveyancer: Porting to unencumbered remortgage can only be done using 'Remortgage own conveyancer' option - can't select free legal service
- Timing: Where new application is submitted before redemption of current mortgage, product can be ported

ERC Waiver Without Porting:
- Can also be waived/refunded without porting if:
  * New mortgage is being taken in last three months of ERC period of current product
  * Apply for new application on new product within three months of existing BM BTL mortgage being redeemed

### PRODUCT TRANSFERS
- Availability: Product transfers are available for non-regulated buy to let and let to buy customers only
- Not Eligible: Regulated mortgages, including residential customers and regulated buy to let, such as family buy to let aren't eligible for product transfers
- Eligibility Check: Once product transfer's been submitted, automated eligibility check will be carried out to make sure customers qualify
- Failure Reasons: If customers don't pass eligibility check, will see on-screen message explaining reason why and what you and/or customers should do next

Reasons Why Customers Might Fail Eligibility Check:
- Account is regulated mortgage
- Account is in arrears
- Account has multiple Direct Debits
- Account has payment arrangement in place
- Tenure of property information is missing from account
- No sub-accounts are available to transfer
- Account requires review of sub-account balances

Product Transfer Timescales:
- Submission: Product transfers can be submitted at any time in month
- Earliest Start: Earliest product transfer can start is 1st of following month
- Early Start Option: If customer is within last three months of existing product, can choose to start new product early - if select this option, will waive any early repayment charge (ERC)
- Forward Dating: Can also choose to forward date new product to start after current product has ended
- Multiple Sub-Accounts: If account has multiple sub-accounts with different ERC periods, can only complete product transfer application on sub-account with ERC expiring soonest
- Four Months Before: When customer is within last 4 months of existing product, can choose to select new product and forward date new product to start after current product has ended

### REGULATED MORTGAGES
- Not Acceptable: No longer accept any changes to existing regulated mortgages held with BM Solutions

This Includes:
- Residential
- Buy to let, if let to family member
- Self-build
- Self-certified
- Sub-prime
- Mortgage plus

- Contractual Changes: Can't offer any contractual changes to existing mortgages including further advances, product transfers, transfer of equity or repayment term/type changes
- Status: If original mortgage is regulated, status of mortgage will never change

Options:
- Specialist Remortgage Team: Customer can contact Specialist Remortgage team on 0345 608 0263
- Whole of Market: Can review whole of market for customer to remortgage to another lender
