## PART A – RESIDENTIAL CRITERIA

---

## SECTION 1 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

- Minimum age: 18.
- Maximum age: 83 at end of term (see Help to Buy and Shared Ownership for product-specific limits).
- Maximum applicants: 4 per application.
- All applicants must reside in the property at completion, except where a family member is assisting (see Family Assist / JBSP).
- 100% income from 2 earners can be used with 50% of one further applicant.
- Where a couple are married or in a long-term relationship (co-habited 12 months or more), both parties must be named on the mortgage except where an applicant does not have permanent rights to reside in the UK. Affordability is based on the number of people residing in the property (see Dependants).
- Residency: applicants must have resided in the UK for 6 months. For non-UK nationals see Foreign Nationals.
- Geographical restrictions: lending in England and Wales only, excluding islands off Great Britain (other than Isle of Wight and Anglesey).
- Guarantor: not available.

### Foreign Nationals

- EU, EEA or Switzerland, resident in UK prior to 31 December 2020: must have been granted settled status; evidence required.
- Outside EU/EEA/Switzerland, or arrived from EU/EEA/Switzerland after 31 December 2020: proof of indefinite leave to remain, permanent rights of residency or settled status required.
- Copy of passport and/or residence permit including Home Office/Immigration stamp or confirmation of settled status must be provided.
- All applicants must have lived in the UK for a minimum of 6 months prior to application.

### Dependants

- Household composition for affordability: number of adults and children who will reside in the property on a regular basis after completion, regardless of employment status.
- Adults over 18 not on the mortgage (e.g. adult children, short-term partners, lodgers) can be excluded where applicant confirms they are self-sufficient and do not add to household expenditure.
- Child at university not residing during term time: include support as committed expenditure (childcare/school fees).

---

## SECTION 2 – CREDIT HISTORY

- No IVA or Bankruptcy Order in force or outstanding at any time within the last 5 years.
- No repossession in the last 6 years.
- No CCJs outstanding in the last 3 years.
- No defaults outstanding in the last 3 years (single defaults for communication suppliers ignored if under £100).
- No debt management plans outstanding in the last 2 years.
- No missed mortgage payments in the last 3 years; for existing customers up to 2 consecutive missed mortgage payments can be considered within the last 2 years subject to none in the last 6 months.
- No missed loan payments in the last 12 months and no more than 2 consecutive missed loan payments within the last 2 years.
- No more than 1 missed credit card payment in the last 3 months and no more than 2 consecutive missed credit card payments in the last 2 years.
- Poor conduct of current accounts (exceeding/reliance on overdrafts, returned payments, adverse spending e.g. gambling) may impact lending — refer with full details.
- Payday loans: not acceptable if used in the last 12 months.

---

## SECTION 3 – INCOME AND AFFORDABILITY

- Maximum 5× LTI; all income annualised.
- Minimum income: one applicant must earn a minimum of £13,000 per annum.
- 3 months' bank statements required for all applicants showing relevant salary credits.

### Commitments

- Loans and credit cards can be excluded where they have less than 6 months to run or are to be repaid at completion. Hire Purchase can only be excluded when it will not need to be replaced/renewed.
- Childcare costs can be reduced/excluded where they will cease or reduce within 6 months (e.g. child's age, change in employment, relocating closer to family for childcare). Full details must be provided.

### Primary Income Types (100% of income can be used)

- **Permanent employment (basic salary):** permanent, open-ended contract; accepted within probation excluding temp-to-perm. Evidence: last 3 months payslips, or available payslips and signed contract or letter of confirmation.
- **Employed by family business:** shareholding less than 20% — must have been employed 3 months on salary used; income consistent with work/experience. Shareholding 20% or more: treat as self-employed. Evidence: last 3 months payslips and bank statements.
- **Newly Qualified Teacher (1 year contract):** must be 1-year fixed term. Maternity cover with clause allowing earlier end acceptable if Head Teacher confirms employment anticipated for full academic year. NQT not yet started: evidence of passed PGCE required prior to offer. Evidence: last 3 months payslips or available payslips and signed contract.
- **Fixed-term contract (PAYE):** 12 months contract history with current employer (or for qualified teachers 12 months current teaching history) with 6 months remaining on current contract at application; or 18 months contract history in same line of work and 6 months remaining. Evidence: last 3 months payslips, copy of signed current and previous contracts, completion of contractor declaration.
- **Self-employed:** average of last 2 years SA302; most recent proofs no more than 18 months before application. Variation more than 20% year to year requires explanation. Greater flexibility for HNW/higher earners under specialist lending. Evidence: last 2 years SA302 plus Tax Year Overviews; for Ltd companies last 2 years full accounts signed by suitably qualified accountant.
- **Music teachers:** 6 months history; employed and/or self-employed; average of last 6 months annualised. Evidence: last 6 months income.
- **Supply:** 2 academic terms in area purchasing; total pay over last 2 terms ÷ total available weeks in terms × 38. Evidence: payslips covering 2 academic terms; supply worker declaration.
- **Bank nurse:** 6 months history in area purchasing; average of last 6 months annualised. Evidence: last 6 months payslips.
- **Locum:** 6 months history in area purchasing; average of last 6 months annualised. Evidence: last 6 months payslips.
- **Private/company pensions and annuities:** must be payable for duration of mortgage (see Retirement). Evidence: projected — full pension projection; current — last 3 months bank statement plus full annuity/pension letter (name, address, company details, on headed paper). Lending into retirement declaration required.
- **State pension:** see Retirement. Evidence: last 3 months bank statements; individual personal projection via Government website (NI number); lending into retirement declaration.
- **Car allowance:** shown on last 3 months payslips.
- **Regular shift allowance:** average of last 3 months payslips.
- **TLR (Teacher Learning & Responsibility):** shown on last 3 months payslips.
- **Mortgage subsidy:** shown on last 3 months payslips.
- **London weighting / large town allowance:** shown on last 3 months payslips.
- **Child benefit:** not available where either applicant's income exceeds £50,000. Available where child aged 13 or under at application. Evidence: bank statements.
- **Second job (zero hours/temporary):** restricted to 60%; must meet conditions for employment type and carried out with main job for 3 months. Evidence: last 3 months payslips.
- **Cabin crew allowance, flight attendance allowance, NHS banding/enhanced pay, other consistent allowances (excluding expenses):** average of last 3 months payslips. For NHS and Police workers 100% of all income can be used.
- **Stipend (clergy):** evidence: last 3 months payslips or bank statement entries.

### Secondary Income Types (60% of income; must be supported by a primary income type)

- **Overtime:** for NHS or Police 100% can be used. Average of last 3 months. Evidence: last 3 months payslips.
- **Monthly commission/bonus:** average of last 3 months. Evidence: last 3 months payslips.
- **Quarterly commission/bonus:** average of last 2 quarters. Evidence: payslip showing last 2 quarterly payments.
- **Annual commission/bonus:** greater flexibility for HNW/financial services under specialist lending. Evidence: payslip showing last annual payment.
- **Child maintenance:** child aged 13 or under; must be on each of last 3 months bank statements; use lowest amount if varies. Not available over 90% LTV or shared ownership. Evidence: last 3 months bank statements.
- **Zero hours/temporary contracts:** 12 months employment history with same employer or same type of work. Evidence: last 12 months payslips.
- **Lodger income:** remortgage only; lodger not related to applicants; must sign consent to mortgage form. Not available over 90% LTV or shared ownership. Evidence: last 3 months bank statements.
- **Tax credits:** use lowest projected figures from award notice; child aged 13 or under; award notice must reflect relationship status, income, childcare. Not available over 90% LTV or shared ownership. Evidence: full Tax Credit Award Notice.
- **Stipend/bursary:** 12 months remaining. Not available over 90% LTV or shared ownership. Evidence: letter confirming amount, frequency and award period.
- **DLA/PIP:** must relate to applicant or dependant. Not available over 90% LTV or shared ownership. Evidence: last 3 months bank statements and award letter.

### Future Income and Pay Rises

- **Newly Qualified Teachers:** can complete 2 months before role commences subject to evidence of passed PGCE prior to mortgage offer.
- **Other applicants:** where new employment is commencing, completion unaffected if affordable on both current and future salary. Where affordability relies on new salary, completion can be 2 months before new role if applicant currently in employment. Family business: 3 months employment history (payslips and bank statements) required.
- **Pay rises:** most recent payslip showing increase or award letter. Family business: 3 months history of pay increase (payslips and bank statements).

### Maternity

- If returning to work within 2 months: full pay can be used subject to reference.
- Otherwise: full affordability on future plans (e.g. after maternity, childcare costs) with evidence of ability to make mortgage payments whilst on maternity.

### Retirement

- Where mortgage term exceeds age 70 or retirement date (whichever earlier), affordability must be met on pre- and post-retirement income. Pension income evidenced by bank statements (current) or projections (future).
- Already retired or term goes into retirement or exceeds age 70: maximum age at end of term 83; maximum 70% LTV; Lending into Retirement Declaration required (intermediary cases).
- Greater flexibility for HNW/higher earners under specialist lending.

### Interest Only

- Considered for HNW/higher earning clients under specialist lending range.

### Foreign Currency Mortgages

- The Society does not lend on mortgages classed as Foreign Currency Mortgages under MCOB/MCD.

## SECTION 4 – DEPOSITS AND FEES

- Minimum 5% deposit from applicant's personal funds or gift required, in addition to government loan schemes (Help to Buy), Forces Help to Buy or builders' incentives (New Build).
- Private vendor deposits not acceptable.
- Deposit from unsecured loan (e.g. personal loan) not acceptable.
- Gifted deposit: Society pro forma gifted deposit form required. Donor limited to parents, grandparents, siblings, aunts and uncles. Allowed only from EU, USA, Canada, Australia and New Zealand; other countries at Credit Committee discretion. If from outside UK: evidence via applicant's UK bank account; certified translation if country is non-English-speaking.
- Gifted deposits subject to 2nd charge acceptable if: no other interest/claim on property; funds from family member; only condition is repayment of original sum on sale of property (no interest, charges or repayments).
- Sale at reduced price where deposit is effectively equity: refer to Sale Under Value.
- **Application and valuation fees:** cannot be added to the loan.
- **Arrangement fees:** can be added provided this does not increase borrowing beyond the LTV for the product (90% or 95%). For 80% products fees can be added to the loan and remain on ≤80% LTV product even if LTV goes over 80%, but must be covered by MIG (reported as >80% LTV with insurance).
- **Forces Help to Buy:** acceptable. Deposit interest-free, repayable over 10 years; instalment classed as financial commitment for affordability. Can be used with other affordable housing schemes if applicant provides 5% from savings or family gift.
- **Help to Buy:** HTB England, Wales & England (Homes and Communities Agency). Customer must provide 5% deposit in addition to equity loan. Maximum age at end of term 70 or retirement if earlier. Where equity loan has less than 15 years to run, consideration of repayment of equity loan must be in affordability assessment.
- **New build incentives:** stamp duty and legal fees incentives acceptable up to 5% of purchase price; over 5% must be referred. Purchase price as on Mortgage Deed; incentives disclosed to valuer; lending on valuation. Mortgage offers valid 6 months. Houses: max LTV 90% (95% of share for shared ownership). Flats: max 85% on open market. Help to Buy: max 75% LTV (95% of share for shared ownership).

---

## SECTION 5 – LOAN PARAMETERS

- **Loan purpose:** house purchase up to 95%; remortgage up to 95%; remortgage with capital raising up to 90%. Capital raising acceptable for home improvements, debt consolidation, releasing equity for deposit for separate purchase by applicants or their children/grandchildren. Capital raising not permitted for business purposes.
- **Minimum loan:** £20,000 (new advance).
- **Maximum loan:** LTV 0–75%: £2,000,000 (5× LTI); LTV 75.01–80%: £1,500,000 (5× LTI); LTV 80.01–95%: £500,000 (5× LTI).
- **LTV 60–90%:** restriction when lending in or into retirement (see Retirement).
- **LTV 90–95%:** no lending past retirement or age 70; must be only property owned; earned income and child benefit only; age at expiry not to exceed 70; remortgage — no additional borrowing.
- **Term:** minimum 5 years; maximum 40 years. Shorter term may be considered for HNW/higher earners under specialist lending.

## SECTION 6 – PROPERTY (RESIDENTIAL)

- **Lending area:** England and Wales (excluding islands off Great Britain other than Isle of Wight and Anglesey).

### Unacceptable Property Types

- Property value less than £60,000.
- Properties not in habitable state.
- Freehold flats/maisonettes/commonhold unless: leasehold interest in flat and freehold is share of freehold in block of no more than 4 units, other flats leasehold, applicant owns overall freehold as security, block no more than 4 units; or property is one of two flats with reciprocated lease/freehold reversion (e.g. Tyneside Flat).
- Leasehold with less than 80 years remaining.
- Agricultural restrictions/ties; small holdings; live/work or mixed residential/commercial; mobile homes/river boats; farms with accommodation; flats in buildings with more than 6 floors (over 4 floors must have lift); studio/basement flats under 30 sq m; bedsit; above/adjacent to commercial likely to impact enjoyment (e.g. fast food, public houses); designated defective under Housing Act 1985; concrete or steel framed houses; any property valuer/Society deem unsuitable.

### Acceptable Property Types With Restrictions

- New build house: max 90% LTV (95% shared ownership).
- New build flat: max 85% LTV (95% shared ownership).
- Studio flats ≥30 sq m: max 80% LTV; purpose-built only.
- Deck access: max 80% LTV.
- Flying freehold: only part of property affected; rights of support, entry and repair enforceable by covenant.
- Coach house freehold flats: garages underneath subject to long lease.
- Above/adjacent commercial deemed acceptable: max 75% LTV.
- Flats in commercial/office conversion: max 75% LTV.
- Thatched and timber framed: latter subject to valuer's comments.
- More complex (mixed use, acreage, annex, conversions): may be considered for HNW/higher earners under specialist lending.

### Leasehold

- New build: minimum 125-year lease.
- Previously owned: minimum 80 years outstanding at mortgage start.
- Ground rent: not to exceed 0.25% of market value (e.g. £150,000 → £375 p.a. max). If reviewable: no more than once every 10 years; increases limited to RPI or equivalent. Event fees for lease changes must be based on reasonable administration cost.

### Right to Buy

- Up to 75% of valuation; no lending over purchase price. Applicants' names must match those on sale document. Where property within pre-emption clause: Deed of Postponement required.

### Sale Under Value

- Purchase under market value only where: vendor is immediate family member; offer subject to Declaration of Solvency and suitable indemnity policy (Section 339 Insolvency Act 1986); for LTV, full market value can be used if materially different from reduced price (minimum 20%); no additional personal deposit required. Existing tenant purchasing below market value: lending/LTV on purchase price (monies changing hands), deposit evidenced, Declaration of Solvency paragraph in offer as above.

### Second Home

- Considered subject to affordability up to 75% LTV where property primarily for applicant's use (e.g. holiday home or closer to place of work).

### Shared Ownership

- New build purchase only. Max 95% of share (see 95% criteria under LTV Restrictions). Remortgage not considered except staircasing to 100%. Lease must contain Mortgagee Protection Clause; landlord to notify Society in advance of forfeiture action. Applicants must be able to staircase to 100%. Max age at end of term 70 or retirement if earlier. Earned income and child benefit only. No other properties owned.

### New Build Warranties

- Properties under 10 years old: NHBC Buildmark, Premier Guarantee, Zurich Municipal BLP, LABC Warranty, Build Zone 10 year, Checkmate New Home Warranty, ICW. Other mainstream warranties/Architects Certificates considered on merit.

---

## SECTION 7 – FAMILY ASSIST (JOINT BORROWER SOLE PROPRIETOR)

- Non-occupying applicant can be on mortgage for affordability but not legal owner (JBSP) subject to: max LTV 80%; non-occupying applicant(s) must be parent(s)/grandparent(s) of one occupying applicant; max 2 non-occupying applicants; max 4 applicants in total; 100% of income (allowed under policy) from all applicants for affordability; affordability compliant with MCOB covering both applicants' and parent(s)/grandparent(s) households; recommendation in offer that parties agree personal future exit plans separately from legal obligations.
- Society must receive independent legal advice that: applicant understands no beneficial interest; acknowledges joint and several liability for full mortgage if sole proprietor cannot pay; is not vulnerable or coerced; reduction in own estate and potential family disputes highlighted; recommended to take independent tax advice.
- Lending on specific Family Assist products (risk priced by Product Governance Committee). Cannot be used with shared ownership or help to buy.
- Where parent(s)/grandparent(s) are named as joint legal owner: above criteria available; ILA not required; solicitors for parent(s)/grandparent(s) must confirm they understand joint and several liability for full mortgage if child cannot pay.

## PART B – BUY TO LET CRITERIA

---

## SECTION 8 – BTL SCOPE AND GENERAL CRITERIA

- Lending on: BTL on single Assured Shorthold Tenancy of no more than 12 months; Holiday Lets (short-term lets of less than 1 month for holiday purposes).
- Consumer BTL accepted. Does not lend on BTL regulated under MCOB.
- Does not lend on multiple tenancies or HMO.
- Let mortgages only for customers who will hold (personally or via limited companies) 3 or fewer mortgaged BTL/Holiday Let properties after completion; exception for existing portfolio landlords with the Society who demonstrate long-term understanding of BTL (full assets and liabilities required).
- All let properties including Holiday Lets: minimum EPC rating E unless exempt.

### Standard BTL and Holiday Let – Key Parameters

- Maximum age at end of term: 83.
- Minimum age: 25 at application.
- Minimum income: 1 applicant must earn from employment minimum £25,000 or both applicants combined minimum £40,000. Where term exceeds age 70, pension income in retirement must also meet these limits.
- Minimum property value: £75,000.
- Maximum loan: £1,000,000.
- Minimum loan: £50,000.
- Maximum LTV: purchase 75%; remortgage 75%.
- Maximum term: 40 years.
- Stress rate: 5.5% or 2% above pay rate, whichever is greater.
- Repayment: capital and repayment or interest only; sale of property acceptable as repayment vehicle.
- Restrictions: not available for debt consolidation; private individuals only; not available on new build, bedsits or studio flats; existing homeowners only; cannot be used with Shared Ownership, Right to Buy or similar schemes.

---

## SECTION 9 – STANDARD BTL (AST) ADDITIONAL CRITERIA

- **Coverage ratio:** higher rate taxpayer 145%; lower rate taxpayer 125%. If using lower tax banding, income evidenced by payslips, P60 and SA302 as appropriate; income must include all income and gross rental for the BTL/holiday let with the Society. If more than 1 applicant, highest tax banding applies.
- **Rental assessment:** annual rent on AST basis.
- **Occupancy:** single AST of no more than 12 months to a single family unit. Multiple tenancies or HMO not acceptable.

---

## SECTION 10 – HOLIDAY LET ADDITIONAL CRITERIA

- **Location:** areas with demand for holiday let properties.
- **Coverage ratio:** 135%.
- **Rental assessment — purchase:** average of low, medium and high season weekly rates × 30; evidenced by letting agent confirmation.
- **Rental assessment — remortgage:** 100% of actual rental receipts over last 12 months; evidenced by letting agent confirmation and/or bank statements. Where less than 12 months rental history use purchase assessment.
- **Restrictions:** not available for properties on holiday parks or with restrictive usage covenants (e.g. holiday let covenant).
- **Marketing:** property can be marketed via Airbnb-type platforms.
