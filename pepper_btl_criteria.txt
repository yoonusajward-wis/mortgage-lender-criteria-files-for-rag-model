## Document Information

This document contains comprehensive buy-to-let and limited company buy-to-let mortgage lending criteria covering all topics from the Pepper Money Intermediaries website.

Note: Our mortgage criteria is designed to help your customers who would benefit from a more human approach to underwriting. This includes first-time landlords, limited company landlords with a newly set-up SPV, and portfolio landlords looking to raise capital through remortgaging.

## Applicant Criteria (for Buy to Lets) and Director Criteria (for Limited Company Buy to Lets)

### Applicant Details

Minimum age: 21

Maximum age:

* 85 at end of term
* For joint applications the age of the oldest applicant is used

Maximum applicants: 2

Guarantors: Not accepted

First-time landlords:

* Accepted where applicants are homeowners

### Director Details

Minimum age: 21

Maximum age:

* 85 at end of term
* For joint applications the age of the oldest applicant is used

Maximum applicants: 4

Guarantors: Not accepted

First-time landlords:

* Accepted where applicants are homeowners

### Maximum Allowable Adverse

CCJs & defaults:

* None in last 12 months
* On Pepper 24, 18, 12 and 6 products we will ignore 2 individual defaults (per application) up to and including £200.00 each where these defaults relate to utilities, communications or mail order providers
* On Pepper 36 products we will ignore 1 individual default (per application) up to and including £200.00 where this default relates to utilities, communications or mail order providers

Mortgage arrears:

* Arrears status of 0 in last 6 months
* None missed in the last 12 months

Unsecured arrears:

* Fixed term: None in the last 6 months
* Revolving credit: Missed payments considered

IVA & Bankruptcy:

* Accepted where applicants have been discharged 6 or more years ago

Debt management plans:

* Accepted if satisfied over 12 months ago

Pay day loans:

* Accepted where the account closed more than 12 months ago

Repossessions:

* Accepted where repossession occurred more than 6 years ago

### Nationality & Residency

Residential status:

* 3 years' UK residential address history required for all applicants

Visa Requirements:

Accepted subject to:

* 3 years UK residency

One applicant must have indefinite leave to remain. Additional applicants must have indefinite leave to remain or right to reside with acceptable visa type:

* EU Settlement Scheme (EUSS) – Pre-Settled Status
* EU Settlement Scheme (EUSS) – Settled Status
* Leave to Remain
* Family Visa
* Skilled worker visa
* UK Ancestry Visa
* Health and Care Worker visa
* British National (Overseas) visa
* Intra company transfer visa

Visa validity: Minimum 12 months remaining

Diplomatic immunity: Not accepted

### Nationality & Residency for Limited Company Buy to Let Directors

Residential status:

* 3 years' UK residential address history required for all applicants

Visa Requirements:

Accepted subject to:

* 3 years UK residency

One applicant must have indefinite leave to remain. Additional applicants must have indefinite leave to remain or right to reside with acceptable visa type:

* EU Settlement Scheme (EUSS) – Pre-Settled Status
* EU Settlement Scheme (EUSS) – Settled Status
* Leave to Remain
* Family Visa
* Skilled worker visa
* UK Ancestry Visa
* Health and Care Worker visa
* British National (Overseas) visa
* Intra company transfer visa

Visa validity: Minimum 12 months remaining

Non EU Nationals accepted subject to:

* 3 years UK residency
* 3 years continuous UK employment history
* Indefinite rights to remain

Diplomatic immunity: Not accepted

## Income Criteria (for Buy to Let Applicants and Limited Company Buy to Let Directors)

### Income

Rental income:

* Accepted as primary income source for individual and limited company landlords

Earned income:

For employed individuals:

* At least one applicant must have a minimum of 3 months in the current job
* 6 months continuous employment required
* Applicant must not be within a probationary period

For self-employed company directors:

* Minimum trading period: 12 months
* Directors with a shareholding of 25% or more will be underwritten as self-employed

For self-employed sole traders:

* Minimum trading period: 12 months

For retired individuals:

* State pension accepted
* Private pension accepted

### Other Income Sources

Pension income:

* State retirement pension (SRP): Latest annual pension statement required
* Private pension: Latest payslip from pension provider or letter from pension provider required

Secondary/additional income:

* Employment or self-employment income from a maximum of 2 sources per applicant considered
* Benefits and maintenance may be included as additional income

## Loan Criteria

Maximum LTV:

* Standard BTL (Individual and Limited Company):

  * 80% LTV for properties with EPC A–C
  * 75% LTV for properties with EPC D
  * 70% LTV for properties with EPC E
* BTL HMO (Individual and Limited Company):

  * 75% LTV for properties with EPC A–C

Maximum term: 35 years

Minimum term: 5 years and 1 month

Minimum loan: £25,001

Maximum loan:

For Buy to Let: Maximum loan size £2 million within the following LTV limits:

* Up to £750,000 available to 80% LTV
* Up to £1 million available to 75% LTV
* Up to £1.5 million available to 70% LTV
* Up to £2 million available to 65% LTV

For BTL HMO: Maximum loan size £1 million within the following LTV limits:

* Up to £500,000 available to 75% LTV
* Up to £1 million available to 70% LTV

Number of mortgages with Pepper:

* Maximum aggregated exposure £4 million with Pepper Money
* Includes all Residential, Buy to Let and Limited Company Buy to Let loans

Remortgage applications:

* Accepted where applicant has been the registered owner for the last 6 months at application

Repayment:

* Capital & interest
* Interest only allowed up to 80% LTV with acceptable repayment strategy
* Maximum age at end of term: 85

Portable: Products are not portable

Offer validity: 90 days

Let to Buy: Not accepted

Consumer Buy to Lets: Not accepted

Buy to Let HMO:

* Accepted with maximum 6 bedrooms
* Maximum 2 kitchens

ICR and borrow rates:

* BTL (Individual and Ltd Co):

  * 125% of pay rate for standard rate taxpayers
  * 140% for higher rate taxpayers
  * 165% for additional rate taxpayers
  * Used for rental calculation on 5-year fixed rates
* Individual BTL HMO:

  * 155% of pay rate for standard rate taxpayers
* Limited Company BTL HMO:

  * 140% of pay rate for standard rate taxpayers

### Deposit Criteria

Evidence of deposit: Required on all purchase applications

Applicants' savings: Accepted

Capital raising remortgage: Accepted

Inheritance: Accepted

Gifted deposit: Accepted from a close relative

Acceptable deposits (Limited Company SPV only):

* Retained profits
* Sale of another SPV property
* Remortgage of another SPV property
* Existing equity from property transferred to SPV structure
* Funds from another SPV with common ownership or group ownership

Unacceptable sources:

* Builder's deposit
* Unsecured borrowing
* Vendor deposit

## General Criteria

New build definition:

* A property is to be considered new if it is a first sale by the builder
* However, properties which have not sold on physical completion will be considered on an individual basis

Minimum property value:

* Buy to Let: £70,000
* BTL HMO: £100,000

Locations considered:

* England, Wales & Isle of Wight

Retentions: Not offered

Acceptable property:

* Houses: Private and ex Local Authority or social housing
* Flats & Maisonettes: Private only
* Licensed HMO properties accepted

Minimum EPC:

* BTL properties with EPC A–E accepted
* EPC A–C: Up to 80% LTV
* EPC D: Up to 75% LTV
* EPC E: Up to 70% LTV
* HMO properties must have EPC A–C

HMO valuation:

* Valuer must access all bedrooms
* If access cannot be obtained a reinspection may be required
* A reinspection fee may be payable directly to Legal & General

### Alternative Construction

Timber framed construction:

* Modern timber framed properties clad with masonry are acceptable
* Timber framed properties not deemed modern masonry-clad will be reviewed individually

Flying freeholds:

* Maximum 20% of total floor area

Coal mining and other extraction:

* Properties with a mining entry or feature within 20m of any building not accepted

Electromagnetic fields:

* Properties within 100m of above ground high voltage electrical supply apparatus not accepted

Contaminated land:

* Pass certificate required from an appropriate body where contamination risk exists

Japanese knotweed:

* Properties with Japanese knotweed located within or immediately adjacent to the site (category 4, 3 or 2 under RICS IP 27/2012) not accepted

Solar panels:

* Properties with leased solar panels may be considered
* Lease amendment may be required

### Tenure

Freehold:

* Houses only accepted

Leasehold:

* Minimum 85 years unexpired lease at completion

Commonhold: Not considered

### Property Reports

Structural reports:

Reports accepted from members of:

* The Institution of Structural Engineers ([www.istructe.org.uk](http://www.istructe.org.uk))
* The Institution of Civil Engineers ([www.ice.org.uk](http://www.ice.org.uk))

Requirements:

* Report must be referred to the valuer for comment
* If not addressed to the applicant, written reliance confirmation required
* Properties with ongoing movement or monitoring requirements will not be accepted

Specialist reports:

Common specialist reports may include:

* Timber and damp
* Electrical
* Trees
* Cavity wall ties
* Mining reports

Additional conditions:

* Reports must be prepared by a reputable firm
* Must be referred to the valuer for review
* Additional reports may be requested by the valuer

### Unacceptable Property

General:

* New build properties without suitable warranty
* Properties with ongoing structural movement or insurance claims
* Grade I & Grade II* listed properties
* Back-to-back or cluster homes
* Properties within 20m of a mine entry or feature
* Properties within 100m of high voltage electrical supply apparatus (excluding small substations)
* Properties subject to compulsory purchase orders
* Flying freeholds greater than 20% of floor area
* Japanese knotweed within or immediately adjacent to the site (RICS IP 27/2012 category 4, 3 or 2)
* Properties without internal WC, bathroom or kitchen
* Properties with onerous occupancy restrictions including agricultural
* Shared ownership or shared equity schemes (unless borrower owns 100% on completion)
* Properties subject to enforcement notices
* Crofting properties or land
* Self-build properties
* Properties with commercial element
* Non-standard construction

Flats/Maisonettes:

* Flats in local authority or ex-local authority owned blocks
* New build flats or maisonettes
* Flats under 35m² gross external floor area
* Flats above commercial premises
* Studio flats
* Freehold flats
* Balcony access flats

Non-standard construction:

* Pre-cast reinforced concrete (PRC)
* Cast-in-situ concrete
* Metal framed construction
* Single skin construction
* Large panel systems (LPS)
* Modern methods of construction (MMC)

Flooding:

* Properties at significant flooding risk or unable to obtain insurance not accepted

Restrictive covenants:

* Properties with restricted occupancy planning conditions not accepted
* Examples include occupancy limited to certain months of the year
* Retirement or sheltered accommodation restrictions not accepted
* Agricultural restrictions limiting marketability not accepted
* Section 106 planning obligations may be acceptable for new builds
* Properties within pre-emption periods not accepted

## Documentation

### Application Requirements

Full mortgage application:

* Original fully signed and dated declaration
* Signed and dated direct debit mandate

Buy to Let portfolio form:

* Required for applicants with existing Buy to Let properties

Proof of deposit (purchase):

* Applicants bank statements

Use of funds (remortgage with further advance):

* Breakdown of use of funds after debts repaid
* Account details and balances of debts to be repaid

Proof of address and identification:

* One applicant must have indefinite leave to remain
* Additional applicants must have indefinite leave to remain or acceptable visa
* Two documents from Table 1 or one from each table required

Table 1:

* Valid passport
* Valid photo card driving licence (full or provisional)
* National identity card
* Firearms certificate or shotgun licence
* Identity card issued by the Electoral Office for Northern Ireland

Table 2:

* Valid old-style full UK driving licence
* Evidence of benefit entitlement
* Instrument of court appointment
* Council tax demand letter or statement
* Non-internet generated bank statements and utility bills

Proof of visa:

Acceptable visa types:

* EU Settlement Scheme (Pre-Settled Status)
* EU Settlement Scheme (Settled Status)
* Indefinite leave to remain
* Leave to remain
* Family visa
* Skilled worker visa
* UK Ancestry visa
* Health and Care Worker visa
* British National (Overseas) visa
* Intra company transfer visa

Visa validity: Minimum 12 months remaining

Rental or mortgage history:

* Tenants must provide 6 months rental payment evidence via bank statements or letting agent reference
* Homeowners may need to provide 6 months mortgage payment evidence if not visible on Equifax

Portfolio landlords:

* Maximum portfolio size: 10 properties

Required portfolio information:

* Written asset and liabilities statement including:
* Rental income per property
* Outstanding mortgages
* Current property values
* Void periods within last 12 months
* Future plans for each property

### Company Details (Limited Company Buy to Let Only)

Company requirements:

* Registered with Companies House
* Must have active accounts
* Company must be active
* No adverse credit history
* No debentures present
* No disqualified directors
* No minimum trading period required
* All directors must be natural persons

SIC code must be one of:

* 68100
* 68201
* 68209
* 68320

Property transfers:

* Transfers from personal ownership to limited company accepted at full market value
* Treated as purchase transactions

Guarantee:

* Personal guarantees required from all directors
