## SECTION 1 – PRODUCT OVERVIEW

- A Step One mortgage loan is also known as a secured loan or second mortgage loan.
- Available to qualifying homeowners and owners of buy to let properties.
- Available in England, Scotland and Wales.
- Secured against available equity in the property.
- Total LTV includes the amount of the current first mortgage.
- Loan amounts available up to £250,000; loans over £250,000 may be considered on a case-by-case basis.

---

## SECTION 2 – LOAN AMOUNTS AND LTV

### Loan Amounts

- Minimum loan size: £5,000.
- Maximum loan size (residential): £250,000.
- Maximum loan size (buy to let): £200,000.
- Loans over £250,000 may be considered on a case-by-case basis.

### Loan to Value

- Maximum LTV (residential): 95%.
- Maximum LTV (buy to let): 90%.
- Total LTV includes the amount of the current first mortgage.

## SECTION 3 – REPAYMENT TERMS

- Repayment periods available from 6 years to 30 years.

## SECTION 7 – APPLICATION ASSESSMENT

All loans are subject to status. The rate offered will depend on personal circumstances, past credit conduct and other related factors.

### Affordability

- Full review of income and outgoings undertaken.
- Assessment to determine affordability both now and in the foreseeable future.

### Employment History

- Employment history reviewed for gaps.
- Length of employment with individual companies considered.
- Longer periods in one role viewed positively compared to frequent job movement.

### Credit History

- Credit history reviewed closely.
- Assessment includes repayment trends and debt management history.
- Consistency in repayments considered important.

### Equity

- Equity calculated as current market value minus outstanding primary mortgage balance.
- A percentage of available equity is used to secure the loan.
- Greater equity may result in better available rates.

## SECTION 9 – IMPORTANT WARNINGS

**Repossession Warning:**
Think carefully before securing other debts against your home. Your home may be repossessed if you do not keep up repayments on a mortgage or any other debt secured on it.

**Debt Consolidation Warning:**
If you are thinking of consolidating existing borrowing, you should be aware that if you are extending the term of the debt you may be increasing the total amount you need to repay.
