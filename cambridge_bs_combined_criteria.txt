## SECTION 1 – ADVERSE CREDIT

### Credit History (standard range)

* A credit search is required on all customers.
* Soft search at DIP stage; hard credit search at mortgage application decision stage (prior to full submission and payment of fees).
* We will consider customers with adverse credit histories providing: no missed mortgage payments in the last 24 months; no more than 2 missed loan, credit card or financial commitment payments in the last 24 months; CCJs and Defaults less than £500 satisfied over 24 months ago; Bankruptcies, IVAs, CCJs and Defaults (£500 and over) discharged/satisfied over 36 months ago.
* If your customer falls outside of the above, see Credit Assist mortgages.

### Credit Assist – Residential (updated 9 April 2025)

* **Credit Assist Mortgage:** Maximum loan size £400,000. Maximum LTV 85%. Suitable for customers with up to 5 missed payments on credit card, mail order, utilities or communications within the last 24 months; up to 2 defaults with combined value of £100, outstanding or satisfied within the last 24 months (if outstanding must be repaid on completion).
* **Credit Assist Mortgage Extra:** Maximum loan size £400,000. Maximum LTV 70%. Three or more missed loan or unsecured financial commitment payments in the last 24 months; five or more missed payments on credit card, mail order, utility or communications in the last 24 months; 1–2 missed secured payments within the last 24 months; DMP, Defaults or CCJ up to £500 outstanding or satisfied within the last 24 months (if outstanding must be repaid on completion); DMP, Defaults or CCJ up to £5,000 outstanding or satisfied within the last 36 months (if outstanding must be repaid on completion); payday loans considered if over 12 months ago.

### Credit Assist – Buy to Let

* **BTL Credit Assist Mortgage:** Maximum loan size £400,000. Maximum LTV 75%. Suitable for customers with up to 5 missed payments on credit card, mail order, utilities or communications within the last 24 months; up to 2 defaults with combined value of £100, outstanding or satisfied within the last 24 months (if outstanding must be repaid on completion). Only acceptable for landlords with up to 3 buy to let properties.

### Payday loans and gambling

* We will not knowingly lend to customers who have had a payday loan or gambling debts during the last 24 months.

## SECTION 2 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

### Age – Residential

* Minimum age for residential repayment or Interest Only: 18.
* Minimum age for residential Retirement Interest Only: 55 at the start of the loan (youngest customer).
* No maximum age for residential repayment mortgages.
* Maximum age for residential Interest Only: 80 at the end of the mortgage term.
* Maximum age for residential Retirement Interest Only: 85 at the start of the loan (youngest customer).

### Age – Buy to Let

* Minimum age: 21. No maximum age limit.

### Number of applicants

* **Residential:** Sole and joint applications. If mortgage LTV is below 80% and there is a family tie, up to 4 customers on a residential purchase and remortgage.
* **Buy to Let:** Sole and joint applications. If there is a family tie, up to 4 customers on a buy to let purchase and remortgage.

### First Time Buyers – Buy to Let

* Buy to let applications are not accepted from First Time Buyers. We define a First Time Buyer as an individual who has not been party to a mortgage or owned a property at any time. To qualify for a Buy to let mortgage your customer must be either an owner outright or an owner with a mortgage (in place for at least 6 months).

### Residency and Visas – Residential (updated 8 January 2025)

* Customers must have been resident in the UK for a minimum of 2 years at the time of applying.
* We will consider mortgages up to 95% LTV for customers with: British citizenship; Settled or Pre-settled status (EU settlement scheme); Permanent Rights to Reside (PRR); Spousal visa (acceptable on joint mortgages where main applicant has British Citizenship, Settled or Pre-settled status OR PRR).
* If your customer holds one of the following Visas and 2 years proof of residency in the UK, we will consider applications up to 90% LTV: Skilled Work Visa; Health and Care Worker Visa; Global Talent Visa. For joint applications where one customer has indefinite leave to remain, we will accept applications up to 95% LTV. The applicant's deposit must be from their own sources.

### Residency and Visas – Buy to Let (updated 15 January 2024)

* Customers must have been resident in the UK for a minimum of 2 years at the time of applying.
* We will consider mortgages for customers with: British citizenship; Settled or Pre-settled status (EU settlement scheme); Permanent Rights to Reside (PRR); Spousal visa (acceptable on joint mortgages where main applicant has British Citizenship, Settled or Pre-settled status OR PRR).
* If your customer holds one of the following Visas and 2 years proof of residency in the UK, we will consider applications up to 80% LTV: Skilled Work Visa; Health and Care Worker Visa; Global Talent Visa. Deposit must be from their own sources; we will only consider a Buy to Let application if your customer is a Residential homeowner.

### Commercial customers

* We do not accept applications from either new or existing commercial customers.

### Vulnerable customers

* We review each case individually; where a vulnerable customer has been identified, consideration will be given as required.

## SECTION 3 – AFFORDABILITY & INCOME

### Affordability

* We will take full account of your customer's income (net of tax and national insurance and any additional deductions) and credit commitments or other monthly committed expenditure.
* Mortgage payment for affordability: Standard Variable Rate + 1% over the term. Where a 5 Year Fixed product is selected, the pay rate is used.
* Affordability assessment uses ONS data for expected expenditure by household composition. Where the loan is interest only, affordability is assessed on interest only over the term; any premium or contribution into a repayment plan must be taken into account.

### Loan to income – Residential

* We will only accept residential loans inclusive of fees under 4.5 times income. For purchases we will consider up to 5 times income subject to: maximum LTV 80%; minimum income of £60,000 (total of all customers); no unsecured debt (excluding car loans and student loans). Applies to residential purchases including porting and residential remortgages where extra capital is being raised. Does not apply to residential remortgages on a like-for-like basis.

### Minimum income

* The minimum income for sole or joint customers is £25,000.

### Income allowance table (updated 10 February 2025)

* Main Earned Income 100%. Allowances 100%. Overtime 50%. Bonus Monthly/Quarterly/Annual 50% (evidence of 2 bonus payments required). Commission 50% (1–2 years evidence). Multiple Jobs 100% (minimum 6 months). Investment Income 100% (refer to Business Development Team). Stipend 100% (maximum 80% LTV and minimum 12 months remaining at offer). Pension 100% (pension statement required). BTL Rental Income 50% if property unencumbered; BTL Rental Income (landlord with 4 or more BTL properties) 100% if constant evidence for 2 years. Room Rental 100% (minimum 6 months evidence). Maintenance 100% (maximum 80% LTV; Court Order or CMS; 6 months payment history; at least 3 years payments receivable). Working Tax Credit 100%. Child Tax Credits 100%. Disability allowance 100%. Child Benefit 100%. Foster Carer 100% (refer to Business Development Team). Maternity Leave 100% (employer's reference required). Car Allowance 100%. Rent Allowance 100%. Fixed Term Contracts 100% (ideally well-established organisations; at least 6 months of contract to run at offer; indication of renewal). Zero Hours Contracts: Not acceptable.

### Benefits

* We will not consider an application which will rely solely on benefits. For benefits we will consider to support other income, refer to Income allowance in this criteria.

### Maintenance

* Child Maintenance will be considered as income up to 80% LTV at Underwriter discretion. Must be Court Ordered or through CMS; amount clear on bank statements; 6 months payment history; at least 3 years payments receivable.

### Directors of companies

* If Director's shareholding is less than 25%, we class them as employees; income by payslips and P60. If shareholding is 25% or more, refer to Self-employed.

### Maternity leave

* We will always require an employer's reference with regards to maternity leave duration and pay.

## SECTION 4 – LOAN AMOUNTS, TERM & LTV

### Loan amounts – Residential (updated 19 March 2024)

* Minimum: New mortgages £20,000 (may vary by product); Additional borrowing £10,000; Remortgages £20,000; Interest Only £75,000; Retirement Interest Only £50,000; Low deposit £95,000; Shared Ownership £50,000; Help to Buy remortgage £50,000.
* Maximum: Shared Ownership £400,000; Help to Buy remortgage £450,000; Credit Assist £400,000; Interest Only £750,000; Retirement Interest only £750,000; Residential purchase and remortgage up to 80% LTV £2,000,000; Lending up to 90% LTV £750,000; Lending up to 95% LTV £500,000. We will only lend in Sterling.

### Loan amounts – Buy to Let (updated 9 April 2025)

* Minimum: New mortgages £20,000; Additional borrowing £10,000; Re-mortgages £20,000; Holiday lets £50,000.
* Maximum: 10 buy to let properties or a total of £4,000,000 for an individual (whichever is higher); maximum loan for buy to let £1,000,000. We will only lend in Sterling.

### Loan term

* Minimum term 5 years. Maximum term 40 years. For leasehold properties, maximum term is influenced by the remaining lease; there must be at least 60 years remaining on the lease after the lending term has expired.

### Loan to value – Residential (updated 27 June 2023)

* LTV = loan amount ÷ property value or purchase price (whichever is lower), expressed as a percentage. We will lend up to 95% on a purchase, 95% on a like-for-like remortgage and 90% on a remortgage with capital raising.

### Loan to value – Buy to Let (updated 28 February 2025)

* LTV = loan amount ÷ property value or purchase price (whichever is lower). We will lend up to 80% on buy to let purchases and remortgages. For buy to let portfolio landlords, expats and holiday lets, maximum LTV is 75%.

### Lending restricted to 80% LTV (updated 7 July 2025)

* We will not consider lending over 80% LTV on: Coach Houses, Quarter Houses and Back to Back Terraced Houses; Multiple applicants; Tied Accommodation; Child Maintenance income; Stipend Income; Foster Carer Income; Right to Buy (we will lend up to 100% of the discounted purchase price); Forces Help to Buy scheme; Shared equity; Part & part mortgages; Applicants in probation periods. Leasehold flats and maisonettes in blocks over 8 storeys constructed after 1 January 2020 will be considered up to 80% LTV subject to valuer recommendation; all blocks must be serviced by a lift. For full details refer to individual sections and the income allowance table.

### Debt consolidation – Residential (updated 29 January 2024)

* Considered at Underwriter discretion up to 90% LTV. Where debt consolidated is greater than £10,000, further information required. First-time debt consolidation: we will exclude the monthly debt commitments from affordability if repaid on completion. Subsequent debt consolidation within 2 years: Underwriters may include the monthly debt commitments in affordability.

### Debt consolidation – Buy to Let (updated 15 January 2024)

* Considered at Underwriter discretion up to 80% LTV. For Expat and Holiday Let Buy to Lets, maximum LTV 75%. Where debt consolidated is greater than £10,000, further information required. First-time: exclude monthly debt commitments from affordability if repaid on completion. Subsequent within 2 years: Underwriters may include monthly debt commitments.

## SECTION 5 – PROPERTY

### Flats – Residential (updated 7 July 2025)

* Leasehold resale flats up to 95% LTV; new build leasehold flats up to 85% LTV, subject to: blocks up to 8 storeys only (basement not included); valuer recommending the property as suitable security; must be serviced by a lift if above 4 storeys; studio flats minimum 35 sq/m. Leasehold flats and maisonettes in blocks over 8 storeys constructed after 1 January 2020: up to 80% LTV subject to valuer recommendation; all blocks serviced by a lift. Case by case: Maisonettes; properties above commercial (new build only); properties within close proximity to commercial. Ex-local authority leasehold flats up to 95% LTV subject to: blocks up to 4 storeys only; valuer recommendation; no deck/balcony access; no commercial attached or within close proximity.

### Flats – Buy to Let (updated 7 July 2025)

* Leasehold flats up to 80% LTV, subject to: blocks up to 8 storeys only (basement not included); valuer recommending the property as suitable security; must be serviced by a lift if above 4 storeys; studio flats minimum 35 sq/m. Leasehold flats and maisonettes in blocks over 8 storeys constructed after 1 January 2020: up to 80% LTV subject to valuer recommendation; all blocks serviced by a lift. Case by case: Maisonettes; properties above commercial (new build only); properties within close proximity to commercial. Ex-local authority leasehold flats up to 80% LTV subject to: blocks up to 4 storeys only; valuer recommendation; no deck/balcony access; no commercial attached or within close proximity.

### New build – Residential (updated 7 July 2025)

* New build = property first registered within the last 2 years. New build houses up to 95% LTV; new build flats up to 85% LTV. Property must be built under a recognised warranty scheme (10 years post completion). Leasehold flats/maisonettes in blocks over 8 storeys constructed after 1 January 2020: up to 80% LTV subject to valuer recommendation; lift required. Accepted warranties: NHBC, LABC certificate, Premier Guarantee, BLP (Buildings Defect Insurance), Build Zone, Checkmate (Castle 10), International Construction Warranties (ICW), ARK, Advantage (AHCI), Global Home Warranties, AEDIS, ABC+, Protek Warranty, Compariqo. We will not consider an architect's certificate.

### New build – Buy to Let (updated 7 July 2025)

* New build = property first registered within the last 2 years. New build houses and flats restricted to maximum 80% LTV. Same warranty and block criteria as Residential. Same accepted warranties list.

### Minimum property value

* The minimum property value is £100,000.

### Construction types

* We will lend on standard construction (brick walls, tiled or slate roofs). Subject to valuer: timber framed, thatched, etc. Acceptable: Wimpey No Fines, Easiform. Acceptable once repaired: Airey, Unity, Waites, Cornish, Calder and Aluminium (prefab bungalows). Unacceptable: British Steel Construction, Woolaway, Anglian Construction, Live Work units, High Aluminium Cement. Unusual construction: speak to Business Development team.

### Energy Performance Certificate – Buy to Let

* From 1 April 2018 it is illegal for landlords to start new tenancies without an EPC of 'E' or higher. All BTL properties must have an EPC rating between A and E. Evidence required for all buy to let properties.

### Property condition and age

* No restrictions on age of property; we do not lend on Grade I listed properties. Physical state as per valuation report. Poor physical state may lead to decline. Satisfactory insurance cover with no exclusions must be arranged.

### Property restrictions

* We will not consider properties with restrictions on occupancy (e.g. only 11 months per year, minimum age of occupant, student lets).

### Properties attached to commercial premises

* We do not accept properties over commercial premises. If the security property is near or next to commercial premises, contact Business Development team. Any approvals subject to valuer confirming suitable security and that commercial premises do not affect saleability and letability.

### Flying freeholds

* We will consider properties with a flying freehold subject to it not having a detrimental effect on the value of the property.

### Japanese Knotweed

* The Society will not lend where Japanese Knotweed is known to be present in the grounds of or within close proximity to the security property.

### Leasehold

* We will accept leasehold properties providing there is a minimum of 60 years left on the lease after the completed term of the loan. We will not lend where: ground rent exceeds 0.1% of the property value or £500 a year (whichever is higher); ground rent is not reasonable or has unreasonable multipliers (e.g. doubling every 5, 10 or 15 years); RPI or similar index escalation is acceptable. We will not consider where freehold and leasehold are held by the same owner in the same capacity.

### Agricultural restrictions

* We do not accept properties that have Agricultural Restrictions. Where restrictions will be waived, we will require this in writing from the Local Authority.

### Self-build properties

* We do not currently offer self-build mortgages.

## SECTION 6 – DEPOSIT & INCENTIVES

### Source of deposit

* Acceptable: Savings; equity from the sale of property; gift from a family member (see list). Where the deposit is a gift, establish if it is non-returnable or whether regular payments are required; written confirmation required. We will accept gifted deposits from: Spouse, Civil Partner; Parents, step parents, parents in law; Siblings, half siblings, step siblings, siblings in law; Grandparents, step grandparents; Aunt, Uncle, Niece, Nephew; Children, Step children, children in law, adopted children. Your customer's solicitor will be required to verify the source.

### Deed of gift

* Required where property is in one name and proposed to transfer into two names where the new party is not purchasing their share; or where a deposit is being gifted from a family member (see Source of deposit). We require the customer's solicitor to arrange an indemnity policy to protect our interests on completion; cost paid by your customer.

### Builders deposit / incentives

* Where a builder/developer provides a financial incentive (e.g. cash back), your customer must still provide a minimum deposit (based on the full purchase price) from their own resources. Maximum incentive is 5% of the property value as confirmed by the valuer. All incentives should be disclosed to the valuer.

## SECTION 7 – EMPLOYMENT

### Self-employed – Residential

* We require the last 2 years income evidence (not more than 18 months old): tax calculations plus corresponding tax year overview; full financial accounts. Where there is an improving trend, the most recent year can be considered provided sufficient evidence that income is sustainable. Further proof or a projection may be required at Underwriter discretion. We will only accept projections and accounts signed by an accountant holding one of: ICAEW, ACCA, AAPA, CIMA, CPAA, AAT, AIA, CIOT, IFA, CIPFA. The source of the evidence must be independent of your customer.

### Self-employed – Buy to Let

* We require proof of income for the last 2 years (not more than 18 months old). For buy to lets up to 80% LTV we will need the last 2 years: tax calculations plus corresponding tax year overview. Where there is an improving trend, the most recent year can be used for loan to income if sustainable. Same accountant qualifications and independence as Residential.

### Contractors and CIS scheme

* Required: Current contracts; previous contracts (if within last 2 years); SA302's (if under CIS); last 6 months invoices; last 6 months bank statements showing invoice credits. Zero hour contracts will not be accepted.

### Contracts of employment

* We accept contracts provided: contracts with well-established organisations; at least 6 months of the contract to run at the time of mortgage offer; an indication of the likelihood of contract renewal; they are not Zero hour contracts.

### Multiple jobs

* Income from multiple jobs will be considered where at least 6 months have elapsed since the additional job commenced, the additional occupation is deemed sustainable and the required documentation can be provided.

### Probation

* We will consider customers within a probation period at Underwriter discretion. We will request an income reference to confirm employment is permanent and at minimum the customer's first payslip before proceeding to mortgage offer. Maximum LTV is 80%.

### Armed Forces personnel

* Individual consideration; not penalised for frequent address changes or living outside the UK. We may require a letter from the customer's Commanding Officer to confirm address history. If deposit is from Forces Help to Buy scheme, we will require the monthly commitment for the loan included in affordability; maximum 80% LTV. We will take into account any 'Notice of Correction' on the credit file. Business Development team can discuss temporary lets for overseas or UK posting.

## SECTION 8 – IDENTIFICATION & PROOF OF ADDRESS

* Under the Money Laundering regulations we must confirm the identity and address details of all mortgage applicants (if present). We require three years address history for each customer at DIP; proof of address once the application has been submitted. We require customers to be on the voters roll at the correspondence address (verified at credit check). If we are unable to locate your customer on the voters roll, we will need further information and possibly further documentation. We will require certified copies to be uploaded to your Online Intermediary Portal. Proof of identification is required once the application has been submitted. If your customer is based abroad, contact your Business Development team.

## SECTION 9 – INTEREST ONLY, PART & PART & RIO

### Interest only – Residential

* Minimum loan size £75,000. Maximum loan size £750,000. Maximum age at end of mortgage term 80. Interest only maximum 70% LTV when using endowment, stocks and shares ISA, UK pension or investment portfolio as repayment plan; maximum 66% LTV when using sale of property. Affordability may be assessed on interest only; if there is a payment into a repayment plan, the premium or contribution must be taken into account. Acceptable repayment strategies: Sale of property (must be the property being mortgaged; customer must have minimum £225,000 equity on completion); Endowment (up to 80% of middle figure of the 3 shown); Stocks and Shares ISA (up to 80% of current value); UK Pension (maximum 50% of projected tax free lump sum); Investment plan/portfolio (up to 80% of current value). Unacceptable: Cash ISAs; General savings; Inheritance; Sale of non-property assets; Overpayments (as total repayment); Sale of security on death; Bonuses. Your customer must sign an interest only mortgage declaration.

### Part and part

* Your customer may take a mortgage on a part repayment and part interest only basis. Our interest only criteria apply only to the interest only part. Maximum LTV is 80% for part and part mortgages.

### Retirement Interest Only (updated 22 November 2024)

* We will consider RIO for purchase, remortgage, product switch or additional borrowing, subject to product availability. Minimum loan size £50,000. Maximum loan size up to 55% LTV is £750,000. Minimum age at start of loan 55 (youngest customer). Maximum age at start of loan 85 (youngest customer). Single or joint borrowers only; for joint applications both customers must demonstrate affordability independently. No minimum equity requirement. We will only consider lending on the customer's main residence; second homes not accepted. Only Pension, Investment Plan and BTL rental income will be included when assessing income. We will assess affordability on an Interest Only basis over the term of the loan.

## SECTION 10 – REMORTGAGE & ADDITIONAL BORROWING

### Additional borrowing (updated 5 July 2023)

* You are able to submit an additional borrowing request on your customer's behalf. We will ensure the existing account has been conducted satisfactorily without any arrears. We will complete the legal work in-house except in the following circumstances (Solicitor appointed by your customer): Shared Ownership where new funds are used to purchase an additional share; Shared Equity; Where a collateral charge is required; Transfers of Equity. There may be additional legal matters not covered by our in-house legals – contact Business Development team. A new valuation may be requested (confirmed at DIP). Refer to Additional borrowing guide for further details.

### Remortgage package – Residential (updated 8 September 2023)

* We will lend up to 90% of the value on a remortgage with capital raising and up to 95% LTV for a like-for-like remortgage, providing your customer has owned the property for a minimum of 6 months. If the property is unencumbered and your customer is capital raising, we treat this as a remortgage and customers can benefit from the remortgage package. Our remortgage package offers free legals and a free valuation for standard remortgage only (excludes Transfer of Equity, Lease Extensions, First Registration, Title Restrictions; these costs covered by your customer – see Fee Tariff). Available on certain products only and subject to minimum loan amounts.

### Remortgage package – Buy to Let (updated 8 September 2023)

* We will lend up to 80% of the value on a remortgage, providing your customer has owned the property for a minimum of 6 months. For buy to let expats and holiday lets, maximum LTV is 75%. If unencumbered and capital raising, we treat as remortgage and customers can benefit from the remortgage package. Free legals and free valuation for standard remortgage only (excludes additional legal work – see Fee Tariff). Available on certain products only and subject to minimum loan amounts.

## SECTION 11 – BUY TO LET SPECIFIC

### AST and letting agents

* We will accept an Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST) for a minimum term of 6 months and maximum term of 24 months. Only buy to lets on a single household basis (individuals, couples or family unit). We will accept no more than 2 persons on a single AST. Non-standard tenancy: contact Business Development team. Rental income achievable will be assessed by the valuer on the basis of an AST to a single household (not per usable bedroom). We do not accept HMOs. If a letting agent is required as an approval condition, a qualified letting agent must be engaged; if your customer can demonstrate relevant experience this condition may be waived.

### Rental income calculation (updated 26 March 2024)

* Main criteria for assessing ability to repay is Interest Coverage Ratio (ICR) against rental income. Gross rental income from the security property must be at least 140% of the required mortgage payment when calculated at the relevant stress rate. Where 2-year fixed or discounted rate: stress rate = pay rate + 2%. Where 5-year fixed: stress rate = pay rate. We will apply the lower of current rent or estimated rental value from the valuer (excluding Holiday Lets). Top slicing may be considered on standard Buy to Lets – see Top Slicing section.

### Rental void

* If the property needs renovating and/or a tenant will not be in place within the first 2 months following completion, we will need confirmation that other funds are available to cover the rental void. Contact Business Development team.

### Let to Buy

* We will consider cases where your customer is looking to turn their current residential property into a Buy to Let and capital raise to support the onward purchase. Maximum LTV is 80%.

### Regulated buy to let – intention to move back to property

* If your customer intends to occupy their buy to let property in the future, this is a regulated buy to let. Your customer will need to choose a residential product and affordability will be assessed as per a residential application using personal income only. Where interest only is selected, the case must fit our residential loan interest only policy. An AST must be in place. Income from the tenant cannot be taken into account when assessing affordability.

### Regulated buy to let – let to a family member

* If your customer is buying or remortgaging a property to let to a family member, this is a regulated mortgage contract. The family member must sign a form of consent. An AST is not required; a residential product must be selected. Family member: Spouse, Civil Partner, Parent, Brother, Sister, Son, Daughter, Grandson, Granddaughter or Grandparent. Affordability as per residential mortgage; payment for the second mortgage must be included as committed expenditure with monthly household costs and stressed per current guidelines. Income from the family member living in the property cannot be taken into account.

### Unacceptable letting types

* We will not accept: Sale and leaseback where the tenant is the previous owner; Letting to directors/shareholders/members of the SPV limited company/LLP which is your customer's or any associated SPV/LLP; Letting to embassies or tenants with diplomatic immunity; Regulated or sitting tenants; Premium leases (all rent paid in advance at start of tenancy); Room-by-room letting with multiple tenancy agreements; Properties subject to restrictions (e.g. less than 52 week occupancy; students only; over 55's only); Mixed use properties.

### Portfolio Landlords (updated 25 September 2024)

* We accept applications from Buy to Let portfolio landlords up to 75% LTV. Portfolio = landlords with four or more mortgaged Buy to Let properties in their personal name or company owned BTL rental properties. At DIP stage the following must be provided: BTL Portfolio Spreadsheet; Financial Statements and/or Tax Returns; Last 2 years Tax Calculations and Tax Year Overview; Statement of Assets and Liabilities; Bank Statements to confirm rental income and mortgage payments of the portfolio. Your customer must sign a Portfolio Landlord mortgage declaration. We can only accept applications where the portfolio can be fully reviewed using the above documentation; we are unable to accept where the portfolio is managed by a Limited Company/Partnership. No limit on number of properties; where Portfolio LTV is greater than 75% this is unacceptable. For multiple applicants tested on an individual portfolio basis. We will not offer Top Slicing for Portfolio Landlords.

### Top Slicing (updated 28 July 2025)

* We will use your customer's personal income to cover any shortfall in rent needed to obtain the loan amount required. Our residential affordability assessment will need to be completed to show the shortfall is affordable (income and monthly expenditure). Top slicing is limited to landlords with 1–3 mortgaged buy to let properties; not available to portfolio landlords, expats or Let to Buy applicants. Properties must be self-sufficient: existing rental income equal to or greater than 110% cover of the pay rate (applies to any other BTLs owned). Where your customer has a high level of debt or Underwriters are concerned about residual affordability, Top Slicing may not be agreed. Speak to Business Development team before submission.

### Holiday Lets (updated 9 April 2025)

* A specific product range is available for Holiday Lets up to 75% LTV (subject to availability). We will also allow Holiday Lets for Expats (Expat product must be selected). Minimum loan size £50,000. Maximum loan size £1,000,000. Minimum income £25,000 per application. We require a letter from a local holiday letting agency confirming high, medium and low season rates; an average over a 30 week period is taken to calculate annual rental income. Gross rental income from the security property must be at least 140% of the required mortgage payment (interest only at the relevant stress rate). EPC rating E or above. Stress rate: 2-year fixed/discounted = pay rate + 2%; 5-year fixed = pay rate. Acceptable: new and established Holiday Lets; Airbnb and similar; property can be personally used up to 90 days per annum. Unacceptable: additional properties on sites; holiday parks; B&Bs; properties with restrictive usage or holiday let covenants; temporary or moveable properties. Top slicing is not available.

### Expatriates – Buy to Let (updated 9 April 2025)

* Buy to let mortgages for expatriates will be considered for British citizens up to 75% LTV, subject to product availability. All expats must have: UK passport; UK bank account; history of living and working in the UK; UK correspondence address (we will carry out a credit search on this address); minimum £25,000 employed income (self-employed only if business is based in the UK). With the exception of financial sanctions, all countries and currencies will be considered at Underwriter discretion. First time landlords considered in line with the above; consumer buy to lets acceptable where the property has been let for a minimum of 24 months prior. We do not allow First time Buyers for Buy to Lets. If your customer owns a property outside the UK, we will require a copy of their mortgage statement to confirm they are an owner/occupier. You must: select an Expat Buy to Let product (maximum loan size £1,000,000); provide confirmation of UK correspondence address; confirm details of your customer's circumstances. Rental calculations: 2-year fixed/discounted stress rate = pay rate + 2%; 5-year fixed stress rate = pay rate. Gross rental income must be at least 140% of the required mortgage payment at the relevant stress rate. Top slicing is not available.

### Investment property loan (professional landlord)

* A professional landlord is defined as someone already renting out a property or getting a mortgage for the specific purpose of running a buy to let business. A professional has more than 1 buy to let property.

## SECTION 12 – RESIDENTIAL SCHEMES

### Shared Ownership (updated 11 September 2023)

* We will consider loans on Shared Ownership properties for purchase and remortgage up to 95% LTV throughout England and Wales. Arrangements must be through a registered Housing Association. The scheme must use the Standard Model Lease which contains a Mortgage Protection Clause (MPC) covering the initial loan and all additional lending/staircasing. We will lend up to 95% of the share (includes new build flats and houses). Minimum loan £50,000. Maximum loan £400,000. 25% of the share upwards can be purchased, maximum 75%. No requirement to have 100% staircasing within the lease but The Cambridge must be able to staircase fully. Repayment mortgages only. Capital raising and additional borrowing considered for staircasing only. We are unable to accept debt exceeding the equity in the share (exception: hire purchase). Affordability includes stressed mortgage payment and rent/service charge to the Housing Association. Existing Shared Ownership customers: refer to Business Development team.

### Shared equity purchase schemes

* We will consider loans on properties that are part of a Shared Equity arrangement through the Cambridge Colleges only. Contact Business Development team.

### Help to Buy Equity Loan – Remortgage

* We will consider a remortgage where the Help to Buy equity loan is remaining on completion, up to 75% LTV. Minimum loan amount £50,000. Maximum loan £450,000. Maximum value £600,000. Staircasing acceptable only to repay part of the equity loan. We will require the same term as the original agreement. Repayment of the equity loan will be required to assess affordability; the equity loan must be declared as a commitment with a monthly payment calculated at 1.75% and included in the affordability assessment.

### Right to Buy (updated 8 January 2025)

* Right to Buy = council tenant buying their council house at a discounted price. We will consider up to 100% of the discounted Right to Buy price (subject to the loan not exceeding 80% of the full value of the property). We are currently only accepting Purchase applications. Customers requesting a mortgage will need to be named on the Section 125 Notice.

### Second homes

* We will accept both purchase and remortgages for second homes providing your customer can demonstrate satisfactory affordability to cover the running cost of the second home alongside their monthly expenditure. We must have confirmation that full insurance cover is arranged when the property is unoccupied; the property must not be permanently let out or used for holiday lettings. Properties to be used as the main residence of an immediate family member will be considered – see Regulated buy to let – let to a family member.

### Tied accommodation

* We will lend to customers living in tied accommodation to purchase another property to be their future residence. The loan is regulated (FCA). We will require a product from our residential mortgage range and will assess affordability using your customer's personal income. As the security property will be let on completion, we will require an AST. Maximum LTV is 80%.

### Temporary consent to let

* Requests for existing customers to let the mortgaged property will be considered on an individual basis. If letting is for up to 24 months the applicant will remain on their existing product and will be required to pay an administration fee. An AST and Letting Agent must be in place. Where the repayment method is being changed to interest only the case must fit our residential loan interest only policy. Requests must be in line with existing buy to let criteria.

## SECTION 13 – OTHER

### Back-to-back mortgages

* We do not accept back-to-back mortgages.

### Foreign currency

* We do not accept foreign currency loans for: Residential mortgages; Transfer of Equity; Porting. For existing customers we can consider using foreign currency income for product switches or additional borrowing – contact Business Development team.

### Payment holidays

* We will ask you to confirm if your customer is currently taking a payment holiday on their mortgage, credit cards, loans or any other financial commitment at DIP or application stage. Where your customer is currently taking a payment holiday or making part repayments on financial commitments, we will be unable to consider any new lending until the payment holiday has finished and they are making repayments in full. Evidence of repayments may be requested at Underwriter discretion.

### Portability (updated 11 October 2023)

* Your customer's existing mortgage may be portable (outlined in their mortgage offer). If your customer can port their mortgage, the redemption of their existing mortgage and the completion of their new purchase must be simultaneous. For how to guide or further information, contact the Business Development Team.

### Lending into retirement

* We have no upper age limit when considering a mortgage application. Our underwriting allows us to make informed individual assessments and recognise mortgage affordability based on current and future incomes. Supporting evidence may be required: pension projections; company/private pensions; investments; rental income. For joint customers, confirmation is to be sought as to the remaining customer's entitlement in the event of the death of the main income earner.

### Repayment methods

* We offer the following, subject to product availability: Capital and interest; Interest only; Part and part (mix of capital and interest and interest only). Buy to lets: Capital and interest; Interest only; Part and part.

### Buildings and Contents Insurance (updated 30 October 2024)

* It is the customer's responsibility to make adequate insurance arrangements and keep these in place for the lifetime of the mortgage.

### Asbestos

* If asbestos is present please refer the case to your Business Development team for guidance.

## SECTION 14 – FEES & PROCURATION

### Fees – Residential (updated 1 August 2024)

* The completion fee for Residential mortgages can be added to the mortgage loan exceeding the maximum LTV. Application fees are non-refundable and payable when submitting the Full Mortgage Application. Your mortgage product is secured once the Application is submitted. If you would like to change your mortgage product after this, you can do so without further fees whilst the case status is pre-mortgage offer. Once the mortgage offer has been produced, to change the product any fees associated with the new rate will be due.

### Fees – Buy to Let (updated 1 August 2024)

* The completion fee for Buy to Let mortgages can be added to the mortgage loan exceeding the maximum LTV, however this is capped at 2% of the loan. Where it is an ex-pat BTL we will accept a maximum fee of £2,000 or a maximum LTV of 77%, whichever is higher. Application fees are non-refundable and payable when submitting the Full Mortgage Application. Product secured once Application is submitted. Product change without further fees whilst case status is pre-mortgage offer; after offer produced, fees for new rate due.

### Procuration fees (updated 28 May 2024)

* We will pay a procuration fee for introducing a mortgage case (residential and buy to let, additional borrowing and product switches). Fees are calculated as a percentage of the loan amount and paid after completion. New business and additional borrowing: Partner networks and mortgage clubs 0.4% gross; Directly authorised and non partner networks 0.3% gross. Product switches 0.3% gross. Partner networks and mortgage clubs: Legal & General, Sesame Bankhall Group, Paradigm, PRIMIS, Mortgage Intelligence, Next Intelligence, Quilter, Tenet, TMA Club, SimplyBiz, Brilliant Solutions. Minimum procuration fee for purchases, remortgages and additional borrowing £150. Product switches minimum £112.50. No maximum. No procuration fee for product switches within the first 6 months from the latest product switch or from completion of the original mortgage. No fee if your client chooses to port their mortgage within 6 months of completion. Fee paid only to PRA and FCA authorised Intermediaries.

## SECTION 16 – JOINT BORROWER SOLE PROPRIETOR & JOINT TENANTS

### Joint Borrower Sole Proprietor (JBSP) (updated 15 April 2024)

* We will consider JBSP applications up to 95% LTV with your customers using the additional income of close family members to support affordability. We can accept up to 4 applicants and incomes, with a maximum of 2 occupying borrowers and 2 non-occupying borrowers. The family members supporting the application will not live in the property (non-occupying borrowers). Only the occupying borrowers will be named as the legal owners and must not own any other property at the time of completion. Supporting family member may be: Spouse, Civil Partner; Parents; Siblings; Grandparents; Aunt, Uncle, Niece, Nephew; Children. Non-occupying borrowers are responsible with the occupier(s) for the total mortgage balance including monthly payments and must take Independent Legal Advice. JBSP is available across our standard residential products, excluding specialist schemes. Repayment mortgages only. For remortgages, additional legal charges apply – see lender guide.

### Joint tenants

* On death, the survivor automatically receives the share of the property of the deceased (even if the first to die made a will leaving their share to someone else). This is the method usually chosen for married couples or those in a Civil Partnership.
