# Overview

Rely Mortgages offers Buy to Let mortgages for limited company applicants. The lender provides flexible BTL products suitable for property investors.

## Key Features

* Improved valuations: AVM and desktop valuations possible, with additional options for complex and long form valuations
* Geographic coverage: England and Wales only
* Security: First legal charge required
* Adverse credit: Accepted (No adverse, Light adverse, Light adverse+)
* Property types: Standard BTL, HMOs, Student Lets, MUFBs, Holiday Lets (via InterBay)
* Limited company lending available for SPVs, LLPs, and other corporate structures
* Intercompany loans accepted subject to specific criteria

# Adverse Credit Criteria

Adverse credit assessment applies to directors/guarantors of the limited company.

## No Adverse

* CCJs: Must be satisfied at time of application, 0 in 72 months where ≥£300
* Defaults: Must be settled at time of application, 0 in 72 months
* Secured missed payments: 0 in 36 months
* Unsecured worst status: 0 in 6 months

## Light Adverse

* CCJs: Must be satisfied at time of application, 0 in 24 months where ≥£300
* Defaults: Must be settled at time of application, 0 in 12 months, 1 in 24 months
* Secured missed payments: 0 in 36 months
* Unsecured worst status: 1 in 6 months

## Light Adverse+

* CCJs: Must be satisfied at time of application, 0 in 12 months, 1 in 24 months (max £3000) where ≥£300
* Defaults: Must be settled at time of application, 0 in 24 months
* Secured missed payments: 0 in 12 months, 1 in 36 months
* Unsecured worst status: 2 in 6 months

# Concrete Construction

The following are unacceptable:

* Properties designated defective under Part XVI Housing Act 1985
* Pre-Cast Reinforced Concrete (PRC) properties where the property and adjoining properties have not been repaired under a scheme licensed by PRC Homes Ltd
* Properties with mundic concrete classified as Class B or Class C

# Consumer Buy to Let (CBTL) Lending

Applications classified as Consumer Buy to Let (CBTL) cannot be considered.

# Convictions

Applications cannot be considered until a criminal conviction is spent (applies to directors/guarantors).

Further details:
[https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rehabilitation-periods](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rehabilitation-periods)

# Energy Efficiency Regulations

Under the Energy Efficiency (Private Rented Property) (England and Wales) Regulations 2015, and related legislation:

* The solicitor must ensure the borrower is aware of the requirement for properties rented in the private sector to normally meet the minimum EPC rating required by law.

# Ex-Local Authority Flats

The following are unacceptable:

* Properties within the Right to Buy pre-emption period
* Flats where there is no active and transactional market within the development

# Exposure Limits

* Limits do not apply where lending on multiple units under a single freehold
* Developments with ≤8 units: no maximum unit exposure
* Developments with >8 units: maximum 20% exposure

# First-Time Landlords Including HMOs

Lending is not acceptable where no applicant (director or guarantor) is an existing homeowner (either residential owner-occupied or Buy to Let).

# Flats

## Acceptable

* Self-contained flats, maisonettes, or apartments with private facilities and direct access via common parts
* Blocks of flats where units are not self-contained or otherwise individually mortgageable, on a block investment basis only

Where the property is above commercial premises or a new build, it may proceed only if no adverse comments are raised by the valuer or conveyancer.

## Blocks of Flats

* Must be readily saleable and mortgageable
* Cannot be constructed of Large Panel Systems (LPS)

Studio flats with less than 30m² gross internal area are not acceptable.

## Ex-Local Authority Flats

Unacceptable where:

* Within the Right to Buy pre-emption period
* There is no active market in the block or development

# Flying Freehold

Flying freeholds are acceptable provided the affected area does not exceed 20% of the total floor area.

# HMOs (Houses in Multiple Occupation)

HMO properties are acceptable.

An HMO is generally defined as a property:

* Occupied by more than one household
* Occupied by more than two people

This may include:

* Bedsits
* Shared houses
* Non-self-contained flats
* Some self-contained flats

Any property meeting these criteria will be treated as an HMO for lending purposes, regardless of tenancy arrangements.

## Planning Permission

HMO properties must have appropriate planning permission:

* Up to 6 bedrooms: C4 planning (where Article 4 removes permitted development rights)
* 7+ bedrooms: Sui Generis planning

Where formal planning permission has not been granted:

* A Certificate of Lawful Use is acceptable.

If neither exists:

* Confirmation of continued lawful HMO use must be obtained by the acting solicitor.

## Licensing

### Remortgages

The solicitor must confirm:

* Sight of the HMO licence, or
* Confirmation that a licence application has been made before funds are released.

### Purchases (≤6 Bedrooms)

* If already licensed: solicitor confirms licence and applicant undertakes to transfer licence into their name
* If not licensed but the applicant holds a licence elsewhere: solicitor confirms licence and applicant undertakes to apply within 60 days of completion
* If the applicant holds no licence: applicant must apply within 60 days of completion

### Purchases (≥7 Bedrooms)

* A copy of the licence on the security, or
* Evidence that the applicant holds a licence on another property

Must be received before funds are released.

# Holiday Lets

If a landlord customer requires a holiday let mortgage, the lender’s sister company InterBay may assist.

Further information: InterBay.co.uk

# Intercompany Loans

Intercompany loans are acceptable subject to the following:

* Majority shareholder must be consistent across the SPV and connected company
* If no single majority shareholder exists, the ownership structures must match
* Must be UK-to-UK companies (no offshore companies)
* Loan must be repayable and documented
* Loan term must not exceed the mortgage term
* Interest rate must reflect HMRC’s ordinary rate
* Any monthly payment must be included in ICR calculations
* The connected company must not hold any interest or charge over the bank’s security

* Japanese knotweed is classified as Class A or Class B
* Classified as Class C without a satisfactory management plan

Where the property is above commercial premises or is a new build, the application may proceed only if no adverse comments are raised by the valuer or conveyancer.

## Remortgage

Remortgages cannot be accepted for the following purposes:

* To shore up a business
* To repay gambling debts

Capital raising is acceptable for any legal purpose. The amount and purpose of capital being raised must be established.

Remortgages will be considered where the security property has been owned for less than 6 months and is not a new build.

* If improvement works have been undertaken, the LTV will be based on the post-works market value.
* If no improvement works have been undertaken, the LTV will be based on the lower of the original purchase price and the current market value.

Where the property has recently been inherited:

* Probate (or letters of administration, where applicable) must be granted prior to application.
* Probate must be verified prior to offer.

## Rental Income Requirements

Rental income requirements depend on the property profile.

### Standard BTL

Definition:

* A single dwelling
* HMO or student let with ≤6 letting rooms
* ≤6 flats in one block under one title

Rental cover requirements:

* Higher/additional rate taxpayer: 140%
* Basic rate taxpayer / Ltd Co / LLP: 125%

### Complex BTL

Definition:

* HMO or student let with more than 6 letting rooms
* More than 6 flats in one block under one loan
* Multiple houses under one title

Rental cover requirements:

* Higher/additional rate taxpayer: 175%
* Basic rate taxpayer / Ltd Co / LLP: 145%

## Source of Deposit

The deposit should normally come from the applicant’s own resources without recourse to additional borrowing.

The following alternative sources may be acceptable.

### Capital Raising Remortgage / Secured Loan

Where the applicant has sufficient equity in an existing property:

* Deposit funds may be raised via a remortgage or second charge.

### Gifted Family Deposit

Subject to the following:

* The amount must be a non-refundable gift.
* Funds must not originate from a child’s bank or savings account.
* The individual providing the gift must have no interest in the property.
* Appropriate deed of gift indemnity insurance must be arranged by the acting solicitor.

### Director Loan

Subject to the following:

* The individual is transferring or purchasing a property into a company structure.
* Purchase price must be at full market value to ensure all tax liabilities are satisfied.
* The person providing the loan must be a shareholder in the company.
* The investment will be recorded as a director’s loan within the company accounts.
* The difference between the mortgage and the purchase price must be covered by the director loan.
* The loan must be non-interest bearing.

### Inter-Company Loan

Subject to the following:

* The majority shareholder must be consistent across the SPV and connected company.
* If there is no single majority shareholder, ownership structures must be consistent.
* Must be UK-to-UK companies (no offshore companies).
* The loan must be repayable with a documented loan agreement.
* Loan term must not exceed the mortgage term.
* Interest rate must reflect HMRC’s ordinary rate.
* Any monthly payments must be included in the ICR calculation.
* The connected company must not hold any interest or charge over the lender’s security.

### Vendor Equity / Deposit and Builder Deposit / Incentive

* The value of the incentive must be deducted from the purchase price or value (whichever is lower).
* Lending will be based on the lower figure.

## Tenancy

The property must be in a suitable condition to be let.

### England

The property may be let under:

* A single Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST), or
* A contractual or common law tenancy (e.g. company let or where annual rent exceeds £100,000).

### Wales

The property may be let under an Occupational Contract in accordance with Welsh Government tenancy rules.

Multiple tenancies are acceptable for HMOs and MUFBs.

### Tenancy Agreement Requirements

A written tenancy agreement must restrict the tenant from:

* Sharing, assigning, sub-letting, multi-letting, charging or parting with possession of the property
* Using the property for anything other than a private dwelling
* Making alterations or allowing the property to fall into disrepair

Additional requirements:

* Maximum fixed term: 36 months.
* Where the tenancy exceeds 12 months, the AST must include a rent review provision at least every 12 months unless the mortgage is fixed for the same duration.

Applications will not be considered where:

* Tenants are family members of the borrower.
* The tenant has or may acquire an overriding interest in the property.

## Tenure

### Freehold

Applicable to:

* Houses
* MUFBs

### Leasehold

* The lease must have at least 50 years remaining at the end of the mortgage term.
* Subject to satisfactory valuation.

Where the remaining lease term is less than 85 years:

* Maximum LTV will be restricted to 75%.
