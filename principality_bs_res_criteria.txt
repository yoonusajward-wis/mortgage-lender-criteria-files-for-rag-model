# Principality Building Society – Residential Mortgage Criteria

---

**LENDER:** Principality Building Society
**PRODUCT:** Residential Mortgage
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Lending Criteria
**LAST UPDATED:** 2025
**FOR INTERMEDIARY USE ONLY**

**Source:** https://www.principality.co.uk/intermediaries/our-mortgage-lending-criteria/our-full-lending-criteria

---

## SECTION 1 – LEGAL FEES

- Where the product allows for legal fee assistance, solicitors used are Optima Legal or O'Neill Patient Solicitor (ONP).

---

## SECTION 2 – LOAN TO VALUE LIMITS (RESIDENTIAL PURCHASES)

### New Purchase

- At 95% LTV: maximum loan size £500,000. The 95% must include any product fees.
- At 75–90% LTV: maximum loan size £650,000.
- At 65–75% LTV: maximum loan size £750,000.
- At below 65% LTV: maximum loan size £1,000,000.

### New Build

- At 95% LTV (excluding flats — capped at 90%): maximum loan size £650,000. The 95% must include any product fees.

### Remortgage

- At 75–90% LTV: maximum loan size £650,000.
- At 65–75% LTV: maximum loan size £750,000.
- At below 65% LTV: maximum loan size £1,000,000.

### Debt Consolidation (Capital Repayment Basis)

- Maximum LTV: 75%.
- Maximum loan size: £750,000.

---

## SECTION 3 – MORTGAGE TERM

- Minimum repayment term: 5 years.
- Maximum term: 40 years.
- Maximum term where any element is interest only: 25 years.
- Minimum applicant age (residential): 18.
- Maximum age at end of term (residential repayment): mortgage must be repaid on or before the eldest applicant's 85th birthday.
- Maximum age at end of term where any element is interest only: mortgage must be repaid on or before the eldest applicant's 70th birthday.
- Leasehold requirement: minimum unexpired lease term at start of mortgage is 85 years.

---

## SECTION 4 – PRODUCT FEE

- Product fee can be added to the loan if total borrowing meets the affordability calculation.
- At 95% LTV: adding a product fee must not exceed the maximum LTV cap for the product.
- All product fees are non-refundable and non-transferable.

---

## SECTION 5 – LENDING IN ENGLAND AND WALES

- Allowed: freehold and leasehold properties in England and Wales.

### Minimum Loan Amounts

- Minimum loan (new residential advance): £5,000.
- Minimum loan (new build, buy to let or holiday let): £25,000.

### Standard Variable Rate

- If the standard variable rate increases or decreases, the discounted rate paid will also increase or decrease accordingly.
- The standard variable rate applies at the end of the fixed or discount period.

### Overpayments

- Overpayments allowed on all non-flexible residential, buy to let and holiday let mortgages.
- Maximum annual overpayment: 10% of outstanding balance as at 1 January each calendar year.
- Regular or lump sum overpayments are allowed.
- Overpayments above the annual allowance may incur an early repayment charge.

---

## SECTION 6 – VALUATIONS

### Desk Valuation

Allowed for residential purchase or remortgage applications with LTV cap of 75%, subject to:
- Property value: maximum £500,000.
- Maximum loan size: £400,000.
- Must be a house.
- Not listed.
- No more than 2 storeys.
- Standard construction.

### All Other Application Types

- A physical valuation is required.

---

## SECTION 7 – FOREIGN NATIONALS

**Requirements:**
- Applicant must be UK resident at time of application.
- Minimum 2 years UK residential address history.
- Address history must be a UK address.

**EEA nationals resident in the UK — lending accepted with:**
- Permanent legal right of residence; or
- Pre-settled status; or
- Settled status.

**Non-EEA nationals resident in the UK — lending accepted with:**
- Indefinite leave to remain; or
- More than 12 months remaining on visa at time of application.

**Joint applications:**
- Only one applicant must meet the visa criteria.

- Maximum LTV for EEA and non-EEA applicants: 95%, subject to successfully passing a credit check.
- Strong cases up to 90% LTV outside criteria may be discussed with the local BDM.

---

## SECTION 8 – DEPOSIT

- Deposit and purchase costs must be from the applicant's own resources.
- Gifts from family, including step-family, are allowed.

---

## SECTION 9 – APPLICANT NUMBERS

- Maximum number of applicants: 4.
- All applicants' incomes are considered.
- All 4 applicants must live at the property.

---

## SECTION 10 – INTEREST ONLY (RESIDENTIAL)

- Maximum LTV: 50%.
- Part interest only and part repayment is allowed, provided total borrowing does not exceed 50% LTV.
- Maximum term: 25 years.
- Maximum age at end of term: repayment required on or before the eldest applicant's 70th birthday.
- Minimum equity required: £150,000.
- Evidence of an acceptable repayment strategy with documentary proof is required.
- All income is assessed using the standard affordability calculator on a repayment basis.
- Capital raising for debt consolidation on an interest only basis: not acceptable.

---

## SECTION 11 – DEBT CONSOLIDATION

- Allowed where the new loan is affordable and part of the advance is used to repay secured or unsecured debts, including agreements with family or friends.

**Conditions:**
- Maximum LTV: 75%.
- Capital repayment only.
- A special condition is added to the mortgage offer requiring repayment of debts listed in the application.
- All current debts (including those being repaid) must be included in the affordability calculation.

**Proof of Address:**
- If the applicant is not on the electoral register, proof of address is required for the last 3 years.
- UK address required.

---

## SECTION 12 – JOINT BORROWER SOLE PROPRIETOR (JBSP)

- Allowed for joint residential new purchase applications between an applicant and a sponsor.

**Acceptable sponsor relationships:**
- Parent.
- Grandparent.
- Child.
- Grandchild.
- Sibling.
- Spouse.
- Legal guardian.

- All income from both applicant and sponsor is used for affordability.
- Sponsor does not need to be on the deeds.
- Sponsor cannot live in the property.
- Available for purchase only — remortgage applications are not acceptable.

---

## SECTION 13 – FORCES HELP TO BUY

- Applications from armed forces personnel are allowed.
- Confirmation document from employer is required.
- Any scheme loan repayment or required residence costs must be included as expenditure in the affordability calculation.

---

## SECTION 14 – LET TO BUY

- Required: copy of Assured Shorthold Tenancy Agreement (England) or Written Statement of Standard Occupation Contract (Wales), or a letting agency letter confirming intention to let.
- If another lender is involved, a consent to let letter is required.

**Rental coverage requirements:**
- 5-year fixed products: 145% of monthly mortgage payment at 5.50%.
- 2-year fixed products: 7.65% as rental coverage.

---

## SECTION 15 – AFFORDABLE HOUSING SCHEMES

### Help to Buy Wales

- Minimum deposit: 5%.

### Right to Buy (Council Schemes)

- Mortgage loan names must match those receiving the discount.
- Maximum lending: 95% of property value.
- Where a resale restriction clause exists (e.g. Section 157 or 106): maximum loan is 95% of the lower of the valuation and the tenant's discounted purchase price.
- Additional funds for home improvements on right to buy: not acceptable.
- Property type restriction: houses only, no flats.

### Shared Equity

- Rent must be included as a cost in the affordability assessment.

---

## SECTION 16 – AGE LIMITS (RESIDENTIAL)

- Minimum age: 18.
- Standard repayment age limit: before the eldest applicant's 76th birthday.
- Underwriter discretion: may consider repayment before the eldest applicant's 85th birthday.
- Interest only: repayment required before the eldest applicant's 70th birthday.

### Joint Borrowers

- Where the term extends beyond the oldest borrower's 76th birthday, the underwriter must be satisfied the loan remains affordable following the death of either borrower.

### Retired Applicants

Where lending extends past age 70 or the intended retirement age (whichever comes first):

If retirement is more than 10 years away AND the term does not extend beyond age 75:
- Affordability assessed using current income.
- Proof of payment into a private pension required (payslip, bank statement, or pension statement).

In all other circumstances:
- Affordability assessed based on projected pension income.

---

## SECTION 17 – PROPERTY TYPES

### Maximum LTV by Buyer Type

- First time buyers: 95%.
- Home movers: 90%.
- Remortgage (residential houses, flats, maisonettes, apartments): 90%.

### Second Homes (for borrower's own use)

- Maximum LTV: 75%.

### Leasehold Properties

- Minimum 85 years remaining on the lease at time of application.

### Flats, Studios, Maisonettes and Apartments

- Allowed if the block is no more than 10 storeys.
- Between 6–10 storeys: only if a good quality residential development for private occupation, built by an established recognised developer.
- Flats and maisonettes must be leasehold.
- Ex local authority or housing association flats are allowed where demand is high and the accommodation is desirable.

### New Builds

- Buy to let new build: maximum LTV 75%.
- Residential new build houses: maximum LTV 95%.
- Residential new build flats: maximum LTV 90%.

### Not Acceptable

- Properties with a restricted occupancy clause (Section 157 or 106).

---

## SECTION 18 – PORTING

### Early Repayment Charge Waiver

An ERC waiver is allowed if:
- Redemption occurs 90 calendar days or less from expiration of the product special rate restriction; and
- There is simultaneous redemption and completion of a new mortgage loan with Principality.

**ERC will not be waived where:**
- The client already owns the property to which the mortgage is being transferred.
- The amount transferred is less than the balance outstanding at the time of transfer.
- The client chooses not to transfer the loan on existing terms.
- Additional borrowing is requested on the transferred amount terms.
- The client or new property does not meet current lending criteria.

### Additional Borrowing

- Must be on a separate non-flexible product from the current range.
