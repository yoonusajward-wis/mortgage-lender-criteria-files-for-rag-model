# Suffolk Building Society – Buy-to-Let and Holiday Let Criteria

---

**LENDER:** Suffolk Building Society
**PRODUCT:** Buy-to-Let and Holiday Let Mortgages
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Lending Criteria
**LAST UPDATED:** 2025
**FOR INTERMEDIARY USE ONLY**

**Source:** https://www.suffolkforintermediaries.co.uk/lending-criteria/?type=btl

---

## SECTION 1 – APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY

- Minimum age: 18 at application.
- No maximum age at end of mortgage term (applicants over 75 may need Independent Legal Advice regarding lending into later life, benefits and long-term care).
- Maximum applicants: 2 (JBSP: UK maximum 4 applicants; Expat maximum 2 applicants).
- Credit reference agency used: TransUnion.

### Residency and Nationality

- Foreign nationals with Indefinite Leave to Remain: accepted on standard criteria.
- Foreign nationals with settled status (EU Settlement Scheme): accepted.
- EU/EEA (including Iceland, Norway, Liechtenstein) and Swiss with pre-settled status: potentially accepted; must have been in UK for 12 months; valid UK work permit/visa with 12 months remaining; must work in: Education, Healthcare, Finance & Banking, IT, Legal, Marketing Professionals, Science Research & Development, Energy Sector, Construction Industry Professionals (not trades) e.g. Architects.
- Foreign nationals without ILR: maximum 80% LTV; same pre-settled criteria as above (12 months in UK, 12 months remaining on visa, specified sectors).
- Visas: Family Visa and Skilled Worker Visa (formerly Tier 2) potentially considered; Skilled Worker applicants should be of professional standing and working in the sectors listed above — discuss with Business Development Team.
- Returning to UK – employed: potentially considered where applicant is returning and going straight into employment with the same company — contact Business Development Team.

### Expat

- Expat BTL and Expat Holiday Let: accepted up to 80% LTV.
- UK taxpayer status used to determine ICR required.
- Self-employed applicants not accepted for Expat applications.
- Most countries and currencies acceptable (excluding UN sanctioned or war-torn areas).
- Regulated BTL (Expat): full affordability applies; income must be in: Sterling, Euro, Swiss Franc, Norwegian Krone, US Dollar, Canadian Dollar, Singapore Dollar, Hong Kong Dollar, UAE Dirham, Kuwaiti Dinar, Qatari Riyal, New Zealand Dollar, Danish Krone, Swedish Krona, Saudi Riyal.
- Minimum income: £25,000 UK£ equivalent.
- UK bank account required.
- Address history: 3 years (UK or abroad).
- Gifted deposits not accepted for Expat applications.
- Source of funds outside UK: expat applications considered where deposit originates from the country the applicant is domiciled in; funds must be in the UK prior to completion.

### First-Time Buyer BTL

- Accepted for both Expat and UK-based applicants.
- Standard BTL: rent must cover loan via ICR; precautionary affordability check also carried out.
- Expat BTL: rent must cover loan via ICR; underwriter approval required; no separate affordability assessment.

### Joint Borrower Sole Proprietor (JBSP)

- Accepted for UK and Expat on BTL.
- UK: maximum 4 applicants; Expat: maximum 2 applicants.
- Joint borrower(s) should be close family relative(s).
- Any applicant may provide the deposit (including non-proprietor). If non-proprietor gifts the deposit they cannot reside in the property.
- Fee assisted legals not available on JBSP applications.

### CIFAS and Criminal Convictions

- CIFAS: customers on the CIFAS register can be considered — refer cases.
- Criminal convictions: conviction must be spent and cannot be monetary related (e.g. fraud, money laundering). Applicant must be able to obtain home insurance on standard terms.

## SECTION 2 – CREDIT HISTORY

- Arrears: historic arrears considered; no arrears in the last 3 years.
- Missed payments: none in the last 12 months.
- CCJs: ignored if satisfied for more than 12 months. Refer to Business Development Team if more than 1 CCJ or CCJs over £1,000 up to 80% LTV.
- Defaults: ignored if satisfied for more than 12 months. Refer to helpdesk if more than 1 default, or defaults over £1,000 up to 80% LTV / over £500 above 80% LTV.
- Arrangements: up to 75% LTV; must have been satisfied 3 years.
- Bankruptcy: must have been discharged for more than 3 years; maximum 75% LTV; no further court orders registered.
- Debt Management Plans: must have been satisfied 3 years; maximum 75% LTV.
- Debt Relief Order (DRO): up to 75% LTV; must have been satisfied 3 years.
- IVA: up to 75% LTV; must have been satisfied 3 years.
- Payday loans: refer details (frequency, value, time period) to Business Development Team.

---

## SECTION 3 – INCOME AND AFFORDABILITY

### Minimum Income

- Minimum £25,000 for UK and Expat (if joint, one applicant must earn minimum £25,000).
- Holiday Let: minimum income £25,000; must be owner occupier (owner occupier not required for Expat Holiday Let).
- Income primarily from benefits: potentially accepted; some income taken at 50% or not at all; applicant must meet minimum £25,000 per annum.

### BTL ICR Calculation

- Stressed rate: pay rate + 2% (minimum 5.5%) or the 5-year rate.
- Non-UK taxpayers: 125% of stressed mortgage payment.
- Basic rate taxpayers: 125% of stressed mortgage payment.
- Higher or additional rate taxpayers: 145% of stressed mortgage payment.
- Joint application: worst case scenario used.
- Arrangement fees can be added to the loan above maximum LTV provided overall loan fits within ICR.

### Topslicing

- Not accepted currently.

## SECTION 4 – LOAN PARAMETERS

- Maximum loan: £1 million (larger loans — contact Business Development Team).
- Maximum LTV: 80% for BTL and Holiday Let (product dependent for Holiday Let).
- Minimum term: 6 years.
- Maximum term: 30 years.
- Minimum property value: £100,000.
- Mortgage offers: standard and new build offer validity 6 months; extensions available — contact if required.
- Overpayment: fee-free overpayments up to 50% of original loan (subject to product).
- Portability: majority of mortgages portable as standard — check product criteria.
- Back-to-back transactions: property must have been owned for 6 months before remortgage application accepted.
- Changes to application post offer: potentially allowed; subject to further underwrite and possibly additional admin fee — contact Business Development Team.

---

## SECTION 5 – DEPOSITS

- Gifted deposit accepted for UK applicants (must be from immediate family members).
- Gifted deposits not accepted for Expat applications.
- Capital raising on another property: considered; funds cannot have originated from Suffolk BS.
- Concessionary purchase: treated as transaction under value; applicant must provide deposit in line with product (e.g. 80% LTV).
- Vendor-paid deposits: in some circumstances accepted, maximum 5%; acceptable where developer pays as part of marketing and valuer confirms full valuation; for small independent builder written confirmation that paying deposit is part of marketing activity required; applicant must also provide 15% deposit.

---

## SECTION 6 – PROPERTY CRITERIA

- Property location: England and Wales only.
- Actual rent vs valuer's rent: current rent from current tenant is not used for affordability when it exceeds the valuer's estimate.

### Flats and Apartments

- Standard flats: 80% LTV.
- New build flats: maximum 80% LTV.
- Maximum 10 storeys.
- Lift required if over 5 storeys.
- Ex-local authority flats: not accepted.
- Minimum lease: 85 years at application.
- Flats above commercial: accepted subject to suitable commercial nearby — contact Business Development Team.

### Other Property Types

- Basement flats: potentially considered; maximum 10 storeys including basement — refer to Business Development Team.
- Coach house: considered; freehold/leasehold and flying freehold to be determined.
- Ex-local authority: houses/bungalows accepted; flats/maisonettes not accepted.
- Grade 1 & 2 listed: potentially considered subject to valuer's comments.
- Next to commercial: potentially accepted — contact Business Development Team.
- Properties needing refurbishment: considered; LTV on current value; detailed costings/quotes at application; property must be liveable/mortgageable at application; only non-structural works; savings evidenced for first 6 months mortgage payments — contact Business Development Team.
- Restrictions: agricultural restrictions potentially accepted — maximum 50% LTV — contact Business Development Team.
- Solar panels: accepted; if leased, mortgage lender repossession break clause required in contract.

### EWS1 and Cladding

- EWS1 forms required.
- No lending on combustible cladding or materials on balconies without EWS1.
- A1, A2 and B1 acceptable subject to valuer's comments.
- Maximum 10 storeys.
- EWS1 forms issued by Tri Fire Ltd not accepted.

### New Build Warranties

- Acceptable: NHBS Guarantee; Premier Guarantee Scheme; Building Life Plans; Build-Zone (includes BuildCare); Checkmate (Castle 10); ICW; LABC; Professional Consultants Certificate.
- Professional Consultants Certificate: Architect's certificate accepted where supervised by a qualified person (Conveyancer must ensure supervisor is member of: RICS (FRICS/MRICS), IStructE (F.I.Struct.E/M.I.Struct.E), CIOB (FCIOB/MCIOB), ASI (FASI/MASI), ABE (FB.Eng/MB.Eng), CIAT, Architect registered with ARB, ICE (FICE/MICE)). Conveyancer must obtain copy of Professional Indemnity insurance with cover of not less than six years.

## SECTION 7 – TENANCY AND LETTING

- Tenancy: Assured Shorthold Tenancy; minimum 6 months AST; maximum 36 months AST.
- Maximum 4 individuals on tenancy (if unrelated professionals, maximum 3).
- Property must be let via standard AST and not classed as HMO.
- Sitting tenants: potentially accepted where tenancy was standard AST and not Protected Tenancy — speak to Business Development Team.
- DWP/Housing Benefit tenants: can consider — contact Business Development Team.
- Property let via Airbnb (Holiday Let): accepted; lettings agent (not Airbnb) must provide known or anticipated letting value; same rental coverage assessment as standard holiday let.

---

## SECTION 8 – HOLIDAY LET

- Accepted; maximum 80% LTV (product dependent).
- Minimum income: £25,000.
- Must be owner occupier (not required for Expat).
- Affordability: average of low, medium and high season weekly rent × 30 weeks (35 weeks if let as holiday let for last 2 years by applicants), divided by 12 for average monthly rental, then standard ICR.
- Lettings agent letter required for known or anticipated rental (not Airbnb).
- Personal use: up to 60 days permitted.

---

## SECTION 9 – SPECIAL APPLICATIONS AND REMORTGAGE PURPOSE

- Consumer BTL: accepted; treated same as standard BTL.
- Let to Buy: accepted.
- Let to Rent: potentially considered where applicant lets existing residential home and moves into rented accommodation — contact Business Development Team.
- Moving in with family: potentially considered BTL remortgage where property is currently applicant's main residence but will be let and applicant moving in with family.
- Non Owner Occupier BTL: potentially accepted — contact Business Development Team. Holiday Let would need to be owner occupier.
- Regulated BTL: acceptable for UK and Expat; full affordability assessment instead of rental calculation; see Expat section for acceptable currencies.

### Remortgage or Additional Borrowing Purpose

- Home improvements: accepted.
- Purchase of BTL or Holiday Let: accepted (≤80%: no simultaneous completion; >80%: simultaneous completion required).
- Purchase property in limited company: must be SPV; all applicants must be named directors and shareholders.
- Repay Help to Buy and let the property: accepted.
- Buy out another party: accepted.
- Debt consolidation: not accepted.
- Extending a lease: accepted.
- Raising money to gift: accepted (£100,000 or over to be referred).
- Paying off residential mortgage: accepted.
- HMO conversion back to standard property: potentially accepted — contact BDM.
- Medical expenses: can consider — contact BDM.
- School fees: can consider — contact BDM.

---

## SECTION 10 – PORTFOLIO LANDLORDS

- Portfolio landlord applications not accepted.
- Maximum 3 properties per applicant (including limited company and including unencumbered and mortgaged).

---

## SECTION 11 – OTHER

- Private local authority searches: potentially accepted if carried out by suitably qualified search agent with adequate indemnity insurance or search insurance protecting the Society, solicitor confirms title good and marketable, and search firm subscribes to the Search Code (Property Code Compliance Board).
