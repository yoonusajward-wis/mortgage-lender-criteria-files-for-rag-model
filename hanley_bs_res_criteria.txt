# SECTION 1 – APPLICANT AGE

## Minimum Age

**All loan types except Retirement Interest Only (RIO):**
Minimum: 18.

**Retirement Interest Only (RIO):**
Minimum: 55.

---

## Maximum Age

**All loan types except RIO:**
Maximum: 85.
Condition: The mortgage must finish before the 85th birthday of the oldest applicant.

**RIO:**
Maximum: No maximum.

---

## Earned Income Age Limit

**Condition:**
Earned income will not be considered beyond age 75.

If the mortgage term extends beyond age 75:

* Affordability must be based on retirement income.

---

## Retirement Planning Requirement

**Requirement:**
Applicants’ plans for working beyond state pension age must be discussed, assessed for reasonability, and documented.

# SECTION 6 – ACCEPTABLE CREDIT HISTORY

**Applicable To:**
Standard, Shared Ownership, Further Advance, Buy to Let, Self-Build, Custom Build.

---

## CCJs and Defaults

**Maximum:** 1 CCJ and 1 Default per applicant.

**Requirement:**
Must have been satisfied at least 12 months prior to application.

---

### CCJ / Default Amount Criteria

**Up to £500**

* Registered in last 12 months: Not acceptable
* Registered 1–3 years ago: Acceptable
* Registered over 3 years ago: Acceptable

**£501 – £2500**

* Registered in last 12 months: Not acceptable
* Registered 1–3 years ago: Not acceptable
* Registered over 3 years ago: Acceptable

**Above £2500**

* Not acceptable in all cases.

---

### Additional Conditions

* Applicants with more than 1 CCJ/Default: Considered case-by-case.
* CCJs/defaults satisfied for less than 12 months: Considered individually.

---

## Mortgage / Loan Arrears

### Below 80% LTV

* Maximum: 3 missed payments in last 24 months.
* Maximum status: 2 at any one time in 24-month period.
* Requirement: Clear of arrears for previous 12 months.

### Above 80% LTV

* Requirement: Clear of mortgage/loan arrears for 24 months prior to application.

---

## Rent Arrears

**Requirement:**
Clear of arrears for 12 months prior to application.

---

## Missed Payments – Revolving Credit

Includes: mail order, credit cards, mobile phone, overdrafts.

### Below 80% LTV

* Maximum: 3 missed payments in last 12 months.
* Total missed payments must not exceed £500.
* No missed payments in last 6 months.

### Above 80% and up to 95% LTV

* Must meet below 80% criteria; AND
* No account may have had missed payment status greater than 1 in last 12 months.

### Above 95% LTV

* No missed payments in last 12 months.

---

## Overdrafts

**Not acceptable (generally):**

* Use of unauthorised overdrafts.
* Exceeding arranged overdraft or credit limit.

Condition: Further approval may be required.

---

## Undisclosed Credit

Not acceptable.

---

## Pay Day Loans

Applicants who have entered into a pay day loan agreement within the last 24 months:
Not acceptable.

---

## Debt Management Plans (DMP)

Applicants must have redeemed the DMP in full more than 24 months ago.

---

## Bankruptcy and Debt Relief Order (DRO)

Applications acceptable where:

* Discharged for minimum 6 years.

---

## Individual Voluntary Arrangements (IVA)

Applications acceptable where:

* Discharged for minimum 3 years.

---

## Repossession

Not acceptable.

# SECTION 5.2 – ACCEPTABLE EMPLOYED SOURCES OF INCOME AND PROOF REQUIRED

---

## Employed

### Criteria

* Must have been continuously employed for at least 3 months.
* Must be on a permanent contract.

### Proof Required

* Latest 3 months payslips (or equivalent if paid weekly).
* Most recent P60.

### Income Used

* 100% of basic salary.
* 100% of guaranteed contractual shift allowance.

---

## Bonus / Overtime / Commission (BOC)

### Criteria

* Amount considered depends on:

  * Length of time in role.
  * Whether payments are guaranteed.
* If additional payments exceed basic salary:

  * Additional payments will be limited to no more than the basic annual salary.

### Length of Service Rules

* Guaranteed BOC:

  * Minimum 3 months with current employer.
* Regular BOC:

  * Minimum 2 years with current employer.
* Regular BOC:

  * Minimum 1 year with current employer.

### Proof Required

* Employment reference requested.

If employment reference cannot be obtained, payslip requirements are:

* Weekly paid: 52 payslips.
* Monthly paid: 12 months payslips showing additional payments.
* Quarterly paid: 4 payslips.
* Half-yearly paid: 2 payslips.
* Annually paid: 1 payslip.

### Income Used

* Guaranteed BOC: 100%.
* Regular BOC (2+ years): 100%.
* Regular BOC (1+ year): 50%.

---

## Fixed Term Contract

### Criteria

* Must have been continuously employed on a fixed-term contract basis for minimum 12 months.
* Must be in the same line of work.
* At least 6 months remaining on current contract.
* Contract must be directly with the company providing services.
* Contracts via third party not acceptable.

### Proof Required

* Latest 3 months payslips (or equivalent if weekly).
* Current contract.
* Previous contract.

### Income Used

* 100% of basic salary.

---

## Zero Hours Contract

### Criteria

* Minimum 12 months continuous employment with same employer.
* Must have a contract of employment.
* Not acceptable on an Interest Only basis.
* Not acceptable for Near Prime applications.

### Proof Required

* Latest 6 months payslips (or equivalent if weekly).
* Contract of employment.

### Income Used

* 100% of average pay.
* Calculation method:

  * Total 6 months income ÷ 6 × 12.

---

## Bank Hours (NHS Professionals Only)

Applies to doctors and nurses only.

### Criteria

* Minimum 12 months continuous employment on bank hours basis.
* Must have a contract of employment.
* Not acceptable on an Interest Only basis.
* Not acceptable for Near Prime applications.

### Proof Required

* Latest 6 months payslips (or equivalent if weekly).
* Contract of employment.

### Income Used

* 100% of average pay.
* Calculation:

  * Total 6 months income ÷ 6 × 12.

---

## Agency Workers

### Criteria

* Minimum 12 months continuous employment.
* Must have contract of employment.

### Proof Required

* Latest 6 months payslips.
* Employment reference from agency.

### Income Used

* 100% of average pay.
* Calculation:

  * Total 6 months income ÷ 6 × 12.

---

## Self-Employed

Not acceptable for Ex Pat lending.

### Criteria

* Self-employed for minimum 2 years.

### Proof Required

* Latest 2 years audited/certified accounts.
* Latest 2 years tax calculations.
* Latest 2 years tax overviews.
* Accountant’s reference (requested by Society).
* Latest 3 months business bank statements (to identify impact of covid/lockdown).

### Income Used

* Average of last 2 years net profit; OR
* Average of last 2 years tax calculations,
* Whichever is lower.
* If latest year is lower than average:

  * Lower figure used.
* Declining profits:

  * Not normally acceptable.
* 1 year accounts:

  * Not accepted.

### Income Used

# 5.8 AFFORDABILITY

* The amount the Society will lend is based upon an applicant's income and expenditure.
* The Society uses ONS data to determine some areas of spending.
* ONS data is based on:

  * Household composition.
  * The geographical area of the property.
* The Society will review:

  * Credit Report.
  * Bank Statements.
* Adjustments will be made where necessary if additional expenditure is identified, for example gambling transactions.
* A stressed mortgage rate is applied when calculating affordability.
* Exception: where the mortgage rate is fixed for 5 years or more.
* Affordability is the overriding factor when assessing a mortgage.
* Income multiples are used as a guide.
* The maximum income multiple is 5 x joint income.
* For all Buy to Let applications where rental income (confirmed by the valuer) does not meet the rental criteria interest coverage ratio (ICR):

  * A full affordability assessment is required.
* There are specific affordability requirements for RIO applicants.

---

# 5.8.1 FINANCIAL COMMITMENTS

The following monthly commitments and balances outstanding must be declared as part of the application:

* Bank Loans
* Hire Purchase
* Car Loans
* Student Loans
* Second Mortgages
* Buy to Let Mortgages
* Child Maintenance
* Car lease agreements
* Nursery education
* Education fees
* Any other financial commitment

Credit card, store card and mail order accounts:

* 3% of the current balance will be used as the monthly commitment.

Commitments may be ignored in affordability calculations if:

* There is less than 3 months remaining to run.
* This includes Klarna / PayPal Pay in 3.

---

# 5.8.2 GAMBLING

* Gambling transactions will be considered for affordability.
* Bank statements will be checked.
* An average of the amount gambled over the last 3 months will be used.
* Additional bank statements may be requested at the underwriter's discretion.

---

# 5.8.3 BANK STATEMENTS

* Where possible, Open Banking will be used to check bank statements.
* If Open Banking is not used:

  * The latest 3 months’ bank statements for each applicant are required.
* Bank statements must show:

  * Salary credits.
  * Other regular expenditure.
* Additional bank statements may be requested by the Underwriter to support the application.

# 5.9 INCOME MULTIPLES

* Single applicant:

  * Income x 5

  * Joint income x 5

# 5.11 BACKGROUND PROPERTIES / BUY TO LETS

* Background properties / buy to lets are not permitted for shared ownership lending.

---

# 5.11.1 BACKGROUND BUY TO LET

* Applicants must have no more than 3 BTL properties per application.

Background BTL will be treated as self-funding where:

* There is at least 3 months rental history, and

  * AST and bank statements showing rental income are required; and
* The rent received is equal to or more than the stressed mortgage payment.

  * Example calculation: £100,000 x current stressed rate x 145%.

If an existing BTL is let on an HMO basis:

* The rent must be evidenced via tax assessments.

The expenditure associated with a Background BTL will be included in affordability if:

* There is no rental history.

  * In this case, the stressed mortgage payment will be used in affordability.
  * No rental income will be considered; and/or
* There is a shortfall between rent received and stressed mortgage payment.

  * The shortfall amount will be included in affordability.

---

# 5.11.2 BACKGROUND PROPERTY (NOT BTL)

* The mortgage (if applicable) will be included in affordability calculations at the current pay rate.
* Additional household costs such as:

  * Council tax
  * Heating
  * Other similar costs
* These will be considered in affordability.

# 5.12 LENDING IN RETIREMENT

Lending in retirement applies where:

* All borrowers are a minimum of 60 years old.
* Borrowers have already retired.
* The only source of income is:

  * Retirement income; and
  * Income derived from investments, including rental property.

Maximum Loan to Value (LTV):

* 70%

# 5.13 LENDING INTO RETIREMENT

Lending into retirement applies where the mortgage term will extend beyond the applicant’s stated retirement age or over the age of 75.

# 6.2 TENURE

---

# 6.2.1 FREEHOLD

Freehold tenure is acceptable for houses and bungalows in England, Wales and Scotland.

Freehold flats and maisonettes are unacceptable.

---

# 6.2.2 LEASEHOLD

Leasehold tenure is acceptable for houses, bungalows, flats and maisonettes in England and Wales.

The unexpired lease term at valuation must be at least 85 years.

Leasehold tenure is acceptable provided that ground rent, service charges and the review process are reasonable as confirmed by the valuer.

Examples of unacceptable terms include:

* Ground rent escalation linked to the value of the property or an index greater than the Retail Price Index (RPR).
* Current ground rent of 0.5% or higher of the property value.
* Rent review period of 5 years or less.
* Ground rent doubling in less than 20 years.
* Ground rent exceeding £250 (£1000 in Greater London).
* For leases issued after June 2022, any ground rent payable other than Peppercorn Rent.
* Disproportionate service charges.

The above list is not exhaustive. The Society will be guided by the valuer and Solicitor when determining whether a leasehold property is acceptable.

## Acceptable for Residential, BTL, Self-Build and Renovation/Conversion

The following property types are acceptable for all four lending types:

* Houses and bungalows of standard construction.
* Grade II and Grade II* buildings, subject to valuation.
* Modern timber framed properties with external brick skin.
* Properties with a thatched roof.
* Modern oak framed properties.
* Houses with a Flying Freehold, subject to valuation and legal opinion.
* Modern steel frame properties, subject to referral and valuer’s comment to HLC.
* Solar panels, provided:

  * Where subject to a lease, confirmation from the Society’s Solicitor and valuer that the lease does not affect the Society’s security.
* Private water supply, subject to certification confirming the water is fit for consumption.

  * Local authority testing required.
  * Shared private water supplies referred to solicitors if anomalies arise.
* Properties with 100% flat roof, subject to referral and valuer’s comments to HLC (excluding “living roof” such as grass or vegetation planting).
* Septic tanks, where:

  * Shared septic tanks require formal documented responsibilities for maintenance and repair/replacement.
  * Confirmation that the tank complies fully with statutory requirements.

## Not Acceptable for Any Lending Type

The following are not acceptable for Residential, BTL, Self-Build or Renovation/Conversion:

* Properties of non-traditional construction deemed defective under the Housing Defects Act 1984 which have not been modernised under an approved scheme.
* Properties with ongoing structural movement.
* Back-to-back terrace or cluster properties.
* Woolaway houses as named in the Building Research Establishment Report 1983 and built between 1945 and 1960.
* High Alumina Cement properties.
* Smallholdings.
* Holiday lets.
* Property or land with commercial use.
* Properties designated as HMO.
* Land only in any circumstances.
* Live/Work units.
* Over 55s residential to retirement properties.
* Log homes, including:

  * Erland House log homes
  * Jim Barna log homes
  * Easiform cavity walled concrete houses
  * Wimpey no fines
  * Elliot eHouse
  * PRC systems designed defective
* Properties with spray foam insulation.

# 10.2 AFFORDABILITY

## Standard Buy to Let

Affordability assessed using anticipated rental income confirmed by valuer.

Interest Coverage Ratio (ICR): 145% using current stressed mortgage rate.

Example:
£100,000 x 7.17% x 145% = £9,657 / 12 = £804.75

Rental income must be equal to or greater than £804.75 to meet ICR.

If ICR not met:

* Top slicing may be considered.
* Full affordability check undertaken.
* Case will refer in system.

Personal income assessed to meet minimum income requirements.

## Consumer Buy to Let

Affordability assessed using:

* Applicant's net income.
* Rental income from mortgaged property.
* Stressed mortgage payment.
* ICR calculation.

Personal income assessed to ensure ability to meet commitments and expenditure.

The lease must be a Model Lease Agreement.

# 12.6.1 ALIENATION PROVISIONS

The lease must:

* Ensure the shared owner cannot sell part or all of the property except through the specified sales procedure.
* Prevent sub-letting or parting with possession of part of the property.

These provisions are required to protect public funds and prevent commercial gain.

---

# 12.6.2 MORTGAGE PROTECTION CLAUSE

The lease must include a Mortgagee Protection Clause.

The Mortgagee Protection Clause:

* Covers the lender’s loss, including from the Provider’s share where appropriate.
* Defines the amount that mortgagees can claim as the "Mortgagee Protection Claim".
* Must allow a mortgagee in possession to:

  * Assign the lease as it is; or
  * Staircase to 100% and then assign.
* There must be no time limits in the lease regarding staircasing.

---

# 12.6.3 RENT REVIEW

All Shared Ownership leases must include rent review provisions.

---

# 12.6.4 SHARED OWNERSHIP STAIRCASE

* Approval from the provider (Housing Association).
* A minimum 10% tranche to be purchased.
* Use of a solicitor.
* A new valuation being obtained.

---

# 12.6.5 RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL (PRE-EMPTION RIGHT)

The originating landlord may:

* Exercise the option to buy back the property at prevailing market value; or
* Nominate another provider to do so.

This applies to pre-final staircasing situations to secure the property for affordable housing use.

Remaining lease term must be at least 85 years at the point of mortgage completion.

* Share being purchased.
* Ground rent.
* Rent payable.
* Service charges.

---

# 12.10 RENT

Shared Ownership rent, service charges and ground rent:

* Must be disclosed on the mortgage application form.
* Will be treated as a commitment for affordability purposes.

---

# 12.11 STAIRCASING

Borrowers may staircase to 100% ownership subject to:

* Approval from the provider (Housing Association).
* A minimum 10% tranche.
* Use of a solicitor.
* A new valuation.

---

# 12.12 SHARED OWNERSHIP PERMITTED SCHEMES

The following schemes are not acceptable:

* Older persons Shared Ownership.
* Designated protected/rural areas.
* Home ownership for people with long term disability (HOLD).

---

# 12.13 ADDITIONAL BORROWING

Additional borrowing is permitted only for staircasing.

Approval from the Housing Association is required before applying with the Society.

---

# 13 RETIREMENT INTEREST ONLY CRITERIA (RIO)

Retirement Interest Only (RIO) is an interest only mortgage for older customers where repayment of capital is not required until a specified life event, usually death or moving into residential care.

---

# 13.1 INCOME

Applicants should:

* Already be retired.
* Be in receipt of retirement income at the point of application.

Applicants who are working may be considered, however:

* Earned income will not be taken into account.
* Affordability will be based on retirement income only.

---

# 13.2 AFFORDABILITY

For joint applications:

* Each applicant must be able to afford the mortgage in their own right.

---

# 13.3 RESTRICTIONS

* For applications with a Lasting Power of Attorney, the applicant must be mentally capable of making financial decisions.
* Applications submitted by the Attorney on behalf of the applicant are not allowed.
* Near Prime applicants will not be considered.

---

# 13.4 OTHER CONSIDERATIONS

If the customer has to move into a temporary home in the future:

* They may need to pay both mortgage and home costs.
* Affordability assessment will consider potential rate rises.
* Affordability assessment will consider potential future changes in the customer's circumstances.

# 14 FURTHER ADVANCE CRITERIA

---

# 14.1 REGULATED V PRE REGULATED APPLICATIONS

Pre G40000:

* Solicitors required.
* Additional borrowing permitted for home improvements only.

G40000 onwards and Pre-Regulated M Accounts:

If the above accounts require additional borrowing for capital raising:

* Treated as a completely new mortgage.

Regulated Mortgage Accounts:

* Additional borrowing permitted for home improvements and capital raising.

# 14.2 RESTRICTIONS

# 15 SELF-BUILD CRITERIA

The following criteria applies to Self-Build lending.

---

# 15.1 PERMITTED LENDING AND RESTRICTIONS

## 15.1.1 Permitted Lending

* New build (ground up).
* Demolition and re-build.
* Renovation/Conversion where the property is currently uninhabitable, including barn conversions.
* All of the above on a second home.

## 15.1.2 Restrictions

* Investment properties are excluded.
* Applications must be for owner occupier only.
* Must be an individual project and not part of a group development.
* Properties offering a Retrospective Warranty are not acceptable.
* Partially completed projects which have changed ownership part way through are not acceptable.
* Where land is divided into plots and sold to individuals:

  * Individual planning permission required.
  * Confirmation applicant is employing their own builder required.
  * Confirmation that an individual warranty will be provided required.
  * Costs must include infrastructure installation.

---

# ECO SELF-BUILD

Criteria are the same as standard self-build plus:

1. Property must achieve EPC rating A or B.
2. Must include at least two of the following energy efficient features:

   * Photovoltaics.
   * Solar panels.
   * Air source heat pump.
   * Ground source heat pump.
   * Rainwater harvesting.
   * Greywater collection.
   * Triple glazing.
   * Mechanical ventilation with heat recovery system.
   * Biomass pellet boiler.
   * Wind turbines.
   * Higher than usual levels of thermal insulation.
   * Geothermal heating.
   * Compostable toilet.
   * Electric car charging stations.

---

# 15.2 SPECIALIST ADVISORS

The Society will use:

* A Valuer.
* A Warranty provider.
* A Quantity Surveyor.

The Quantity Surveyor will:

* Review costings.
* Assess feasibility.
* Provide written recommendation to accept or decline costings.

---

# 15.3 PARTIALLY COMPLETE PROPERTIES / PROJECTS

Minimum stage required:

* Self-build: wall plate level.
* Renovation/Conversion: full structural overhaul including roofing and damp-proof course.

Warranty requirements:

* Must be in place at outset.
* Retrospective warranties are not acceptable.

Warranty sign-off required at application stage:

* New build / Re-build: foundations and wall plate sign off and building regulation approval.
* Renovation / Conversion: full structural overhaul sign off and building regulation approval.

---

# 15.4 DURATION OF PROJECT AND TIMESCALES

* Work must commence within 6 months of mortgage completion.
* All works must be completed and final warranty sign off received within 2 years from first release of funds.

---

# 15.5 SECOND HOMES

Second home definition:

* Used by borrower(s) for residential purposes only.
* Examples: holiday home, weekend home, future retirement property.

Restrictions:

* Cannot be let out.
* Cannot be in same location as current residence.
* Properties within same postcode are not acceptable.
* Full affordability for 2 households required.
* Only 1 second home permitted.
* Not permitted for Shared Ownership applicants.

Specialist advisors / project managers required with full details provided.
